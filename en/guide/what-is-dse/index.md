# What is the DSE? Complete Guide for International Students

The Hong Kong Diploma of Secondary Education (HKDSE), commonly known simply as the DSE, is Hong Kong's sole public examination for senior secondary students. If you are considering studying in Hong Kong, or if you hold a DSE qualification and want to understand how it is recognised internationally, this guide covers everything you need to know.

## A Brief History: Why Was the DSE Created?

Before 2012, Hong Kong ran a two-tier examination system inherited from its British colonial era:

- **HKCEE** (Hong Kong Certificate of Education Examination) — taken at the end of Form 5 (roughly equivalent to GCSE level)
- **HKAL** (Hong Kong Advanced Level Examination) — taken at the end of Form 7, the gateway to university

This structure required students to sit two rounds of high-stakes public exams over three years, causing significant stress and inefficiency. In line with a broader curriculum reform and the global shift toward 12 years of pre-university education (the "3-3-4" structure — three years junior secondary, three years senior secondary, four years university), Hong Kong merged the two exams into a single qualification: the HKDSE, first awarded in 2012.

The change aligned Hong Kong more closely with international norms, making the DSE more comparable to A-Level, IB Diploma, and other pre-university qualifications recognised worldwide.

## Grade Structure

The DSE uses a **level-based grading system**, not a percentage score. Each subject is graded on a scale of 1 to 5, with the top performers receiving starred grades:

| Grade | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 5** | Outstanding (top ~1–2% of candidates in each subject) |
| 5* | Excellent |
| 5 | Good |
| 4 | Proficient |
| 3 | Adequate |
| 2 | Basic |
| 1 | Minimal |
| U | Unclassified (not graded) |

For JUPAS university admissions, each grade is converted to a numerical score (5** = 7 points, 5* = 6, 5 = 5, and so on), and most universities consider a student's Best 5 subjects for their overall score. The maximum possible score is 35 points.

## Subject List

DSE subjects fall into two categories:

**Core Subjects (compulsory for all):**
- Chinese Language
- English Language
- Mathematics (Compulsory Part)
- Citizenship and Social Development (replaced Liberal Studies in 2021 reform)

**Elective Subjects (typically 2–3 chosen):**

Students select from a wide range including:
- Sciences: Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Combined Science
- Humanities: Chinese History, History, Geography, Economics, Tourism and Hospitality Studies, Ethics and Religious Studies
- Business: Accounting, Business, Management and Business Strategy
- Mathematics Extension: Module 1 (M1, Calculus & Statistics) and Module 2 (M2, Algebra & Calculus)
- Arts: Visual Arts, Music
- Applied Learning (ApL): vocational and professional education pathways

## University Admissions: JUPAS and Non-JUPAS

### JUPAS (for local students)

JUPAS (Joint University Programmes Admissions System) is the primary route for Hong Kong secondary school leavers applying to government-funded (UGC-funded) universities. Students submit preferences for up to 20 programmes across eight institutions, and offers are made based on DSE results released in July.

Most programmes require a minimum of "3322" — Level 3 in Chinese and English, and Level 2 in two other core subjects — as the basic eligibility threshold. Competitive programmes such as Medicine or Law typically require scores of 30+ out of 35 and may specify minimum grades in particular subjects (e.g., Chemistry Level 5 for medicine).

### Non-JUPAS (for non-local and international students)

Students who did not take the DSE — including holders of A-Levels, IB Diploma, SAT/ACT, Gaokao, or other international qualifications — can apply via Non-JUPAS routes directly to individual universities. Each institution sets its own equivalency standards and selection criteria.

DSE holders who missed the JUPAS cycle (e.g., due to repeating a year or applying late) may also use Non-JUPAS channels.

## How DSE Compares to Other Qualifications

| Feature | DSE | A-Levels (UK) | IB Diploma | SAT (US) |
|---------|-----|---------------|------------|----------|
| Duration | 3 years senior secondary | 2 years post-GCSE | 2 years | Test-based (no fixed duration) |
| Number of subjects | 4 core + 2–3 electives | Typically 3 subjects in depth | 6 subjects + core components | 1 exam (Math + Evidence-Based Reading) |
| Grading | Level 1–5** | A*–E | 1–45 points | 400–1600 |
| University recognition (HK) | Primary route (JUPAS) | Widely accepted (Non-JUPAS) | Widely accepted (Non-JUPAS) | Accepted at some institutions |
| University recognition (global) | Growing, mainly for HK unis | Global (UK, Commonwealth, beyond) | Global | Primarily US |

**Key differences to note:**
- A-Levels allow students to study 3 subjects in great depth; DSE requires breadth across 6–7 subjects with less depth per subject.
- The IB Diploma places strong emphasis on extended essay writing and the Theory of Knowledge component; DSE has no direct equivalent.
- For students applying to UK universities from Hong Kong, A-Levels remain more universally understood, though UCAS has clear conversion tables for DSE results.

## International Recognition of DSE

While the DSE was designed primarily for admission to Hong Kong universities, it is increasingly recognised by institutions in the UK, Australia, Canada, and mainland China. UK universities, for example, typically equate DSE Level 5** and 5* to A-Level grade A* and A respectively.

Mainland China universities accept DSE results for Non-JUPAS admission under the national scheme for Hong Kong, Macao, and Taiwan students, and the DSE is sometimes accepted as an alternative to the Gaokao for Hong Kong students seeking mainland admission.
