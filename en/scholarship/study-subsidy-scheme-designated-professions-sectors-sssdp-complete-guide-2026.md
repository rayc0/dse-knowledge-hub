# Study Subsidy Scheme for Designated Professions/Sectors (SSSDP) Complete Guide 2026/27

For DSE students who do not secure a UGC-funded place at one of Hong Kong's eight public universities, the self-financing sector is the most common fallback. The problem is cost: a self-financing bachelor's degree often runs HK$90,000–130,000 per year in tuition, more than three times the roughly HK$42,100 charged for a government-funded programme. The **Study Subsidy Scheme for Designated Professions/Sectors (SSSDP)** exists precisely to close that gap for students entering fields where Hong Kong faces a manpower shortage.

This guide explains how SSSDP works for the 2026/27 cohort, exactly how much you save, which programmes qualify, who is eligible, and how to apply through both JUPAS and non-JUPAS channels.

## What SSSDP Is

SSSDP is a recurrent government scheme administered by the Education Bureau (EDB). It directly subsidises the tuition of students enrolled in selected **self-financing** undergraduate and sub-degree programmes that train graduates for ten (for degrees) industries identified as having keen manpower demand. The subsidy is paid to the institution and deducted from the tuition you owe — you do not receive cash, and you do not repay it. Because it is tied to designated programmes rather than to the student's financial circumstances, **SSSDP is not means-tested**: every admitted student in a subsidised place receives the same subsidy regardless of family income.

It is important to understand what SSSDP is *not*. It is not a scholarship awarded for academic excellence, and it is not a loan. It is a tuition discount attached to specific programme places. If you are admitted into an SSSDP-designated place, the subsidy is automatic.

## How Much You Save in 2026/27

For the 2026/27 academic year, the annual subsidy amounts for **undergraduate** programmes remain unchanged from the previous year:

| Programme type | Annual subsidy (2026/27) |
|---|---|
| Non-laboratory-based degree | HK$46,780 |
| Laboratory-based degree | HK$81,450 |
| Applied degree — non-laboratory-based | HK$51,880 |
| Applied degree — laboratory-based | HK$89,620 |

"Applied degree" programmes — a newer vocationally-oriented degree category combining academic study with substantial workplace learning — receive an additional top-up on top of the standard rate, which is why their figures are higher.

For **sub-degree** (higher diploma / associate degree) programmes, the 2026/27 annual subsidy amounts are:

| Programme type | Annual subsidy (2026/27) |
|---|---|
| Non-laboratory-based sub-degree | HK$23,390 |
| Laboratory-based sub-degree | HK$40,730 |

To put the degree figures in context: if a self-financing laboratory-based programme charges HK$120,000 in annual tuition and is SSSDP-designated, you pay roughly HK$38,550 out of pocket — comparable to, and sometimes lower than, the tuition at a UGC-funded university. Over a four-year programme, a laboratory-based subsidy is worth more than HK$325,000. This is the single largest reason ambitious students treat SSSDP places as genuine alternatives to public-university offers rather than a consolation prize.

The subsidy is granted for the normal duration of the programme and applies to **both new and continuing eligible students**, so a student admitted in 2026/27 continues to receive it in subsequent years provided they remain in good standing and progress normally.

## Scale of the 2026/27 Scheme

The 2026/27 cohort is sizeable:

- **Undergraduate:** 4,781 subsidised places across 57 programmes — 3,425 places in 57 first-year-first-degree (FYFD) programmes and 1,356 places in 45 top-up degree (TUD) programmes. Sixteen of these are applied degree programmes receiving enhanced support.
- **Sub-degree:** 2,131 subsidised places across 32 sub-degree programmes offered by six post-secondary institutions.

The number of places per programme is capped, which matters strategically: a popular SSSDP programme can fill its subsidised quota before all admitted students are covered, so competitive applicants should treat these places as selective.

## The Designated Disciplines

For 2026/27 undergraduate programmes, SSSDP covers ten disciplines identified as facing strong manpower demand:

1. Architecture and engineering
2. Computer science
3. Creative industries
4. Financial technology (FinTech)
5. Healthcare
6. Insurance
7. Logistics
8. Sports and recreation
9. Testing and certification
10. Tourism and hospitality

Sub-degree programmes cover a narrower set of seven disciplines: architecture and engineering, computer science, creative industries, healthcare, sports and recreation, testing and certification, and tourism and hospitality.

Healthcare is consistently the largest and most sought-after cluster, including nursing, occupational therapy, physiotherapy, medical laboratory science, and other allied-health programmes — fields where Hong Kong's ageing population drives persistent demand. FinTech, computer science, and testing and certification have expanded in recent cohorts in line with government priorities.

## Participating Institutions

SSSDP-designated programmes are delivered by self-financing degree-awarding institutions and the self-financing arms of public universities. Institutions that regularly feature in the scheme include Hong Kong Metropolitan University (HKMU), the Hang Seng University of Hong Kong (HSUHK), Hong Kong Shue Yan University, Tung Wah College, Hong Kong Chu Hai College, Caritas Institute of Higher Education, the Technological and Higher Education Institute of Hong Kong (THEi under VTC), and the self-financing colleges affiliated with HKU SPACE and other universities. The exact list of designated programmes and their place quotas is published each year by the EDB and on the dedicated SSSDP portal run by the Committee on Self-financing Post-secondary Education (CSPE) — always confirm against the official current-year list before applying.

## Eligibility

The core eligibility conditions are straightforward:

- You must be admitted into a designated SSSDP programme place.
- You must be eligible to study in Hong Kong — in practice this means local students (Hong Kong permanent residents and those with the right to land or to remain that qualifies them as "local"). SSSDP is a local-student scheme; non-local students admitted on a student visa are generally **not** eligible for the subsidy.
- You must not already hold a publicly-funded bachelor's degree or equivalent. SSSDP, like other government tuition support, is aimed at students pursuing their first publicly-supported undergraduate qualification.
- For top-up degree places, you typically need a relevant recognised sub-degree (associate degree or higher diploma) to articulate into the final years of the bachelor's programme.

There is no minimum DSE score set centrally by SSSDP; entry requirements are set by each programme. However, admission into the designated *place* — not merely the programme — is what triggers the subsidy, so meeting the programme's general entrance requirements (commonly the "3-3-2-2" minimum or each institution's stated standard) is the practical threshold.

## How to Apply

There is no separate SSSDP application form. You apply for the programme through its normal admissions channel, and if you are offered a designated place, the subsidy is applied automatically.

**Through JUPAS (for current DSE candidates).** Many SSSDP first-year-first-degree programmes are listed in the Joint University Programmes Admissions System alongside UGC-funded programmes. You add them to your JUPAS programme choices like any other. Allocation to FYFD SSSDP places is **merit-based** through JUPAS, so band placement and your DSE results determine whether you secure a subsidised seat. A practical tip: because SSSDP programmes sit in the self-financing tier but cost about the same as funded programmes after subsidy, they are strong Band B/C "safety" choices for students targeting competitive UGC programmes — see our [JUPAS Band A strategy guide](/en/admission/jupas-band-a-strategy) for how to structure choices around them.

**Through non-JUPAS / direct application.** Top-up degree places and many programmes for sub-degree holders, mature students, and applicants with non-DSE qualifications are filled through **direct institutional admission**. You apply on the institution's own admissions portal, and the institution allocates designated places to qualified admitted students. Deadlines differ by institution and often run on a rolling basis, so apply early.

In both routes, when you receive your offer the institution will state explicitly whether your place is SSSDP-subsidised. If the offer letter does not say so, ask admissions directly before accepting — an identical programme may have both subsidised and non-subsidised seats.

## SSSDP vs Other Funding Routes

It helps to position SSSDP against the alternatives a DSE student faces:

- **UGC-funded place:** Lowest tuition (around HK$42,100/year), most prestigious, most competitive. Always the first choice if your results allow it.
- **SSSDP place:** Self-financing programme with a large fixed subsidy; net cost often near UGC level for laboratory-based programmes. No means test. Excellent value when your target field is on the designated list.
- **Non-means-tested / means-tested loans (NMTSS / TSFS):** Available on top of, or instead of, SSSDP to cover remaining tuition and living costs, but these are loans you repay with interest.
- **Full-fee self-financing place:** No subsidy; highest cost. Only sensible when no funded or SSSDP route into your chosen field exists.

A common and sound strategy is to stack support: take an SSSDP place to slash tuition, then apply separately to the Working Family and Student Financial Assistance Agency (WFSFAA) for grants or loans if you still need help with the residual tuition and living expenses. SSSDP eligibility does not affect your eligibility for those need-based schemes.

## Key Things to Confirm Before You Commit

1. **Is the specific place SSSDP-designated?** Programmes can have a mix of subsidised and unsubsidised seats with the same programme code.
2. **Is the subsidy guaranteed for all years?** It normally is for the standard duration, but confirm progression conditions — repeating a year or changing programme can affect continuation.
3. **Laboratory vs non-laboratory classification.** This determines whether you get the higher or lower rate; the EDB's published list states each programme's classification.
4. **Top-up vs first-year-first-degree.** Sub-degree holders should check the articulation requirements for TUD places and whether their associate degree is recognised.
5. **Use the official current-year list.** Designated programmes and quotas are reset annually; never rely on a prior year's list.

## Bottom Line

SSSDP is one of the most financially significant levers available to Hong Kong DSE students who do not land a UGC-funded seat in a shortage profession. With up to HK$89,620 a year deducted from tuition, no means test, and automatic application through JUPAS or direct admission, an SSSDP place can bring the cost of a self-financing degree down to public-university levels in healthcare, computing, FinTech, engineering, and the other designated fields. Treat designated programmes as serious primary or safety choices in your JUPAS list rather than a last resort, verify the subsidised status of any offer in writing, and always check the EDB's official 2026/27 programme list before you apply.
