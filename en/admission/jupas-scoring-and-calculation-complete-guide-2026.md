# JUPAS Scoring and Calculation Complete Guide 2026

If you are applying to one of Hong Kong's eight UGC-funded universities through the Joint University Programmes Admissions System (JUPAS), the single number that decides whether you receive a conditional offer is your **JUPAS admission score**. But unlike A-Levels or Gaokao, JUPAS does not use one universal score. Each programme decides its own formula — which subjects to count, how many to count, how to weight Mathematics Extended Modules (M1/M2), whether English Language gets multiplied by 1.5, and whether the 6th subject earns half points or full points.

This guide walks you through the entire 2026 JUPAS scoring system, from the raw HKDSE level-to-point conversion through the most common programme formulae, with worked examples for medicine, engineering, business, law and education. By the end you will be able to **calculate three different "versions" of your own JUPAS score** — Best 5, Best 6, and a programme-specific weighted score — and use those numbers to design a realistic Band A list.

## 1. The Foundation: How DSE Levels Convert to JUPAS Points

Every JUPAS score starts with the same conversion from your HKDSE level to a numerical point value. The 2026 conversion remains:

| HKDSE Level | JUPAS Points (Category A subjects) |
|---|---|
| 5** | 8.5 |
| 5* | 7 |
| 5 | 5.5 |
| 4 | 4 |
| 3 | 3 |
| 2 | 2 |
| 1 | 1 |

Two important nuances:

- **5\*\* is worth 8.5, not 7.** A common mistake among new applicants is to assume linear progression (5 → 6 → 7 → 8). The jump from 5* to 5** is bigger than every other step. This is why one extra 5** can make a programme go from "competitive" to "guaranteed" interview.
- **Citizenship and Social Development (CSD) is not counted in the 4C+2X formula.** From the 2024 cohort onwards, CSD is reported as Attained / Attained with Distinction (I or II) and is **excluded from JUPAS score calculation**. You still must achieve at least "Attained" to satisfy the minimum entrance requirement, but it does not contribute points.
- **Applied Learning (ApL)** subjects can be counted as elective subjects in some programmes, with "Attained with Distinction (II)" treated as Level 4 equivalent.

## 2. The 4C+2X Backbone: Minimum Entry Requirement

Before any score calculation begins, every JUPAS applicant must meet the **General Entrance Requirement (GER)**, often written as **332A33** or **3322+33**:

- **Chinese Language**: Level 3
- **English Language**: Level 3
- **Mathematics (Compulsory Part)**: Level 2
- **Citizenship and Social Development**: Attained
- **Two elective subjects**: Level 3 each (electives can include M1, M2, Category B Applied Learning, or Category C other languages)

This is the **floor**, not the target. Failing any one of these — for example, scoring Level 2 in English — means your application is rejected before any university even looks at your subject mix. For competitive programmes such as medicine, the actual cutoff is far higher; the GER is essentially a bar you must clear to be considered at all.

## 3. The Three "Versions" of Your JUPAS Score

Once you meet the GER, your score can be calculated in three different ways depending on the programme:

### 3.1 Best 5 (most common)

The university adds the JUPAS points of your **best five subjects**, regardless of whether they are core or elective. This is the single most common formula and is the default at most non-competitive programmes.

- Maximum: 5 × 8.5 = **42.5 points**
- A 5**5**5*5*5 student would score 8.5 + 8.5 + 7 + 7 + 5.5 = **36.5 points**

### 3.2 Best 5 + Bonus 6th

Some programmes count your **best five** at full value and then add **half** of your 6th best subject's points, or add a small bonus (typically 1–3 points) if the 6th subject is at Level 4 or above.

### 3.3 Best 6 (full weighting)

The university sums the points of all **six subjects** at full value. Maximum is 6 × 8.5 = **51 points**. This formula slightly favours all-rounders who don't have one weak subject.

### 3.4 4C + 2X (rigid)

Some programme formulae lock you into the **four core subjects (Chinese, English, Mathematics, plus M1/M2 in some cases) and your two best electives**. This rewards strong core performance and is common at programmes that explicitly require Physics or Chemistry.

## 4. Programme-Specific Weightings: Where the Real Differences Appear

The reason no two universities publish identical "cutoff scores" for the same student is that **each programme applies its own multipliers** before adding the points. Common patterns:

### 4.1 Medicine (HKU MBBS, CUHK MBChB)

- **Formula**: Best 6 with weighting
- **Required**: Chemistry **and** Biology (most years), plus Mathematics core
- **Weighting**: English Language ×1.5; Chemistry, Biology, Mathematics often ×1.5 or ×2 in CUHK depending on programme variant
- **Typical competitive score**: 42–48 (weighted)
- **Note**: Both medical schools also weight interview performance heavily; a top score does not guarantee admission.

### 4.2 Engineering (HKUST, HKU, PolyU, CityU)

- **Formula**: 4C + 2X or Best 5
- **Required / Recommended**: Physics or Combined Science (Physics), plus M1/M2 strongly preferred
- **M1/M2 weighting**: HKUST commonly applies **×1.5 to M1/M2** in engineering streams; selected programmes apply **×2** for M1/M2 plus another science subject
- **Trade-off**: Without M1/M2, you can still apply but compete against candidates whose scores are ~5–8 points higher after weighting

### 4.3 Business / BBA (HKU, CUHK Global Business, HKUST Business, CityU)

- **Formula**: Best 5 or Best 6 with weighting
- **Required / Recommended**: English Language at Level 5 or above for top programmes
- **Weighting**: English Language ×1.5 or ×2 at flagship programmes (HKU BBA, CUHK Global Business, HKUST Business)
- **Bonus**: Some programmes give M1/M2 ×1.25 weighting

### 4.4 Law (HKU LLB, CUHK LLB, CityU LLB)

- **Formula**: Best 5
- **Required / Recommended**: English Language at Level 5 (HKU expects 5* or above to be competitive)
- **Weighting**: English ×1.5 to ×2; Liberal Studies / CSD's exclusion means humanities electives such as History, Economics, Geography matter more relative to other faculties
- **Note**: HKU and CUHK Law also conduct interviews (and increasingly written assessments)

### 4.5 Education (EdUHK, CUHK Faculty of Education)

- **Formula**: Best 5 + bonus, or Best 6
- **Language weighting**: At EdUHK programmes specialising in language teaching, the corresponding language subject is weighted; English-major programmes weight English ×1.5

### 4.6 Architecture (CUHK, HKU)

- **Formula**: Best 5 + portfolio
- **Required / Recommended**: Mathematics at Level 4+, Visual Arts welcomed
- **Special component**: Portfolio review and interview can shift the effective competitive score by 4–6 points

## 5. Worked Example: A Mainland DSE Candidate Targeting CUHK Medicine

Consider Candidate A with the following 2026 DSE results:

| Subject | Level | Points |
|---|---|---|
| Chinese Language | 5 | 5.5 |
| English Language | 5* | 7 |
| Mathematics (Compulsory) | 5* | 7 |
| Citizenship and Social Development | Attained | (excluded) |
| Biology | 5** | 8.5 |
| Chemistry | 5** | 8.5 |
| Mathematics M2 | 5* | 7 |

### Best 5 calculation
Top 5 subjects: Biology (8.5) + Chemistry (8.5) + English (7) + Math (7) + M2 (7) = **38**

### Best 6 calculation
All 6: 8.5 + 8.5 + 7 + 7 + 7 + 5.5 = **43.5**

### CUHK Medicine weighted (illustrative formula: Best 5 with English ×1.5, Biology ×1.5, Chemistry ×1.5)
- English: 7 × 1.5 = 10.5
- Biology: 8.5 × 1.5 = 12.75
- Chemistry: 8.5 × 1.5 = 12.75
- Math: 7 × 1 = 7
- M2: 7 × 1 = 7
- **Weighted total = 50** (out of a weighted maximum of ~63)

This score places Candidate A within the realistic interview band for CUHK MBChB in recent admissions cycles. Without the M2, the candidate would be replaced with Chinese (5.5) and the weighted total would drop to **48.5** — a meaningful difference at the top end.

## 6. The M1/M2 Decision: Is It Worth Taking?

M1/M2 is one of the most strategically important DSE choices. The rules are:

- **M1/M2 is treated as a Category A elective** for JUPAS purposes
- It can be included in the Best 5 or Best 6 if it's higher than your worst counted subject
- In some formulae, if M1/M2's points exceed your 6th best subject, the 6th best is **replaced by half of itself plus half of the M1/M2 score** (a hybrid rule used in selected programmes)
- Engineering programmes at HKUST and HKU apply ×1.5 to ×2 weighting

**Decision rule**: If you are targeting **engineering, computer science, actuarial science, quantitative finance, or data science**, M1 or M2 is effectively required. If you are targeting medicine, business or law, M1/M2 is **optional but valuable** — taking M2 and scoring 5* is worth more than scoring an extra 5 in a weak elective.

## 7. Language Requirements and Waivers

For mainland and international applicants, two technical issues come up regularly:

- **Chinese Language waiver**: JUPAS allows certain non-local applicants to apply for a Chinese Language waiver, but **not all programmes accept it**. Programmes in Chinese Studies, Translation, Education (Chinese), and selected humanities require full DSE Chinese performance.
- **Category C languages**: French, German, Spanish (CIE/IGCSE A2 or above), Japanese (JLPT N3 or above), Korean (TOPIK Grade 3 or above) and Urdu (Grade E or above) can be counted as elective subjects, typically at Level 3 to Level 5 equivalent depending on the grade. This is a useful way for trilingual mainland and international students to add a 6th elective without an extra DSE paper.

## 8. Common Mistakes That Lower Your Effective Score

Three errors come up every admissions cycle:

1. **Picking electives that don't help**: Choosing a Category A subject you are weak in just to "have six subjects" is counterproductive. A 6th subject scoring Level 3 is worth only 3 points; in Best 5 formulae it is invisible, and in Best 6 it dilutes your average.
2. **Ignoring the language weighting**: Many candidates aiming for law or business optimise their science scores while leaving English at Level 4. Because the multiplier on English is often ×1.5 or ×2, **one extra level in English is worth more than one extra level in any other subject** at these programmes.
3. **Underestimating M1/M2 for engineering**: Without M1/M2, your effective score in HKUST Engineering, HKU Computer Science or PolyU Data Science is calculated against a baseline that *assumes* most competitors have it. Even a Level 4 in M2 contributes 4 × 1.5 = 6 weighted points.

## 9. How to Build Your Personal JUPAS Score Sheet

A workable approach for any DSE candidate:

1. **List your 7 most likely subjects** with realistic predicted levels (be honest — use your mock results, not your hopes)
2. **Calculate three numbers**: Best 5, Best 6, Best 5 + 0.5 × 6th
3. **Pick 3 target programmes** from each university's website and copy their published 2026 admission score formulae
4. **Recompute your weighted score for each programme**
5. **Compare your weighted score against the 25th–75th percentile** of last year's admitted students (published in JUPAS aggregate statistics each November)
6. **Distribute your Band A**: Two reach programmes (your weighted score is at the 25th percentile), two match programmes (50th percentile), one safety programme (above the 75th percentile)

Most universities — HKU, CUHK, HKUST, PolyU, CityU — publish their own JUPAS score calculators online. Use the official tool, not a third-party calculator, as the formulae are revised annually and a tool from two years ago may apply outdated weightings.

## 10. Final Strategic Notes for 2026

The 2026 JUPAS cycle includes several adjustments worth tracking:

- **Engineering subject weighting**: Several engineering departments at the major universities have revised their weightings in 2026 — check each programme's published formula in November 2025 / early 2026 rather than relying on prior-year information
- **CSD continues to be excluded** from score calculation but remains a minimum requirement (Attained)
- **Applied Learning subjects** are increasingly accepted at "Attained with Distinction (II)" as Level 4 equivalent in selected programmes — a useful angle for vocationally-oriented applicants
- **Interview weighting**: At medical, dental and law programmes, interview can carry 30–50% of the final admission decision. Your JUPAS score gets you to the interview; the interview gets you the offer.

Treat your JUPAS score as a moving target tied to specific programmes, not a single number. The student who scores 36 with a perfectly weighted profile for HKUST Engineering will outcompete a student who scores 38 with a profile mismatched to the formula. Calculate three versions of your score, map them to three programme tiers, and revisit the calculations after every mock exam and after the official DSE results release in mid-July.
