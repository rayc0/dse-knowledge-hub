## Why English Proficiency Matters for Hong Kong Admission

Hong Kong's eight UGC-funded universities — HKU, CUHK, HKUST, PolyU, CityU, HKBU, Lingnan, and EdUHK — teach almost entirely in English at the undergraduate level. Whether you are a mainland Chinese student applying through the Gaokao or independent admission track, an international school graduate, or a local DSE candidate seeking a non-JUPAS route, you will need to prove English proficiency through a recognised standardised test.

The two dominant tests are **IELTS Academic** (administered by the British Council, IDP, and Cambridge) and **TOEFL iBT** (administered by ETS). Both are accepted at all eight universities, but the minimum required scores, accepted test formats, and practical logistics differ. Choosing the wrong test — or misunderstanding the new TOEFL scoring system introduced in January 2026 — can delay your application or force a costly retake.

## The 2026 TOEFL Scoring Overhaul — What Changed

On **21 January 2026**, ETS launched a redesigned TOEFL iBT with a new **1–6 band score scale** (in half-point increments) aligned to the Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR). This replaces the legacy 0–120 total score.

### Key changes at a glance

| Feature | Old TOEFL (pre-Jan 2026) | New TOEFL (from 21 Jan 2026) |
|---|---|---|
| **Overall score range** | 0–120 (sum of four sections) | 1–6 bands (average of four sections) |
| **Section score range** | 0–30 per section | 1–6 per section (half-point increments) |
| **CEFR alignment** | Indirect concordance | Direct band-to-CEFR mapping |
| **Test duration** | ~2 hours | ~85 minutes (shorter, adaptive format) |
| **Score reports** | 0–120 only | Both new 1–6 band AND old 0–120 shown side by side (transition period 2026–2028) |
| **Score availability** | 4–8 days | ~72 hours |

### Score conversion reference

During the transition period, both old and new scores appear on your score report. Here is the official ETS concordance:

| New Band | Old 0–120 Equivalent | Approximate CEFR |
|---|---|---|
| 6.0 | 114–120 | C2 |
| 5.5 | 107–113 | C1+ |
| 5.0 | 95–106 | C1 |
| 4.5 | 86–94 | B2+ |
| 4.0 | 72–85 | B2 |
| 3.5 | 58–71 | B1+ |
| 3.0 | 44–57 | B1 |

**Why this matters**: A university that previously required "TOEFL 80" now states a new-scale equivalent. The numbers are not always a clean match — for example, old 80 falls in the Band 4.0 range, but old 86 pushes into Band 4.5. Read each university's updated requirement carefully.

### Important observations

1. **HKU is the outlier**: IELTS 6.5 and TOEFL 93 is significantly higher than every other university. If HKU is your top choice, you need to prepare for a higher bar.
2. **CityU and CUHK sit in the middle**: They require IELTS 6.5 (like HKU) but accept a lower TOEFL of 79–80. This creates a gap — if you are stronger at TOEFL, CityU/CUHK is relatively more TOEFL-friendly than HKU.
3. **HKUST's new TOEFL band of 4.5**: On the old scale this equates to approximately 86, which is higher than the previous 80. If you are targeting HKUST with a TOEFL taken after January 2026, aim for Band 4.5 or above.
4. **Home editions and online tests are almost universally rejected**: The only exception is that some universities accept them "for reference" — they do not satisfy the formal requirement. Always book a centre-based test.

## Which Test Should You Take? Decision Framework

### Choose IELTS if:

- **You are also applying to UK universities** — UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI) requires IELTS for Tier 4 student visa applications. If Hong Kong is your backup and the UK is your primary target, taking IELTS covers both.
- **You prefer face-to-face Speaking** — IELTS Speaking is a 11–14 minute conversation with a human examiner. Many test-takers find this more natural than speaking into a microphone.
- **You are stronger at British English conventions** — IELTS reading passages may include British spelling and vocabulary. If you studied in a British-curriculum school, this can feel more familiar.
- **Your target university requires IELTS 6.0** — For HKUST, PolyU, HKBU, Lingnan, and EdUHK, IELTS 6.0 is a moderate bar that many applicants can clear with 4–8 weeks of preparation.
- **You want more test centre options in Hong Kong** — IELTS offers 14+ venues across Hong Kong and Macau through British Council and IDP.

### Choose TOEFL if:

- **You are also applying to US or Canadian universities** — TOEFL is the default in North America. One test covers HK + US/Canada applications.
- **You want a shorter test day** — At 85 minutes (2026 format), TOEFL is nearly half the duration of IELTS. Less fatigue, especially for younger test-takers.
- **You prefer typing over handwriting** — All TOEFL responses are typed. IELTS Writing is also typed on computer-delivered tests, but paper-based IELTS still requires handwriting.
- **You perform better on multiple-choice Reading/Listening** — TOEFL Reading and Listening are predominantly multiple-choice, while IELTS includes sentence completion, matching, and short-answer formats.
- **You want faster results** — TOEFL 2026 delivers scores in approximately 72 hours, compared to 3–13 days for IELTS.

### Consider taking BOTH if:

- You are applying to HKU (TOEFL 93 / IELTS 6.5) AND UK universities (IELTS only for UKVI). You might sit IELTS for your UK applications and TOEFL as a backup for HK if your IELTS score falls short of 6.5.
- You sat one test and scored below your target. Rather than retaking the same test, switching to the other format may play to different strengths (e.g., switching from IELTS Writing Task 1 chart description to TOEFL's email-response format).

## Test Preparation Tips Specific to Hong Kong Admissions

### 1. Start early — at least 6 months before the application deadline

Most Hong Kong universities close non-JUPAS applications between November and January for September entry. Work backwards:

- **September–October**: Take the test for the first time
- **November–December**: Retake if needed (scores usually arrive in 3–8 days)
- **January**: Submit application with final scores

### 2. Understand the "one sitting" rule

HKU, PolyU, HKUST, and others explicitly require scores achieved "in one sitting" or "in the same attempt." TOEFL's MyBest Scores feature (which combines best section scores across multiple tests) is **not accepted** at most Hong Kong universities. Similarly, IELTS One Skill Retake is rejected at all universities except Lingnan.

### 3. Check validity windows

Both IELTS and TOEFL scores are valid for **2 years** from the test date. Most universities require the score to be valid as of September 1 of the entry year. A test taken before September 2024 will not be valid for 2026 entry.

EdUHK has a stricter rule: the test must be dated **on or after 1 January 2024** for 2026/27 entry.

### 4. Register through official channels only

- **IELTS in Hong Kong**: British Council (britishcouncil.hk) or IDP (ielts.idp.com/hongkong)
- **TOEFL in Hong Kong**: HKEAA (hkeaa.edu.hk) is the authorised administrator; register via the ETS website
- **IELTS in mainland China**: British Council via the NEEA IELTS portal (ielts.neea.cn)
- **TOEFL in mainland China**: NEEA TOEFL portal (toefl.neea.cn)

### 5. Aim above the minimum

Published minimums are the floor, not the target. Competitive applicants to HKU and CUHK typically hold IELTS 7.0+ or TOEFL 100+ (old scale) / Band 5.0+ (new scale). A score at the minimum may not be enough when competing against applicants from English-medium international schools.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Can I submit Duolingo English Test (DET) scores to Hong Kong universities?**
A: As of 2026, no UGC-funded university in Hong Kong accepts Duolingo as a primary English qualification for undergraduate admission. EdUHK explicitly rejects it. Some universities may accept it "for reference" alongside a centre-based IELTS or TOEFL, but it cannot replace them. If Duolingo is your only English test, you will need to sit IELTS or TOEFL before applying.

**Q: Is PTE Academic accepted?**
A: CUHK accepts PTE Academic (minimum 64, no band below 60). Other universities may accept it on a case-by-case basis, but IELTS and TOEFL remain the safest choices across all eight institutions.

**Q: I took TOEFL before January 2026. Will my old 0–120 score still be valid?**
A: Yes. Scores remain valid for two years regardless of the scoring change. During the 2026–2028 transition period, ETS score reports display both the legacy 0–120 score and the new 1–6 band score side by side. Universities will accept either format as long as the score was obtained within the validity window.

**Q: My IELTS is 6.0 but HKU requires 6.5. Can I still apply?**
A: You can apply, but your application may be automatically screened out if HKU enforces the minimum strictly. It is strongly recommended to retake the test and achieve 6.5 or higher before the application deadline. Some faculties at HKU may exercise discretion for borderline scores if other credentials are exceptional, but this is not guaranteed.

**Q: Does the IELTS score need sub-scores above a certain level for HK universities?**
A: At the undergraduate level, most Hong Kong universities state only an overall minimum band score without sub-score requirements. However, individual programmes (especially in law, journalism, or education) may set higher sub-score requirements. Always check the specific programme's admission page.

**Q: Can I combine IELTS and TOEFL scores from different sittings?**
A: No. Each university requires the English proficiency requirement to be met by a single test result from one sitting. You cannot combine an IELTS Writing score with a TOEFL Speaking score, or combine scores from two different IELTS sittings (except at Lingnan, which accepts IELTS One Skill Retake).

**Q: I already have a high DSE English score. Do I still need IELTS/TOEFL?**
A: If you are applying through JUPAS using DSE results, you do not need IELTS or TOEFL — the DSE English exam fulfils the English requirement. IELTS/TOEFL is required for non-JUPAS applicants, international qualifications holders, and mainland Chinese students applying through direct admission schemes.

**Q: How does the new shorter TOEFL format affect my preparation?**
A: The 2026 TOEFL is approximately 85 minutes (down from ~2 hours), uses an adaptive format, and includes revised task types (e.g., an email-writing task replaces the independent essay). Study materials published before 2026 may not reflect the new format. Use official ETS practice materials updated for the January 2026 changes.
