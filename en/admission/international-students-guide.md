# Complete Guide for International Students Applying to Hong Kong Universities 2026

Hong Kong has quietly become one of Asia's most compelling destinations for international undergraduates. Four of its universities consistently place in the global top 100. The city operates under a common law framework, conducts most degree programmes in English, and sits at the intersection of mainland China and the wider world — a combination that is genuinely difficult to replicate elsewhere.

For students educated outside Hong Kong's local curriculum (HKDSE), the admissions landscape is navigable but requires deliberate planning. This guide covers every stage of the journey: selecting the right pathway, meeting qualification thresholds, submitting a competitive application, budgeting for four years, securing a student visa, and understanding your options after graduation.

---

## 1. Why Study in Hong Kong?

### Academic standing

The 2025 QS World University Rankings place the University of Hong Kong (HKU) at 17th globally, HKUST at 47th, and CUHK at 51st. These are not honorary placements — research output, employer reputation scores, and faculty-to-student ratios all contribute to consistent top-50 results across major ranking systems.

### Language

The majority of degree programmes at HKU, HKUST, and HKBU are delivered entirely in English. At CUHK, PolyU, and CityU, English is the medium for most programmes with Cantonese or Mandarin appearing in specific professional or local-oriented streams. International students who are not Cantonese speakers function comfortably in academic and professional settings.

### Location and career access

Hong Kong is the world's third-largest financial centre by assets under management. Graduates from HK universities have structured pathways into finance, technology, logistics, and trade roles across both the Hong Kong and Greater Bay Area job markets. The IANG visa (covered in Section 11) allows graduates a renewable 24-month post-study work period with no employer sponsorship requirement.

### Costs relative to peers

Tuition fees for international students run approximately HKD 145,000–198,000 per year depending on programme and institution. This is meaningfully lower than equivalent programmes at UK Russell Group universities (GBP 30,000–45,000/year) or comparable US institutions.

## 3. Application Pathways for International Students

### 3.1 Non-JUPAS Direct Application

JUPAS (Joint University Programmes Admissions System) is the centralised platform used by students sitting the Hong Kong Diploma of Secondary Education (HKDSE) examination. **International students who hold non-HKDSE qualifications do not use JUPAS.** They apply through each university's own Non-JUPAS admissions channel.

Accepted qualifications under Non-JUPAS vary by institution but broadly include:

- **International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma**
- **GCE A-Level** (UK, Singapore, and other international variants)
- **SAT + AP courses** (US curriculum)
- **Australian Tertiary Admission Rank (ATAR)** with applicable subject prerequisites
- **National Certificate of Educational Achievement (NCEA)** — New Zealand
- **Canadian provincial transcripts** (Ontario OSSD and equivalents)
- **European Baccalaureate**
- **Other nationally-recognised secondary leaving certificates** assessed on a case-by-case basis

Each university publishes specific minimum entry requirements for each qualification type. These can be verified on the relevant admissions pages linked above.

### 3.2 Mainland China Gaokao Route

The National College Entrance Examination (Gaokao / 全国高考) is treated as a distinct admissions route — separate from the general Non-JUPAS process. HKU, CUHK, HKUST, PolyU, CityU, HKBU, and LU all accept Gaokao applicants.

Key features:

- **Quota-based**: Each university has a fixed annual intake quota for Gaokao applicants. Total Gaokao intake across all eight UGC universities is subject to UGC guidelines.
- **Score benchmark**: Most HK universities require scores significantly above the national 一本线 (First-tier cut-off line). Indicative requirements are 一本线 + 30 to 100 points depending on programme and university, though exact thresholds are announced each year by each institution.
- **Direct application**: Gaokao applicants apply directly to HK universities using their 高考成绩单 and relevant supporting documents. Applications typically open in June after national scores are released.
- **Interview**: Several programmes at HKU and HKUST require a Mandarin or English-medium interview for Gaokao applicants.
- **Scholarship overlap**: Gaokao applicants are eligible for the same scholarship pools as other Non-JUPAS applicants at most institutions.

Mainland students enrolled in international curricula (IB, A-Level) or studying at international schools in China should apply via the Non-JUPAS pathway, not the Gaokao route.

### 3.3 IB Direct Entry

The IB Diploma is among the most widely recognised and strongly preferred qualifications at HK universities. IB applicants apply through the Non-JUPAS system (not a separate stream), but are typically assessed with higher admission rates relative to other qualification types, particularly at HKU and HKUST.

## 4. Required Qualifications by Programme Area

### 4.1 Medicine and Health Sciences

Medical programmes in HK are among the most competitive in Asia.

**HKU MBBS (Medicine)**
- IB: Minimum 38–42 points; mandatory HL Biology and Chemistry at grade 6 or above
- A-Level: A\*A\*A with Chemistry and Biology required
- SAT: 1540+ with strong AP Chemistry and AP Biology (5s expected)
- Gaokao: Top provincial scorers only; science stream required; typically within top 0.5% of provincial candidates
- Additional: BMAT or HKUMSQ entrance assessment; English interview

**CUHK Medicine (MBChB)**
- IB: 38–42 points; HL Chemistry and Biology (grade 6+)
- A-Level: A\*AA minimum
- Additional: UCAT ANZ strongly recommended; medical interview mandatory

### 4.2 Business and Finance

**HKU BBA (Business Administration)**
- IB: 35+ overall; HL Mathematics (Analysis and Approaches) grade 5+
- A-Level: AAB minimum; Mathematics preferred
- SAT: 1480+ with Mathematics score 750+

**CUHK Business Administration**
- IB: 34–36; HL subjects in relevant disciplines
- A-Level: ABB–AAB
- SAT: 1430–1500

**HKUST Business (incl. BBA, Finance)**
- IB: 36–40; HL Mathematics required for quantitative tracks
- A-Level: AAA for most BBA programmes; Mathematics required
- SAT: 1500+

### 4.3 Engineering and Computer Science

**HKUST Engineering / Computer Science**
- IB: 36–40; HL Mathematics and Physics required; Chemistry advantageous
- A-Level: AAA; Mathematics and Physics required
- SAT: 1500+ with 780+ in Mathematics

**HKU Engineering**
- IB: 35–38; HL Mathematics and Physics required
- A-Level: AAB–AAA; Mathematics and one science required
- SAT: 1470+

**CityU Computer Science**
- IB: 32–36
- A-Level: ABB–AAB

### 4.4 Arts, Humanities and Social Sciences

Requirements are generally lower than for STEM and professional programmes, with greater emphasis on written assessments, portfolios (for fine arts, design), and the personal statement.

**HKU Arts (BA programmes)**
- IB: 32–36
- A-Level: ABB–AAB; relevant humanities subjects preferred

**HKBU Communication / Journalism**
- IB: 30–34
- A-Level: ABB

All minimum scores above are indicative and based on published institutional guidelines and historical data. Verify directly with each university's admissions office before applying, as thresholds can change year to year.

## 5. English Language Requirements

### Standard requirements

Almost all UGC universities require proof of English proficiency for applicants whose medium of prior education was not English. Standard benchmarks:

| Test | Typical minimum | Competitive score |
|---|---|---|
| IELTS Academic | 6.0–6.5 overall (no band below 5.5) | 7.0+ for top programmes |
| TOEFL iBT | 80–90 | 100+ for top programmes |
| Cambridge C1 Advanced | Grade C | Grade B or A |
| Cambridge C2 Proficiency | Grade C | Any grade |
| Duolingo English Test | 105 | 120+ |

Programme-specific requirements frequently exceed institutional minimums. HKU Medicine, for example, typically requires IELTS 7.0+ or TOEFL iBT 100+. Always confirm requirements for your specific target programme.

### English proficiency waivers

You are generally exempt from English tests if:

1. Your secondary education was **conducted entirely in English** for at least three years immediately prior to applying (this includes students at English-medium international schools)
2. You hold qualifications from a country where English is an official language and the medium of instruction — this commonly includes the UK, Ireland, USA, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Singapore, and South Africa
3. You have completed (or are completing) GCE A-Levels or IB Diploma in English — these are often treated as sufficient evidence of English competency

Submit documentary evidence (school transcripts noting language of instruction, or a letter from your school principal) alongside your application if claiming a waiver.

## 6. Application Timeline — Month by Month

The admissions cycle for most UGC universities follows a common pattern, though specific deadlines vary. Plan for the following:

| Month | Action |
|---|---|
| **August–September** (Year 12 / final year) | Research programmes; shortlist 5–8 universities and programmes; request teacher recommendation letters |
| **October** | Most universities open Non-JUPAS applications; begin drafting personal statement; request predicted grades from school |
| **October–November** | Submit applications to early-closing programmes (HKU typically closes in late November/early December for most programmes) |
| **November–December** | Submit remaining applications; confirm English test booking if not yet taken |
| **December–January** | Receive conditional or unconditional offers from some universities; respond to any document requests |
| **January–February** | Sit final examinations (IB, A-Level internal assessments); final English tests |
| **February–March** | Supplementary admission rounds; some universities issue further offers |
| **March–April** | IB and A-Level predicted grades submitted; accept preferred offer |
| **May–July** | Final IB/A-Level results released; universities confirm places; arrange student visa |
| **August** | Arrive in Hong Kong; orientation programmes; register for courses |

Gaokao applicants follow a compressed timeline: Gaokao takes place in early June; scores are released mid-June; HK university applications for Gaokao applicants open in June–July with offers typically made by July–August.

## 7. Required Documents Checklist

Prepare all documents well before the application deadline. Most universities require:

- [ ] **Online application form** completed via the university's admissions portal
- [ ] **Application fee** (typically HKD 200–400 per programme application)
- [ ] **Certified copies of academic transcripts** (years 10–12 or equivalent; official translation to English if originals are in another language)
- [ ] **Predicted grades / school report** (issued by your school on official letterhead)
- [ ] **Personal statement** (500–1,000 words; programme-specific motivation and fit)
- [ ] **Teacher reference letters** (typically 1–2; written in English on school letterhead; sealed and submitted directly or via the application portal)
- [ ] **English language test score report** (IELTS/TOEFL certificate; or proof of waiver eligibility)
- [ ] **Copy of valid passport** (main page)
- [ ] **Proof of nationality** (national ID or passport)
- [ ] **Programme-specific materials**: supplementary portfolio (Design, Architecture, Fine Arts), research proposal (certain BBA or senior entry programmes), BMAT / UCAT score (Medicine)
- [ ] **Photograph** meeting official specifications
- [ ] **Gaokao score notification** (高考成绩单) — Mainland applicants only

Documents must typically be submitted as PDF uploads through each university's portal. Physical certified copies may be required at the point of enrolment confirmation. Keep originals safe.

## 9. Scholarships for International Students

Financial support for non-local undergraduates has expanded significantly in recent years. Below are the major university-administered scholarships that international students are typically eligible for:

### HKU

**HKU Foundation Entrance Scholarship**
- Value: Up to HKD 120,000 per year (renewable for up to 4 years)
- Eligibility: Exceptional academic achievement; open to all non-local students
- Application: Automatically considered upon full application to HKU; no separate form

**HKU Presidential Scholarship for Global Excellence**
- Value: Full tuition coverage for the degree period
- Eligibility: Highest-achieving applicants globally; requires additional nomination

### CUHK

**Vice-Chancellor's Scholarship**
- Value: HKD 120,000 per year (renewable)
- Eligibility: Top non-local undergraduate applicants; GPA equivalent 3.8+ or IB 40+
- Application: Separate scholarship application via CUHK admissions portal

**CUHK Merit Award**
- Value: HKD 40,000–80,000 per year
- Eligibility: Strong academic standing; broader eligibility than Vice-Chancellor's Scholarship

### HKUST

**HKUST President's Scholarship (Non-local)**
- Value: Full tuition waiver + housing subsidy for 4 years
- Eligibility: Top 1–2 candidates per admissions cohort; exceptional academic and extracurricular record
- Process: Invited to a scholarship assessment day

**HKUST Academic Excellence Award**
- Value: HKD 50,000–100,000 per year
- Eligibility: Automatically considered based on admissions application

### PolyU, CityU, HKBU

Each institution offers merit-based entrance scholarships in the range of HKD 20,000–80,000/year for high-achieving non-local students. Applications are typically embedded in the regular admissions process.

### External scholarships

- **Hong Kong Scholarship for Excellence Scheme (HKSES)**: Government-funded; for students from economies with existing bilateral agreements. Check with your home country's education ministry.
- **Belt and Road Scholarship**: Targeted at students from economies along the Belt and Road Initiative; covers tuition and living allowances. See: [www.ugc.edu.hk/eng/ugc/activity/br/scholarship.html](https://www.ugc.edu.hk/eng/ugc/activity/br/scholarship.html)

Apply for all relevant scholarships at the same time as submitting your admissions application. Do not wait for an offer before starting scholarship applications.

## 10. Student Visa Application Process

International students admitted to a full-time undergraduate programme in Hong Kong require a student visa issued by the Immigration Department (IMMD).

**Process overview:**

1. **Receive unconditional offer letter** from your university (issued after final results)
2. **University lodges visa application on your behalf** through the Document Submission and Processing Centre (DSPC) system — you do not apply directly to IMMD yourself
3. **Provide documents to the university's admissions or registry office:**
   - Completed visa application form (Form ID 995A)
   - Passport copy (main page)
   - Two recent passport-size photographs
   - Proof of academic qualifications (certified transcripts and certificates)
   - Financial proof — bank statements or sponsorship letter demonstrating ability to fund your studies. IMMD requires evidence of at least HKD 220,000 per year or equivalent for tuition plus living expenses
   - Letter of acceptance from the university
4. **University submits the application** to IMMD via DSPC
5. **Processing time**: Typically 6–8 weeks. Apply no later than 6 weeks before your intended arrival date
6. **Visa granted**: You receive a visa label in your passport. Your study visa is typically valid for the duration of your programme plus a short buffer

Students from certain nationalities may be eligible for visa-on-arrival or pre-clearance arrangements. Confirm your nationality-specific requirements at: [www.immd.gov.hk/eng/services/visas/study.html](https://www.immd.gov.hk/eng/services/visas/study.html)

**Important**: Do not travel to Hong Kong on a tourist visa and attempt to convert to a student visa on arrival. Begin the student visa process as soon as you receive your offer.

## 11. After Graduation: IANG Visa

The Immigration Arrangements for Non-local Graduates (IANG) is one of Hong Kong's most graduate-friendly post-study work policies globally.

**Key features:**

- **No job offer required at point of application**: Graduates can apply for IANG immediately upon graduation without having secured employment
- **Initial validity**: 24 months, allowing unrestricted work rights in HK
- **Renewal**: Extendable based on demonstrated employment or evidence of continued contribution to HK's economy. Employment-based renewals have been routinely granted for qualifying graduates
- **Policy trajectory**: The HKSAR Government has periodically expanded the scope and conditions of IANG as part of its talent attraction strategy. The 2023 policy update extended flexibility for graduates from non-local universities with HK campuses — verify current conditions at [www.immd.gov.hk/eng/services/visas/IANG.html](https://www.immd.gov.hk/eng/services/visas/IANG.html)
- **Path to permanent residency**: Graduates who remain employed and ordinarily resident in HK for 7 years may apply for the right of abode as permanent residents

**Eligibility**: Must have completed a full-time locally-accredited degree programme at a HK-based institution (includes all eight UGC universities).

The IANG visa, combined with the breadth of employment opportunities in finance, technology, and trade, makes Hong Kong one of the most direct post-study pathways in Asia from graduation to long-term residency.

## 12. Common Mistakes and Rejection Reasons

Understanding why applications fail is as useful as knowing what makes them succeed.

### Academic threshold miscalculations

Many applicants underestimate programme-level requirements. Institutional minimums published on websites are floor thresholds — actual competitive offers are typically made to students meaningfully above those minimums. For top programmes at HKU, HKUST, and CUHK, assume you need to be in the top 5–15% of applicants from your qualification type.

### Applying too late

Applications to HK universities are accepted on a rolling basis in most cases. Early applications receive earlier offers, and strong early candidates lock in places before later applicants receive consideration. Aim to submit all applications before November.

### Weak personal statement

A personal statement that is generic, reads as a list of achievements, or does not address the specific programme and why Hong Kong specifically is your destination will not distinguish you. Admissions tutors read thousands of applications; clarity of motivation and programme fit are weighted heavily.

### Inadequate English proficiency evidence

Submitting an IELTS score of 6.0 for a programme with a stated minimum of 6.0 is technically compliant but weakens your application. Competitive candidates for flagship programmes at HKU or HKUST typically present 7.0+. Retake the test if necessary.

### Missing predicted grades

Many international school students assume their final results will be sufficient. HK universities make conditional offers based on predicted grades during application, before final results are available. Ensure your school submits a formal predicted grades report.

### Not requesting recommendation letters early enough

Experienced referees — particularly at international schools during busy periods — may need 4–6 weeks to write a meaningful letter. Request letters in September for November application deadlines. A generic letter from a busy teacher is worse than a specific, warm letter from one who knows you.

### Ignoring programme prerequisites

Engineering at HKUST requires HL Mathematics and Physics in the IB — not just a general IB score. Submit an application without meeting prerequisite subject requirements and it will be rejected regardless of your total score. Read the subject prerequisites on the programme page, not just the headline score.

### Visa delays due to financial documentation

IMMD requires evidence of financial capacity. Applications delayed by insufficient bank statements or unclear sponsorship letters push visa processing past safe arrival windows. Prepare financial documentation as soon as you receive an offer.

## 13. Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I apply to multiple universities simultaneously?**
Yes. There is no restriction on how many non-JUPAS applications you submit. Apply to 4–8 programmes across 3–5 institutions to maintain optionality. Pay each application fee separately.

**Is the application fee refundable?**
No. Application fees at HK universities are non-refundable regardless of outcome.

**Do I need to submit my final IB or A-Level results before receiving an offer?**
No. Most offers are conditional, based on predicted grades. Conditional offers are confirmed or withdrawn after final results are released (July for IB; August for UK A-Levels).

**Can I defer my offer by a year?**
Some programmes permit deferral for compelling personal reasons (medical, family). This is discretionary. Contact the admissions office of the specific programme. Gap year deferral is less commonly granted in HK than at UK universities.

**What if my final results fall slightly below my conditional offer requirements?**
Contact the admissions office immediately. Some programmes will maintain an offer if results miss by a narrow margin, particularly if the rest of your application is strong and you accept quickly. Do not wait.

**Are there part-time work restrictions on a student visa?**
Student visa holders in Hong Kong are permitted to work part-time for up to 20 hours per week during term time and full-time during official vacation periods, without needing a separate work authorisation.

**Can mainland Chinese students apply via both Gaokao and Non-JUPAS routes?**
No. Mainland applicants must choose one route. If you are completing a Gaokao-based curriculum, you apply via the Gaokao route. If you are completing an international curriculum (IB, A-Level, international school), you apply via Non-JUPAS.

**Is there a minimum age requirement?**
No explicit minimum age, but applicants must have completed a full secondary education equivalent to HKDSE Year 12 or equivalent to be considered for undergraduate entry.

**Is studying through Chinese medium possible for international students?**
Yes. CUHK offers significant Chinese-medium teaching, particularly in humanities. Lingnan University has programmes in both mediums. For students fluent in Mandarin or Cantonese, this can be an advantage.

**What is the difference between non-local and international student status?**
In HK, "non-local student" is the official classification used for fee and quota purposes and includes mainland Chinese, Macau, Taiwan, and foreign passport holders. The term "international student" is colloquially used but is not a formal legal classification at HK universities.
