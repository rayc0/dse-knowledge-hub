# JUPAS Explained 2026 — How Hong Kong's University Admission System Actually Works

Almost every Hong Kong DSE student who wants a government-funded bachelor's degree goes through one system: the **Joint University Programmes Admissions System (JUPAS)**. It is the single channel through which local HKDSE candidates apply to the nine participating institutions and their publicly funded (and SSSDP-subsidised) full-time undergraduate programmes.

If you have already read our scoring guide or Band A strategy guide, this article is the missing piece: the **end-to-end mechanics** — what JUPAS is, the exact timeline for the 2026 cycle, how the band structure works, and how an offer actually lands in your account. Understanding the machine before you read the strategy guides will make every later decision sharper.

## What JUPAS is (and is not)

JUPAS is a **centralised matching system**, not an admissions office. It does not decide who gets in — the nine institutions do that, using their own scoring formulae and programme-specific requirements. JUPAS simply collects your one set of programme preferences, passes your data to every institution you applied to, and then enforces a single rule that makes the whole thing work: **you receive at most one offer**, and it is always for the highest-priority programme that is willing to take you.

The nine JUPAS participating institutions are HKU, CUHK, HKUST, PolyU, CityUHK, HKBU, Lingnan, EdUHK and the Vocational Training Council (VTC). Self-financing programmes under the SSSDP scheme also recruit through the JUPAS account.

JUPAS is for **local HKDSE candidates**. Mainland Gaokao students, IB/A-Level holders and other non-local applicants use the separate non-JUPAS / international admission routes — a common point of confusion worth stating plainly.

## The 2026 timeline at a glance

The 2026 JUPAS exercise opened on **9 October 2025** and runs all the way to results day in summer 2026. The key dates:

| Stage | 2026 Cycle Date |
|---|---|
| Application period opens | 9 October 2025 (9:00 am) |
| Main application deadline | 3 December 2025 (5:00 pm) |
| OEA information & supporting documents deadline | 2 January 2026 (5:00 pm) |
| Late application period | 5 December 2025 – 27 May 2026 |
| "Updating of choices" window (before results) | 5 December 2025 – 27 May 2026 |
| OEA review / amended info viewable | 10 March 2026 |
| HKDSE results released | mid-July 2026 |
| Modification of choices (after results) | 16–18 July 2026 (allocated time slot) |
| Main Round offer announced | 5 August 2026 (9:00 am) |
| Acceptance fee (HK$5,000) payment | 5–6 August 2026 |

Two deadlines trip students up every year. First, the **3 December 2025** main deadline is not the end of choice-making — it is the end of the cheap, flexible early window. Second, the **OEA deadline of 2 January 2026** is final; missing it means losing every non-academic credential you might have used to strengthen borderline applications.

## The 20-choice, five-band structure

This is the heart of JUPAS. You may list up to **20 programme choices**, ranked 1 to 20. Those 20 slots are grouped into five **bands**:

- **Band A** — choices 1, 2, 3
- **Band B** — choices 4, 5, 6
- **Band C** — choices 7, 8, 9
- **Band D** — choices 10, 11, 12
- **Band E** — choices 13 through 20

Here is the mechanic that makes ordering matter so much. Until the Main Round results are announced, institutions **do not see your exact rank order**. They only see which *band* you placed their programme in. A programme cannot tell whether you ranked it choice 1 or choice 3 — both look like "Band A" to it. But it absolutely can tell the difference between Band A and Band B.

Most institutions give meaningful preference to Band A applicants, and some competitive programmes (notably medicine and other high-demand offerings) effectively only admit from Band A. JUPAS's own statistics show the overwhelming majority of offers come from Band A choices. The practical consequence: your three Band A slots are not "favourites" — they are your **realistic, competitive targets**. Bands B–E are your safety net, and they should genuinely descend in selectivity so that at least one lower choice is a near-certain offer.

This is why we treat Band A ordering as a strategy problem in its own right; see the dedicated [JUPAS Band A strategy guide](/en/admission/jupas-band-a-strategy) for how to sequence those three slots.

## OEA: the non-academic side of your application

**Other Experiences and Achievements in Competitions / Activities (OEA)** is JUPAS's structured way of recording what you did outside exams — competitions, leadership, sports, volunteering, music, debating, and so on. You enter up to a fixed number of items and may upload supporting documents, all before the **2 January 2026** deadline.

Three realities about OEA:

1. **It is mostly a tie-breaker, not a lever.** For competitive programmes assessed primarily on DSE score, OEA rarely overrides a weak academic profile. Where it matters is at the margin — two applicants with near-identical scores, one of whom demonstrates relevant, sustained achievement.
2. **Relevance beats volume.** A national-level achievement tied to your target discipline carries far more weight than a long list of generic participation certificates.
3. **Some programmes ignore it entirely; others weight it explicitly.** Always check each programme's stated selection criteria rather than assuming OEA helps everywhere.

Alongside OEA, you also complete a **Student Learning Profile** and, for some programmes, a personal statement — covered separately in our [personal statement guide](/en/admission/personal-statement-guide).

## School Principal's Nominations (SPN)

For students still attending a Hong Kong secondary school, **School Principal's Nominations (SPN)** lets the principal nominate a small number of students whose abilities are not fully captured by the DSE alone. Nominated applicants are considered by the institutions for their **Band A** programme choices. It is not a guaranteed offer and does not replace meeting requirements — think of it as a contextual flag that can help a borderline Band A case. Self-study (private) candidates without a current school do not have access to SPN, which is one reason the repeater/private-candidate route requires sharper score targeting.

## Changing your choices: two windows, two sets of rules

JUPAS gives you two distinct opportunities to revise your list, and they work very differently.

### Before results — "Updating of Programme Choices"

From **5 December 2025 to 27 May 2026**, you may freely add, replace, reorder or delete programmes (still capped at 20 total). This window costs **HK$100 per submission**. Use it deliberately: this is when you respond to mock exam performance, predicted grades, open-day impressions and updated admission statistics. There is no limit on the number of submissions, but each one carries the fee, so batch your changes rather than editing impulsively.

### After results — "Modification of Programme Choices"

Once HKDSE results are released, every applicant is given a **personal time slot during 16–18 July 2026** in which they may modify choices **once only, free of charge**. In that single edit you may add or replace **up to five new programmes**, reorder, or delete choices. This is the critical recalibration moment: you now know your real grades, and you adjust your Band A and safety choices to match reality before the Main Round runs. Miss your slot and your pre-results list stands as-is.

This post-results edit is where many offers are won or lost; our [post-results modification strategy](/en/admission/jupas-band-a-strategy) thinking applies directly here — be aggressive enough to aim at programmes your actual score now reaches, but keep a genuine safety choice.

## How the Main Round offer is made

After the post-results modification window closes, each institution runs its selection using its own scoring formula and your banded choices. JUPAS then applies the **single-offer rule**:

- The system finds, among all programmes willing to admit you, the one you ranked **highest**.
- You receive **exactly one Main Round offer** — that one.
- All your lower-priority applications are automatically withdrawn; all your higher-priority ones simply did not offer.

Because of this, you can never be "stuck" with a low choice while a higher one would have taken you — the system always gives you the best available. The **Main Round offer is announced on 5 August 2026 at 9:00 am**, and you must pay the **HK$5,000 acceptance fee on 5–6 August 2026** to secure your place. The acceptance fee is later credited toward tuition; missing the payment forfeits the offer.

Applicants who receive no offer, or who decline, may be considered in subsequent **Clearing / supplementary rounds**, where remaining vacancies are released — typically far fewer and more competitive than the Main Round, which is why your initial list should never rely on Clearing as a plan.

## Score calculators and simulation tools

You do not have to guess your competitiveness blind. For the 2026 cycle, HKU, CUHK, PolyU, HKUST, CityUHK, Lingnan and others publish **programme-specific score calculators** that apply each programme's own subject weightings and bonus rules. CUHK typically releases its updated calculator between May and June 2026; HKUST publishes simulated scores derived from 2025 admissions data under the 2026 formulae. Run your projected and actual grades through several of these and compare against published past admission scores before finalising both your pre-results and post-results lists. For the underlying mechanics of how these numbers are built (Best 5, weightings, bonus points), see our [JUPAS scoring and calculation guide](/en/admission/jupas-scoring-and-calculation-complete-guide-2026).

## A practical sequence to follow

1. **Oct–Dec 2025:** Register, draft a 20-choice list, and submit before 3 December. Treat Band A as competitive targets, Band E as safety.
2. **By 2 January 2026:** Finalise OEA and upload all supporting documents — this deadline does not move.
3. **Dec 2025–May 2026:** Use mock results and updated statistics to refine choices in batched HK$100 updates.
4. **Mid-July 2026:** Get real DSE results; in your allocated 16–18 July slot, make your one free modification — recalibrate honestly.
5. **5 August 2026:** Check the Main Round result at 9:00 am; pay the HK$5,000 acceptance fee within the window.
6. **If no offer:** Watch for Clearing-round vacancies, and review parallel routes such as [associate degree articulation](/en/admission/associate-to-bachelor) and [non-JUPAS applications](/en/application/non-jupas-application-guide).

JUPAS rewards students who understand the machine early. The single-offer rule, the band-only visibility before results, the two very different modification windows, and the hard OEA deadline are not bureaucratic trivia — each one directly shapes how you should rank your 20 choices. Learn the system first; the strategy guides will then make a great deal more sense.
