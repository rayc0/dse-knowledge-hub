# JUPAS OEA, SPN & Special Admission Schemes Complete Guide 2026

Most JUPAS applicants treat their HKDSE score as the only thing that matters. For competitive programmes that is mostly true — but not entirely. The nine JUPAS participating institutions run a cluster of mechanisms that let your achievements *outside* the exam hall influence an offer: **Other Experiences and Achievements (OEA)**, the **School Principal's Nominations (SPN) Scheme**, the **School Nominations Direct Admission Scheme (SNDAS)**, and the UGC's **Student-Athlete Learning Support and Admission (SALSA) Scheme**.

These pathways are widely misunderstood. Some students over-invest in them expecting a magic bypass of the DSE; others ignore them and lose a genuine edge. This guide explains exactly what each one does, who it helps, the 2026 deadlines, and how to use them strategically.

## The big picture: how non-academic factors fit into JUPAS

JUPAS admission is built around your **DSE results**, but institutions explicitly state that academic results are *one of several* considerations. The other channels exist because some talent — a national-level athlete, a published researcher, a community-service leader — is simply not visible in five or six subject grades.

There are two categories to keep straight:

1. **Score-supplementing information** you supply yourself, considered alongside your DSE results within the normal JUPAS process. This is **OEA**.
2. **Special admission/nomination schemes** that can lead to a *conditional or firm offer outside the standard score-based competition*: **SPN**, **SNDAS**, and **SALSA**.

The most important rule up front: **OEA submission is entirely optional, but it is the foundation** that many programmes draw on, and the deadline is unforgiving. Miss it and you cannot amend later.

## Part 1 — Other Experiences and Achievements (OEA)

### What OEA is

OEA lets you record up to **10 items** describing your achievements and experiences in competitions and activities — academic olympiads, sports, music and the arts, debating, community service, leadership roles, internships, MOOCs, and so on. The nine JUPAS participating institutions, plus the SSSDP (Study Subsidy Scheme for Designated Professions/Sectors) institutions, may consider this information in addition to your HKDSE results.

OEA does **not** carry a fixed point value. Each programme decides whether and how to weight it. For a borderline candidate at the cut-off, a strong, relevant OEA record can be the deciding factor; for a programme that admits purely on score, it may have little effect. Treat it as a tie-breaker and a context-builder, not a score booster.

### 2026 deadlines (critical)

- **Submission of OEA information via your JUPAS account: 2 January 2026, 5:00 pm.** Late submission or further amendment is **not** accepted.
- **Uploading required supporting documents: also 2 January 2026, 5:00 pm.** Items flagged in pink in your account require documentary proof; items not highlighted need no supporting documents.
- **Access to verified OEA information: from 10 March 2026, 9:00 am** via your JUPAS account.

Because there is no grace period and no post-deadline editing, finalise OEA in **December 2025**, not on the last day.

### How to write strong OEA entries

- **Relevance over volume.** Ten weak entries are worse than four targeted ones. Align your strongest items with the disciplines of your Band A choices — research and olympiads for science/medicine, debating and community work for law and social sciences, portfolios and competitions for design and creative media.
- **Quantify the achievement.** "Hong Kong-wide finalist (top 1%)" beats "participated in competition." State the level (school / territory / national / international), the result, and your specific role.
- **Recent and verifiable.** Prioritise senior-secondary achievements. Keep certificates organised so the pink-flagged items can be uploaded immediately.
- **No inflation.** OEA entries can be checked, and some institutions interview. A fabricated or exaggerated claim that collapses under scrutiny damages your whole application.

## Part 2 — School Principal's Nominations (SPN) Scheme

### Purpose

SPN gives due recognition to applicants who have **contributed to social services** or **demonstrated outstanding achievements in non-academic areas** — creative activities, art, community service, leadership, music, other cultural activities, and sport. It is a route for the well-rounded student whose impact is broader than their grades.

### How it works

Your **school principal** nominates you (the application is school-driven, not self-initiated), and the nomination is submitted through the JUPAS Online Application System. Each school's capacity is limited, so internal selection at your school matters — express your interest to your teachers and principal early in S6.

### 2026 timeline

- **Deadline for principals to submit SPN nominations: 11 May 2026, 5:00 pm.**
- **Nominated applicants can view feedback and related information: 10 July 2026, 9:00 am.**
- **Final admission decisions announced with the JUPAS Main Round results: 5 August 2026, 9:00 am.**

SPN does not waive academic minimums. It is best understood as a way for a nominated student to receive favourable consideration at the margins, still within the Main Round timeline.

## Part 3 — School Nominations Direct Admission Scheme (SNDAS)

### Purpose

SNDAS recognises **exceptional talent in specific disciplines that the HKDSE cannot fully assess**, and — crucially — can lead to a **firm offer before DSE results are released**. This is the most powerful of the special pathways for genuinely outstanding students.

### Eligibility and quota

- Open to **local students** following the local secondary curriculum, sitting the **2026 HKDSE**, and holding a **valid 2026/27 JUPAS application** in parallel.
- Nomination is by your **secondary school**, with a strict quota: **two nominees per school**, in specific disciplines or areas where the student shows exceptional talent and interest.
- The eight UGC-funded universities have designated **over 300 publicly-funded undergraduate programmes** for the fifth cohort.

### 2026 timeline

- **Nomination period: 9 October to 3 December 2025.**
- **Interviews for all nominees: January to early June 2026** (written or practical assessments may also be arranged).
- **Firm offers to successful nominees: June 2026 — before the HKDSE results are released.**

A firm SNDAS offer is conditional on meeting the programme's stated requirements but removes the score-competition uncertainty entirely. That is why the two-per-school quota is fiercely contested internally.

### Important interaction rule

A student nominated under **SNDAS is not eligible** for nomination under **SPN**, nor for the universities' direct admission schemes for prospective student-athletes under SALSA. You effectively choose one premium track. Discuss with your school which scheme best fits your profile **before** the SNDAS nomination period closes in early December 2025.

## Part 4 — Student-Athlete Learning Support and Admission (SALSA) Scheme

### Purpose

Launched by the UGC and running since the 2022/23 academic year, **SALSA** supports outstanding athletes pursuing **dual-track development** — elite sport alongside a degree — at the eight UGC-funded universities. Each university operates its own branded version (e.g. SAAS at HKUST, SALSA at CityU/EdUHK/PolyU), with sport-specific selection and learning-support provisions such as flexible study arrangements.

### Who it is for

Athletes with a strong competitive record — typically Hong Kong representative or national-squad level, or equivalent ranked performance. Selection is run by each university, often with the Hong Kong Sports Institute involved, and may operate through both JUPAS and Non-JUPAS routes depending on the university.

### Strategy note

Because SNDAS nominees are barred from the student-athlete direct admission route, an elite athlete must decide which channel maximises their chance. If your sporting record is your single strongest asset and a university's athlete scheme covers your target programme, the dedicated SALSA route is usually the better fit; if your talent spans a broader academic-cultural profile, SNDAS may serve you better.

## Decision framework: which pathway is for you?

| Your profile | Best-fit pathway |
| --- | --- |
| Solid all-rounder, score near cut-off | **OEA** (well-targeted) + strong DSE |
| Broad non-academic contribution / leadership / service | **SPN** (ask your principal) |
| Exceptional, discipline-specific talent or research | **SNDAS** (compete for the school's 2 slots) |
| Elite competitive athlete | **SALSA** / university athlete scheme |

Everyone eligible should complete **OEA** — it costs only time, is optional with no downside, and feeds into the other channels. The nomination schemes are selective and mutually constraining, so plan the sequence early.

## Common mistakes to avoid

1. **Treating special schemes as a DSE substitute.** None of them removes academic minimums; they tilt borderline decisions or accelerate timelines for genuinely outstanding profiles.
2. **Missing the 2 January 2026 OEA deadline.** No amendments, no late uploads. Finalise in December.
3. **Padding OEA with irrelevant items.** Programmes value depth and relevance, not a list of certificates.
4. **Discovering SNDAS too late.** The nomination window *closes 3 December 2025* — earlier than most students start thinking about JUPAS strategy.
5. **Ignoring the mutual-exclusivity rule.** Choosing SNDAS forfeits SPN and the athlete schemes. Decide deliberately with your school.

## Action checklist for 2026 entry

- **By October 2025:** Tell your teachers and principal about SNDAS/SPN interest; the SNDAS nomination period opens 9 October 2025.
- **By early December 2025:** SNDAS internal selection and nomination concluded (period closes 3 December 2025).
- **December 2025:** Draft, refine, and finalise all OEA entries; organise supporting documents.
- **By 2 January 2026, 5:00 pm:** Submit OEA and upload all pink-flagged documents.
- **From 10 March 2026:** Verify OEA information in your JUPAS account.
- **By 11 May 2026:** SPN nominations submitted by principals.
- **June–August 2026:** SNDAS firm offers (June), SPN feedback (10 July), Main Round results and final decisions (5 August).

## Bottom line

Your DSE grades remain the engine of a JUPAS application — but OEA, SPN, SNDAS, and SALSA are the steering. OEA is a no-cost edge every applicant should use well. The nomination schemes are narrow doors reserved for genuinely exceptional or well-rounded students, and they demand decisions as early as **October–December 2025** for 2026 entry. Map your profile to the right pathway now, keep your school in the loop, and never let the OEA deadline slip.

*Always confirm exact dates and programme-specific rules on the official JUPAS site (www.jupas.edu.hk) and each university's admissions pages, as details are updated annually.*
