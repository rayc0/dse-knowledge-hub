# Hong Kong PGDE Complete Guide 2026

For most graduates who decide — sometimes during their final undergraduate year, sometimes a decade into another career — that they want to teach in a Hong Kong school, the **Postgraduate Diploma in Education (PGDE)** is the bridge. It is the recognised one-year (full-time) or two-year (part-time) initial teacher education qualification that the **Education Bureau (EDB)** accepts as professional training for becoming a **Registered Teacher** in the public-sector and aided school system.

A PGDE does not, on its own, automatically grant qualified teacher status — registration is a separate EDB process — but it is the most common, cleanest, fastest path. This guide walks through the five main providers (HKU, CUHK, EdUHK, HKBU, HKMU/OUHK), the structural choices you need to make (level, mode of study, subject specialisation), the 2026-27 tuition picture, and the language proficiency hurdle that still trips up many applicants.

## What a PGDE actually is — and what it is not

The PGDE is a **professional initial teacher education qualification**. It assumes you already hold a relevant subject bachelor's degree, and it adds the pedagogy, curriculum knowledge, classroom management, assessment skills and supervised teaching practice that the EDB requires for teacher registration.

A PGDE is **not** a Master of Education (MEd) — it does not award a master's-level academic qualification, and it is not designed for research training. It is also not the same as a **Bachelor of Education (BEd)**: the BEd is a five-year integrated undergraduate route that combines a subject degree with teacher training from day one. A PGDE is the post-degree equivalent for people who have already finished a non-Education bachelor's degree and now want to convert into the profession.

What a PGDE does give you, on graduation:

- A qualification recognised by the **EDB** as meeting initial teacher education requirements
- Eligibility to apply directly to the EDB Teacher Registration Team for **Registered Teacher (RT)** or **Permitted Teacher (PT)** status
- Subject-method training in your **major teaching subject** (and often a minor)
- Documented supervised teaching practice / school experience in Hong Kong schools
- For English / Putonghua language teachers, often an **LPR exemption** if your degree, your PGDE specialisation and your supervised practice all meet EDB criteria

## The five PGDE providers in 2026

Five Hong Kong institutions offer EDB-recognised PGDE programmes for the 2026-27 academic year.

### 1. The Education University of Hong Kong (EdUHK)

EdUHK is by far the largest provider, and the only one for which teacher education is its core mission. It runs four parallel PGDE streams:

- **PGDE (Primary)** — PGDE(P), with subject-specialisation tracks (TSS — Teaching Specific Subjects, including Chinese Language, English Language, Mathematics, General Studies / now restructured under the new primary curriculum, and others)
- **PGDE (Secondary)** — PGDE(S), again with major-subject specialisations (English, Chinese, Mathematics, Liberal Studies / Citizenship and Social Development, Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Geography, History, Economics, BAFS, ICT, Visual Arts, Music, PE and others, subject to demand each cycle)
- **PGDE (Early Childhood Education)** — PGDE(ECE), the route into kindergarten teaching
- **PGDE (Professional and Vocational Education)** — PGDE(PVE), aimed at instructors in vocational and applied-learning contexts

EdUHK's PGDE(P) and PGDE(S) both run in **one-year full-time** and **two-year part-time** modes. From the **2026/27 cohort onwards**, students admitted to PGDE(S) must fulfil the new **Exit Requirements for Teachers' Professional Conduct and Ethics**, reflecting the EDB's tightened expectations on professional ethics for serving teachers.

### 2. The University of Hong Kong (HKU)

HKU's PGDE, run by the Faculty of Education, is **government-funded (UGC)** for the full-time stream — meaning the tuition for local students is the standard subsidised UGC postgraduate fee (HK$47,000 per annum for 2026-27), not a self-financing rate.

Two modes:

- **Full-time (one academic year, September to June)**: targeted at fresh graduates without teaching experience. Includes weekly School Experience in Hong Kong schools and a teaching practice block in the second semester.
- **Part-time (two academic years)**: aimed at serving teachers with at least one year of relevant teaching experience. Classes typically run 6:00–8:30/9:00 p.m. on two evenings per week.

HKU also runs a separate self-funded **PGDE in Teaching Chinese as a Second Language (PGDE-TCSL)** for those targeting non-Chinese speaking learners or international-school CSL roles.

### 3. The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK)

CUHK's Faculty of Education offers PGDE in three streams — **Early Childhood Education**, **Primary** and **Secondary** — with each stream available in full-time and part-time modes.

For the **2026-27 admissions cycle**, the application window is **17 November 2025 (12:00 noon) to 16 December 2025 (12:00 noon)**, with selection interviews in **March 2026**. Applicants choose a Major Subject Studies area, which should normally align with their first degree's major.

### 4. Hong Kong Baptist University (HKBU)

HKBU runs PGDE / Diploma in Education programmes through its Department of Education and Psychology. Its part-time PGDE has historically appealed to in-service teachers and to graduates who want to keep working while training. Specialisations are narrower than EdUHK's catalogue but typically include core academic subjects.

### 5. Hong Kong Metropolitan University (HKMU, formerly OUHK)

HKMU offers part-time, distance-flexible PGDE study options that suit working professionals, mature career-changers, and serving non-graduate teachers who have completed a degree and now need formal initial teacher education to satisfy registration requirements.

## Mode of study: full-time vs part-time — which one is for you

This is the first decision after you know which level you want to teach.

**Full-time (one year)** is the default for:

- Final-year undergraduates / fresh graduates with no teaching contract
- Career-changers who can take a year out of paid work
- Non-local applicants on a student visa (note: part-time programmes generally do **not** sponsor a student visa, so non-local applicants almost always must enrol full-time)

**Part-time (two years)** is the default for:

- Serving teachers in schools that hired you on a Permitted Teacher (PT) basis pending qualification
- Career-changers who cannot give up income
- Local residents whose family or financial situation rules out a year off

A practical caution for the part-time route: contact hours are concentrated in the evenings and on Saturdays, and teaching practice still needs to be done in real schools during normal school hours, which usually means using your existing teaching post (if you have one) as the practice site. If you are not already teaching, the full-time mode is almost always cleaner.

## Choosing your major teaching subject

Your **major teaching subject** is the single biggest determinant of which schools will hire you. The PGDE rule across all providers is the same in spirit: your major method-subject must be substantively backed by your bachelor's degree. You cannot, for example, take a History BA and credibly request a Mathematics PGDE major.

Two further constraints worth knowing:

1. **EDB subject-knowledge requirement.** For each subject you teach, the EDB expects you to have studied that subject area to a recognised standard at degree level (typically a stated number of credits). Schools and the EDB will look at your transcript, not just your PGDE certificate.
2. **Demand cycles.** Each provider opens or closes specific subject majors year by year depending on practicum-school capacity and demand. For 2026-27 the offered list is published per provider — confirm before applying that your intended major is actually running.

For 2026-27 the **Citizenship and Social Development (CSD)** subject — which fully replaced Liberal Studies at the senior-secondary level — continues to drive specific PGDE demand at EdUHK and CUHK, and aspiring CSD teachers should look for that named pathway rather than assuming a generic Liberal Studies PGDE will still be recognised.

## Tuition fees for 2026-27

The fee picture varies by institution and by funding category. Indicative published figures for **2026-27**:

**CUHK PGDE**

- Local full-time: **HK$47,000** per annum
- Local part-time: **HK$23,500** per annum
- Non-local full-time: **HK$214,000** per annum
- Non-local part-time: **HK$107,000** per annum (subject to University approval)

**HKU PGDE**

- Full-time (UGC-funded local rate): **HK$47,000** per annum
- HKU's PGDE is offered on a government-funded basis for the full-time stream

**EdUHK PGDE**

- Application fee: **HK$300** local / **HK$400** non-local
- Tuition for local PGDE programmes is set within the UGC-funded framework for the funded places; applicants should verify the exact 2026-27 rate on the EdUHK website at the time of application

**HKBU and HKMU**

- Programme-specific self-financing rates apply; check each institution's published fee schedule

For comparison: a HK$47,000 / HK$214,000 split between local and non-local students is consistent with the standard UGC-subsidised vs non-local fee structure across Hong Kong's eight publicly funded universities, and it is one of the reasons local Hong Kong residents have a very strong financial argument for treating the PGDE as a fast, cost-effective career conversion.

## Admission requirements

Across providers the headline requirements are similar:

- A recognised **bachelor's degree**, normally with classification (Honours equivalent) — most successful applicants hold a Second Class Honours or above
- A **relevant degree major** in the subject you intend to teach (matching your PGDE Major Subject Studies)
- **English language proficiency** evidence: typically IELTS 6.0+ overall, TOEFL iBT 80+, or an equivalent recognised qualification, with English-medium degree often accepted as evidence
- **Chinese language proficiency** for Chinese-medium teaching subjects: HKDSE Chinese Language Level 3+ or equivalent, with **Putonghua proficiency** required for Putonghua-as-medium tracks
- A successful **interview** (CUHK and EdUHK both interview shortlisted applicants in spring)
- A **personal statement** explaining your motivation for teaching and your subject-area reasoning

Non-local applicants additionally need a **student visa** sponsored through the host university and should plan around full-time enrolment.

## The school experience / teaching practice block

Every recognised PGDE programme includes substantial **supervised teaching practice** in Hong Kong schools. This is not optional and it is not done by distance. For full-time students it usually consists of:

- A **block teaching practice** of around 6 to 10 weeks in semester two
- **Weekly school experience** visits and observed lessons across both semesters
- Regular **tutor-supervised** lesson observations with structured feedback
- A **reflective portfolio** documenting your teaching, planning and assessment

For part-time students who are already in post, your existing school typically becomes your practicum site — but this needs the school's formal cooperation and a signed agreement, and it is something the PGDE coordinator will arrange with your principal at the start of the programme.

## The Language Proficiency Requirement (LPR)

This is the single most misunderstood part of becoming a Hong Kong teacher.

The **EDB Language Proficiency Requirement (LPR)** applies to **all English Language teachers and all Putonghua teachers** in regular posts in public-sector schools and local private day primary/secondary schools offering a formal curriculum. It does **not** apply to the same degree to teachers of, say, Mathematics, Physics or History — though those teachers still need adequate language skills for classroom medium-of-instruction purposes.

There are two ways to satisfy LPR:

**Route A — Sit the assessments.** Take the Language Proficiency Assessment (LPA) in the relevant language (English LPA or Putonghua via the State Language Commission test for Putonghua), and obtain "attained" results in the written and speaking components. New / newly deployed teachers must also obtain "attained" in the **Classroom Language Assessment** within their first school year of teaching the language subject.

**Route B — LPR exemption via degree + ITE.** If you hold a recognised degree majoring in English (or Putonghua / Chinese with Putonghua specialisation) **and** completed initial teacher education with a specialisation in English (or Putonghua) **and** completed supervised teaching practice in that language subject — that combination of three things together can earn an LPR exemption. This is the route a properly chosen English-major PGDE(S) graduate should aim for.

This is why your degree subject + your PGDE major + your practicum placement subject must all line up. A History BA who decides during the PGDE that they want to teach English will almost certainly still need to sit and pass the LPA, regardless of how strong their English is in absolute terms.

## From PGDE graduate to Registered Teacher: the EDB process

PGDE graduation is a **necessary** but not **sufficient** condition for being a Registered Teacher.

The application path:

1. **Graduate** from a recognised PGDE programme.
2. **Apply directly to the EDB Teacher Registration Team** (this is a separate process from your PGDE programme — the university does not register you).
3. Submit your **PGDE certificate, transcript, identity documents** and other supporting evidence.
4. The EDB checks your subject-knowledge background, your initial teacher education, and any LPR requirements relevant to your intended subject(s).
5. Once approved, you become a **Registered Teacher (RT)** — the formal status that allows you to hold a regular post in a Hong Kong public-sector or aided school.

If you take up a teaching post **before** your registration is finalised — common for fresh PGDE graduates who get a school offer in summer — you will typically be appointed as a **Permitted Teacher (PT)** on a time-limited basis, with the school understanding that you will convert to RT status once the EDB approves your registration.

## Career outcomes: where PGDE graduates go

PGDE graduates feed into:

- **Government and aided schools** (the bulk of the Hong Kong public-sector system) — the standard graduate teacher pay scale, civil-service-aligned benefits, and a stable career structure governed by the Master Pay Scale
- **Direct Subsidy Scheme (DSS) schools** — flexibility on curriculum and fees; pay structures vary by school
- **Private independent schools** — sometimes including international or English-stream schools, where a PGDE is well-regarded but specific schools may also require their own curriculum-aligned credentials (IB workshops, Cambridge subject training, etc.)
- **Kindergartens** — for PGDE(ECE) graduates, including subsidised kindergartens under the EDB's Kindergarten Education Scheme
- **Tutorial centres and EdTech companies** — where a PGDE is increasingly valued as a quality signal even though it is not strictly required

The starting salary for a fresh graduate teacher entering a government / aided school on the standard pay scale is one of the most competitive among entry-level professional roles in Hong Kong, and the long-run pay scale together with the pension-equivalent provident fund makes teaching one of the more financially stable career paths available to a PGDE graduate.

## Application timeline for the 2026-27 cycle — a working calendar

Use this as an indicative template; confirm exact dates with each university before applying.

- **September–October 2025**: Decide your level (ECE / Primary / Secondary / PVE), subject major, and mode (full-time / part-time). Order transcripts. Sit IELTS / TOEFL if not already in hand.
- **November 2025**: CUHK PGDE applications open (mid-November). Most other providers open around the same window.
- **December 2025**: CUHK PGDE applications close (16 December 2025). Submit personal statement and references.
- **January–February 2026**: Document review and shortlisting.
- **March 2026**: Interviews (CUHK conducts interviews mid-March 2026; EdUHK and HKU interview around the same period).
- **April–May 2026**: Conditional and full offers issued in waves.
- **June–August 2026**: Confirm acceptance, settle deposits, complete student visa applications for non-local entrants.
- **September 2026**: Programme starts. Full-time students begin school experience in week one or two.
- **June–July 2027**: PGDE graduation; apply to EDB for Registered Teacher status.

## Common mistakes applicants make

1. **Applying for a PGDE major you cannot back with your degree transcript.** The EDB and the universities both check transcripts. Match your PGDE major to your bachelor's major.
2. **Underestimating LPR if you want to teach English or Putonghua.** Plan your degree, PGDE specialisation and practicum placement together — or be prepared to sit the LPA.
3. **Choosing part-time without an existing teaching post.** Part-time mode assumes you have a school to do practice in. If you don't, take the full-time route.
4. **Treating PGDE graduation as automatic teacher registration.** It is a separate EDB application; start it the moment you graduate.
5. **Ignoring the new ethics requirements.** From the 2026/27 PGDE(S) cohort at EdUHK, the **Exit Requirements for Teachers' Professional Conduct and Ethics** are mandatory; this is a real hurdle, not paperwork.
6. **Non-local applicants assuming they can do part-time.** Almost all non-local applicants need a student visa, which generally requires full-time enrolment.

## Bottom line

For the 2026-27 cycle, the Hong Kong PGDE landscape offers five recognised providers, two modes (one-year full-time / two-year part-time), and four levels (ECE, Primary, Secondary, PVE). Tuition for local students at the UGC-funded providers sits at HK$47,000 per annum — a remarkably accessible price for a year-long professional conversion that opens the door to one of the most stable career paths in Hong Kong.

The decisions that matter most happen **before** you apply, not during the programme: which level, which subject major, which mode, and how that combination interacts with EDB subject-knowledge expectations and the LPR. Get those four decisions right, and the rest of the PGDE — coursework, school experience, and EDB Registered Teacher application — becomes a year of structured, well-supported professional preparation rather than a year of catch-up.
