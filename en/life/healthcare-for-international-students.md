# Healthcare & Medical Insurance for International Students in Hong Kong 2026 — Complete Guide

> **Summary** — International students on a student visa staying longer than 180 days qualify for a Hong Kong Identity Card (HKID), which grants access to the public healthcare system at heavily subsidised Eligible Person rates (A&E HK$400, inpatient HK$300/day from 1 Jan 2026). All eight UGC-funded universities require non-local students to hold medical insurance. On-campus university health services provide free GP consultations. This guide covers public vs private care, mandatory insurance, university clinics, costs, emergencies, dental, mental health, and a pre-arrival checklist.

## Hong Kong's Dual Healthcare System — How It Works

Hong Kong operates a **dual public–private healthcare system**. The public system — managed by the Hospital Authority (HA) — runs 43 public hospitals and 122 outpatient clinics across seven hospital clusters, providing heavily subsidised care. The private system operates in parallel through independent clinics, specialist practices, and 13 private hospitals, charging market rates.

For international students, the critical question is: **are you an "Eligible Person" or a "Non-Eligible Person" under the Hospital Authority's fee structure?**

### Eligible Person vs Non-Eligible Person

| Category | Who qualifies | Public hospital rate |
|---|---|---|
| **Eligible Person (EP)** | Holders of a valid Hong Kong Identity Card (HKID); Hong Kong permanent residents; children under 11 who are HK residents | Heavily subsidised |
| **Non-Eligible Person (NEP)** | Anyone without a valid HKID — tourists, short-stay visitors, students who have not yet obtained their HKID | Near-private rates (5–25× higher) |

**The key fact most guides miss**: international degree students (undergraduate, postgraduate taught, postgraduate research) staying longer than 180 days are required to apply for an HKID within 30 days of arrival. Once you hold a valid HKID, you are classified as an **Eligible Person** and pay the subsidised public hospital rates — the same rates as a local Hong Kong student.

**Exchange students** staying fewer than 180 days typically do not qualify for an HKID and are classified as Non-Eligible Persons, paying significantly higher rates. This is one reason universities strongly advise exchange students to purchase comprehensive private medical insurance before arriving.

## Public Hospital Fees — 2026 Rates (Effective 1 January 2026)

The Hospital Authority implemented a major fee reform effective 1 January 2026. Below are the current rates.

### Eligible Persons (HKID holders, including degree students)

| Service | Fee (HKD) |
|---|---|
| Accident & Emergency (A&E) | $400 per visit |
| Inpatient — acute / general ward | $300 per day |
| Inpatient — convalescent / rehabilitation / psychiatric | $200 per day |
| Specialist outpatient (SOPC) — first attendance | $250 |
| Specialist outpatient (SOPC) — follow-up | $250 |
| General outpatient clinic (GOPC) | $150 per visit |
| Dressing / injection | $50 per visit |
| Community nursing (general) | $100 per visit |

**Annual fee cap**: HK$10,000 per person per year for all fee items (excluding self-paid medications and medical devices). This cap is new from 2026.

### Non-Eligible Persons (no HKID — exchange students, tourists, new arrivals before HKID)

| Service | Fee (HKD) |
|---|---|
| Accident & Emergency (A&E) | $2,100 per visit |
| Inpatient — general ward | $7,400 per day |
| Intensive care (ICU) | $35,600 per day |
| Specialist outpatient | $850 per visit |
| General outpatient clinic | $500 per visit |
| Obstetrics — booked package | $74,000 |

Non-Eligible Persons must pay a deposit on admission for inpatient services. There is no annual fee cap for NEPs.

**Practical implication**: getting your HKID as soon as possible after arrival is not just a legal requirement — it can save you thousands of dollars if you need emergency or hospital care. If you visit A&E before receiving your HKID, you will be charged HK$2,100 instead of HK$400.

## Mandatory University Medical Insurance

All eight UGC-funded universities in Hong Kong require non-local students to hold medical insurance for the duration of their studies. Some universities operate their own group insurance scheme; others accept equivalent private plans.

### University Insurance Requirements at a Glance

| University | Compulsory for non-local? | Scheme type | Approximate annual premium (HKD) |
|---|---|---|---|
| HKU | Yes | University group scheme via HKU Medical Insurance Scheme | $1,500–$2,200 |
| CUHK | Yes | University group scheme | $1,400–$2,000 |
| HKUST | Yes | Compulsory Medical Insurance for all non-local degree students on student visa | $1,500–$2,100 |
| PolyU | Yes | Group Medical and Public Liability Insurance Scheme | $1,200–$1,800 |
| CityU | Yes | University-contracted insurance plan | $1,200–$1,800 |
| HKBU | Yes | Group insurance scheme | $1,200–$1,800 |
| EdUHK | Yes | University group scheme; separate voluntary dental plan ($215–$485/year) | $1,200–$1,600 |
| LingU | Advisory (strongly recommended) | Student Medical Benefits Scheme — does NOT cover hospital bills; students advised to purchase comprehensive private insurance before arrival | Varies |

### Typical Coverage Under University Plans

| Coverage area | Typical limit (HKD) |
|---|---|
| Inpatient hospitalisation | $500,000–$1,000,000 per year |
| Outpatient services | $15,000–$30,000 per year |
| Emergency and accident | $100,000+ |
| Dental | Usually limited or excluded |
| Maternity | Usually excluded |
| Pre-existing conditions | Usually excluded or limited |
| Mental health (inpatient) | Limited coverage |
| Repatriation / medical evacuation | Usually included |

### Should You Buy Supplementary Insurance?

University plans are basic — designed to cover emergencies and standard medical needs, not comprehensive healthcare. Consider supplementary insurance if:

- You have a pre-existing condition that needs ongoing management
- You want private hospital access with direct billing (no upfront payment)
- You want dental, optical, or mental health coverage
- You plan to travel frequently outside Hong Kong during your studies

Major insurers offering plans to international students in Hong Kong include AXA, BUPA, AIA, Cigna, and Allianz. Annual premiums for comprehensive supplementary plans range from HK$4,000–$15,000 depending on coverage level and age.

Hong Kong also offers the government-regulated **Voluntary Health Insurance Scheme (VHIS)**, which any Hong Kong resident (including HKID-holding students) aged 15 days to 80 years can purchase. VHIS plans are standardised and transparent, though the associated tax deduction benefit is only useful if you are a Hong Kong taxpayer.

## University Health Services — Free On-Campus Care

Every major Hong Kong university operates an on-campus health centre providing free or low-cost primary care to enrolled students. These are typically the best first-line option for routine medical needs.

### HKU — University Health Service (UHS)

- **Location**: Main campus
- **Services**: Free GP consultation, nurse services, vaccinations, travel health, women's health, basic lab tests
- **Dental**: On-campus dental clinic with nominal charges
- **Hours**: Monday–Friday, 9:00 am – 1:00 pm, 2:00 pm – 5:45 pm; closed weekends and public holidays
- **Website**: uhs.hku.hk

### CUHK — University Medical Service Office (UMSO)

- **Location**: Main campus
- **Services**: Free GP consultation, dental (nominal fee), physiotherapy, medical laboratory, minor operating theatre, dispensary
- **Booking**: Online booking system available
- **Website**: umso.cuhk.edu.hk

### HKUST — Medical and Dental Clinic

- **Location**: Main campus
- **Services**: Free outpatient service for full-time students
- **Dental**: Available at HK$70 per 20 minutes + HK$10 oral examination fee
- **Website**: hseo.hkust.edu.hk/clinic

### PolyU — University Health Service

- **Location**: Main campus and West Kowloon campus (for HKCC students)
- **Services**: Free primary clinical consultations and medications

### CityU, HKBU, EdUHK, LingU

All operate equivalent on-campus health services with free or subsidised GP consultations for enrolled students.

### What University Clinics Typically Provide

- General medical consultation (cold, flu, fever, minor injuries)
- Prescription dispensing
- Women's health (contraception, pap smear)
- Travel medicine and vaccinations (HPV, hepatitis, influenza)
- Mental health counselling referrals
- Basic lab tests (blood work, urinalysis)
- Referral letters for specialists or hospitals

### What University Clinics Do NOT Typically Provide

- Comprehensive dental care (basic only at CUHK and HKUST)
- Specialist consultation (referred to hospital or private)
- Surgery (referred to hospital)
- Advanced radiology (referred to imaging centre)

## How to See a Doctor — Step by Step

### Option 1: University Health Service (Best for Routine Issues)

1. Walk in or book online via the university health portal
2. Bring your student ID card
3. Consultation is free or nominal cost
4. Best for: cold, headache, minor injuries, vaccinations, prescription refills, referral letters

### Option 2: Private GP Clinic

1. Walk in at any private clinic — no appointment needed at most clinics
2. Search for English-speaking doctors on FindDoc (finddoc.com) or DocDoc
3. Cost: HK$300–$800 per visit (including basic medication)
4. Pay upfront, collect receipt, submit to insurance for reimbursement
5. Best for: evenings, weekends, when university clinic is closed, or when you want faster service

### Option 3: Public General Outpatient Clinic (GOPC)

1. Pre-book via the HA phone line (1836 115) — walk-in waits can be 2–4+ hours
2. Cost: HK$150 per visit (EP) or HK$500 per visit (NEP)
3. Best for: chronic disease follow-up, complex conditions, when cost is a major concern
4. Bring your HKID

### Option 4: Accident & Emergency (A&E)

1. Go directly to the nearest public hospital A&E department
2. Cost: HK$400 (EP) or HK$2,100 (NEP)
3. Triaged by severity — minor issues may wait 3–6 hours
4. Best for: genuine emergencies only (serious injury, chest pain, breathing difficulty, suspected stroke, severe allergic reaction)

## Dental Care

Dental care in Hong Kong is **not covered by public healthcare subsidies** and is **usually excluded or limited under university insurance plans**.

### Cost Reference

| Procedure | Approximate cost (HKD) |
|---|---|
| Dental exam + cleaning | $400–$1,200 |
| Basic filling | $800–$2,500 |
| Root canal | $5,000–$15,000 |
| Wisdom tooth extraction | $3,000–$8,000 |
| Crown | $5,000–$12,000 |

### University Dental Options

- **HKUST**: On-campus dental clinic at HK$70 per 20 minutes + HK$10 oral exam
- **CUHK (UMSO)**: Dental unit with nominal fees
- **HKU**: On-campus dental clinic with subsidised charges
- **EdUHK**: Voluntary Student Dental Plan at HK$215/year (UGC-funded students) or HK$485/year (other students)

**Recommendation**: get a dental check-up and cleaning at home before arrival. Budget HK$1,000–$3,000 per year for out-of-pocket dental expenses in Hong Kong.

## Mental Health Services

Every major university offers free, confidential mental health support:

| University | Service |
|---|---|
| HKU | Counselling and Psychological Services (CEDARS) |
| CUHK | Wellness and Counselling Centre |
| HKUST | Counselling and Wellness Centre |
| PolyU | Student Counselling Service |
| CityU | Student Development Services — Counselling Section |
| HKBU | Counselling and Development Centre |
| EdUHK | Student Affairs Office Counselling |
| LingU | Student Counselling Team |

**Services include**: free individual counselling (limited sessions/year), group therapy, crisis intervention, referral to psychiatrists, workshops on stress, sleep, relationships, and academic pressure.

**Confidentiality**: counselling records are completely confidential. Your professors, classmates, family, and immigration office will never see your records without your written consent. Using counselling services does not affect your visa, grades, or housing.

**Psychiatric care**: for conditions requiring medication (severe depression, bipolar disorder, ADHD, anxiety disorders), university counselling will refer you to a psychiatrist. Private psychiatric consultation costs HK$1,500–$3,500 per visit. Public psychiatric care through HA is cheaper but has long non-urgent wait times.

## Common Medical Scenarios

**"I have a cold or fever"**
→ University health service first. Free, quick, English-speaking. Only escalate to private GP or A&E if symptoms are severe (high fever >39°C persisting, difficulty breathing).

**"I twisted my ankle"**
→ University health service for minor sprains. If you suspect a fracture (severe swelling, cannot bear weight, visible deformity), go to A&E.

**"I need a prescription refill from home"**
→ Bring your home doctor's prescription (translated to English if needed). See a private GP who can issue a local prescription. Check with Watsons or Mannings pharmacists — some medications have different brand names in Hong Kong.

**"I need contraception"**
→ University health service provides basic contraception (oral contraceptive pill, condoms). For IUD, implant, or specialist care, request a referral to a gynaecologist.

**"I have a toothache"**
→ Private dentist — most university clinics have limited dental. Budget HK$800–$2,500 for a typical filling.

**"I'm having a mental health crisis"**
→ University counselling service immediately. For after-hours crises: Samaritans Hong Kong (2389 2222) or nearest A&E.

**"I'm travelling outside HK — am I covered?"**
→ Check your university insurance policy. Many plans have limited or no coverage outside Hong Kong. Buy temporary travel insurance (HK$100–$300 for a short trip) for trips to mainland China, Southeast Asia, or elsewhere.

## Vaccinations

**Recommended vaccinations for Hong Kong**:

- MMR (measles, mumps, rubella) — should be current
- DTaP/Tdap (diphtheria, tetanus, pertussis) — booster if >10 years
- Hepatitis A + B — recommended
- Influenza — annual (available free or low-cost at university clinic)
- HPV — recommended if under 26
- COVID-19 — follow current Hong Kong Department of Health guidance
- Varicella (chickenpox) — if not already immune

Most vaccinations are available at the university health service for free or at minimal cost. Travel vaccinations (typhoid, Japanese encephalitis, yellow fever) for trips outside Hong Kong can be obtained at the same clinics or at the Department of Health Travel Health Centre.

## Pre-Arrival Checklist

1. **Vaccination records** — bring documentation of all completed vaccinations, translated to English
2. **Medical history summary** — one-page document summarising chronic conditions, allergies, surgeries, current medications
3. **Prescription medications** — bring 3 months' supply with a letter from your doctor; check Hong Kong's controlled substances list before importing
4. **Dental check-up** — have cleaning and exam at home before departure
5. **Eye exam** — bring current prescription for glasses/contacts
6. **Insurance documentation** — physical and digital copies of your university insurance policy and any supplementary plans
7. **Emergency contacts** — record family contact information and home country embassy details
8. **Download HA Go app** — the Hospital Authority's official app for booking GOPC appointments, checking A&E wait times, and managing health records

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Am I eligible for subsidised public healthcare as an international student?**
A: Yes — if you are a degree student staying longer than 180 days, you are required to apply for a Hong Kong Identity Card (HKID). With a valid HKID, you are classified as an "Eligible Person" and pay the same subsidised rates as local residents at all public hospitals and clinics.

**Q: What if I need medical care before my HKID is ready?**
A: You will be charged at the Non-Eligible Person rate (e.g. HK$2,100 for A&E instead of HK$400). Keep receipts — you may be able to claim the difference through your university insurance. Apply for your HKID as soon as possible after arrival (within 30 days as required by law).

**Q: Is university medical insurance enough, or do I need private insurance?**
A: University plans cover emergencies and standard medical needs adequately. However, they typically exclude or limit dental care, mental health, pre-existing conditions, and private hospital stays. If any of these are relevant to you, supplementary insurance is worth considering.

**Q: Can I use my home country's insurance in Hong Kong?**
A: Some international insurance plans provide worldwide coverage, but most home-country plans do not cover Hong Kong. Check with your insurer before relying on this. Even if your home plan covers Hong Kong, you will likely still need to enrol in your university's mandatory scheme.

**Q: How do I find an English-speaking doctor?**
A: English is widely spoken by healthcare professionals in Hong Kong. University health services operate in English. For private GPs, use FindDoc (finddoc.com) to search by language. All public hospital doctors speak English.

**Q: Is dental care covered?**
A: Usually not under university insurance plans. Some universities (HKUST, CUHK, HKU) offer subsidised on-campus dental services. EdUHK offers a voluntary dental plan at HK$215–$485/year. Budget for out-of-pocket dental expenses.

**Q: What about mental health — is counselling really free?**
A: Yes. Every major university offers free individual counselling through its student affairs or wellness centre. Sessions are confidential and do not affect your visa, grades, or housing. If medication is needed, you will be referred to a psychiatrist (private or public).

**Q: What happens if I have a medical emergency?**
A: Call 999 for an ambulance or go directly to the nearest public hospital A&E. With an HKID, the A&E charge is HK$400. Emergency care in Hong Kong is world-class — do not hesitate to use it.
