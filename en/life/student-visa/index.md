# Student Visa Guide for Mainland and International Students in Hong Kong 2026

Navigating Hong Kong's immigration system is one of the practical hurdles that non-local students must clear before they can enroll. The process is well-defined and manageable, but it requires attention to deadlines, financial documentation, and post-graduation planning. This guide explains every stage from initial application to what happens when your degree is complete.

---

## Do You Need a Student Visa?

**Mainland Chinese students**: Yes. Despite the close relationship between Mainland China and Hong Kong, Mainland residents are not Hong Kong permanent residents and require a formal **study visa** (also called a study permit) to study and reside in Hong Kong for the duration of their degree.

**International students (non-Chinese passport holders)**: Yes. All non-Hong Kong residents require a study visa regardless of nationality.

**Hong Kong permanent residents (HKPR holders)**: No visa is required. You may study freely.

**HKID holders who are not permanent residents**: This category covers holders of various HKID types (including holders of Dependent Visas, Employment Visas, etc.). Check with the Immigration Department whether your existing visa status allows full-time study. In many cases, existing visa holders must apply to change or extend their visa to cover full-time study.

## Types of Hong Kong Student Visas

### Study Visa (Student Dependent Visa / Study Visa)
The standard study visa issued to students admitted to a full-time undergraduate or postgraduate program at a Hong Kong university. This is the visa type that the vast majority of non-local students hold.

**Key features**:
- Typically issued for the duration of your program (e.g., 4 years for an undergraduate degree)
- Does not automatically permit employment — see the Part-Time Work guide for details
- Requires annual confirmation that you remain enrolled and in good academic standing

### IANG Visa (Immigration Arrangements for Non-local Graduates)
Not a student visa, but critically important for post-graduation planning. The IANG visa allows non-local graduates of Hong Kong universities to remain in Hong Kong for **12 months after graduation** to seek employment — without a confirmed job offer. This effectively gives graduates a full year to find work before immigration status becomes uncertain.

After securing employment, IANG holders can convert to an employment visa for continued residency.

## The Study Visa Application Process

### Step 1: Receive Your University Admission Offer
Your admission offer letter from the university is the foundational document for your study visa application. Do not begin the visa process until you have a confirmed admission offer (not just a conditional offer).

### Step 2: Obtain Sponsorship from Your University
Hong Kong universities act as sponsors for their non-local students' visa applications. Once you confirm acceptance of the offer, notify the International Student Services Office (or equivalent) at your university. They will:
- Issue a formal letter of sponsorship / student registration confirmation
- Guide you through the university-specific steps for initiating the visa application

### Step 3: Prepare Your Documents

Required documents for a standard student visa application:

| Document | Details |
|---|---|
| Application form (ID 995A) | Available from Immigration Department website (immd.gov.hk) |
| University admission letter | Original or certified copy |
| University sponsorship letter | Issued by your university's student services office |
| Proof of financial resources | See financial proof section below |
| Passport-size photos | 2 recent photos meeting HK Immigration specifications |
| Copy of valid travel document / passport | All pages with existing stamps |
| Academic transcripts | Secondary school / prior university transcripts |
| Proof of legal status in place of application | E.g., proof of your current student/resident status in home country |

### Step 4: Submit the Application

**For applicants outside Hong Kong**: Submit the application to the nearest Chinese Consulate/Embassy or Chinese Commissioner's Office in your country. Processing is typically handled through the Beijing Representative Office for countries without a Consulate.

**For applicants already in Hong Kong** (e.g., on a visitor permit): Submit directly to the Immigration Department in person (address: Immigration Tower, 7 Gloucester Road, Wan Chai, Hong Kong). Walk-in and appointment-based services are both available.

**Mainland Chinese students**: Apply through the mainland Entry/Exit Administration Bureau or via the permit application process for One-way / Two-way Permits. CUHK, HKU, and other universities maintain dedicated liaison offices to guide Mainland students through this process — use them.

### Step 5: Processing Time and Notification

Standard processing time: **4–8 weeks** from receipt of complete application.

For most international students, the visa is returned as an endorsement in your passport or issued as a separate visa document. Keep this document safe — you will need to present it when entering Hong Kong each time.

## Financial Proof Requirements

The Immigration Department requires evidence that you (or your family/sponsor) have sufficient funds to cover your education and living costs for the duration of your study period. There is no single fixed threshold published, but as a practical guide:

- Demonstrate funds equivalent to **at least 1 year of study costs** (tuition + living expenses)
- For a 4-year degree at CUHK or HKUST, this means demonstrating approximately **HKD 200,000–250,000** in readily accessible funds

Accepted forms of financial documentation:
- Bank statements (3–6 months) showing regular balance or lump sum deposits
- Fixed deposit certificates
- Scholarship award letters (scholarships reduce the personal funds requirement significantly)
- Parental bank statements and/or employment verification letter if parents are funding your education

Banks in Mainland China may issue official English or bilingual statements on request — ensure the statement includes the account holder's name, account number, and is stamped/certified by the bank.

## Extending Your Visa During Studies

Study visas are periodically renewed rather than covering all 4 years automatically in some cases. Your university's student services office will notify you when renewal documentation is required. Typically:

- Renewal is an administrative process handled largely by the university
- You must be in good academic standing (passing grades, enrolled full-time)
- Processing takes 2–4 weeks — do not let your current visa expire before renewal is submitted

Overstaying a visa in Hong Kong is a criminal offence — begin renewal at least 6 weeks before expiry.

## Post-Graduation: The IANG Visa

This is arguably the most important visa planning consideration for non-local students studying in Hong Kong.

**What is IANG?**
The Immigration Arrangements for Non-local Graduates scheme allows graduates of Hong Kong universities to:
- Remain in Hong Kong for 12 months after graduation without a job offer
- Seek employment and change to an Employment Visa once hired

**Why it matters**:
In most countries, a student visa expires shortly after graduation, forcing graduates to leave immediately. IANG gives HK graduates a genuine runway to job hunt in the local market — this is a significant advantage compared to studying overseas where visa cliffs create pressure.

**Eligibility for IANG**:
- Awarded a degree from a recognised Hong Kong institution (UGC-funded universities, and selected other institutions — check the full list at immd.gov.hk)
- Applied within 6 months of graduation

**Extending beyond IANG**:
After finding employment under IANG, apply for a standard Employment Visa. Hong Kong employers sponsor Employment Visa applications through the same Immigration Department process. With 7 years of continuous ordinary residence in Hong Kong, you become eligible to apply for Permanent Residency (Right of Abode).

## Practical Tips

1. **Apply early.** Visa processing delays are real. Apply within 1 week of accepting your university offer to avoid scrambling before September enrollment.

2. **Keep all paper copies.** Hong Kong immigration officers do check documents at borders — carry physical copies of your visa, admission letter, and sponsorship letter when traveling.

3. **Notify the Immigration Department of any address changes.** Legally required and occasionally audited.

4. **Understand the IANG timeline.** If you plan to stay in HK after graduation, apply for IANG *before* your study visa expires — do not wait until the last minute after receiving your degree certificate.

5. **Use your university's international office.** Every major HK university has staff whose specific job is helping international students navigate immigration. Use this resource — they know the current processing times and any year-to-year changes to requirements.
