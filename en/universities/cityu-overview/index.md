# City University of Hong Kong (CityU) Complete Guide 2025: Programmes, Admission Requirements and Why It's Becoming HK's Rising Star

City University of Hong Kong (CityU) is arguably the most interesting story in Hong Kong higher education right now. Ranked **#62 globally and #5 in Hong Kong** by QS World University Rankings 2024, CityU has climbed faster than any other Hong Kong university over the past decade — driven by deliberate investments in law, business, engineering, creative industries, and cross-disciplinary innovation.

For DSE students building their JUPAS list, CityU offers a compelling mix: strong programmes in high-demand fields, a genuinely urban campus in Kowloon Tong, an entrepreneurship-focused culture that differs markedly from the more traditional HKU and CUHK environments, and a 30% international student body that makes for a cosmopolitan campus atmosphere.

## CityU's Rise: What Is Driving It?

CityU's trajectory from Hong Kong's fifth-ranked university to a globally recognised institution reflects deliberate strategic choices made over the past 15 years:

**Law School investment**: The CityU School of Law, established in 1994, has built a reputation as one of Asia's leading law faculties with a distinctive dual common law and Chinese law focus. It attracts faculty from Oxbridge, Yale, and leading Australian law schools.

**Creative media and design**: The School of Creative Media (established 1998) pioneered interdisciplinary creative education in Hong Kong, combining art, technology, and cultural theory in ways that traditional universities were slower to adopt.

**Research output growth**: CityU's citation impact and research volume have grown substantially, moving the university from a teaching-focused polytechnic into a recognised research institution. Fields like energy materials, data science, and biomedical engineering have built international profiles.

**Entrepreneurship culture**: CityU's CityU Entrepreneurship Centre, HK Tech 300 programme, and deep connections with Kowloon Tong's technology ecosystem (Kowloon Tong is adjacent to Kai Tak and the Kowloon Bay tech district) have made it a genuine starting point for Hong Kong startups. The HK Tech 300 programme has funded hundreds of student-led ventures since 2021.

## Strongest Programmes and JUPAS Admission

### School of Law (LLB — JS1042)

CityU Law is the headline programme — the one that most dramatically signals CityU's ambitions.

| Metric | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| JUPAS Code | JS1042 |
| Band A (estimated, 2020–2025) | Approximately 27–30/45 (rising trend) |
| Duration | 4 years |
| Key DSE subjects | English ≥4 strongly recommended; no specific elective requirement |
| Graduates | Solicitors, barristers, legal professionals across HK and the region |

**What makes CityU Law distinctive**: The programme offers a dual track in Hong Kong Common Law and Chinese Law, which is uniquely valuable given Hong Kong's legal position as the gateway between common law and mainland civil law systems. Students can pursue qualifications relevant to both legal systems — a career advantage unavailable at most overseas law programmes.

The Law school has a dedicated mooting programme, legal aid clinic, and exchange partnerships with law schools at UCL, Melbourne, Singapore NUS, and NYU.

**Band A admission trend**: CityU Law has seen the steepest increase in JUPAS Band A cutoffs among all Hong Kong university programmes from 2020 to 2025. The estimated Band A floor has risen from approximately 27/45 in 2020 to approximately 29–30/45 by 2024–2025. This reflects growing recognition of CityU Law's quality and career outcomes.

| Metric | Detail |

| JUPAS Code | JS1060 |
| Band A (estimated, 2020–2025) | Approximately 21–23/45 |
| Duration | 4 years |
| Additional requirement | Portfolio submission (Folio) |
| Sub-disciplines | Film and Television; Digital Games; Creative Technology; Animation |

CityU Creative Media is the only programme of its kind among Hong Kong's top universities — combining technical production skills (cinematography, game design, 3D animation, interactive installation) with cultural theory, media studies, and critical practice. Graduates work in Hong Kong's film and broadcasting sector (TVB, ViuTV, RTHK), the video game industry (several studios maintain Hong Kong offices), and the growing creative technology sector.

Portfolio quality matters significantly. The Folio submission is evaluated independently of DSE scores, and creative ability demonstrated through films, animations, game prototypes, or interactive works can differentiate candidates.

CityU Law does not require the LNAT (Law National Aptitude Test) used in some UK admissions contexts. International applicants are encouraged to submit a supplementary personal statement specifically addressing their interest in Hong Kong common law and Chinese law.

## Student Life and International Community

### A Cosmopolitan Campus

With approximately 30% international students, CityU's campus is genuinely diverse. Students from mainland China, Southeast Asia, Europe, North America, and South Asia form a visible part of campus life. This creates both networking opportunities and a more globally-oriented peer environment than some of CityU's Hong Kong peers.

### Exchange Programme Network

CityU's 300+ partner universities enable international study opportunities across:
- North America (Michigan, Toronto, UBC, Boston)
- Europe (Amsterdam, Copenhagen, Paris, Zurich, Edinburgh)
- Asia (NTU Singapore, Fudan, Seoul National University, Waseda)

Students typically complete one or two semesters of exchange in years 2–3. Funding through the HKSAR government's exchange grant programme helps offset costs for eligible local students.

## The Entrepreneurship Focus: HK Tech 300 and CityU's Innovation Ecosystem

One of CityU's most distinctive features is its structured commitment to entrepreneurship. The **HK Tech 300** programme, launched in 2021 with a HKD 500 million commitment, aims to create 300 technology startups from CityU research and student projects.

Key components:
- **Seed funding**: Eligible teams receive initial funding of HKD 100,000 without equity dilution
- **Mentorship network**: Connections to investors, entrepreneurs, and corporate partners
- **Incubator space**: Dedicated coworking and prototype development facilities
- **Pre-accelerator programme**: Structured curriculum for early-stage ventures

For students interested in building technology ventures — particularly at the intersection of engineering, data science, or creative media — CityU's HK Tech 300 ecosystem provides structured support that is difficult to find at more traditional universities.

## Research Profile

CityU's research ambitions are backed by significant government and competitive funding. Key research areas:

**Energy and Environmental Sustainability**: CityU's School of Energy and Environment conducts applied research on solar energy, air quality, and sustainable building materials. The school's research on perovskite solar cells has been published in leading international journals.

**Data Science and AI**: The Computational Intelligence Laboratory and affiliated groups work on machine learning, computer vision, and natural language processing, with applications in healthcare, finance, and logistics.

**Materials Science**: CityU has built a strong materials science research cluster, with particular strength in biomaterials and functional coatings. The research group has earned top-tier publications and international collaborations.

**Law and Governance**: The CityU Law school operates several research centres including the Centre for Chinese and Comparative Law and the Herbert Smith Freehills Centre for the Legal System and Financial Markets.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is CityU's law degree recognised for practice in Hong Kong?**
Yes. CityU LLB graduates are eligible to apply for the Postgraduate Certificate in Laws (PCLL) at HKU, CUHK, or CityU — the qualification required to practice as a solicitor or barrister in Hong Kong.

**Can CityU students do international exchanges?**
Yes. CityU's 300+ partner network includes universities across North America, Europe, and Asia. Most programmes incorporate an exchange opportunity in years 2–3, with financial support available for eligible students.

**Is CityU good for mainland Chinese students?**
CityU is particularly popular with mainland students, who constitute a significant portion of its non-local population. The Mandarin-speaking community on campus is large, there are mainland student associations, and CityU's dual Common Law / Chinese Law programme in the law school is directly relevant to students interested in cross-boundary legal careers.

**How does CityU compare for employment outcomes?**
CityU graduates in law, computing, and business report strong employment outcomes in Hong Kong's finance, legal, and technology sectors. The Creative Media programme produces graduates who enter Hong Kong's entertainment and technology industries. CityU is not a primary target for magic circle law firm or bulge bracket banking recruitment — those pipelines favour HKU — but for roles in mid-market professional services, technology, and creative industries, CityU graduates are well-regarded.
