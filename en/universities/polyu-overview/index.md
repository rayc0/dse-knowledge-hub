# PolyU Complete Guide for International Students 2026

The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU) is one of Hong Kong's most distinctive higher education institutions. While HKUST and HKU attract students targeting research careers, PolyU has carved out a reputation for programs that bridge academic rigour with professional practice — particularly in design, engineering, hospitality, health sciences, and applied business. For students who want industry-relevant skills and strong job placement without sacrificing academic credentials, PolyU deserves serious consideration.

## Why PolyU?

PolyU occupies a unique market position in Hong Kong higher education. The tuition at **HKD 98,600 per year** for non-local students is significantly lower than CUHK (HKD 145,000) or HKUST (HKD 168,000), while delivering programs in fields where PolyU genuinely leads:

- **Design (School of Design)** — Regularly ranked in the global top 30 for art and design. The school has produced graduates who have won Red Dot, iF Design Award, and other major international prizes.
- **Hotel and Tourism Management (School of Hotel and Tourism Management, SHTM)** — Consistently ranked **#1 in Asia and top 3 globally** for hospitality education by QS and Shanghai Rankings.
- **Engineering** — Civil, Electrical, Mechanical, and Industrial/Systems Engineering programs with industry placement partnerships.
- **Nursing and Health Sciences** — Among the strongest nursing programs in Asia; graduates are highly sought by HK's Hospital Authority.
- **Optometry** — PolyU's optometry program is globally recognised; one of very few programs of its calibre in Asia.
- **Applied Sciences (Fashion, Textile, Building)** — Unique among HK universities for these applied disciplines.

## Program Highlights

### School of Design
PolyU Design offers undergraduate programs in:
- Product and Industrial Design
- Communication Design (Graphic, Advertising, Interaction Design)
- Environment and Interior Design
- Fashion and Textiles Design

The school occupies a dedicated building with state-of-the-art making facilities, digital fabrication labs, and direct industry project briefs from corporate partners including Cathay Pacific, HSBC, and various Mainland Chinese design companies.

Admissions for design programs typically require a portfolio submission in addition to academic qualifications.

### School of Hotel and Tourism Management (SHTM)
The #1-ranked hospitality school in Asia. Programs:
- BSc in Hotel Management
- BSc in Tourism and Event Management

Graduates enter management training programmes at Marriott, Four Seasons, Hilton, IHG, and leading Macau properties. The school operates its own teaching hotel on campus (Hotel ICON) — students gain hands-on industry experience during their degree.

### Faculty of Engineering
PolyU Engineering programs emphasise applied project work over theoretical research. Students regularly complete capstone projects with industry partners, and the faculty has specific strengths in:
- Civil and Environmental Engineering (infrastructure projects)
- Electrical Engineering (power systems, smart grid)
- Mechanical Engineering (manufacturing, robotics)
- Industrial and Systems Engineering (supply chain, operations)

## Hung Hom Location: An Urban Advantage

Unlike CUHK in Shatin or HKUST at Clear Water Bay, PolyU is located in **Hung Hom, Kowloon** — a dense urban neighbourhood with exceptional transport connectivity:

- Direct MTR access (Hung Hom Station, East Rail Line and cross-harbour connections)
- 5 minutes to Tsim Sha Tsui, 20 minutes to Causeway Bay
- Direct rail link to Guangzhou East, Guangzhou South, and other Pearl River Delta cities
- West Kowloon High Speed Rail terminal (China destinations) a short taxi or MTR ride away

For mainland students who travel home regularly, or for students who want to maximise internship access in Hong Kong's commercial and financial districts, PolyU's urban location is a practical advantage.

## Direct Gaokao Pathway

PolyU has one of Hong Kong's most transparent and accessible pathways for mainland gaokao candidates.

**Key details for 2026 entry**:

- **Quota**: Approximately 300–400 mainland non-local places across all programs
- **Application**: Apply through PolyU's Non-JUPAS admissions portal — separate from the gaokao application process
- **Score benchmark**: Generally above 一本线 (First-Choice Line) for most programs; competitive programs such as SHTM Hotel Management and School of Design require higher scores and/or portfolio
- **Subject requirements**: Different programs have specific subject combinations — engineering programs require high Math scores; science programs require Chemistry/Biology; SHTM and Business have flexible subject requirements
- **Application window**: Applications typically open in October for the following September intake; offer letters issued from February onwards
- **Interview**: Some programs (notably Design) require a portfolio review or interview as part of the process

For mainland students who did not achieve scores sufficient for CUHK or HKUST but still want a quality Hong Kong education with strong job prospects, PolyU is often the optimal choice — particularly for design, hospitality, health sciences, or engineering tracks.

## Campus and Student Life

PolyU's campus is compact (about 9.4 hectares) and densely built — this is an urban university. However, recent redevelopment has dramatically improved the campus environment:

- **The Wing** — flagship academic tower (2023), bringing major new lecture and lab space
- **jc:deSign** — Design School building with public gallery spaces
- **Student amenities**: Cafeterias, convenience stores, student union facilities, sports centre
- **Student residences**: PolyU operates student halls both on-campus and in nearby Hung Hom properties. Non-local first-year students are given priority placement. Hall fees typically HKD 2,500–4,500 per month.

## Graduate Outcomes

PolyU tracks graduate employment outcomes carefully. Recent data shows:

- **SHTM graduates**: >90% employment within 3 months, median starting salary HKD 18,000–22,000/month
- **Engineering graduates**: >85% employment within 6 months, median HKD 18,000–25,000/month
- **Design graduates**: Variable by sub-discipline; portfolio quality is the main salary driver
- **Nursing graduates**: Near 100% employment; guaranteed positions within Hong Kong's Hospital Authority for qualifying graduates

PolyU is not the right choice for students targeting McKinsey or Goldman Sachs recruitment pipelines — those are dominated by HKUST and HKU. But for students targeting specific applied fields, PolyU's outcomes are excellent and often superior to higher-ranked generalist universities.

## Application Checklist

- Academic transcripts and certificate of senior secondary results (or predicted results for current-year students)
- English proficiency: IELTS 6.0 overall (5.5 in each band) or TOEFL 79+
- Personal statement (500–800 words for most programs)
- Portfolio or audition for relevant programs (Design, Music, etc.)
- One referee report

Apply via: polyu.edu.hk/admission (Non-JUPAS/Mainland applicant portal)
