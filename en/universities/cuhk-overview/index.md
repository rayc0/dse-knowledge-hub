# CUHK Complete Guide for International Students 2026

The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK) consistently ranks among Asia's top universities and is one of the most internationally welcoming institutions in the region. Ranked **#36 globally in the QS World University Rankings 2024**, CUHK offers a distinctive bilingual academic environment, a sprawling residential college system, and direct admission pathways for both DSE and gaokao candidates.

## Why Choose CUHK?

CUHK occupies a 137-hectare hillside campus overlooking Tolo Harbour in Shatin — one of the most scenic university campuses in Asia. Unlike the compact urban campuses common in the region, CUHK's residential college system (nine colleges total) means that most students live, eat, and study within walking distance of each other. This creates a genuine campus community that is rare in Hong Kong.

CUHK is particularly strong in:

- **Business and Finance** — CUHK Business School consistently ranks in the top 50 globally for MBA programs
- **Medicine** — Faculty of Medicine holds exceptional clinical training partnerships with Prince of Wales Hospital, directly adjacent to campus
- **Engineering and Computer Science** — Internationally recognised, with strong industry partnerships
- **Social Sciences and Humanities** — The most comprehensive humanities offering among HK's research universities
- **Chinese Studies** — Arguably the finest in the world for the intersection of traditional Chinese scholarship and modern research

## English-Taught Programs

CUHK operates on a genuinely bilingual basis, and the majority of undergraduate programs at CUHK are taught primarily or entirely in English. Key fully English-medium programs include:

- BSc in Computer Science
- BEng programs (Electronic Engineering, Information Engineering, Systems Engineering)
- BBA (Global Business Studies, Finance, Professional Accountancy)
- BSocSc programs (Economics, Global Studies, Government and Public Administration)
- MBBS (Medicine) — English medium with clinical Cantonese support

International students with strong English proficiency will find CUHK comfortable from day one. Cantonese is used more heavily in certain humanities and education-focused programs, but CUHK explicitly accommodates non-Cantonese speakers.

## The 一试两用 Gaokao Pathway

CUHK operates one of Hong Kong's most established gaokao admission programs. Under the **一试两用 (One Exam, Dual Application)** scheme, mainland Chinese students can use their gaokao scores directly to apply for admission to CUHK without taking any additional entrance exams.

Key details for the 2026 intake:

- **Eligible Applicants**: Mainland Chinese students who sit the National College Entrance Examination (高考)
- **Quota**: Approximately 200–250 mainland undergraduate places per year
- **Score Benchmark**: Generally First-Choice Line (一本线) plus a competitive premium — top departments require scores near provincial top 1–2%
- **Application Window**: March to May each year for September entry
- **Application Portal**: Apply directly through CUHK's Non-JUPAS admission platform (no UCAS or Common App)
- **Required Documents**: Gaokao scores, high school transcript, personal statement, one reference letter
- **Scholarship Availability**: CUHK offers merit scholarships of HKD 30,000–80,000 per year for top gaokao admits

This pathway makes CUHK one of the most accessible top-ranked universities for mainland students who excel in gaokao but may not have additional international qualification scores.

## Campus Life in Shatin

Shatin is a mature New Territories town with excellent transport connections. CUHK's campus is served by its own MTR station (University Station, East Rail Line), making the city centre (Hung Hom, Kowloon, Central) accessible in under 40 minutes.

Within the campus you will find:

- Nine residential colleges, each with its own dining hall, common rooms, and student societies
- Full-scale university hospital (Prince of Wales) adjacent to campus
- Shopping malls and restaurants within 10 minutes of campus (New Town Plaza, Shatin Town Hall)
- Nature trails, waterfronts, and open green space — unusually pleasant for Hong Kong

Most non-local students are guaranteed residential college housing in their first year.

## Application Summary

- **Non-JUPAS (Mainland/International)**: Apply via CUHK's Non-JUPAS portal, typically opens October–November for the following September intake
- **DSE applicants**: Apply via JUPAS (Hong Kong residents) or Non-JUPAS (non-residents)
- **Gaokao applicants**: Apply via the dedicated mainland admission portal (March–May window)
- **English requirement**: IELTS 6.5+ or TOEFL 79+ (waived for applicants from English-medium secondary schools)

CUHK is a strong first-choice option for international students who want world-class research credentials, genuine bilingual exposure, and a liveable campus environment — all at a cost that remains competitive by global standards.
