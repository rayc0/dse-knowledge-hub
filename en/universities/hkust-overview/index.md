# HKUST Complete Guide for International Students 2026

The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST) is arguably the most impressive research university story of the past three decades in Asia. Established in 1991, HKUST reached the **QS World University Rankings #40 globally in 2024** — a position that took most universities a century to achieve. For students targeting technology, engineering, business, or data science careers, HKUST occupies a rare position: elite global ranking, strong industry placement, and a compact but deeply connected campus culture.

## Why HKUST?

HKUST was designed from the ground up as a research-intensive science and technology university. Unlike older institutions that evolved from liberal arts or colonial colleges, HKUST had a clear mandate from founding: produce world-class graduates in STEM and business, with deep ties to industry.

The result is a university culture that is relentlessly pragmatic. Professors hold joint industry appointments. Coursework integrates real project briefs. The career centre has established pipelines to firms that other HK universities cannot match.

Top strengths:

- **Computer Science and AI**: QS ranks HKUST #17 globally in Computer Science (2024). Research focus areas include machine learning, robotics, computer vision — aligned with what the industry is actually hiring for.
- **Engineering**: Chemical, civil, electronic, industrial engineering programs are consistently top-ranked in Asia.
- **Business (HKUST Business School)**: Full-time MBA ranked **#7 globally by Financial Times (2024)**. Undergraduate BBA program benefits from same faculty and corporate links.
- **Data Science and Technology (DST)**: Interdisciplinary program launched 2021 — one of Asia's first purpose-built data science undergrad degrees.
- **Environmental and Ocean Science**: Unique programs benefiting from the coastal campus and HKUST's strong oceanography research centre.

## Programs and Structure

HKUST has four schools at undergraduate level:

**School of Science**
- BSc in Mathematics, Chemistry, Physics, Environmental Management, Life Science
- BSc in Data Science and Technology (joint with Engineering)

**School of Engineering**
- BEng in Computer Engineering, Computer Science, Electronic Engineering, Chemical and Biological Engineering, Civil Engineering, Industrial Engineering and Logistics

**School of Business and Management**
- BBA in Accounting, Finance, General Business Management, Global Business, Information Systems, Marketing, Operations Management
- Dual Degree programs with selected partner universities (UC Berkeley, Carnegie Mellon)

**School of Humanities and Social Science**
- BSocSc (smaller cohort — more for students wanting HKUST's research and career environment with a social science major)

All programs at HKUST are conducted in English. HKUST is the most English-dominant of Hong Kong's eight UGC-funded universities. Cantonese is essentially a social language on campus; academic and professional life runs in English.

## The Internship and Industry Network

HKUST's location — and its deliberate investment in corporate relationships — produces an internship pipeline that is unusually strong for an Asian university. Regular recruiters include:

- **Technology**: DJI, Google, Meta, Apple, Samsung, Huawei, Tencent, ByteDance, NVIDIA
- **Finance**: Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan, Morgan Stanley, HSBC, UBS, BlackRock, Jane Street, Citadel
- **Consulting**: McKinsey, BCG, Bain, Accenture, Deloitte
- **Engineering and Industry**: Arup, Mott MacDonald, Shell, CNOOC

The Career Centre runs structured internship programmes, employer information sessions, and mock interview series throughout the year. For non-local students, HKUST's brand opens doors across Asia-Pacific that would take years to build through other routes.

HKUST's Silicon Valley Center in San Jose also facilitates US exposure for students targeting North American tech careers post-graduation.

## Clear Water Bay Campus

The campus is built into a hillside above Clearwater Bay on Hong Kong's eastern coast. The setting is dramatic — glass-and-concrete research buildings stepping down a cliff face toward the South China Sea. It is visually unlike any other campus in Asia.

Practically:

- **Transport**: Minibus routes to Hang Hau MTR Station (Tseung Kwan O Line) — approximately 20 minutes to Kowloon, 40 minutes to Central. HKUST operates shuttle buses.
- **On-campus housing**: HKUST guarantees on-campus housing for all year-one non-local students. Halls are modernised, mixed-gender, and competitively priced.
- **Facilities**: Olympic-size pool, full gym, sports halls, multiple restaurants and cafeterias, convenience stores, campus health centre
- **Environment**: The isolated coastal location creates a genuine campus community — students tend to socialise on campus rather than commuting out nightly, which accelerates peer relationships and study group culture

## Admission for Non-Local Students

**Mainland students (Gaokao)**:
HKUST accepts mainland students via the Non-JUPAS mechanism. Applicants should demonstrate high-ranking gaokao results (typically in the top 1% provincially for competitive programs like Computer Science and Finance). HKUST does not operate a dedicated gaokao scoring table publicly but considers score, provincial ranking, subject combination, and a personal statement.

**Other international students**:
HKUST accepts A-Levels, IB, SAT/ACT, and other national qualifications. See the comparison guide for grade equivalencies. Minimum English requirement: IELTS 6.5 or TOEFL 79.

**Application window**: October–January for the following September intake (Non-JUPAS portal).
