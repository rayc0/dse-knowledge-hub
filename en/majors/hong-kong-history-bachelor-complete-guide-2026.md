# Hong Kong History Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

History is the oldest of the humanities and one of the most quietly versatile undergraduate degrees a DSE student can pursue. In Hong Kong, the subject has become unusually relevant: the city sits at the crossroads of Chinese, British colonial, maritime Asian, and global modern histories, and its universities have built strong departments to match. For the 2026 JUPAS cycle, five UGC-funded institutions admit students into a History major — the University of Hong Kong (HKU), the Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK), Hong Kong Baptist University (HKBU), Lingnan University, and the Education University of Hong Kong (EdUHK). This guide walks through programme codes, admission scores, curriculum design, faculty strengths, careers, and the realistic question every DSE student asks: *can I actually make a living with a History degree in Hong Kong in 2026?*

## Why study History at a Hong Kong university

A History BA in Hong Kong is not a "stuck in the past" degree. The curricula at HKU, CUHK, HKBU, and Lingnan all centre four trainable skills: archival research, evidence-weighted argument, long-form writing, and the ability to read and synthesise complex sources in multiple languages. Employers in Hong Kong — particularly in law, public service, journalism, financial research, heritage management, and the GBA cultural economy — increasingly value graduates who can reason from primary evidence rather than from templates.

Three structural factors make Hong Kong especially attractive for the subject:

1. **Bilingual archival access.** HKU's Special Collections, the Public Records Office at Kwun Tong, CUHK's University Library, and the HKBU Library Special Collections together hold one of the densest concentrations of Chinese and English colonial-era records in Asia. Undergraduates routinely work with these in seminar courses.
2. **GBA cross-border research.** Field-trip components — Macau, Guangzhou, Foshan, Shenzhen, occasionally Taipei — are a normal part of the Asian-history strands. The Greater Bay Area is a living laboratory for economic, urban, and migration history.
3. **Global comparative pathways.** All five institutions offer overseas exchange typically in Years 2–3. HKU and CUHK have particularly deep exchange partnerships with Oxford, Cambridge, Edinburgh, Yale, Toronto, and Kyoto for humanities students.

## The five JUPAS routes into History in 2026

### HKU — JS6054 Bachelor of Arts (History major)

HKU does not run a stand-alone History BA. Students apply to **JS6054 Bachelor of Arts** through JUPAS, take introductory courses in Year 1, and **declare a Major in History** before progressing to Year 2. To complete the major, students take at least 6 credits of introductory History courses and at least 54 credits of advanced courses, with a minimum of 12 credits in Asian history and 12 credits in Western history. Advanced courses split into *survey* (broad geographic or period overviews) and *seminar* (deep-dive on primary sources and historiography) formats.

Recent strengths in the department include modern China, Hong Kong colonial and post-handover history, the British Empire, the history of science and medicine, and South-East Asian maritime history. Indicative JUPAS admission scores for JS6054 in 2025 sat at a median of around 24–25 (best-five-subjects, HKU formula with 5\*\*=8.5, 5\*=7, 5=5.5). The programme is not gate-kept against humanities-leaning students who score well in English, History, and one extension subject.

### CUHK — JS4001 Bachelor of Arts (broad admission) / Department of History

CUHK admits most BA students through **JS4001 Bachelor of Arts (broad admission)**. Students declare a major at the end of Year 1; History is one of the most stable and well-resourced choices, run by the Department of History at the Faculty of Arts. CUHK's History major is famously strong in Chinese history — particularly Ming-Qing, Republican, and Cold War-era China — and has growing strands in environmental history, food history, and women's history.

Programme-specific requirements for JS4001 typically expect strong results in English, Chinese, and either History, Chinese History, or a humanities elective. The median JUPAS admission score for CUHK BA broad admission in 2025 was around 22–24 (best-five, CUHK formula). CUHK also offers a dedicated double major and minor architecture, so students can combine History with Anthropology, Cultural Studies, Government & Public Administration, Translation, or Japanese Studies without lengthening the degree.

### HKBU — JS2610 Bachelor of Arts (Hons) / Bachelor of Social Sciences (Hons) broad-based admission

HKBU runs an unusual but useful broad-based humanities admission. Students apply to **JS2610**, which clusters European Studies (French/German stream), Geography, Global & China Studies, Government & International Studies, **History**, and Sociology. Students complete a foundation year and then declare a major via internal application before Year 2.

HKBU's History department emphasises a balanced China-and-world curriculum, with notable depth in modern Chinese intellectual history, religion, Hong Kong local history, and public history. Career-oriented strands include archival work, museum studies, and journalism — supported by the university's strong Communication faculty. Indicative JUPAS scores for JS2610 in 2025 sat around 19–21 (HKBU formula with 5\*\*=7), making it the most accessible of the broad humanities entry points among the seven UGC universities.

### Lingnan University — JS7709 Bachelor of Arts (Hons) in History (single-major dedicated programme)

Lingnan is the only Hong Kong UGC university that runs a **single-major dedicated History BA** through JUPAS — students applying to **JS7709** are admitted directly into the History programme, not a broad faculty pool. Two **optional concentrations** are available: **Asian History** and **Global History**. This is a meaningful structural difference: students start specialist training in Year 1 rather than Year 2.

Lingnan's liberal-arts residential model — small classes, all-undergraduate focus, mandatory hostel living for at least three years — suits History particularly well. Class sizes for senior seminars are often in the 8–15 range, allowing for the kind of close supervision and primary-source workshopping that is rare in larger faculties. Median JUPAS admission scores for JS7709 in 2025 ranged roughly from 16 to 19 (Lingnan formula). For students with a clear humanities vocation but more modest DSE scores, this is the most direct route into the discipline.

### EdUHK — humanities and BEd pathways with History content

The Education University of Hong Kong does not offer a pure single-major BA in History, but it offers **Bachelor of Education (BEd) Honours in Chinese History** and humanities-track programmes for students who plan to teach. EdUHK is the dominant source of secondary-school Chinese History and Liberal Studies / Citizenship and Social Development teachers in Hong Kong, and its BEd is the most efficient route into government and aided-school teaching. Indicative JUPAS admission scores for EdUHK humanities BEd programmes in 2025 sat around 18–21.

## Curriculum: what you actually study in Years 1–4

Across the five programmes, a typical History BA Year-by-Year shape looks like this:

- **Year 1** — Introductory survey courses: usually one in modern China, one in modern Europe or world history, plus a "doing history" methods course covering source criticism, citation, and historiographical reading. University Common Core and language requirements run in parallel.
- **Year 2** — Geographic and thematic surveys: South-East Asia, the British Empire, the Cold War, environmental history, history of science. Students typically also begin a second language (Japanese, Korean, French, German) or take ancient/classical Chinese for archival reading.
- **Year 3** — Seminar courses, exchange semester, and the first capstone research paper (usually 6,000–8,000 words). This is where most students discover their thesis area.
- **Year 4** — Final-year project / dissertation (typically 10,000–15,000 words supervised one-to-one), advanced seminars, and electives that may double-count toward a minor.

HKU's BA structure mandates the Asian/Western split. CUHK's structure allows more flexibility through the second major / minor mechanism. HKBU's broad-based entry compresses some methods training into the foundation year. Lingnan's dedicated structure front-loads disciplinary identity in Year 1.

## Admission scoring — how the four formulas differ

DSE applicants should understand that each university converts DSE grades to different point scales when computing the best-five total. Quick reference:

- **HKU:** 5\*\* = 8.5, 5\* = 7, 5 = 5.5, 4 = 4, 3 = 3, 2 = 2, 1 = 1.
- **CUHK:** typically 5\*\* = 8.5, 5\* = 7, 5 = 5.5 with programme-specific subject weighting; an updated calculator is released between May and June each cycle.
- **HKBU:** 5\*\* = 7, 5\* = 6, 5 = 5, 4 = 4, 3 = 3, 2 = 2, 1 = 1.
- **Lingnan:** uses a 7-point scale similar to HKBU for most BAs.

Across the four arts faculties, a strong DSE result in **English (4 or above)**, **Chinese (3 or above)**, and **History** or **Chinese History** (4 or above) is the typical realistic entry profile. Mathematics at level 2 or 3 is usually sufficient. None of the History programmes require an extension Mathematics module.

## Hong Kong History — the local specialism

One unique opportunity in Hong Kong is the depth of **Hong Kong History as a sub-discipline**. HKU offers a postgraduate Master of Arts in the field of Hong Kong History, and undergraduate seminar courses on Hong Kong's colonial period, post-1949 migration, the 1967 disturbances, the handover, and post-handover political history are taught at HKU, CUHK, and HKBU. Students who plan to work in government, heritage, museum, or local journalism roles often build their final-year dissertation in this area, sometimes drawing on the Public Records Office or oral-history archives.

## Careers — the honest picture

History graduates in Hong Kong do not have a single dominant career corridor the way Accountancy or Nursing graduates do. Instead, the degree feeds a wide spectrum:

- **Government / Administrative Officer grade / Executive Officer grade** — historically a major employer of CUHK and HKU arts graduates; the AO grade in particular looks favourably on humanities reasoning skills.
- **Education** — secondary-school Chinese History, History, and Citizenship & Social Development teachers, especially via the PGDE route at HKU, CUHK, EdUHK, or directly through EdUHK BEd.
- **Law (postgraduate JD route)** — Hong Kong's two-year JD at HKU, CUHK, and CityU welcomes History graduates; the analytical writing trains directly into legal reasoning.
- **Journalism and publishing** — SCMP, RTHK, HK01, and Chinese-language outlets continue to hire humanities graduates with strong language skills.
- **Heritage, museums, archives** — the Hong Kong Museum of History, the Heritage Museum, the M+ research function, and the Antiquities and Monuments Office hire small but consistent numbers of History graduates.
- **Financial research** — equity research desks at banks occasionally hire humanities graduates for sector research roles requiring deep reading, writing, and synthesis. This is not the median path but it exists.
- **Postgraduate study** — MA or MPhil at HKU/CUHK/HKBU, or international MA (Oxford, Cambridge, LSE, SOAS, Yale, Edinburgh, Toronto) — a meaningful 15–20% of strong History BAs continue into research training.

Starting salaries for fresh History graduates in Hong Kong in 2025 cluster around HK$18,000 to HK$25,000 per month in administrative or media roles; AO grade and JD-route trajectories rise far above that within five years. The honest framing for parents: a History BA is not the *highest* starting-salary route, but its **ten-year career flexibility** is among the highest of any humanities degree.

## How to choose between the five programmes

A practical decision framework:

- **You want the broadest exit options and the deepest archive access:** HKU JS6054.
- **You want strong Chinese history and a flexible double-major architecture:** CUHK JS4001.
- **You want a humanities entry with room to switch into Geography, Sociology, or Government in Year 2:** HKBU JS2610.
- **You are certain you want History and want a small, intensive, single-major experience from Day 1:** Lingnan JS7709.
- **You want to become a secondary-school Chinese History teacher with the most direct route:** EdUHK BEd Chinese History.

## What to do in S5 and S6 if you are aiming for a History BA

1. **Read outside the syllabus.** A History admissions interviewer at HKU or CUHK is not interested in your DSE syllabus answers — they want to know what you have read on your own. Suggested entry points: Jonathan Spence on China, Eric Hobsbawm on the modern world, Frank Dikötter on the Mao era, Steve Tsang on Hong Kong, Patricia Buckley Ebrey on Chinese society.
2. **Keep your English strong.** All five History BAs are taught primarily in English. Aim for DSE English Level 4 or above.
3. **Consider an extension subject in History or Chinese History.** A 5 or 5\* in History strengthens both your raw score and your SLP narrative for any humanities programme.
4. **Plan your SLP and OEA entries** around historical or cultural-heritage activities — Hong Kong History Project tours, museum docent volunteering, archival transcription work, district council records projects. These are exactly the activities that JUPAS readers reward in interviews.
5. **Visit info days.** HKU Arts, CUHK Arts, HKBU Arts and Social Sciences, and Lingnan all run open days in October and December — the conversations with current students are usually more useful than the marketing brochures.

A History BA in Hong Kong in 2026 is a serious, employable, intellectually demanding degree. The five available JUPAS routes give students at a wide range of DSE bands a realistic path into the discipline — and the city's archives, languages, and cross-border access make Hong Kong one of the more interesting places in the world to study the past.
