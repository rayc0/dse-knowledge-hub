# Hong Kong Physiotherapy Bachelor (PolyU JS3636) Complete Guide 2026

In Hong Kong, "physiotherapist" is a regulated allied health title carrying social standing, pay and job security comparable to many medical specialties. To practise legally, a graduate must register with the **Physiotherapists Board of Hong Kong** under the Supplementary Medical Professions Ordinance (Cap. 359). The fastest and most direct undergraduate route to that licence is a single programme: **The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU) Department of Rehabilitation Sciences, JS3636 Bachelor of Science (Honours) in Physiotherapy**. Together with Occupational Therapy and Medical Imaging, Physiotherapy is one of "PolyU's three treasures" - perennially among the highest-scoring, most competitive non-medical undergraduate offers in the city.

This guide is written for 2026 entrants and prospective DSE, Non-JUPAS and Mainland Gaokao applicants. It walks through curriculum structure, admission requirements, clinical placements, professional registration, graduate destinations and salary scales, with practical notes on subject choice and interview preparation.

## 1. Why PolyU Physiotherapy? Hong Kong's only direct registration pathway

Under Cap. 359, no person in Hong Kong may use the title "physiotherapist" or practise independently without registration with the Physiotherapists Board. For decades, the only locally-trained, full-time undergraduate qualification recognised for direct registration has been **PolyU JS3636**. Hong Kong Metropolitan University (HKMU) and the Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK) have launched or planned new undergraduate physiotherapy programmes in recent years, but PolyU remains the long-established institution whose degree is fully recognised by **World Physiotherapy** (formerly WCPT) and whose graduates qualify for Hong Kong registration **without sitting Part I/Part II licensure examinations**.

Direct registration is the headline benefit. After completing the BSc (Hons) and the minimum 1,000 hours of supervised clinical practice required by the Board, PolyU graduates apply to the Physiotherapists Board and receive their Registered Physiotherapist (RPT) status through documentary review alone. Holders of overseas physiotherapy degrees, by contrast, must submit their qualifications for assessment and most are required to pass a written examination and a clinical competency assessment before registration is granted.

## 2. Programme structure: four years, 132 credits, 17% clinical

### 2.1 Basics

- **JUPAS code**: JS3636
- **Duration**: 4 years, full-time
- **Total credits**: 132 (approximately 23 of which are clinical/fieldwork credits)
- **Department**: Rehabilitation Sciences, within the Faculty of Health and Social Sciences
- **Entry**: September 2026
- **Intake**: 150 students per cohort (combined JUPAS and Non-JUPAS), making it one of the smaller and more selective allied health intakes in Hong Kong
- **Funding status**: Government-funded (UGC)
- **Award**: BSc (Hons) in Physiotherapy

### 2.2 Curriculum spine

The four-year curriculum is built on a science-led foundation followed by progressive clinical specialisation:

- **Year 1** - Anatomy, physiology, biomechanics, kinesiology, foundational behavioural sciences and an introduction to physiotherapy practice. Students also complete University Core, GUR (General University Requirements) including Service Learning, Chinese, English and Healthy Lifestyle components.
- **Year 2** - Pathology, exercise physiology, electrophysical agents, manual therapy, musculoskeletal and cardiopulmonary physiotherapy basics, evidence-based practice. The first short hospital observations begin.
- **Year 3** - Neurological physiotherapy, paediatric physiotherapy, women's health, sports physiotherapy, geriatric rehabilitation. Block clinical placements alternate with academic blocks. Students are expected to begin a Capstone or Honours research project.
- **Year 4** - Advanced clinical practice, professional ethics and management, capstone project completion, and intensive clinical internship rotations across acute, sub-acute, community and outpatient settings.

### 2.3 Clinical placements: 1,000+ hours across nine specialty areas

Clinical hours are the spine of the programme, and the Physiotherapists Board requires at least **1,000 hours** of supervised practice for registration. Placements rotate through the major Hospital Authority clusters (Queen Mary, Prince of Wales, Pamela Youde, United Christian, Princess Margaret and others), private rehabilitation centres, sports institutes and community settings such as elderly care homes and special schools. Placements cover at least nine specialty areas: musculoskeletal, neurological, cardiopulmonary, paediatrics, geriatrics, sports, women's health, ICU/acute care and community/primary care.

In Years 3 and 4, eligible students may apply for an optional **5-week international clinical placement**. Past destinations have included Australia, Canada, Denmark, Finland, Sweden, Switzerland, Taiwan, Thailand and the USA. Spaces are limited and selection is competitive based on GPA and clinical performance.

## 3. JUPAS admission: subject weighting and scoring

### 3.1 No compulsory science, but science weighting is real

The programme has **no compulsory subject** beyond the standard 4C+2X profile (Chinese, English, Mathematics, Citizenship & Social Development at Level 2/Attained, plus two electives). However, the admission score formula heavily favours science:

- **Best 5 subjects + 1 bonus**, with strict subject weighting
- **Higher weighting** for English, Mathematics (Compulsory or Extended Module M1/M2), Biology, Chemistry, Physics and Combined Science (with Biology/Physics components)
- **Standard weighting** for Chinese and other electives
- Citizenship & Social Development is not counted in the best-5

Most successful candidates take **Biology + Chemistry** as their core electives, with Physics or M1/M2 as the third X. A Combined Science (Biology + Physics) profile is acceptable but tends to produce a slightly lower score because it is treated as a single subject in the best-5 calculation.

### 3.2 Indicative admission scores (HKDSE 7-level)

Based on the most recently published JUPAS admission figures, JS3636 sits firmly inside the top tier of allied-health programmes:

- **Median best-5 + weighting score**: approximately 31-32 points (close to 5*5*5*5*5 territory)
- **Lower quartile**: around 30 points
- **Upper quartile**: around 33 points
- Conversion to PolyU's own weighted scale typically falls in the **215-220 range**, broadly in line with second-batch Medicine programmes and ahead of most non-medical professional degrees

In practice, candidates aiming at JS3636 plan for an "almost-medicine" academic profile: 5* in core subjects, 5 minimum in two science electives, and a strong English score (Level 5 or above) to clear interview shortlisting comfortably.

### 3.3 Interview

Shortlisted candidates are invited to a **group or individual interview**, usually held in May-June after the JUPAS application close. The interview assesses:

- Communication skills in English (and sometimes Cantonese)
- Awareness of the physiotherapist's scope of practice and the differences from doctors, occupational therapists and chiropractors
- Ethical reasoning - typical scenarios involve elderly patients, paediatric cases or refusal of treatment
- Commitment to a healthcare career and physical readiness for a clinically demanding profession

Interview performance does not replace the academic score but is used to break ties among candidates with similar DSE results. A weak interview can move a borderline candidate down to the waiting list.

## 4. Non-JUPAS, international and Mainland Gaokao routes

### 4.1 Non-JUPAS Year 1 entry

Non-JUPAS Year 1 entry is open to applicants holding GCE A-Level, IB Diploma, SAT/AP, or other recognised qualifications. Indicative requirements:

- **GCE A-Level**: typically AAA in three subjects, including at least one of Biology, Chemistry, Physics
- **IB Diploma**: 38-42 points, including HL Biology or Chemistry at 6 or 7
- **SAT**: 1380+ (Evidence-Based Reading & Writing, Math) plus AP scores of 4-5 in two relevant subjects
- **English**: IELTS 6.5 (no sub-score below 6.0) or TOEFL iBT 80, unless the qualification is taught and examined in English
- **Application deadline**: typically 5 February of the entry year

### 4.2 Mainland Gaokao route

PolyU operates outside the Mainland Mass Enrolment Scheme and recruits Gaokao candidates through its own **Mainland Direct Admissions** track. For physiotherapy:

- **Gaokao total score**: well above the local first-tier (一本/特殊类型) line, typically equivalent to top-30 Mainland university level
- **English subscore**: 130/150 or higher
- Subject preference: science track (理科/物理类) with strong Biology and Physics
- Interview: required, usually conducted in English with some Cantonese

### 4.3 International applicants

International applicants apply through PolyU's International Admissions portal with a deadline of around mid-May. The same English thresholds apply, plus a clinical-practice readiness check (no relevant communicable disease restrictions, ability to cope with physical workload).

## 5. Tuition, scholarships and total cost

- **UGC tuition (local students)**: HK$44,500 per academic year
- **Non-local tuition**: HK$170,000-180,000 per academic year (for 2026 entry; refer to PolyU's official figures for the latest)
- **Hostel** (limited, allocated by points): HK$15,000-22,000 per year
- **Off-campus housing in Hung Hom / Ho Man Tin**: HK$6,000-10,000 per month for a shared flat
- **Books, uniform, lab consumables**: HK$3,000-5,000 per year; clinical placements require a uniform set, name badge and a stethoscope/goniometer kit
- **Insurance**: PolyU clinical placements require professional indemnity coverage, included via student membership of the Hong Kong Physiotherapy Association

Major scholarships include the HKSAR Government Scholarship, PolyU Talent Award, Reaching Out Award (for international placements) and various Hospital Authority bonded scholarships that subsidise tuition in exchange for a service commitment after graduation.

## 6. Registration and the pathway to practise

After graduation, the registration steps are:

1. **Apply for full registration** with the Physiotherapists Board of Hong Kong (Supplementary Medical Professions Council)
2. Submit transcripts confirming completion of the BSc (Hons) and 1,000 hours of supervised clinical practice
3. Pay the registration fee and provide certificate of good standing if applicable
4. Receive Registered Physiotherapist (RPT) status, valid subject to renewal and Continuing Professional Development (CPD) requirements

Most graduates begin work as **Physiotherapist II** in the Hospital Authority, in private hospitals, or in NGO/community settings. A growing minority go straight into private practice or sports/performance settings, often with a dual qualification in dry needling, manual therapy or sports rehabilitation.

## 7. Salary, career progression and overseas mobility

### 7.1 Hospital Authority pay scale

- **Physiotherapist II (entry)**: starting salary on Master Pay Scale (MPS) Point 16, approximately **HK$36,000-39,000 per month**, with annual increments
- **Physiotherapist I**: typically reached after 5-8 years, MPS 25 onward, **HK$55,000-65,000 per month**
- **Senior Physiotherapist**: department head or specialty lead, **HK$80,000-100,000+ per month**
- 13th-month pay, housing allowance, government pension contribution (or MPF) and continuing education leave are standard benefits

### 7.2 Private sector

Private hospitals and clinics offer base salaries roughly comparable to the HA, with bonus structures based on clinical caseload. Sports physiotherapists embedded with professional teams or elite training centres can earn HK$60,000-100,000+ per month with performance incentives. A self-employed physiotherapist running a Hung Hom or Central clinic with three to five years of experience often grosses HK$100,000+ per month, before rent and overheads.

### 7.3 Overseas mobility

Because PolyU's degree is recognised by World Physiotherapy, graduates are well-placed to register in Australia (via APC), the UK (HCPC), Canada (PCC after the PCE) and Singapore (AHPC). Each jurisdiction may require a written and/or clinical exam, but PolyU's curriculum maps closely to international competency standards, and a meaningful share of each cohort emigrates within five to ten years of graduation.

## 8. Choosing physiotherapy: who should apply

JS3636 is well suited to applicants who:

- Have a strong, sustained interest in human anatomy, movement and rehabilitation
- Are physically active and comfortable with hands-on, full-body patient contact
- Communicate clearly in both Cantonese and English (the patient mix is bilingual, often with Mandarin and other dialects too)
- Can sustain a clinically intense schedule from Year 2 onwards
- Want a healthcare career with **direct patient impact, professional autonomy and predictable hours** without the length and intensity of a medical degree

Applicants who are uncertain between Medicine, Physiotherapy and Occupational Therapy should shadow a working physiotherapist before committing - the day-to-day work of a physiotherapist is physical, repetitive and deeply collaborative, and the right "fit" is more important than chasing the highest-scoring offer.

## 9. Practical timeline for a 2026 applicant

- **Now to October 2025**: Lock in best-5 subject combination (target Biology, Chemistry plus one of Physics/M1/M2/Economics)
- **December 2025**: Submit JUPAS application with JS3636 in Band A, position 1 or 2
- **April 2026**: HKDSE examinations
- **May-June 2026**: Interview if shortlisted
- **Mid-July 2026**: HKDSE results release; JUPAS post-results modification within 24 hours
- **Early August 2026**: Main round JUPAS offer
- **September 2026**: Programme commencement, white-coat ceremony, first anatomy labs

PolyU JS3636 is a high-stakes, high-reward target: the bar is steep, but the destination is one of the most secure, well-respected and portable healthcare careers Hong Kong offers. For students with the academic profile and the clinical disposition, no other local programme delivers a faster route from secondary school to a Registered Physiotherapist title.
