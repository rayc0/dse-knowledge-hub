# Hong Kong Accountancy Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

Accountancy is one of the most reliably employable undergraduate degrees in Hong Kong. The city is one of Asia's largest financial centres, the regional headquarters for hundreds of multinationals, and home to the Big 4 firms (Deloitte, EY, KPMG, PwC) plus a deep ecosystem of mid-tier firms, in-house finance teams and listed-company audit committees. A professionally accredited accounting degree is the single most direct route into that ecosystem — and, eventually, to the Hong Kong CPA designation.

This guide explains what an accountancy bachelor's degree in Hong Kong actually involves, which universities offer it, how to get in through JUPAS or as a non-local applicant, how professional accreditation works, and what graduate careers and salaries look like in 2026.

## What an accountancy degree covers

A Hong Kong accounting degree is almost always delivered as a **BBA (Bachelor of Business Administration)** with an Accounting or Professional Accountancy major, lasting four years under the 334 system. The first year is broad business foundation — economics, statistics, information systems, business law and management — before specialisation deepens from Year 2.

Core accounting subjects typically include:

- **Financial accounting** — preparing and interpreting financial statements under HKFRS/IFRS
- **Management accounting** — costing, budgeting and decision support
- **Auditing and assurance** — audit methodology, risk and professional ethics
- **Taxation** — Hong Kong profits tax, salaries tax and increasingly cross-border/China tax
- **Corporate finance and financial management**
- **Business and company law**
- **Accounting information systems and data analytics** — a fast-growing area, with most programmes now embedding Python, data visualisation, ESG reporting and audit automation

Modern programmes have shifted noticeably toward technology. "Digital finance", analytics and sustainability reporting now appear in core curricula because the profession itself is automating routine bookkeeping and moving accountants up the value chain into advisory, controls and data work.

## The major programmes in Hong Kong

Every UGC-funded university with a business school offers an accounting pathway. The exact JUPAS programme codes and intake numbers change each cycle, so always confirm on [jupas.edu.hk](https://www.jupas.edu.hk/en/) before finalising choices — but the landscape in 2026 looks like this.

### The University of Hong Kong (HKU Business School)

HKU offers accounting through its **BBA in Accounting and Finance** (BBA(Acc&Fin)) and related streams. HKU's accounting degrees — including BBA(Acc&Fin), BBA(IBGM), the BBA(Law) variants and the integrated BBA — carry full HKICPA accreditation. HKU is the most competitive entry point and the strongest brand for investment banking and consulting crossover.

### The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK Business School)

CUHK's **School of Accountancy** is one of the oldest and most prestigious in the region. Accounting is offered as a focused programme and also as a concentration within CUHK's Integrated BBA Programme, allowing students to keep options open before declaring a major. CUHK Business School has exceptionally strong Big 4 and corporate placement.

### The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST Business School)

HKUST admits business students through a broad-based BBA scheme and lets students declare an **Accounting major** later. HKUST's quantitative reputation makes it attractive for students aiming at advisory, transaction services and finance-adjacent roles. JUPAS applicants must meet HKUST's general "332A33" core-subject minimum.

### The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU Faculty of Business)

PolyU's **BBA (Hons) Scheme in Accounting and Finance — JUPAS code JS3060** is one of the clearest accounting routes in the system, with three award options: *Accountancy*, *Accounting and Finance*, and *Digital Finance and Investment*. The 2026/27 minimum requirement is Chinese Level 3, English Level 3, Mathematics (Compulsory) Level 2, Citizenship and Social Development "Attained", plus two electives at Level 3 (or one elective at Level 3 plus a Mathematics Extended Module at Level 3). English and Mathematics carry the highest weighting in the admission score, and applicants with 25.0+ in their best five subjects are guaranteed their most preferred major.

### City University of Hong Kong (CityU College of Business)

CityU offers a dedicated **BBA Accountancy** programme. It is HKICPA-accredited and known for strong industry connections, professional-body partnerships and a practical, exam-oriented curriculum that maps closely to the CPA syllabus.

### Hong Kong Baptist University (HKBU School of Business)

HKBU offers accounting within its AACSB-accredited School of Business, frequently combined with a law or analytics element. Smaller cohorts mean closer faculty contact.

### Lingnan University

Lingnan's **BBA (Hons) major in Accounting and Corporate Governance** is HKICPA-accredited as a Category 1 programme, with a liberal-arts business model and small classes — a good option for students who want professional accreditation outside the largest universities.

> **Self-financing route:** Programmes such as those at HKMU, Hang Seng University and CityU SCOPE also offer accredited or articulation-friendly accounting degrees, which can be relevant for associate-degree holders or students who narrowly miss UGC-funded cut-offs.

## Admission requirements through JUPAS

Most accounting programmes set the standard "**332A33**" minimum: Level 3 in Chinese and English, Level 2 in Mathematics (Compulsory), "Attained" in Citizenship and Social Development, plus Level 3 in two electives. In practice, competitive admission scores sit far above the minimum — top business schools routinely admit students with best-five scores well into the mid-20s and above, and English plus Mathematics are almost always the most heavily weighted subjects.

Useful (though not always required) electives include **BAFS, Economics, Mathematics Extended Module M1/M2 and ICT**. A strong Mathematics result is genuinely advantageous: accounting has become more quantitative and analytics-heavy, and admissions weightings reward it.

Selection is primarily score-based, but band choices and, for some programmes, supplementary information matter. Placing the programme in **Band A** signals genuine interest and can affect borderline decisions.

## Non-local and Mainland applicants

International and Mainland students apply through each university's **non-JUPAS / international admissions** route, not JUPAS. Hong Kong accounting degrees are popular with Mainland students because the CPA designation and Big 4 brand transfer well to Greater Bay Area and Mainland careers.

Key points for non-local applicants:

- Apply directly via each university's admissions portal using your own qualification (Gaokao, IB, A-Level, AP, etc.)
- Demonstrate English proficiency (typically IELTS 6.5+ or TOEFL ~80+, or equivalent — check programme specifics)
- Non-local students need a **student visa** sponsored by the university
- After graduation, the **IANG visa** allows graduates to stay and work in Hong Kong — accountancy graduates are well placed to use it because Big 4 and commercial finance roles actively recruit fresh graduates

## Professional accreditation and the CPA pathway

This is the most important part of choosing an accounting programme. An **HKICPA-accredited degree** lets you enter the Hong Kong Institute of Certified Public Accountants' **Qualification Programme (QP)** without first completing a separate conversion programme. Graduating from an accredited degree typically grants exemptions from the QP's foundational/Associate-level modules, leaving you to focus on the professional and capstone levels.

The standard route to becoming a **Hong Kong CPA** is:

1. Complete an HKICPA-accredited bachelor's degree
2. Register as a QP student and complete the remaining QP modules and final examination
3. Accumulate the required practical work experience (commonly around three years) under an authorised employer/supervisor
4. Be admitted as a CPA

Most Hong Kong accounting degrees are also recognised by **ACCA** and **CPA Australia**, often with generous paper exemptions, which is valuable if you plan to work regionally or internationally. Always verify the exemption table on the relevant professional body's website for the specific programme and intake year, because accreditation status is reviewed periodically.

If you take a general business or non-accredited degree, you can still qualify as a CPA, but you will need a **conversion/pre-entry programme** (offered by HKICPA-recognised providers) before joining the QP — an extra cost in time and money. This is why accreditation should be a top selection criterion.

## Careers and graduate outcomes

Accountancy is consistently one of the strongest degrees for graduate employment in Hong Kong. Typical destinations:

- **Big 4 (Deloitte, EY, KPMG, PwC):** audit, tax, advisory, transaction services, risk and increasingly technology consulting. The Big 4 are the largest single recruiters of Hong Kong accounting graduates and run structured campus recruitment from Year 3.
- **Mid-tier and local firms:** BDO, Grant Thornton, Mazars/Forvis and others, often offering broader early responsibility.
- **Commercial / in-house finance:** financial reporting, FP&A, internal audit and controllership at listed companies, banks and multinationals.
- **Financial services:** banking operations, fund accounting, product control and audit within investment banks and asset managers.
- **Government and regulators:** the Inland Revenue Department, Audit Commission, ICAC and the AFRC.

The career structure is well defined: most graduates join an audit or tax firm, qualify as a CPA within roughly three to four years, then either progress toward manager/partner or move "in-house" to a corporate finance role — a transition that usually comes with a meaningful pay rise.

## Salary expectations in 2026

Hong Kong salary surveys for 2026 point to continued upward pressure on entry-level accounting and finance pay, with firms competing for digitally skilled graduates and entry-level increases reported at up to around 8% year on year as recruitment markets stay tight.

Indicative ranges (gross, vary by firm, role and qualifications):

- **Big 4 first-year associate:** broadly in the region of **HK$19,000–24,000+ per month**, rising quickly with CPA progress and overtime
- **Newly qualified CPA (≈3 years):** often a substantial step up, frequently into the **HK$35,000–50,000+ per month** range
- **In-house / commercial finance** typically pays more than practice at the same experience level and offers better hours, which is why many qualified CPAs make the move

Treat all figures as indicative — actual offers depend on firm, division, performance and the broader hiring cycle.

## Is an accountancy degree right for you?

Accountancy rewards students who are systematic, detail-oriented, comfortable with structured rules and numerate without needing to be highly mathematical. It offers something many degrees do not: a **clear, portable professional qualification** with strong demand across audit, corporate finance and the public sector, and good mobility into the Greater Bay Area and internationally.

The trade-offs are real: early-career hours in audit can be demanding around busy season, the professional exams require sustained effort alongside work, and routine compliance work is being automated. The graduates who thrive lean into the parts machines cannot do well — judgement, advisory, controls, data analytics and client relationships.

If you want a degree with a defined route to a respected qualification and consistently strong employment, an HKICPA-accredited Hong Kong accounting bachelor's degree remains one of the safest, highest-return choices in the JUPAS system. Prioritise **accreditation status**, the **strength of the business school's recruitment pipeline**, and a curriculum that takes **data and technology** seriously — those three factors will matter far more to your career than small differences in entry score.
