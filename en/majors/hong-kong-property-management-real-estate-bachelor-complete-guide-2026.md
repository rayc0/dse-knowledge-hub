# Hong Kong Property Management & Real Estate Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

Hong Kong's property sector is one of the world's most densely capitalised real estate markets — a city of about 7.5 million people that sustains five Hong Kong Exchange-listed property developers in the global top 30 by market capitalisation, more than HK$200 billion of Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT) assets, and a managed property portfolio in excess of 1.3 billion square feet. Behind that market sits a specialised undergraduate ecosystem covering surveying, building services, real estate finance, housing management and facility/property management. For DSE candidates and international students considering a built-environment career in Hong Kong, this guide walks through the main 2026 JUPAS routes, the differences between "real estate," "surveying" and "property management" degrees, professional registration with HKIS, HKIH and RICS, and the employment market across developers, REITs, the Housing Authority and Hong Kong's "big four" property management firms.

## 1. Why Hong Kong is a global hub for Property and Real Estate education

Three structural features make Hong Kong's built-environment programmes distinctive:

- **Vertical density.** Hong Kong is the world's tallest city by number of skyscrapers (over 550 buildings above 150 m). Managing high-rise residential estates with 30,000-plus residents per development created an entire sub-discipline of "estate management" that does not exist in most overseas curricula.
- **Land lease system.** All non-Crown land in Hong Kong is leasehold (typically 50 years from 1997), which makes lease modification, premium assessment and land valuation core technical skills taught in local surveying programmes — far more than in freehold-dominated jurisdictions like the US or Australia.
- **Mandatory licensing.** The *Property Management Services Ordinance* (Cap. 626), fully effective since 2020, requires licensed Property Management Practitioners (PMPs) and Property Management Companies (PMCs) for most multi-unit residential buildings. A recognised bachelor degree is the fastest route to a Tier-1 PMP licence.

## 2. The four families of "real estate" bachelor degrees in Hong Kong

Prospective students often conflate four quite different programmes. Choose carefully — they lead to different professional bodies and different jobs.

### 2.1 Surveying (General Practice / Quantity / Building / Land)

The "professional" route accredited by the **Hong Kong Institute of Surveyors (HKIS)** and the **Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors (RICS)**. Graduates can sit the Assessment of Professional Competence (APC) after two years of structured training and become a Chartered Surveyor (MRICS / MHKIS).

- **PolyU JS3789 — BSc (Hons) in Surveying**: traditional core, with streams in General Practice (valuation, agency, asset management) and Quantity Surveying (cost consultancy, contracts).
- **HKU BSc in Surveying** (under the Faculty of Architecture, BRE department): emphasises real estate economics and land law.
- **CityU JS1011 — BSc in Surveying**: relatively newer programme, integrated with construction management.

### 2.2 Real Estate (Finance and Investment focus)

These degrees are taught inside business schools or BRE departments and weight finance, valuation modelling, REITs and capital markets more heavily than measurement or construction.

- **HKU BSc in Real Estate** (BRE department, Faculty of Architecture): a finance-leaning real estate programme strongly placed into developers (Sun Hung Kai, CK Asset, Henderson) and REIT managers (Link, Champion, Fortune).
- **HKUST has no dedicated real estate undergraduate degree** but Finance majors often take Real Estate Finance electives.

### 2.3 Construction & Environment (Building, Building Services, Building Engineering)

Engineering-oriented degrees focused on the *delivery* and *operation* of the physical asset.

- **PolyU JS3004 — Bachelor's Degree Scheme in Construction and Environment**: a Year-1 admission scheme funnelling students into Building Engineering & Management, Building Services Engineering, Surveying, or Property Management at the end of Year 1. Total scheme intake is ~327 students. The scheme is the safest entry route for candidates uncertain which built-environment discipline suits them.
- **PolyU JS3211 — BEng (Hons) in Building Sciences and Engineering**.
- **PolyU JS3214 — BSc (Hons) in Architectural Studies** (architecture-track, separate professional pipeline).

### 2.4 Property and Housing Management

The newest and most fast-growing family, aligned to the Hong Kong Institute of Housing (HKIH) and PMP Tier-1 licensing.

- **HKMU — BBA (Hons) in Property Management** (Lee Shau Kee School of Business and Administration): one of the few standalone "Property Management" bachelor degrees in Hong Kong, fully accredited by HKIH and recognised under the PMSA for PMP Tier-1 licensing. Strong articulation route from HD/AD in Property Management at IVE/HKU SPACE.
- **PolyU also offers Property Management as a Year-2 stream within JS3004**.
- **HSUHK** offers a BBA with concentrations in real estate that lean towards agency and investment rather than estate operations.

## 3. JUPAS admission scores (2026 intake — indicative)

JUPAS scores are typically quoted on the "Best 5" or "Best 4 + Maths" basis (out of 35 with M1/M2 bonus). The figures below are indicative medians from the most recent published admission statistics for the 2025 intake — 2026 final figures are released by each university in August.

| JS6963 | BSc in Surveying | HKU | ~24–25 |
| JS6062 | BSc in Real Estate | HKU | ~24–25 |

Candidates around the median should still place the programme in Band A to maximise admission probability — HKIS-accredited degrees historically clear at the median, not above the upper quartile.

### Subject prerequisites

- All programmes require: Chinese Language Level 3, English Language Level 3, Mathematics (Compulsory Part) Level 2, and Citizenship & Social Development "Attained."
- PolyU JS3004 and JS3789 typically require **Mathematics Level 3 or above** for competitive applicants; M1 or M2 is welcomed and gives bonus points.
- HKU Surveying / Real Estate prefer English Level 4+ in practice (the published minimum is Level 3).

## 4. Curriculum at a glance — what you actually study

A typical four-year HK surveying or real estate degree breaks down roughly as:

- **Year 1 (Foundation):** Construction technology, building materials, microeconomics, mathematics for surveyors, GE courses (Chinese, English, Common Core).
- **Year 2 (Core technical):** Land law in Hong Kong, town planning, real estate economics, valuation principles, measurement (BCIS/SMM4), property finance.
- **Year 3 (Specialisation):** APC-track electives — General Practice (asset management, agency, REIT analysis), Quantity Surveying (cost planning, contract administration, FIDIC), Building Surveying (defects, dilapidation, refurbishment), or Real Estate (development appraisal, urban renewal, PropTech).
- **Year 4 (Capstone + Industrial Attachment):** Final-year dissertation, mandatory 8–12 weeks of supervised industrial training (counts towards APC), capstone projects often co-supervised by industry (Knight Frank, JLL, Savills, CBRE, Sun Hung Kai, MTR Corp).

PolyU's JS3004 students *select their target stream at the end of Year 1* — your GPA in Year 1 determines whether you can transfer into the most competitive stream (Surveying and Real Estate are typically over-subscribed; Building Services Engineering has more open spots).

## 5. Professional registration — the licence map

The single most important thing to understand before enrolling: in Hong Kong, your *degree* alone does not let you practise. You need a **professional designation** layered on top.

### 5.1 HKIS — Hong Kong Institute of Surveyors

Required for chartered status as a Surveyor. Routes:

1. Complete an **HKIS-accredited** bachelor degree (PolyU JS3789, HKU BSc Surveying / Real Estate, CityU JS1011).
2. Sign up for the **APC scheme** with a sponsoring firm.
3. Complete **24 months of structured training** logged in a Training Diary.
4. Sit the **APC Final Assessment** (written submission, critical analysis, professional interview).
5. Be elected MHKIS — usually achieved 2.5–3 years after graduation.

Reciprocity: MHKIS is mutually recognised with **MRICS** (UK) for most divisions, opening the entire Commonwealth job market.

### 5.2 HKIH — Hong Kong Institute of Housing

For property and housing managers. Routes:

1. Complete an **HKIH-accredited** programme (HKMU BBA Property Management, HKU SPACE PD in Housing Management, CityU MA Housing Stream).
2. Complete **2–5 years** of relevant work experience (varies by membership grade).
3. Submit the **Practical Experience Requirement** report and sit the **Professional Assessment**.

Full Member of HKIH (MHKIH) is one of the two **specified professional qualifications** accepted for the PMP Tier-1 licence under the PMSA, the other being MHKIS (General Practice).

### 5.3 PMP Licensing under the PMSA

Since 1 August 2020, the *Property Management Services Authority* (PMSA) regulates the industry. Two licensing tiers:

- **PMP Tier 1**: requires a degree-level qualification AND a specified professional designation (MHKIS, MHKIH, MCIH, MRICS, MCIOB, HKIE Building Services) AND minimum 3 years of management-level experience.
- **PMP Tier 2**: requires a sub-degree (HD/AD) qualification AND 1 year of relevant experience.

A graduate of HKMU Property Management or PolyU Property Management stream is on the **fastest possible path** to a Tier-1 licence: ~3 years from graduation to fully licensed practitioner.

## 6. Career outcomes — where graduates actually go

Based on the most recent first-employment surveys (PolyU SCE 2024 and HKU Faculty of Architecture 2024), the major employer categories are:

### 6.1 Property Developers (largest single category)

Sun Hung Kai Properties, CK Asset Holdings, Henderson Land, New World Development, Sino Land, Wheelock, Swire Properties, Hongkong Land. Typical graduate roles:

- Assistant Surveyor (Asset Management)
- Project Coordinator (Development)
- Sales Executive (Residential / Commercial leasing)
- Starting salary: HK$22,000–28,000 / month (developers traditionally pay 13–14 months + bonus).

### 6.2 International Surveying Consultancies

JLL, CBRE, Knight Frank, Savills, Colliers, Cushman & Wakefield. Highest prestige path; almost all graduates here pursue MRICS in parallel with MHKIS.

- Graduate Surveyor (Valuation / Capital Markets / Research)
- Starting salary: HK$24,000–30,000 / month, plus performance bonus.

### 6.3 REITs and Real Estate Asset Management

Link REIT (the largest Asia-Pacific retail REIT), Champion REIT, Fortune REIT, Sunlight REIT, Prosperity REIT. Roles include Investment Analyst, Asset Manager, Portfolio Analyst — increasingly recruiting Real Estate graduates with finance double-major or CFA Level 1.

### 6.4 Property Management Companies

Kai Shing (SHKP), Hong Yip (SHKP), Goodwell (CK Asset), Urban Group (Sino Land), Citybase (Henderson). The "big five" PMCs each manage over 60 million sq ft. Property Management graduates typically start as Assistant Property Officer or Property Management Trainee at HK$18,000–22,000 / month.

### 6.5 Public Sector

- **Housing Authority / Housing Department**: Assistant Housing Manager (HM) grade is one of the most stable government careers, starting at MPS Point 16 (~HK$36,000 / month after recent pay scale revision).
- **Lands Department**: Land Surveyor / Estate Surveyor.
- **Buildings Department**: Building Surveyor.
- **MTR Corporation**: Property Development & Investment Department.

### 6.6 Emerging — PropTech, ESG and Construction Tech

Faster-growing sub-sector hiring real estate graduates with data, BIM or sustainability skills:

- BIM Coordinator, ESG Consultant (Arcadis, Atkins Realis, Arup)
- PropTech analyst at platforms like Spacious, Squarefoot, 28Hse
- Green building consultancy (BEAM Plus, LEED certification)

## 7. International students and non-JUPAS routes

Non-JUPAS (international, IB, A-Level, Gaokao) applicants apply directly to each university. Typical conditional offers:

- **HKU BRE**: IB ≥ 38, A-Level AAA, SAT ≥ 1450 + 3 subject achievements.
- **PolyU JS3004**: IB ≥ 32, A-Level BBB–ABB.
- **CityU**: IB ≥ 30, A-Level BBC.

**Gaokao route**: HKU/CUHK/HKUST recruit Mainland Gaokao candidates through their non-JUPAS Mainland Schemes, requiring Gaokao scores in the top ~3% of one's province plus English Gaokao ≥ 130/150. PolyU and HKMU recruit through standalone applications.

Student visa (Form ID 995A) is processed by the Immigration Department in approximately 6–8 weeks. Property Management / Real Estate is an eligible field for the **IANG (Immigration Arrangements for Non-local Graduates)** scheme — graduates may stay 24 months unconditionally to seek work and apply for permanent residence after seven years of ordinary residence.

## 8. Choosing between programmes — a decision tree

Ask yourself, in order:

1. **Do you want to be a Chartered Surveyor (MHKIS / MRICS)?** If yes → HKU Surveying / Real Estate, PolyU JS3789 (or JS3004 → Surveying stream), CityU JS1011.
2. **Are you more interested in operating the building than building it?** If yes → HKMU Property Management, PolyU JS3004 → Property Management stream.
3. **Do you want a real estate finance / REIT / capital markets career?** If yes → HKU BSc Real Estate (or HKUST BBA Finance + Real Estate electives).
4. **Do you want construction project management, cost or technical engineering?** If yes → PolyU JS3004 → Building Engineering & Management or Building Services Engineering, or JS3211 BEng Building Sciences.
5. **Below DSE Level 4 in core subjects?** Consider HD in Surveying / Real Estate at IVE → top-up to Year 3 of PolyU / HKMU bachelor (the "2+2" pathway).

## 9. Common myths and mistakes

- **"Property management = security guard."** False. Tier-1 PMPs manage budgets of HK$50–500 million per estate, handle multi-party stakeholder disputes, and supervise hundreds of frontline staff. The Manager of a single luxury residential estate often earns HK$80,000–120,000 / month within ten years of graduation.
- **"Real estate is recession-proof in Hong Kong."** False. Surveyors saw real income drops of 5–15% during the 2022–24 office vacancy spike. Pursue divisions with counter-cyclical demand (dilapidation, insolvency-related valuation, government-side work) for resilience.
- **"A general business degree is just as good."** Not for the licence path. PMSA Tier-1 and MHKIS/MHKIH all require *accredited* degrees. A pure BBA does not qualify.
- **"AI will replace surveyors."** Routine valuation desk-work is partly being automated, but on-site inspection, lease negotiation, and tribunal-grade expert witness work remain firmly human — and command premium fees.

A career in Hong Kong real estate offers something rare in 2026's job market: a defensible licence-backed profession, anchored in a physical asset class that cannot be offshored, with clearly priced progression and global mobility through RICS reciprocity. For DSE candidates willing to invest in the licence path early, it remains one of the most reliable routes from university to a six-figure HKD monthly income within a decade.
