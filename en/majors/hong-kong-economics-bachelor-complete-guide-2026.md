# Hong Kong Economics Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

Economics sits at the crossroads of Hong Kong's two defining industries — global finance and public policy. For DSE and international students who want analytical training that opens doors into investment banking, central banking, consulting, data analytics or postgraduate research, an economics degree from one of Hong Kong's leading universities is one of the most flexible and high-return choices available. This guide walks through the JUPAS codes, admission requirements, curriculum design, programme differences and career outcomes you need to plan a strong application for the 2026 cycle.

## Why Study Economics in Hong Kong?

Hong Kong is one of the world's largest financial centres, home to a deep capital market, the Hong Kong Monetary Authority (HKMA), regional headquarters of global banks, and a government that runs a research-driven, data-heavy policy machine. An economics graduate here is trained in the exact analytical language those employers use: incentives, markets, statistical inference and policy evaluation.

Three structural advantages stand out:

- **Quantitative rigour with employability.** Modern economics programmes are heavily mathematical and statistical, giving graduates skills that transfer directly into finance, data science and consulting — not just "economist" roles.
- **A genuine China–global vantage point.** Hong Kong programmes routinely study the mainland Chinese economy, the Greater Bay Area, and Hong Kong's role as an offshore RMB and trade hub, alongside US and global macro analysis.
- **Strong postgraduate pipelines.** Top departments place graduates into master's and PhD programmes in economics, finance and public policy worldwide.

## The Main Programmes and JUPAS Codes

Hong Kong's three research-intensive universities offer the most selective economics pathways. Each frames the subject slightly differently, which matters for your choice.

### HKU — JS6767: Bachelor of Economics / Bachelor of Economics and Finance

The University of Hong Kong admits students under a single code, **JS6767**, then lets them enrol in either the **Bachelor of Economics (BEcon)** or the **Bachelor of Economics and Finance (BEcon&Fin)**. BEcon trains students in economic theory, statistical tools and the application of theory to real problems in trade, banking, labour markets and the comparative performance of the Chinese, Hong Kong and global economies. BEcon&Fin adds a structured finance core — corporate finance, investments, financial markets — for students aiming squarely at the banking and asset-management track. Sharing one admission code gives you flexibility to decide after you arrive.

### HKUST — JS5331: BSc in Economics and Finance

HKUST's Business School offers a **BSc in Economics and Finance (ECOF)** under **JS5331**. It is one of the more quantitatively demanding economics-and-finance degrees in the city, with strong econometrics and financial-economics content. Admission is competitive and an **interview is normally compulsory** — Band A applicants who show strong potential for ECOF study are typically invited to engagement activities and interviews between May and July 2026. HKUST also offers economics within its broader School of Business and Management single-code admission.

### CUHK — Economics within the Social Science / Business pathways

The Chinese University of Hong Kong offers a strong economics major. Students can enter economics through the Faculty of Social Science broad admission or via the Business Administration programmes, depending on whether they want a social-science-flavoured or business-flavoured degree. CUHK's economics department has a long research tradition and well-developed exchange and postgraduate links. Always confirm the exact JUPAS code on CUHK's current programme list, as faculty-based admission codes are periodically restructured.

Beyond the "big three," **City University of Hong Kong**, **Hong Kong Baptist University**, **Lingnan University** and **PolyU** also offer economics or applied/business economics degrees, generally with somewhat lower entry scores and a more applied orientation — useful Band B and Band C choices.

## Admission Requirements (JUPAS, 2026 Entry)

### Minimum entrance requirement

All UGC-funded bachelor's degrees require the standard university entrance threshold, commonly summarised as **"332A33"**: Level 3 in English Language and Chinese Language, Level 3 in Mathematics (Compulsory Part), "Attained" in Citizenship and Social Development, and Level 3 in two elective subjects. For competitive economics programmes you should treat this as a floor, not a target.

### Competitive scores and subject weighting

Economics programmes at HKU, CUHK and HKUST are popular and selective. The realistic admitted profile at the top programmes typically sits well above the minimum — strong candidates usually present **Level 5 / 5* results in English and Mathematics plus high elective grades**, with Mathematics often weighted more heavily because the degree is mathematically intensive.

Key points on scoring:

- **Mathematics matters disproportionately.** Many economics programmes apply extra weighting to Mathematics (Compulsory Part) and value **M1 or M2 Extended Modules**. Taking M1/M2 is strongly recommended and is often effectively expected by competitive applicants.
- **Subject combinations.** Score calculations generally consider Category A subjects and M1/M2. Universities publish their own JUPAS score calculators (HKU, CUHK and HKUST each have one) — use them with your predicted or actual grades.
- **Reference only.** Published median and lower-quartile admission scores from prior years are guidance, not predictions. Selection also weighs subject-level results, band choices and, for some programmes (notably HKUST ECOF), interview performance.

### Practical takeaways for applicants

1. **Band A discipline.** For HKUST ECOF and other interview-based programmes, placing the programme in Band A is often a prerequisite for being shortlisted for interview.
2. **Maximise Maths.** Sit M1 or M2 unless you have a strong reason not to; it materially strengthens an economics application.
3. **English is non-negotiable.** Economics is taught and assessed in English with heavy reading and quantitative writing; a high English grade is both a requirement and a survival skill.
4. **Non-JUPAS / international applicants** typically apply with IB, A-Levels or equivalent plus IELTS/TOEFL, with each university publishing equivalency tables — check the relevant programme page for exact thresholds.

## What You Actually Study

Although departments differ, a Hong Kong economics degree generally has four pillars:

**1. Core economic theory.** Microeconomics (consumer and firm behaviour, market structure, game theory) and macroeconomics (growth, inflation, monetary and fiscal policy, open-economy macro). These are taught at increasing levels of mathematical formality across the years.

**2. Quantitative methods.** Calculus, linear algebra, probability and statistics, and — crucially — **econometrics**, the discipline of estimating and testing economic relationships from data. Strong econometrics skills are the single most marketable component of the degree.

**3. Applied and field economics.** Electives such as international trade, financial economics, labour economics, development and the Chinese economy, industrial organisation, behavioural economics, public economics and health economics. Hong Kong programmes typically offer rich China- and Asia-focused options.

**4. Finance integration (for Economics & Finance tracks).** Corporate finance, investments, derivatives, financial markets and institutions — bridging economic theory with practical financial decision-making.

Most programmes also embed a final-year project, capstone or research seminar, and offer exchange semesters with partner universities in North America, Europe and Asia. Internships during summers — in banks, consultancies, regulators or research houses — are increasingly the differentiator between an average and a strong graduate CV.

## BEcon vs Economics & Finance: Which Should You Choose?

This is the most common decision point. A useful heuristic:

- **Choose pure Economics (BEcon-style)** if you are drawn to policy, research, government, central banking, international organisations, or a future master's/PhD in economics. You will go deeper into theory and econometrics and keep more flexibility across applied fields.
- **Choose Economics & Finance (BEcon&Fin / ECOF)** if your target is investment banking, asset management, equity research, corporate finance or fintech. You trade some breadth for a structured finance core that maps directly onto front-office recruiting.

Because HKU admits both under JS6767, HKU offers the lowest-risk way to keep the decision open. If you already know you want finance and can handle a quantitatively heavy load, HKUST ECOF is purpose-built for that path.

## Career Outcomes and Salaries

Economics is one of the most occupationally flexible degrees in Hong Kong. Typical destinations:

- **Banking and finance.** Investment banking, asset and wealth management, equity and macro research, risk and quantitative roles. Economics & Finance graduates compete directly for front-office analyst programmes.
- **Central banking and government.** The **HKMA Graduate Economist Programme** is a flagship entry point — a structured two-year programme for graduates interested in economic research, with reported fixed monthly pay in the order of **HK$49,000+** plus a completion lump sum. The Government Economist grade, statistical and policy roles, and bodies like the Census and Statistics Department also recruit economics graduates.
- **Consulting and data analytics.** Strategy and economic consulting, competition/regulatory economics, and increasingly data-science roles that prize econometrics and causal-inference skills.
- **Corporate and international organisations.** Strategy, pricing, and economic-analysis functions in multinationals, plus development and research bodies.

On pay: fresh graduates across the board in Hong Kong earn widely varying starting salaries depending on sector — entry-level generalist graduate roles cluster around the high-teens to low-HK$20,000s per month, while competitive finance analyst tracks and the HKMA programme pay materially more. With experience, economist salaries rise substantially — survey data points to gross pay in the order of HK$500,000+ at the 1–3 year mark and well above HK$900,000 for senior economists with 8+ years. Public-sector roles tend to pay a premium over equivalent private-sector positions in the same function. Treat all figures as indicative ranges that vary by employer, role and year.

## Postgraduate and Long-Term Pathways

Economics is an unusually strong springboard for further study. The quantitative core (especially econometrics and theory) prepares graduates for:

- **Master's degrees** in economics, financial economics, data science, public policy or finance — in Hong Kong or abroad.
- **PhD programmes**, for which top Hong Kong departments have credible international placement; strong maths grades, research experience and faculty references are decisive here.
- **Professional finance qualifications** such as the CFA, which pair naturally with an Economics & Finance background.

If a research career appeals, prioritise theory and econometrics electives, seek a research assistantship, and build a relationship with a faculty member early — references and a research writing sample matter far more than grades alone for PhD admission.

## Application Strategy: A Short Checklist

1. **Lock in Maths and English.** Aim high in both; sit M1 or M2.
2. **Decide your flavour.** Policy/research → pure Economics; finance front office → Economics & Finance / ECOF.
3. **Use Band A wisely.** Interview-based programmes (e.g. HKUST ECOF) generally reward a Band A placement.
4. **Run the official calculators.** HKU, CUHK and HKUST each publish a JUPAS score calculator — model your real combination.
5. **Build the non-academic case.** Economics competitions, a quantitative project, or a finance/research internship strengthens borderline applications and helps in interviews.
6. **Hedge your band list.** Pair a top-three Band A choice with realistic CityU/HKBU/Lingnan/PolyU economics options in lower bands.

## Conclusion

An economics degree from a leading Hong Kong university combines rigorous quantitative training with direct lines into the city's two great engines — finance and public policy. The decisive levers in your 2026 application are simple to state and hard to fake: a high Mathematics result (with M1/M2), strong English, a clear choice between the pure-economics and economics-and-finance routes, and a band list that puts your interview-based targets in Band A while keeping realistic fallbacks. Get those right and economics remains one of the highest-flexibility, highest-return undergraduate choices in Hong Kong.
