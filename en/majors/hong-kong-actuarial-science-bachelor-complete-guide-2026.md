# Hong Kong Actuarial Science Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

Actuarial science has held a permanent slot on the Hong Kong Government's Talent List for almost a decade, and for good reason. It is one of the few undergraduate disciplines in Hong Kong that simultaneously offers professional accreditation, six-figure graduate salaries and immigration policy preference. Among the eight UGC-funded universities, four institutions run programmes with an actuarial core: HKU's BSc in Actuarial Science (JS6729), CUHK's BBA in Insurance, Financial and Actuarial Analysis (JS4238) and the smaller Quantitative Finance and Risk Management Science programme (JS4276), and HKUST's Financial and Actuarial Mathematics track within the Mathematics broad-based admission JS5102, supplemented by the Risk Management and Business Intelligence programme JS5814. This guide maps every JUPAS pathway for 2026 entry — from cut-off scores and curriculum structure to professional exemptions and graduate salary curves at the Big Eight insurers.

## 1. The actuarial degree map: Science faculties versus business schools

The actuarial profession demands two literacies at once. Graduates must understand the mathematics of risk — life tables, interest theory, loss distributions, stochastic processes — and the business of insurance — product pricing, solvency capital, regulatory reporting. Hong Kong's four mainstream programmes therefore split into two families.

**The science-faculty family (mathematics-first)**
- **HKU BSc(ActuarSc) JS6729**: the only Hong Kong programme accredited by the UK Institute and Faculty of Actuaries (IFoA), now hosted by the newly formed School of Computing and Data Science with an annual JUPAS intake of around 50.
- **HKUST BSc Mathematics JS5102, Financial and Actuarial Mathematics Track**: a Mathematics major with a third-year FAM stream that emphasises stochastic calculus and derivatives pricing.

**The business-school family (industry-first)**
- **CUHK BBA-IFAA JS4238**: a four-year BBA major awarded the Society of Actuaries (SOA) Centre of Actuarial Excellence in 2011 — one of only about 40 such centres globally — covering accounting, finance and insurance regulation alongside actuarial mathematics.
- **CUHK QFRM JS4276**: a cross-faculty programme jointly run with the Department of Statistics and Data Science, with a JUPAS intake of around 20 students per cohort, making it one of the smallest and most selective business-related programmes at CUHK.
- **HKUST BSc RMBI JS5814**: a joint programme between the Business School and the School of Engineering, focused on risk management and business intelligence, with a Band A interview requirement.

> **Choosing between families.** If your goal is traditional life, general or pension actuarial work, the mathematical training of HKU and HKUST Mathematics is the deeper preparation. If you want to move into financial risk, asset-liability management, or regulatory capital roles at the HKMA or Insurance Authority, the business-school grounding of CUHK IFAA and QFRM tends to dominate campus recruitment for those tracks.

## 2. JUPAS 2026 admission scores and entrance requirements

### 2.1 HKU JS6729 BSc Actuarial Science

HKU calculates JS6729 on a **Best Six weighted score**: the four DSE core subjects (Chinese, English, Mathematics Compulsory, Citizenship and Social Development) plus M1 or M2 plus three Category A electives, with the best six counted. The score conversion is 5\*\* = 8.5, 5\* = 7, 5 = 5.5, 4 = 4. Citizenship and Social Development is graded only as Attained or Unattained and contributes no points. The programme score considers Category A subjects and M1/M2 only — Category C languages are excluded.

The minimum entrance requirement is the standard 4 cores plus 2 electives at 332A33 (English Level 3, Chinese Level 3, Mathematics Level 2, Citizenship Attained, two electives at Level 3). In practice the median admitted profile sits at roughly **5\*\* 5\* 5\* 5 5 5** — equivalent to 36 to 38 points — placing JS6729 consistently in HKU's top five STEM programmes by intake score over the past three cycles. M2 attracts a 0.1 to 0.2 weighting bonus in mathematics-heavy programmes and is strongly recommended.

The annual intake is 50. Since the 2024/25 reorganisation under the School of Computing and Data Science, JS6729 students can elect a minor in Computer Science or Data Science, sharpening the data-engineering profile that Hong Kong's life insurers now expect from new analysts.

### 2.2 CUHK JS4238 BBA-IFAA

JS4238 is a major-based BBA admission in CUHK's Faculty of Business Administration. The minimum entrance is 4 cores plus 2 electives at 3322 + 33, with Mathematics Compulsory at Level 4 or above. English and Chinese typically need to reach Level 4 in practice. CUHK Business uses a **Best Five weighted score with English mandatory**, and IFAA's median admitted score sits in the **30–32 point** range. Holding the SOA Centre of Actuarial Excellence designation since 2011 (one of around 40 worldwide), IFAA graduates qualify for SOA Validation by Educational Experience (VEE) credits in Economics, Accounting and Finance, and Mathematical Statistics.

### 2.3 CUHK JS4276 QFRM

This cross-faculty programme plans an intake of only **20 places** for 2026, making it the rarest actuarial-adjacent seat in the CUHK system. Scoring uses a 4U2X formula with Mathematics weighted at 1.5, English at 1.0, and other subjects at 1.0. With small numbers and a heavy quantitative profile, the median is comparable to IFAA but volatile — the lowest admitted score has approached **34 points** in recent cycles.

### 2.4 HKUST JS5102 Science Group A — to the Mathematics FAM track

HKUST runs a "school-first, major-later" model: JS5102 Science (Group A) covers mathematics, physics, chemistry, life science, ocean science and biochemistry / cell biology. Students choose the BSc in Mathematics at the end of Year 1, then enter the Financial and Actuarial Mathematics (FAM) track at the end of Year 2. The minimum entrance is 332A33 and Group A's overall admitted median sits at roughly **27–30 points**, but the FAM track has a strict GPA gate — students typically need an A− or above in first-year calculus and linear algebra to secure their place.

### 2.5 HKUST JS5814 Risk Management and Business Intelligence

JS5814 is a joint Business / Engineering programme requiring Band A applicants to attend an admission interview. The intake is small and the median admitted score sits around **28–30 points** on Best Five. The curriculum mixes operations research, statistics and information systems and is most useful for graduates targeting risk-analytics and quantitative consulting roles rather than traditional life insurance pricing.

## 3. Curriculum structure: how the four-year degree maps to professional examinations

Hong Kong's actuarial undergraduates follow a 4-year curriculum in which the first two years build the mathematical and statistical core and the final two years deliver applied actuarial subjects together with a final-year project or internship. The curriculum is designed against either the IFoA syllabus (HKU) or the SOA syllabus (CUHK IFAA, HKUST FAM and RMBI).

**Mathematical core (Years 1–2)**: calculus, linear algebra, mathematical analysis, probability theory, mathematical statistics, regression and time-series analysis. HKU and HKUST add stochastic processes; CUHK IFAA layers in microeconomics and accounting in parallel.

**Actuarial core (Years 2–3)**: financial mathematics and interest theory, life contingencies, survival models, loss models, credibility theory, ruin theory, life and general insurance product design, pension mathematics. These courses are the direct map to IFoA's CM1, CM2, CS1, CS2, CB1, CB2 and to SOA's FAM, ALTAM, ASTAM and FM examinations.

**Applied and finance layer (Year 3–4)**: derivatives pricing and Black-Scholes, asset-liability management, IFRS 17 reporting, Solvency II / Hong Kong RBC capital, predictive modelling and data analytics, machine learning for insurance. HKU and HKUST students typically combine this with a programming core in Python and R; CUHK IFAA students take CFA-aligned investment courses.

**Capstone**: a final-year actuarial project, often built on a real insurer dataset under supervision, and a structured 8 to 12-week summer internship at a Big Eight insurer or a Big Four consulting actuarial team between Year 3 and Year 4.

## 4. Professional exemptions: IFoA and SOA pathways

Becoming a credentialed actuary requires passing a sequence of professional examinations on top of the bachelor's degree. The two dominant accreditation bodies in the Hong Kong market are the UK's Institute and Faculty of Actuaries (IFoA) and the US Society of Actuaries (SOA).

**IFoA exemptions at HKU.** Under the latest accreditation agreement, HKU's BSc(ActuarSc) graduates who achieve the required marks in specified courses can secure exemptions for the maximum **CS1, CS2, CM1, CM2, CB1 and CB2** — the entire Core Principles stage. This is the deepest exemption block available in Hong Kong, leaving only Core Practices (CP1, CP2, CP3) and Specialist subjects to complete after employment. HKU is one of a small group of universities globally to hold the maximum CS-CM-CB block.

**SOA exemptions and VEE at CUHK.** CUHK's IFAA, as a Centre of Actuarial Excellence, lets graduates claim SOA's three Validation by Educational Experience credits (Economics, Accounting and Finance, Mathematical Statistics) automatically and structures coursework around the FAM, FM and SRM examinations so students typically clear three to four early-stage SOA exams during their bachelor's degree. HKUST FAM students follow a similar SOA-aligned route via the BSc Mathematics curriculum.

**Hong Kong fellowship.** Whether you sit IFoA or SOA exams, eventual fellowship of the Actuarial Society of Hong Kong (ASHK) requires recognised qualification with one of the IAA member bodies (IFoA, SOA, CAS or comparable). ASHK Associateship and Fellowship therefore stack on top of IFoA or SOA fellowship rather than replacing it, which means the choice between HKU's IFoA path and CUHK's SOA path is largely a choice of which international examination system you prefer to study within.

## 5. Internship pipeline and Big Eight insurer recruitment

Hong Kong's actuarial graduate market is dominated by the **Big Eight insurers** — AIA, Prudential, Manulife, AXA, Sun Life, FWD, China Life, and Chubb — together with reinsurers (Munich Re, Swiss Re, RGA, Hannover Re) and the Big Four consulting actuarial practices (Deloitte, PwC, KPMG, EY) and specialist firms (Milliman, WTW, Mercer).

Final-year placement begins with summer internships in the year between Year 3 and Year 4. Conversion rates from internship to full-time Actuarial Trainee offers at major insurers typically run at 50–70 percent for top candidates. HKU and CUHK IFAA cohorts dominate the campus shortlists, but HKUST FAM and RMBI students are competitive at consulting firms and reinsurance.

## 6. Salary, exam study allowances and career progression

Entry-level Actuarial Trainee or Actuarial Analyst positions at Hong Kong's Big Eight insurers in 2026 typically pay an annual base of **HKD 380,000 to 480,000** for a fresh bachelor's graduate, climbing to **HKD 520,000 to 600,000** with a one-year master's or after the first 12 months on the job. Total compensation including bonus and exam study leave commonly reaches **HKD 560,000 to 650,000** in year one for top performers.

The standard exam-study package at major insurers includes around **100 hours of paid study leave per examination**, exam fees, study materials, and a salary increment of **HKD 2,000 to 4,000 per month** for each professional examination passed. Newly Qualified Actuaries (associate level, around 4 to 6 years post-graduation) typically reach **HKD 900,000 to 1.2 million** total compensation. Fellowship typically arrives 6 to 8 years post-graduation, with Fellow-level total compensation in Hong Kong commonly above **HKD 1.5 million** at insurers and above **HKD 2 million** at reinsurance and consulting houses.

## 7. Talent List, immigration and overseas mobility

Actuarial science is one of the 51 occupations on the **Hong Kong Talent List**, which gives Mainland and overseas applicants accelerated points under the Quality Migrant Admission Scheme (QMAS) and supports General Employment Policy (GEP) sponsorship for fresh graduates. Combined with the Top Talent Pass Scheme (TTPS) for graduates of selected world-ranked universities, this creates one of the most permissive immigration channels available to any STEM graduate in Hong Kong.

Internationally, IFoA and SOA fellowships are recognised across the UK, EU, Singapore, Australia (subject to AAA local-practice modules), Canada (CIA mutual recognition with SOA) and the United States. Hong Kong actuarial graduates therefore have one of the most portable professional credentials of any DSE major — an HKU BSc(ActuarSc) followed by IFoA fellowship is a working ticket to the City of London, Singapore CBD and Sydney's actuarial market in equal measure.

## 8. How to choose your Band A in 2026

For applicants with a 33+ Best Five profile, strong mathematics (M1 or M2 at Level 5 and above) and a clear preference for the deepest mathematical training and the IFoA route, **HKU JS6729** is the default Band A choice. For applicants with a strong overall profile, an interest in business and finance alongside actuarial work, and a preference for the SOA route, **CUHK JS4238 IFAA** is the natural pick — and for the smallest, most quantitative cohort with the strongest peer effect, **CUHK JS4276 QFRM** is the alternative if your DSE results clear the higher cut-off.

For applicants who prefer to enter through a broad Mathematics admission and decide on actuarial specialisation in Year 2, **HKUST JS5102** with the FAM track is the safest route and the lowest Band A floor for an actuarial career start. **HKUST JS5814 RMBI** is best treated as a complementary Band B for risk-analytics-leaning students who want a Business / Engineering hybrid rather than a pure actuarial degree.

Whichever programme you choose, the actuarial career compounds slowly and steeply: the salary curve does not look impressive in Year 1, but by the time you cross fellowship in your late twenties, your total compensation will outpace almost every other DSE-era undergraduate cohort in Hong Kong, with a credential that travels the world.
