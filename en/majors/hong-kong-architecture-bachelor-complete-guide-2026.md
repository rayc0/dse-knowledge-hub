# Hong Kong Architecture Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

Architecture in Hong Kong is one of the most demanding and most rewarded undergraduate degrees in the city. The path is famously long — a four-year Bachelor of Arts/Science in Architectural Studies, followed by a two-year Master of Architecture (M.Arch), then a minimum of two years of recognised professional practical experience, and finally the joint HKIA/ARB Professional Assessment that earns the title of Registered Architect. The combined "4+2+2" is at least eight years from JUPAS offer to your name on the Architects Register, but graduates emerge into one of the world's densest design markets, where the buildings you walk past on Hong Kong Island, in Kowloon and across the New Territories are the textbook of your profession.

This guide explains the four JUPAS-funded architecture pathways for the 2026 entry cycle — HKU's JS6004 Bachelor of Arts in Architectural Studies, CUHK's JS4812 Bachelor of Social Science (Architectural Studies), PolyU's JS3214 BSc (Hons) in Architectural Studies, and CityU's JS1201 broadband programme containing the BSc Architecture and Surveying option — together with the portfolio that decides almost every interview, the M.Arch professional bottleneck, and the salary scales that wait for you in the Architectural Services Department, the Housing Department and private practice.

## Why architecture is its own admissions track

Unlike most JUPAS programmes, architecture cannot be admitted on DSE scores alone. Every Hong Kong architecture school operates a portfolio-plus-interview filter on top of academic results, because the discipline measures spatial imagination, drawing fluency and design judgement that no public examination can. A Band A applicant with a 25-point profile but no portfolio rarely beats a 22-point applicant whose sketchbook shows real curiosity. The DSE score is the gate; the portfolio is the entry.

Architecture is also one of the few JUPAS programmes where the bachelor's degree by itself does not qualify you for professional practice. To use the title "architect" in Hong Kong you must complete the M.Arch, log practical experience, pass the seven-paper HKIA/ARB Professional Assessment and register with the Architects Registration Board under the Architects Registration Ordinance (Cap. 408). Choosing the right undergraduate is therefore choosing the school that will most likely also admit you into its M.Arch programme two years later.

## HKU — Bachelor of Arts in Architectural Studies (JS6004)

The University of Hong Kong's Faculty of Architecture is the city's oldest and largest architecture school, founded in 1950 and consistently ranked in the global top 20 for architecture and built environment. The four-year Bachelor of Arts in Architectural Studies (BA(AS)) under JUPAS code JS6004 is the only direct route into HKU's two-year Master of Architecture, which is the qualification recognised by HKIA and ARB for the Professional Assessment.

The 2026 admission profile keeps a tight portfolio requirement. HKU asks all JUPAS Band A applicants to submit a portfolio of creative work formatted as a PDF of no more than five A4 portrait pages. The brief explicitly tells applicants that the portfolio is the chance to demonstrate "potential in architectural thinking, drawing and making" — interviewers are not looking for finished architecture, they are looking for spatial curiosity, freehand drawing, model-making, photography and any evidence that you observe the built environment. Selected applicants are invited to an admission interview, normally between February and May.

Programme score for JS6004 is calculated on the best four core subjects (Chinese, English, Mathematics Compulsory, Citizenship and Social Development) plus the best one elective from Category A, with Mathematics Extended Module M1 or M2 or a Category C language treated as one elective if it raises the total. The minimum entrance requirement is the standard 332A33 (Level 3 in Chinese and English, Level 2 in Mathematics Compulsory and Citizenship, Level 3 in two electives). Recent admissions show median JUPAS scores in the 27–30 range out of best-five, with successful candidates typically holding Level 4–5 in English and Mathematics and a Level 5 in at least one elective such as Visual Arts, Physics or Geography.

After the BA(AS), nearly all graduates apply to HKU's Master of Architecture (M.Arch), a two-year studio-based professional degree validated by HKIA and ARB. M.Arch admission is competitive even for HKU's own graduates and depends on cumulative GPA, design studio grades and a fresh portfolio.

## CUHK — Bachelor of Social Science (Architectural Studies) (JS4812)

The Chinese University of Hong Kong's School of Architecture, founded in 1991, takes a slightly different stance: its bachelor's is a Bachelor of Social Science in Architectural Studies (BSSc(AS)), placing architectural study within the social sciences and emphasising the cultural, historical and urban dimensions of design. The JUPAS code is JS4812.

CUHK runs a two-stage assessment for 2026 entry. JUPAS applicants are encouraged to submit a design portfolio to the School's Application Materials Collection Platform by 17 May 2026. Selected applicants are then invited to an admission test in which a design question is released and applicants must complete and submit drawings within one week. The combination of pre-submitted portfolio and timed design test is the closest local equivalent to a Western architecture school's entrance exam, and it tests speed of conception alongside drawing skill. JUPAS applications themselves close on 3 December 2025 for the 2026 cycle.

CUHK's score calculation uses the best five Category A subjects plus a 6th-subject bonus where the sixth subject scores Level 3 or above. The subject weighting is uniform — no subject is double-weighted — which gives applicants strong in humanities and social sciences a fair chance against applicants strong in mathematics and physics.

The CUHK BSSc(AS) feeds into CUHK's two-year Master of Architecture, a HKIA/ARB-validated professional degree. CUHK's M.Arch is known for an emphasis on urbanism, sustainability and Greater Bay Area context, and many graduates progress directly into its M.Arch with a guaranteed-progression style assessment for top BSSc(AS) graduates.

## PolyU — BSc (Hons) in Architectural Studies (JS3214)

The Hong Kong Polytechnic University's School of Design and Department of Building and Real Estate share the architecture territory, with the four-year Bachelor of Science (Honours) in Architectural Studies under JUPAS code JS3214 sitting in the Faculty of Construction and Environment. PolyU positions its programme strongly around technology, building performance, BIM and the construction-industry interface — graduates often find the PolyU degree most valued by large local construction consultancies.

For 2026 entry, selected Band A applicants are required to submit a personal portfolio to showcase design ability and to attend aptitude tests and/or interviews, expected to be conducted between January 2026 and June 2026. PolyU's portfolio brief is more open than HKU's five-page rule — applicants are encouraged to demonstrate any creative discipline, including film, product design, photography and digital fabrication, alongside drawing and model work.

JS3214 score calculation uses best five Category A subjects with English and Mathematics weighted at 1.0 and other subjects at 1.0 — a flat-weighting profile that rewards balanced applicants. The PolyU BSc(Hons)AS connects to the two-year Master of Architecture (MArch) at PolyU's School of Design, which is HKIA/ARB-validated.

## CityU — Architecture and Civil Engineering broadband (JS1201)

City University of Hong Kong restructured its architecture and built-environment offerings into a single broadband under JUPAS code JS1201, "Architecture and Civil Engineering", admitting all first-year entrants into a common curriculum and allocating majors after Year 1. The three majors available within JS1201 are BEng Architectural Engineering, BEng Civil Engineering and BSc Architecture and Surveying.

For 2026 entry, JS1201 has approximately 87 UGC-funded local first-year places. The programme operates a top-40-percent free-choice rule: students in the top 40 percent of Year 1 GPA freely choose their major, while the remaining 60 percent are allocated based on availability and selection criteria. Applicants who specifically want the BSc Architecture and Surveying or BEng Architectural Engineering major must therefore aim for a strong Year 1 GPA, not just a strong DSE score.

CityU's BSc Architecture and Surveying is not an HKIA-validated degree by itself — it is a hybrid that gives a foundation in both architectural design and quantity surveying, and graduates wishing to register as architects must continue to a HKIA-validated M.Arch elsewhere. Students wanting the most direct route to Registered Architect status should treat CityU JS1201 as a fallback, not the first choice, and target HKU JS6004, CUHK JS4812 or PolyU JS3214 as Band A picks.

## Portfolio strategy: the five rules every Hong Kong architecture applicant needs

Across all four schools, portfolio review is the deciding filter. The five rules below are derived from published portfolio briefs and admission tutor commentary across HKU, CUHK and PolyU.

**1. Show observation, not architecture.** Tutors are wary of seventeen-year-olds presenting "concept buildings" — they are looking for evidence that you observe the city. A page of carefully drawn HKU campus stairwells, MTR ventilation grilles or a Sham Shui Po stairway is worth more than a render of a fictional skyscraper. Sketch from life.

**2. Hand drawing is non-negotiable.** Every Hong Kong architecture school's portfolio brief asks for evidence of hand-drawn work. CAD, Rhino, SketchUp and AI-generated images cannot replace a sketchbook. Include perspective sketches, axonometrics, plan diagrams and freehand sections.

**3. Make physical models.** A photograph of a chipboard, foamcore or paper model is one of the strongest portfolio entries because it shows three-dimensional thinking. Models do not have to be sophisticated — abstract spatial studies in white card are highly regarded.

**4. Stay under the page limit.** HKU's five-page A4 limit is binding; PolyU and CUHK accept longer portfolios but tutors will spend at most ten minutes on any submission. Edit ruthlessly. Five strong pieces beat fifteen mediocre ones.

**5. Caption everything.** Every image needs a one-sentence caption explaining what you were testing, observing or making. Captions distinguish a sketchbook from a portfolio.

## The professional pathway: M.Arch and HKIA/ARB Professional Assessment

The Hong Kong Institute of Architects (HKIA) and the Architects Registration Board (ARB) jointly administer the Professional Assessment under the HKIA/ARB Professional Assessment Handbook. To sit the assessment, a candidate must have a HKIA/ARB-validated bachelor's plus master's qualification and at least two years of recognised professional practical experience. Candidates must have accumulated a minimum of 12 months of recognised practical experience before sitting Papers 3, 4 and 5.

The Professional Assessment is structured as seven papers covering legislation and practice, contract administration, construction technology, design, and oral examination. Papers 6 and 7 require submission of scaled drawings to be mechanically drawn — not freehand — and tested for technical accuracy. Practical experience must cover Stages A to F of the standard architectural workflow: inception and brief, concept design, scheme design, statutory submissions, detail design and construction documentation, tendering, and contract administration with site coordination during construction.

After passing the Professional Assessment, candidates apply to the Architects Registration Board for entry on the Register of Architects. Only registered architects may use the title "Architect" in Hong Kong and sign Authorised Person submissions to the Buildings Department under the Buildings Ordinance (Cap. 123). The MoU signed between ARB Hong Kong and ARB UK has improved cross-recognition routes for HKIA-qualified architects seeking UK registration.

## Salary expectations: what the eight-year pathway actually pays

Fresh BA(AS) or BSc(Hons)AS graduates without an M.Arch typically enter practice as Architectural Assistants or Junior Designers earning HKD 18,000–24,000 per month at small to medium private practices, and HKD 22,000–28,000 at large firms such as Aedas, P&T, Wong Tung, AGC, Ronald Lu and Partners or international offices like Foster + Partners and OMA's Hong Kong studio. Average graduate architect compensation in Hong Kong is around HKD 213,000 per year for those with one to three years of experience and HKD 286,000 across all graduate-level roles.

After completing the M.Arch and accumulating practical experience, an Architectural Designer (often called "Part 2 Architect" in firms following the British convention) earns HKD 30,000–45,000 per month. After passing the HKIA/ARB Professional Assessment and registering, a newly Registered Architect typically earns HKD 50,000–75,000 per month in private practice.

Government practice is the most predictable salary track. The Architectural Services Department (ArchSD) and the Housing Department both recruit Architects on the Master Pay Scale (MPS). A Housing Department Architect post is advertised at MPS Point 32 (HKD 72,225 per month) up to MPS Point 44 (HKD 112,925 per month), and requires candidates to be Members of HKIA and Registered Architects. The civil service pay freeze announced for 2025/26 was confirmed in the February 2025 Budget, with the Pay Trend Survey resuming in 2026/27, so the MPS scales for 2026 entrants remain at the 2024/25 levels.

## How to choose between the four programmes

If you know you want to be a Registered Architect and your DSE score supports it, **HKU JS6004** offers the strongest brand, the largest alumni network and the most reliable progression to a HKIA/ARB-validated M.Arch. **CUHK JS4812** is the natural alternative for applicants drawn to urbanism, social science framing and Greater Bay Area context, and its admission test format suits applicants who perform well under timed design conditions. **PolyU JS3214** is the most technically oriented programme and a strong choice for applicants interested in BIM, building performance and the construction-industry interface; it is also slightly more accessible by DSE score than HKU and CUHK. **CityU JS1201** should be a Band B or Band C safety choice — the broadband structure dilutes architectural specialisation and the BSc Architecture and Surveying major is not directly HKIA-validated.

The rule of thumb experienced JUPAS counsellors give: place HKU and CUHK as Band A choices if portfolio strength and DSE projection both support it; place PolyU as Band A or B as a realistic mid-band offer; and place CityU as Band B if you want a built-environment career that may not require HKIA registration. Whatever order you choose, start the portfolio in S5, not S6 — a sketchbook of two-hundred drawings cannot be assembled in a single Christmas holiday.
