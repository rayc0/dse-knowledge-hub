# Hong Kong Japanese Studies Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

Hong Kong is one of the few Asian cities with extraordinarily deep commercial and cultural ties to Japan. More than 24,000 Japanese residents live in the city — the third largest expatriate Japanese community in Asia after Shanghai and Bangkok — and roughly 1,400 Japanese companies run regional headquarters or offices here. Mitsubishi Corporation, Mitsui & Co., Itochu, Marubeni, Sumitomo, MUFG, SMBC, Mizuho, Sony, Panasonic, UNIQLO, MUJI, Don Don Donki, Aeon and an army of Japanese F&B operators all use Hong Kong as a Southeast Asia gateway. That structural demand sustains a steady appetite for bilingual professionals fluent in **Cantonese / English plus working Japanese**, and keeps Japanese Studies one of the most competitive humanities majors in JUPAS.

This guide is written for 2026 DSE candidates, sub-degree articulators, and overseas students considering Hong Kong's Japanese Studies bachelor pathways. It covers JUPAS codes, 2024–2025 admission scores, curriculum design, the compulsory year-in-Japan, JLPT N5→N1 progression, and graduate starting salaries in Japanese corporates, government agencies and the translation market.

## 1. Major, Minor or Private BA — The Three Hong Kong Pathways

Only **two Hong Kong universities** offer a standalone undergraduate Japanese Studies major within JUPAS:

| University | JUPAS Code | Programme | Major Structure |
|---|---|---|---|
| **HKU** | **JS6054** | Bachelor of Arts (Japanese Studies as one of 16 Arts Majors) | Year 1 broad; declare Japanese Studies major from Year 2 |
| **CUHK** | **JS4068** | BA in Japanese Studies | Major fixed at admission; standalone Department of Japanese Studies |

Every other UGC-funded university offers only a **minor or elective sequence**, not a standalone major:

- **HKBU** — Faculty of Arts JS2020 (BA in Chinese / Creative Writing / English / Humanities / Translation) offers Japanese language electives; Translation majors can pair Japanese as a second language.
- **PolyU** — Department of Chinese and Bilingual Studies offers a Minor in Japanese (multilingual business communication focus).
- **CityU** — Minor in Japanese Studies (15 credits).
- **EdUHK** — Department of Linguistics and Modern Language Studies runs a Japanese Studies Minor / Second Major.

A private alternative exists via **UOW College Hong Kong (UOWCHK)** — BA (Hons) in Japanese Studies, around HK$120,000/year for 2026 entry, applied directly (no JUPAS).

> **The key takeaway:** if you genuinely intend to make Japanese your primary professional language, **HKU JS6054 (declaring Japanese Studies in Year 2) and CUHK JS4068 are the only true undergraduate Japanese Studies degrees in Hong Kong**. A minor only really works as part of a "Business / Hotel / Translation major + Japanese minor" combo strategy.

## 2. JUPAS Codes and 2024–2025 Admission Scores

### CUHK JS4068 BA in Japanese Studies (2024 intake, 4C2X scoring)

CUHK Japanese Studies officially published the following admission scores for 2024 entry:

- **Upper Quartile (UQ): 27.5**
- **Median (M): 25**
- **Lower Quartile (LQ): 23**

Scoring formula: four core subjects (Chinese, English, Mathematics, Citizenship & Social Development) plus two electives (X1 + X2), with 5\*\* = 7, 5\* = 6, 5 = 5, 4 = 4, 3 = 3. If you sat DSE Japanese as an elective, A = 5, B = 4, C = 3.

### Band A demand and quota (2024)

- Band A applicants: **334**
- Total banded applicants: **1,905**
- Year 1 intake quota: **21**
- Senior Year intake: **4**

Very small quotas mean admission scores stay materially above other "less popular" Faculty of Arts majors such as Religious Studies or Philosophy.

### HKU JS6054 BA (Japanese Studies stream)

HKU's BA is an "umbrella" programme — one JUPAS code admits students across 16 Arts majors, and major selection happens at the end of Year 1. The 2024 overall median for JS6054 sits around **22 (Best 5)**. Because Japanese Studies is a popular direction, Year 2 major selection typically requires a **Year 1 GPA of 3.0+ and pass marks in introductory Japanese language courses**. No prior Japanese is required at admission, but a DSE Japanese Grade B or above adds bonus points.

### Comparison summary

| Dimension | HKU JS6054 BA | CUHK JS4068 Japanese Studies |
|---|---|---|
| Major fixed at admission | No (Year 2 internal selection) | Yes |
| 2024 Median (approx.) | 22 (Best 5) | 25 (4C2X) |
| Quota | ~350 across whole BA | 21 |
| Year in Japan | Optional (strongly encouraged) | **Compulsory** (except Senior Year Entrants) |
| Double major flexibility | Yes (Double Major / Minor combinations) | One core major + optional Minor |

If your DSE scores land in the **22–24 range**, HKU JS6054 is the safer entry: get in first, then compete for Japanese Studies on Year 1 GPA. If you score **25+** and are clear that you want a fully immersive Japanese degree, **CUHK JS4068** provides the structured compulsory year in Japan and a tighter cohort.

## 3. Curriculum Structure and Japanese Language Progression

### CUHK JS4068 (123 credits, 4 years)

CUHK Japanese Studies organises its major around three pillars:

1. **Core Language Sequence (three years of structured Japanese)** — Year 1 starts at Elementary (zero-Japanese students welcome); Year 2 moves to Intermediate; Year 3 in Japan reaches Advanced.
2. **Disciplinary Electives** — Japanese history, literature, popular culture, film, religion and society.
3. **Capstone** — Year 4 senior thesis in Japanese or a Capstone Project.

**Compulsory year in Japan** — All Year-1-entry students spend Year 3 on exchange (two semesters) at one of CUHK's partner universities: Tokyo, Kyoto, Waseda, Keio, Sophia, Ritsumeikan, Hokkaido, Kyushu and a dozen others. Budget roughly **HK$70,000** for living expenses and airfares (CUHK tuition still applies). Funding options include the **JASSO monthly stipend (around 80,000 yen ≈ HK$4,000)**, CUHK Overseas Learning Grant, and the HKSAR government's Overseas Learning Programme top-up.

Admission requires **no prior Japanese**, but applicants holding N2 or higher must sit a placement test. Senior Year Entrants (sub-degree articulators going straight into Year 3) must hold **JLPT N2 or equivalent**.

### HKU JS6054 (Japanese Studies major track)

HKU's Department of Japanese Studies sits inside the School of Modern Languages and Cultures (SMLC). The Japanese Studies major totals **96 credits**:

- **Core Language Pathway** — JAPN1001 → JAPN3072, six semesters and roughly 36 credits of language training; target endpoint approaches **JLPT N1 (advanced)**.
- **Disciplinary Modules** — Japanese popular culture, literature, film, samurai-era social history, post-WWII political economy, Kyoto art history etc.
- **Capstone** — JAPN4005 Final Year Project (optionally written in Japanese).

**Exchange opportunities** — HKU holds bilateral agreements with 50+ Japanese institutions including Tokyo, Kyoto, Tohoku, Osaka, Hitotsubashi, Aoyama Gakuin and ICU. Students can apply for the **Japanese government MEXT scholarship** and JASSO funding. Most students take a full or single-semester exchange in Year 3; a few combine Japanese Studies with the joint bachelor with Sciences Po (France) for multi-region research.

## 4. JLPT Progression Strategy: N5 → N1 in Four Years

The Japanese-Language Proficiency Test (JLPT) is the most important external credential for a Japanese Studies undergraduate. In Hong Kong it is administered twice yearly (July and December) at AsiaWorld-Expo by the Hong Kong Society of Japanese Language Education.

| Year | CUHK / HKU Course | JLPT Target | Hong Kong Fee (2025) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Year 1 Sem 1 | Elementary Japanese I | N5 (foundation) | HK$590 |
| Year 1 Sem 2 | Elementary Japanese II | N4 | HK$590 |
| Year 2 | Intermediate Japanese | **N3** | HK$650 |
| Year 3 (Japan exchange) | Advanced Japanese | **N2** (employer baseline) | ~5,500 yen (sat in Japan) |
| Year 4 | Specialised Japanese | **N1** (ideal endpoint) | HK$650 |

Critical thresholds:

- **N2** is the recruitment baseline for almost every Japanese corporate in Hong Kong. SMBC, Mizuho, UNIQLO HK and Sony HK accept Graduate Trainees from N2.
- **N1** is the entry ticket for "Japanese as a primary working language" roles — JETRO Hong Kong, Consulate-General of Japan, Itochu / Mitsubishi Hong Kong, and professional Japanese-Chinese interpretation (conference, legal, medical translation) typically require **N1 plus at least six months in-Japan study or work experience**.

For CUHK and HKU graduates, **N2 at graduation is effectively the default**. **N1 depends almost entirely on personal effort** — the smart move is to sit the December test during the Japan exchange year, when daily immersion makes the listening and vocabulary sections far easier.

## 5. Career Pathways: Japanese Corporates, the Consulate, and Translation

### 5.1 Japanese corporates (sogo shosha, banks, retail, manufacturing)

Hong Kong's Japanese employer base breaks down into four buckets:

- **Sogo Shosha (general trading houses)** — Mitsubishi Corp, Mitsui & Co., Itochu, Marubeni, Sumitomo, Sojitz. Local bilingual Graduate Trainees start at **HK$22,000 – 28,000/month** plus expat allowances and 2 – 4 months of year-end bonus; first-year total comp lands around **HK$330,000 – 420,000**.
- **Japanese banks and securities** — MUFG Hong Kong, SMBC, Mizuho, Nomura, Daiwa, Aozora. Graduate programme salaries run **HK$25,000 – 32,000/month**, noticeably higher than shosha, but they enforce a stricter N1 requirement.
- **Retail and F&B** — UNIQLO, MUJI HK, Don Don Donki, Yoshinoya, Aeon, City'super (Japanese-affiliated), Sushiro. Management Trainee salaries sit at **HK$19,000 – 24,000/month**.
- **Manufacturing and industrial goods** — Sony HK, Panasonic Asia Pacific, Canon HK, Mitsubishi Electric, Toyota Tsusho, Crown Motors (Toyota HK), Honda HK. Technical sales or marketing planning roles pay **HK$22,000 – 30,000/month**.

### 5.2 Japanese government bodies and Hong Kong–based organisations

- **Consulate-General of Japan in Hong Kong** — small annual hire of Local Staff (political affairs, press, cultural exchange) at around **HK$30,000+/month** under the MOFA overseas employee scale; requires N1 and fluent English.
- **JETRO Hong Kong** (Japan External Trade Organisation) — supports Japanese SMEs entering the Hong Kong market; local Research / Coordinator roles **HK$25,000 – 30,000**.
- **JNTO Hong Kong** (Japan National Tourism Organisation) — Hong Kong outbound visits to Japan are back to ~2 million per year, and JNTO continues hiring marketing officers.
- **Japan Foundation Hong Kong Office** — Japanese cultural promotion and language education grants.

### 5.3 Translation, interpretation and teaching

- **Professional Japanese-Chinese interpretation** — conference simultaneous interpretation pays **HK$3,000 – 5,000 per hour**; legal / medical translation goes for **HK$800 – 1,500 per 1,000 characters**. The Hong Kong market values the rare combination of **Cantonese + Mandarin + fluent Japanese + business context**, but the work is freelance and requires reputation building.
- **Japanese language teachers** — international schools (Hong Kong Japanese School HKJS, Kingston International School), community colleges and private language schools (ABK, Mantra, Sakuragaku) pay full-time teachers **HK$22,000 – 35,000/month**.
- **Media and publishing** — Japanese drama / anime / variety show translation, Viu and Disney+ HK subtitling, manga rights agencies (Tong Li, Comicworld).

### 5.4 Travel and aviation

- **Japan Airlines (JAL) and All Nippon Airways (ANA) Hong Kong** — ground operations, sales, customer relations; starting **HK$18,000 – 24,000/month**.
- **Hospitality** — Japanese hotel brands (Imperial, Okura, New Otani) and Hong Kong hotels with heavy Japanese guest flow (Conrad, Mandarin Oriental, Peninsula) prefer Japanese-speaking concierge and banquet sales staff.
- **Inbound tour operators** — JTB Hong Kong, HIS, Kintetsu International. Tour Planner / Operations roles start at **HK$18,000 – 22,000**.

## 6. Postgraduate and Further Study

Common progression pathways:

1. **MEXT Japanese Government Scholarship** — full funding (147,000 yen/month stipend, full tuition, return airfare) for research master's or PhD at Tokyo, Kyoto, Tohoku, Osaka and similar institutions, with intakes in October and April. Around 70 places per year are allocated to Hong Kong/Macau — among the most accessible national scholarships available to HK graduates.
2. **CUHK / HKU MA in Japanese Studies / Translation** — study locally while continuing Hong Kong employment.
3. **HKU MA in Translation (with Japanese-Chinese stream), CUHK MA in Translation** — professional Japanese-Chinese / Japanese-English interpretation training.
4. **MBA + JLPT N1 pathway** — after 5 years of work, applying to Hitotsubashi ICS Tokyo, Keio Business School or Waseda WBS, often with sponsorship from a Hong Kong-based Japanese employer.

## 7. Application Preparation: DSE Subject Choice and Personal Statement

### DSE elective combinations

- **Core**: Chinese, English, Mathematics, Citizenship & Social Development (compulsory).
- **X1 strong suggestion**: a foreign language (Japanese, French, German, Spanish). DSE Japanese taken at school (Grade C or above strongly recommended) is positively weighted for both HKU and CUHK.
- **X2 recommended**: History, Geography, Economics, Chinese Literature, Visual Arts, Literature in English. Humanities/social sciences fit the interdisciplinary nature of Japanese Studies.
- **Avoid**: a pure science combination (M2 + Physics + Chemistry + Biology) wastes elective slots under 4C2X scoring — unless you intentionally plan a "Japanese Studies + Science Double Major" via HKU JS6054.

### Personal statement angles

CUHK and HKU do not interview Japanese Studies applicants at the JUPAS stage, but PSs are reviewed during the MAR (Multiple Admission Round) and any band reshuffling. Strong PS angles include:

- Concrete Japan exposure — exchange trips, host family stays, anime research projects, tea ceremony classes — beyond "I like Japanese pop culture".
- Existing language credentials such as N5 / N4 self-study.
- Genuinely interdisciplinary curiosity (history + economics + Japan) rather than a single fandom hook.
- A specific long-term career direction (sogo shosha trader, diplomat, academic researcher) tied directly to the curriculum's strengths.

## 8. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Can absolute beginners apply for CUHK JS4068?**
Yes. Year 1 starts at Elementary, and zero-Japanese students are explicitly welcome. You will, however, share classrooms with peers who studied Japanese since junior secondary or attended Japanese cram schools, so the first two semesters feel intense. Self-study hiragana + katakana and aim for N5 self-assessment during the summer before Year 1.

**Q2: How do students fund the Japan exchange year?**
CUHK and HKU both administer overseas learning grants and institutional scholarships; the HKSAR government's Overseas Learning Programme (OPS) covers part of living costs. JASSO pays around 80,000 yen/month (≈ HK$4,000), and the MEXT inter-university student programme pays 80,000 yen/month for designated exchange students. Stack two or three of these and basic living costs for one year are essentially covered.

**Q3: Can a Japanese minor (PolyU / CityU / HKBU / EdUHK) lead to a job in a Japanese company?**
Yes, but with a higher bar. Employers willingly accept a "Business major + Japanese minor (N2)" combination, especially shosha and retail. However, roles where Japanese **is** the primary professional skill — Consulate political affairs, professional interpretation, corporate communications — go almost exclusively to graduates of the two full majors.

**Q4: Can Japanese Studies graduates apply for Hong Kong Civil Service?**
Yes. EOII (Executive Officer II), AO (Administrative Officer), and ICO (Immigration Officer) all accept any degree discipline, and a humanities + foreign language background is neutral-to-positive. The Education Bureau also recruits Japanese language teachers (NET-style, requiring an additional PGDE or IB teaching qualification).

## 9. Final Decision Framework

| Your Situation | Recommended Pathway |
|---|---|
| Best 5 score 25+, clear plan to make Japanese a career core | **CUHK JS4068** (compulsory year in Japan, dedicated department) |
| Best 5 score 22–25, broad humanities interests, want flexibility | **HKU JS6054** → Year 2 declare Japanese Studies major |
| Best 5 score 20–22, want "Business + Japanese" dual track | **PolyU JS3868 BBA + Minor in Japanese** or **CityU JS1014 BBA + Minor in Japanese Studies** |
| Best 5 score 18–20, looking for alternative entry | **HKBU JS2020 BA (Humanities)** with Japanese electives, or **UOWCHK BA in Japanese Studies** by direct application |

As Asia's regional hub for Japanese corporates — and given that over a million Hong Kong residents visit Japan each year — the **Cantonese + English + Japanese trilingual profile, anchored in Hong Kong local context** remains a scarce and well-paid combination. Whatever pathway you choose, the genuine yardstick is treating **JLPT N1 as a hard target before graduation**. That certificate, paired with a meaningful Japan exchange or internship, gives you real bargaining power across sogo shosha, foreign banks, government agencies and the translation market.
