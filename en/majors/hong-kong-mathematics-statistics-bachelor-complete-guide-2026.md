# Hong Kong Mathematics & Statistics Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

Mathematics and statistics are quietly two of the most powerful degrees you can take in Hong Kong. They do not have the brand-name pull of medicine or law, and your relatives may ask "but what job is that?" — yet graduates fan out into quantitative trading desks, actuarial consultancies, data science teams, machine-learning labs, government statistics units, and PhD programmes at the world's top universities. A maths degree is the closest thing to a universal passport in the modern knowledge economy: it teaches you to reason precisely about abstract structure, and almost every high-paying technical career is downstream of that skill.

This guide walks DSE and Mainland applicants through every major mathematics and statistics pathway in Hong Kong's eight UGC-funded universities for 2026 entry — the JUPAS codes, admission scores, the all-important M1/M2 question, what the curriculum actually contains, and where the degree leads.

## Why mathematics and statistics, and what is the difference?

A **pure/applied mathematics** degree is built around analysis, algebra, differential equations, optimisation, and proof. It rewards students who enjoy *why* a result is true, not just how to compute it. **Statistics and data science** degrees keep a strong mathematical core but pivot toward probability, statistical inference, regression, stochastic processes, and computation with R and Python. In practice most Hong Kong programmes blend the two and let you specialise in later years, so the choice of *university and structure* matters more than agonising over the label at age 17.

Three career engines pull strongly on these degrees in Hong Kong:

- **Quantitative finance** — trading desks, risk, and quant research at investment banks and hedge funds in Central.
- **Data science and AI** — every bank, tech firm, logistics company and government bureau now hires statisticians and ML engineers.
- **Actuarial and risk** — insurance is a huge Hong Kong industry, and a maths/stats base plus professional exams is a direct route in.

## The major programmes by university

### HKUST — School of Science and Mathematics-Economics

HKUST admits most science applicants through a broad **School of Science** group (JUPAS group code JS5102), after which students choose a major including **Mathematics**, with streams in pure mathematics, applied mathematics, statistics, and financial mathematics. HKUST's mathematics department is research-intensive and consistently ranked among the strongest in Asia, with deep links into the data science and operations research programmes.

For students sure they want a finance-flavoured maths degree from day one, HKUST offers **BSc in Mathematics and Economics (JS5813)** — a tightly designed double-discipline programme combining real analysis and optimisation with microeconomics and econometrics. It is small (around 20 places), competitive, and may involve a selective interview. Entry follows HKUST's general "332A33" minimum (Chinese 3, English 3, Maths 2, Citizenship & Social Development "Attained", plus two electives at 3), but realistic competitive applicants score far above the minimum, and **M1 or M2 is effectively expected** for the mathematics-heavy programmes.

### CUHK — a wide family of quantitative programmes

CUHK runs one of the broadest quantitative portfolios in Hong Kong. Relevant 2026 JUPAS codes include:

- **Enrichment Mathematics (JS4682)** — an accelerated, research-oriented mathematics track for the strongest mathematics students, with early exposure to advanced analysis and algebra and a clear PhD trajectory.
- **Risk Management Science (JS4719)** — applied statistics and actuarial/financial risk modelling; a strong route into insurance and risk analytics.
- **Quantitative Finance (JS4252)** and **Quantitative Finance and Risk Management Science (JS4276)** — interdisciplinary programmes combining mathematics, statistics, finance and computing, popular with future quants.
- **Mathematics and Information Engineering (JS4733)** — a maths-plus-computing double thread aimed at algorithmic and engineering roles.

CUHK also offers a general **Mathematics** major and **Statistics** within its Science broad-based admission. CUHK selection is competitive and holistic — public-exam results matter, but the score calculator (an updated version is released around May–June 2026) heavily weights mathematics and the relevant electives, and **M2 in particular is strongly favoured** for the enrichment and quantitative streams.

### HKU — Bachelor of Science with Mathematics and Statistics majors

HKU admits into a single broad **Bachelor of Science (JS6901)** and lets students choose a major within roughly two years. The Faculty of Science offers **Mathematics**, **Mathematics (Intensive)** for the most able students, and **Statistics** as majors, with no internal quota — if you get into JS6901 you can declare these majors freely. HKU's scoring for JS6901 takes the best five subjects, which must include English Language and Mathematics plus the best science elective, and gives a **1.5× weighting to science electives, Mathematics, and M1/M2** — a clear signal that taking an extended mathematics module materially helps. HKU also offers **Statistical Decision Sciences (JS6779)**, a more applied analytics-and-decision programme with business orientation.

### CityU — Computing Mathematics with finance and research streams

CityU's **BSc Computing Mathematics (JS1206)** is explicitly built for the computational era, offering streams in **Enriched Mathematics**, **Financial Mathematics**, and a research-and-entrepreneurship track (GREAT). It pairs a solid mathematics core with heavy programming and modelling, which fits students who want maths but also want to be obviously employable in fintech and data roles. CityU's data science programmes sit alongside it and credit transfer between quantitative majors is generally feasible.

### PolyU — Applied Mathematics and Finance Analytics

PolyU takes the most vocational angle. Its mathematics offering combines mathematics, statistics and computing under an **Applied Mathematics and Finance Analytics** umbrella, with options such as **Investment Science and Finance Analytics** and **Quantitative Finance and FinTech**, plus a data-science-and-analytics route. PolyU's strength is industry connection and structured internships, making it attractive for students who want a direct line into banking analytics and fintech rather than a research career.

## The M1/M2 question — read this carefully

If you remember one thing from this guide: **for competitive mathematics and statistics admission in Hong Kong, take M1 or M2.** It is not formally compulsory at every programme, but:

- HKU explicitly applies a 1.5× weighting to M1/M2 for JS6901.
- HKUST and CUHK quantitative programmes treat extended mathematics as a near-expectation and weight it heavily in their score formulae.
- The intellectual gap between core Maths only and core + M2 is large; students arriving without an extended module often struggle in first-year analysis and proof courses.

**M2 (Algebra and Calculus)** is generally the better choice for a mathematics, statistics, or quant degree because it builds calculus, vectors, matrices and proof technique that map directly onto first-year university content. **M1 (Calculus and Statistics)** is more applied and is perfectly acceptable for statistics, risk and actuarial pathways. Taking *neither* is a real disadvantage for the top mathematics programmes — possible to overcome with an exceptional core profile, but you are competing uphill.

## Indicative admission scores

Universities stress that past scores "are for reference only" and the 2026 calculators are still being finalised, so treat the following as broad bands, not guarantees. Using a best-five (or programme-specific) score out of roughly 35–42 depending on the formula:

- **HKUST Mathematics & Economics / School of Science (maths-heavy)**: highly competitive; strong applicants typically present a profile equivalent to mid-to-high 5 and 5* grades across maths and sciences with M1/M2.
- **CUHK Enrichment Mathematics / Quantitative Finance / QFRM**: among the most competitive quantitative programmes at CUHK, with the strongest mathematics applicants in each cohort.
- **HKU BSc (JS6901)**: competitive at the faculty level; the Mathematics (Intensive) major then self-selects the strongest within.
- **CityU Computing Mathematics (JS1206) / PolyU Applied Mathematics**: more accessible than the JS5102/JS4682/JS6901 research-track routes while still expecting a solid maths and science profile.

The honest summary: a candidate with strong English and Chinese, 5 or above in core Mathematics, M2 at a good grade, and one or two strong science or quantitative electives is in real contention across the board. Weak English is the single most common reason strong "maths people" miss offers, because every programme requires it and many weight it.

## Curriculum — what you actually study

A typical Hong Kong mathematics/statistics degree runs four years:

- **Year 1**: calculus, linear algebra, introduction to proof, probability, and programming (Python/R), alongside university common-core and language requirements.
- **Year 2**: real analysis, abstract algebra, multivariable calculus, statistical inference, numerical methods.
- **Year 3**: specialisation — stochastic processes, optimisation, machine learning, mathematical finance, differential equations, or advanced statistics; many students take an exchange semester here.
- **Year 4**: a capstone or final-year project, advanced electives, and increasingly an internship or research placement.

Programmes with a finance or analytics name front-load more econometrics, derivatives pricing, time series and computational finance; enrichment/intensive tracks front-load more proof, topology and pure structure aimed at graduate study.

## Where the degree leads — careers and further study

**Quantitative finance**: trading, quant research, structuring and risk roles at investment banks and hedge funds in Central. These are among the highest-paid graduate destinations in Hong Kong, often requiring strong programming alongside the mathematics. A maths/stats degree plus internships is a recognised feeder.

**Data science and machine learning**: banks, tech firms, telecoms, logistics, consultancies and government all hire statisticians and ML practitioners. The statistics + Python/R combination from these degrees is exactly what employers screen for.

**Actuarial and insurance**: Hong Kong is a major insurance hub. A maths or risk-science degree combined with professional exams (IFoA/SOA) leads to actuarial analyst roles at AIA, Manulife, Prudential and consultancies — see our actuarial science guide for the exemption details.

**Government and research**: the Census and Statistics Department, HKMA and regulatory bodies recruit statisticians; and a strong mathematics degree, especially an intensive/enrichment track, is the standard launchpad into MPhil/PhD study locally or at top overseas universities.

**Teaching**: with a PGDE, mathematics graduates are perennially in demand in secondary schools given chronic shortages of qualified maths teachers.

## Practical advice for applicants

1. **Lock in M2 (or at least M1) early.** It is the highest-leverage decision you control.
2. **Do not neglect English.** It gates and weights every programme; "I'm a maths person" is not a defence the calculator accepts.
3. **Choose structure over label.** A broad-admission route (HKUST JS5102, HKU JS6901) buys flexibility; a named programme (CUHK JS4682, JS4252; CityU JS1206) buys focus and a clearer narrative for interviews.
4. **Build a quantitative signal.** Olympiad participation, a Kaggle/coding project, or research exposure differentiates you in a field where many applicants look similar on paper.
5. **Band your JUPAS choices realistically** — pair a reach research-track programme with a solid applied-maths option so a strong-but-not-top exam day still yields a quantitative degree.

Mathematics and statistics will not be the easiest degree you could choose, but few degrees keep as many doors open for as long. In a Hong Kong economy increasingly defined by finance, fintech, data and AI, it remains one of the smartest bets a capable DSE student can make.

*Always confirm JUPAS codes, programme structures, score formulae and entrance requirements on the official JUPAS and university admissions websites before submitting your application, as programmes and codes are updated each cycle.*
