# Hong Kong Speech-Language Pathology Bachelor (HKU JS6157) Complete Guide 2026 - The Only Local Direct-Registration Pathway, Five-Year Curriculum, 300+ Clinical Hours, HKIST Registration, Hospital Authority Salary Scales and Career Prospects

If you are scanning Hong Kong's allied-health degrees for the "unicorn" programme - a single-cohort, ultra-selective course where every graduate walks straight into a regulated profession - the Bachelor of Science in Speech-Language Pathology (Honours) at the University of Hong Kong (HKU), formerly known as the Bachelor of Science in Speech and Hearing Sciences, JUPAS code **JS6157**, has to be on the shortlist. It is the **only** undergraduate speech-therapy degree in Hong Kong, the only local route to register as a Speech Therapist on the Department of Health's Accredited Register, and it admits roughly 48 students per year - making it as selective as Medicine and Dentistry on most JUPAS dashboards.

This guide, written for the 2026 JUPAS admission cycle, covers the curriculum structure, entry requirements, Cantonese-English language demands, interview format, clinical placement (300+ supervised hours), HKIST registration pathway, Hospital Authority Speech Therapist salaries, graduate destinations, and overseas mobility for HKU graduates - in one read.

## 1. Why Speech-Language Pathology Is a "Unicorn" Major in Hong Kong

Speech-language pathologists (SLPs, locally called Speech Therapists) work across the full age spectrum:

- **Paediatric** caseload: late-talkers, developmental language disorder, autism-spectrum communication, articulation/phonology, stuttering, literacy difficulties, cleft palate speech.
- **Adult acquired** caseload: post-stroke aphasia and dysarthria, traumatic brain injury, head-and-neck cancer (post-laryngectomy voice and swallowing), Parkinson's, motor neurone disease, dementia communication support.
- **Voice and upper airway**: professional voice users, vocal fold pathology, transgender voice, tracheostomy and ventilator weaning support.
- **Dysphagia (swallowing)**: instrumental assessment (VFSS, FEES) and rehabilitation across acute, sub-acute and community settings.
- **Hearing-impaired** children, working alongside audiologists to develop spoken language.

With Hong Kong's rapidly ageing population, expanding stroke and dementia caseloads, and a growing special-school sector, demand for SLPs in the Hospital Authority (HA), Department of Health, special schools, NGO rehabilitation centres and private practice consistently outstrips supply. Yet **HKU is the only institution in Hong Kong offering an undergraduate speech-therapy degree** - PolyU, CUHK, EdUHK and CityU do not. Anyone wanting to register locally without studying abroad must come through JS6157. This single-pipeline reality is why the cut-off score sits with Medicine and Dentistry rather than with most allied-health programmes.

## 3. Five-Year Curriculum at a Glance

The programme follows a translational-learning model: foundational sciences first, integrated clinical reasoning in the middle years, and a heavy clinical-plus-research load in the final two years.

### Year 1 - Foundations
- Anatomy and physiology of speech, hearing and swallowing
- Phonetics and Cantonese/English linguistics
- Introduction to communication disorders
- Common Core Curriculum (HKU university-wide requirement)
- Early observation placements in clinics and schools

### Year 2 - Building Clinical Science
- Language acquisition and development (Cantonese and English)
- Audiology fundamentals and screening
- Neuroanatomy and neurology for SLPs
- Statistics and research methods
- Small-group case-based learning begins

### Year 3 - Disorder-Focused Modules
- Paediatric speech and language disorders
- Adult acquired language disorders (aphasia)
- Motor speech disorders (dysarthria, apraxia)
- Fluency disorders (stuttering, cluttering)
- Voice disorders
- Initial supervised clinical placements with real clients

### Year 4 - Specialisation and Internship
- Dysphagia assessment and management
- Cleft palate and craniofacial disorders
- Augmentative and alternative communication (AAC)
- Counselling and inter-professional practice
- External clinical block placements (hospitals, special schools, NGOs)

### Year 5 - Capstone
- Final-year research project (empirical thesis)
- Advanced clinical placements
- Professional issues, ethics, and the HKIST Code of Practice
- Preparation for the HKIST registration application

Total supervised clinical hours exceed **300** across the five years, surpassing the minima required by HKIST and most overseas regulators.

Biology is **strongly recommended** but not compulsory. Successful applicants commonly bring Biology plus one of Chemistry, Physics, Psychology (M2 also helps for the research-methods component), and most carry a 5* or 5** in English given the bilingual demand.

### Score calculation (Best-5 weighting)

HKU JS6157 uses a Best-5 calculation with English **x1.5** and Mathematics (Compulsory) **x0.5**. Recent admission cohorts have settled around **27-30 (Best-5)** at the median, with the lower 10th percentile near 26 and the upper 10th near 32 - placing JS6157 in the same league as MBBS at HKU/CUHK and BDS at HKU.

## 5. Selection: Band A, Interview, and Cantonese Fluency

Only applicants who place **JS6157 in Band A** and meet the academic minima are shortlisted for interview. Two filters then apply:

1. **Academic merit** - DSE score, with weight on English and any science electives.
2. **Interview performance**, weighted heavily.

The interview is conducted partly in **Cantonese** and partly in English. Expect a structured panel covering:

- Why speech therapy, why HKU, and what you understand about the profession's scope (red flags: confusing SLP with English-language teaching or with audiology).
- Ethical scenarios (e.g., disagreeing with a parent about a child's intervention plan).
- Brief language tasks to gauge bilingual fluency, articulation and listening discrimination - applicants must be able to take a clinical history in conversational Cantonese.
- Personality and team-fit cues; SLPs work daily with parents, teachers, doctors, OTs, PTs, audiologists.

**Cantonese fluency is not optional.** Hong Kong's clinical caseload is overwhelmingly Cantonese-speaking, and supervisors will not place a student on a Cantonese ward if their Cantonese cannot support an assessment. Mandarin speakers from the Mainland or overseas-returnee applicants who are not yet conversational in Cantonese should plan a year of intensive Cantonese before applying, or consider non-JUPAS routes only after demonstrable proficiency.

## 6. Clinical Placement: 300+ Hours Across Five Years

Placements are arranged by HKU's clinical education team and rotate students through:

- **Hospital Authority** acute and rehabilitation wards (paediatric and adult)
- **Department of Health** Child Assessment Centres and special preschool services
- **Special schools** (physical disability, intellectual disability, hearing impairment)
- **NGO** rehabilitation and elderly day-care centres
- **HKU Speech Therapy Clinic** (on-campus teaching clinic)
- Selected **private practices** for breadth in paediatric and voice work

Each placement is supervised by a registered Speech Therapist and counts toward the HKIST registration minima. Students receive structured competency assessments using the Hong Kong adaptation of the COMPASS framework (or its successor used by HKU at the time of placement).

## 7. HKIST Registration After Graduation

The Hong Kong Institute of Speech Therapists (HKIST) operates the Department of Health's Accredited Register for speech therapists. HKU JS6157 is the only Hong Kong undergraduate programme **directly accredited** by HKIST - graduates apply for the Accredited Register without sitting a separate qualifying examination, provided clinical hours and academic standing are met.

Maintaining registration requires annual continuing professional development (CPD) hours and renewal under the HKIST Code of Practice. Hospital Authority and Department of Health employment offers are typically conditional on Accredited-Register status.

## 8. Hospital Authority Speech Therapist Salary 2026

The Hospital Authority salary scale for newly qualified speech therapists generally follows the **Master Pay Scale (MPS) Point 16 starting**, rising on the standard increment ladder. As of 2026:

- **Speech Therapist (entry, MPS Point 16)** - approximately HK$44,000 - HK$48,000 per month, plus contractual end-of-contract gratuity for fixed-term appointments and pension/MPF contributions for permanent appointments.
- **Senior Speech Therapist** - typically reached after 6-10 years of service plus internal selection; mid-MPS range with additional managerial allowance.

Private-practice rates vary widely. Recently graduated SLPs in established paediatric clinics earn HK$40,000-50,000 per month rising quickly with caseload, while senior independent practitioners with established referral networks can earn substantially more. NGO and special-school rates broadly track the HA scale, sometimes with allowances for rural or specialised settings.

Public reference points cited by salary aggregators put the Hong Kong Speech Therapist average around **HK$43,000-45,000 per month** in 2026, consistent with the HA-weighted distribution of the workforce.

## 9. Graduate Destinations

Typical first-job destinations for JS6157 graduates:

- **Hospital Authority** (the largest single employer): acute hospitals, rehab hospitals, community geriatric assessment teams.
- **Department of Health**: Child Assessment Centres, Maternal & Child Health, special preschool services.
- **Special schools** (subvented by the Education Bureau).
- **NGO rehabilitation centres** (Heep Hong Society, SAHK, Caritas, etc.).
- **Private clinics** focusing on paediatric language and literacy, adult voice, or international-school caseloads.
- **Academia / research** via PhD, particularly for students who excel in the Year-5 thesis.

A growing minority pursue cross-border practice in the Greater Bay Area, particularly in international schools and private rehabilitation networks in Shenzhen, Guangzhou and Macau, where HKU graduates command a premium for their English-Cantonese-Mandarin skill set.

## 10. Overseas Recognition and Mobility

Because HKU's curriculum aligns with the SLP scope used internationally, graduates have been able to register or qualify (with assessment) in:

- **Australia** - Speech Pathology Australia (SPA), via the Overseas-Qualified SLP assessment route. HKU graduates are well-represented among internationally-qualified members.
- **New Zealand** - NZSTA, via portfolio assessment and possibly bridging coursework.
- **United Kingdom** - Royal College of Speech and Language Therapists (RCSLT) and HCPC, typically via the international application route.
- **Canada** - SAC and provincial regulators (e.g., CASLPO in Ontario) generally require a master's-level qualification, so HKU graduates often progress via a North American MSc/MA before registering.
- **United States** - ASHA Certificate of Clinical Competence (CCC-SLP) requires a master's degree in the US system; HKU's bachelor's serves as a strong foundation for that pathway.

A useful mental model: HKU's BSc(SLP) is treated as **entry-to-practice** in jurisdictions whose entry-level qualification is a bachelor's (Hong Kong, Australia, NZ, UK), and as a **strong undergraduate prerequisite** in master's-entry jurisdictions (US, Canada).

## 11. Non-JUPAS, Mainland and Overseas Routes

International and Mainland applicants apply through HKU's non-JUPAS channel using IB, A-Level, Gaokao, SAT/ACT, or equivalent qualifications. Indicative thresholds in recent cycles:

- **IB** - 41+ with HL 6,6,6 typical for shortlisting; English HL 6 and a science HL strongly preferred.
- **A-Level** - A*A*A typical, with English-language proficiency separately assessed.
- **Gaokao** - admission via HKU's Mainland scheme, but **Cantonese fluency by interview** remains the binding constraint - applicants without conversational Cantonese are unlikely to be admitted to JS6157 regardless of academic profile.
- **IELTS / TOEFL** - IELTS 7.0 (no band below 6.5) or TOEFL iBT 100+ typically required.

## 14. Should You Apply? Honest Self-Test

**Apply if you can answer "yes" to all of the following:**

1. Your DSE forecast is in the 27-32 Best-5 range (with English at 5* or above), or you have an equivalent IB/A-Level profile.
2. You are conversationally fluent in **Cantonese** - able to take a 30-minute case history without a script.
3. You can articulate a concrete reason for SLP rather than Medicine, Audiology, Psychology or English Education.
4. You are comfortable with five years of intense clinical training, including frequent evaluation and feedback on your own communication style.
5. You are willing to commit to a regulated profession with annual CPD and ethical oversight.

**Reconsider if** you are using JS6157 only as a "Medicine fallback" - the interview panel will spot this within minutes, and the workload is too heavy to sustain without genuine motivation.

## 15. Bottom Line

HKU's Bachelor of Science in Speech-Language Pathology (JS6157) is Hong Kong's **only** local undergraduate route into a fast-growing, regulated allied-health profession with strong Hospital Authority demand, robust private-practice options, and good overseas mobility. The combination of a Medicine-level cut-off, mandatory Cantonese fluency, 300+ supervised clinical hours, and a five-year duration filters tightly for vocation-driven students - but for those students, JS6157 offers career certainty that few other JUPAS programmes can match.

If you have the DSE profile, the Cantonese, and a clear answer to "why speech therapy?", JS6157 belongs at the top of your Band A. If any of the three is missing, build it deliberately over the next admission cycle - and consider an overseas master's-entry route as a parallel option.
