# Hong Kong Physics Bachelor Complete Guide 2026: HKU, CUHK, HKUST, CityU — JUPAS Scores, Curriculum, Quantum Tracks & Careers

Physics is the foundation discipline behind almost every wave of technology Hong Kong is now betting on: quantum computing, semiconductor materials, photonics, gravitational-wave astronomy, AI hardware, and the city's emerging InnoHK research clusters in Hong Kong Science Park. For HKDSE candidates who love the why behind nature — and who want a degree that opens doors into both academia and high-paying STEM industry — a BSc in Physics from one of the eight UGC-funded universities remains one of the most flexible and intellectually rigorous choices available through JUPAS.

This 2026 guide covers everything a Band A applicant needs: the JUPAS codes and projected enrolment, score weightings and 2025 reference medians, curriculum structures across HKU, CUHK, HKUST, CityU and PolyU, the theoretical-physics enrichment stream at CUHK, the quantum-technology pipeline at HKUST, research and exchange opportunities, and realistic career pathways after graduation.

## Why study Physics in Hong Kong in 2026

Hong Kong has poured significant public funding into quantum and advanced-materials research over the past five years. The InnoHK initiative now hosts more than 30 research centres, several of which — including the Hong Kong Quantum AI Lab and the Hong Kong Centre for Cerebro-Cardiovascular Health Engineering — recruit physics graduates directly into research-assistant and engineer roles. HKU, CUHK, HKUST and CityU all sit inside the QS World University Rankings top 100 for Physics & Astronomy, giving Hong Kong-based Physics undergraduates access to laboratories, exchange programmes and PhD pipelines that rival most G5 and Ivy-League destinations.

Beyond academia, Physics graduates are heavily recruited by the actuarial and quantitative-finance industry (HSBC, JPMorgan, Jane Street, Citadel HK), by semiconductor design firms (ASMPT, Synopsys, ASE), and by the Hong Kong Observatory and Civil Aviation Department. The transferable analytical skills — modelling, programming, statistical reasoning — also make Physics one of the highest-converting majors into Hong Kong's data-science and AI job market.

## The JUPAS landscape: codes, programmes and projected intake 2026

Unlike Medicine or Law, Physics in Hong Kong is rarely a stand-alone four-year direct-entry programme. Most universities admit students into a broad Science faculty stream in Year 1 and let them declare a Physics major from Year 2. The exceptions are CUHK's enrichment stream and a handful of joint programmes.

The main 2026 entry routes are:

- **HKU — JS6212 Bachelor of Science (BSc):** broad-based Science admission, with Physics as one of around 20 declarable majors after Year 1. HKU also offers Physics as part of JS6963 (BSc Actuarial Science / Quantitative Finance / Statistics) and within the JS6224 BASc (Applied AI) ecosystem.
- **CUHK — JS4601 Science (broad-based):** Year-1 broad Science entry; Physics major declared in Year 2. CUHK also runs **JS4690 Enrichment Stream in Theoretical Physics**, a separate direct-admission stream for students committed to theoretical physics from day one, with a projected 2026 enrolment of 19.
- **HKUST — JS5102 Science (Group A):** physical-science focused Year-1 entry; Physics, Mathematics, or Chemistry as eligible majors. HKUST also offers JS5103 Science (Group B) for life-science leaning students.
- **CityU — JS1106 BSc Computing Mathematics / Physics options** and the broader School of Science admission stream.
- **PolyU — JS3868 BSc (Hons) Investment Science and Finance Analytics** uses physics-style mathematical training, while pure Physics is offered jointly with Applied Physics tracks.
- **EdUHK — JS8590 BEd (Hons) Physics** for those who want to teach DSE Physics in secondary schools.

This guide focuses on the four research-led routes: JS6212, JS4601 / JS4690, JS5102 and the CityU Physics option.

## Admission scores and subject weighting

Hong Kong universities converted to the official 2026 JUPAS scoring scale: 5** = 8.5, 5* = 7, 5 = 5.5, 4 = 4, 3 = 3, 2 = 2, 1 = 1. Physics-oriented programmes typically count Best 5 or Best 6 subjects with bonus weighting on Mathematics, M1/M2 and Physics.

**HKU JS6212 BSc** historically required a Best-5 median in the high 27-29 range in 2025 entry, with Physics-major bound applicants usually scoring 30+. HKU applies a 10% deduction if Chinese, English or two elective subjects fall below Level 3 (but still Level 2 with strong overall performance) — Band A placement and an interview are required in those cases. There is no explicit Physics-major prerequisite at JUPAS entry, but at internal major-declaration stage students need to have passed Year-1 PHYS1XXX core modules with a competitive GPA.

**CUHK JS4601 Science** uses a Best-5 weighting with English or Mathematics × 1.5, M1/M2 × 2, and one of Biology/Chemistry/Physics × 2. Median Best-5 scores for 2025 entry sat in the 27-28 band, with students aiming at the Physics major scoring higher in Mathematics and M2.

**CUHK JS4690 Enrichment Stream in Theoretical Physics** is the most academically demanding undergraduate physics entry point in Hong Kong. The programme uses Best-5 scoring with Mathematics × 1.5, M1 or M2 × 1.5 and Physics × 2, plus bonus points for the 6th and 7th subjects. The minimum bar is a Best-5 score of at least 29 with strong performance in Physics, Mathematics and M1/M2. Median entry scores have historically been in the 32-34 range — competitive with HKUST Mathematics and HKU Statistics. Only around 19 students enter the stream each year, and it explicitly prepares students for PhD study in theoretical or mathematical physics.

**HKUST JS5102 Science (Group A)** applies a 4C + 2X general formula with weighting that favours Physics, Mathematics and M1/M2. Minimum admission attainment is "332A33" across the four core subjects plus two electives. Median Best-5 scores in 2025 sat around 26-28 depending on weighting; the Group A pool is heavily physics- and mathematics-oriented and students opting for the Physics major typically came in above the median.

**CityU School of Science** Physics options track JS1106 and adjacent BSc Computing Mathematics / Applied Physics codes, with 2025 admission medians around 22-24 Best-5 — the most accessible research-level Physics route in Hong Kong, while still offering strong Quantum and Computational Physics labs.

The 2026 JUPAS Score Calculator updates were scheduled for release between May and June 2026, so applicants should confirm the latest weightings on the official institutional admission pages (admissions.hku.hk, admission.cuhk.edu.hk, join.hkust.edu.hk) before locking in Band A choices.

## Curriculum structure: what you actually study

All four research-led universities follow a roughly similar four-year structure: a broad Year 1 with Science Foundation and Common Core / GE courses, two years of physics core (classical mechanics, electromagnetism, quantum mechanics, statistical mechanics, mathematical methods, computational physics, advanced experimental physics labs), and a Year 4 with research project, advanced electives and capstone.

**HKU's BSc Physics major** requires around 78 credits in Physics on top of Common Core and Science Foundation. Core blocks include classical mechanics, electrodynamics, quantum mechanics I & II, statistical mechanics, mathematical methods, and modern experimental physics. Specialisation electives span astrophysics, condensed-matter physics, optics, quantum computing, particle physics and biophysics. HKU's Department of Physics is one of the few in Asia conducting frontier work in quantum cryptography, cold-atom systems, gravitational-wave detection (HKU is a LIGO Scientific Collaboration member), and topological materials.

**CUHK's standard Physics major (via JS4601)** carries 51 units of Physics core plus electives, with sub-disciplines in particle and high-energy physics, astrophysics, condensed-matter physics, biophysics and quantum information. CUHK's department runs the only undergraduate astrophysics observatory programme in Hong Kong, with access to the SCUBA-2 instrument at the James Clerk Maxwell Telescope through international consortium agreements.

**CUHK JS4690 Enrichment Stream** replaces several elective slots with mandatory advanced theoretical courses: general relativity, quantum field theory, advanced quantum mechanics, group theory in physics, and a compulsory research thesis. Stream students are matched with a research supervisor from Year 2 and are explicitly prepared for direct entry into PhD programmes at MIT, Caltech, Princeton, Oxford, Cambridge or HKUST.

**HKUST's Physics BSc (via JS5102)** is famously rigorous, with strong emphasis on quantum-technology applications. The school co-runs the IAS Centre for Quantum Technologies, and undergraduates can take dedicated tracks in quantum information science, photonics and nanotechnology. Course themes range from quantum computing, superconductivity and nanotechnology to quarks and black holes. HKUST also offers a popular dual-degree pathway pairing Physics with Mathematics for students aiming at theoretical-physics PhDs or quant finance.

**CityU and PolyU** both lean toward applied and computational physics — semiconductor device physics, photonic engineering, materials characterisation, and computational modelling. They are stronger choices for students who want to enter Hong Kong's tech-and-materials industry directly after graduation rather than pursue academic research.

## The Theoretical Physics Enrichment Stream (JS4690) — what makes it special

CUHK's JS4690 deserves a separate section because it is genuinely unique in Hong Kong. The stream was created to retain top-end DSE physics talent who might otherwise have left for MIT or Cambridge undergraduate physics programmes.

Key features include:

- **Tutorial-style teaching:** small-class delivery of advanced topics, often by full professors rather than teaching assistants.
- **Compulsory research apprenticeship:** all students take on a faculty-supervised research project starting from Year 2, with the expectation of producing a senior thesis publishable in a peer-reviewed journal.
- **Mandatory advanced coursework:** general relativity, quantum field theory, advanced statistical mechanics, group theory and Lie algebras, and advanced mathematical methods — content normally reserved for masters-level study.
- **Exchange priority:** stream students get priority access to one-semester or one-year exchange at MIT, ETH Zurich, Caltech, the University of Tokyo, and the Perimeter Institute Scholars International Program.
- **PhD pipeline:** the majority of JS4690 graduates have historically proceeded to PhD programmes at top global institutions, with significant cohorts entering MIT, Princeton, Stanford, Cambridge and HKUST.

Applicants should be aware that the stream is competitive — only roughly 19 places are offered each year, and the de-facto Best-5 cutoff has trended toward 32+ in recent admission cycles.

## Research, internships and exchange opportunities

Physics undergraduates in Hong Kong are unusually well-positioned for research exposure. The Research Grants Council's Undergraduate Research Fellowship (URF) scheme funds summer placements for around 60 Physics-stream students per year across the eight UGC institutions. HKU, CUHK and HKUST also operate dedicated Summer Research Programmes that send physics undergraduates to Caltech, Princeton, Tsinghua, the Chinese Academy of Sciences and the Perimeter Institute.

Major research hubs for undergraduate physicists include:

- **HKU Laboratory for Space Research** (LSR) — exoplanet detection, astrobiology, planetary science.
- **HKU Department of Physics Quantum Information group** — superconducting qubits, photonic quantum computing.
- **CUHK Institute of Theoretical Physics** — string theory, holography, quantum gravity.
- **HKUST William Mong Institute of Nano Science and Technology** — 2D materials, topological insulators.
- **HKUST Centre for Quantum Technologies** — quantum cryptography, cold-atom platforms.
- **InnoHK Quantum AI Lab** — joint HKU-Oxford-Microsoft research on quantum machine learning.

Exchange partners for Physics majors typically include UC Berkeley, UCLA, Toronto, Imperial College, the University of Edinburgh, Tokyo Tech, KAIST, NUS and the National Taiwan University.

## Internship and industry pathways

Year-3 and Year-4 Physics majors compete successfully for industry internships at:

- Quant trading firms (Jane Street HK, Optiver, IMC Pacific, Citadel Securities)
- Investment-bank technology teams (HSBC Global Banking & Markets, JPMorgan QR, Goldman Sachs Strats)
- Semiconductor companies (ASMPT, TSMC HK Office, ASE Group, Synopsys Solutions HK)
- Hong Kong Science Park photonics and quantum start-ups (Origin Quantum, MicroCloud Hologram, SpinQ HK)
- The Hong Kong Observatory (research assistant intern scheme)
- ASTRI (Hong Kong Applied Science and Technology Research Institute)

For students leaning toward applied research, the InnoHK clusters now offer paid undergraduate research assistant positions during the summer, often paying HKD 16,000–22,000 per month — competitive with internship rates at investment banks.

## Career outcomes and starting salaries

A Hong Kong Physics BSc opens four broad post-graduation pathways:

1. **PhD / academic research** — about 35-45% of HKU, CUHK JS4690 and HKUST top-cohort Physics graduates proceed directly to PhD study in Physics, Engineering, Applied Mathematics or Computer Science, mostly overseas. Stipends in the US and UK typically range USD 30,000-45,000 per year tax-free.
2. **Quantitative finance** — Hong Kong's quant industry hires roughly 80-120 entry-level analysts a year, and Physics is one of the most overrepresented degrees in the talent pool. Total compensation in Year 1 sits at HKD 1.4-2.2 million.
3. **Tech and AI engineering** — large tech employers (DJI, ByteDance HK, Tencent HK, ASMPT, Sense Time, NVIDIA HK) recruit Physics graduates into ML-engineer, hardware-engineer and physics-simulation-engineer roles, with starting salaries of HKD 35,000-55,000 per month.
4. **Government and education** — the Hong Kong Observatory, Civil Aviation Department, Marine Department and EMSD all hire Physics graduates as scientific officers. EdUHK PGDE plus a Physics BSc remains the standard pathway into secondary-school Physics teaching, with starting Master Pay Scale Point 15 salaries at HKD 39,090 per month in 2026.

## Choosing the right programme: a decision framework

A pragmatic way to choose:

- **Want to do theoretical / mathematical physics research and PhD overseas:** CUHK JS4690, then HKU JS6212 → Physics major, then HKUST JS5102 → Physics major.
- **Want quantum technology, photonics, condensed-matter research:** HKUST JS5102 first, then HKU JS6212.
- **Want astrophysics and astronomy:** HKU JS6212 (LSR access) first, then CUHK JS4601.
- **Want applied physics → industry job after BSc:** CityU School of Science Physics, PolyU Applied Physics, then HKUST JS5102.
- **Want a Physics + Finance / Statistics dual track:** HKU JS6963 BSc combined major in QFin / Stats, or HKUST JS5102 with Mathematics double-major.
- **Want to teach DSE Physics:** EdUHK JS8590 BEd (Hons) Physics or a research BSc plus PGDE.

## Application strategy and Band A advice

For 2026 JUPAS Round 2 changes, applicants should:

1. **Lock in M1 or M2.** Almost every Physics programme weights M1/M2 at × 1.5 or × 2. Without it, your competitive Best-5 ceiling drops by 5-10 points.
2. **Apply for JS4690 first if you have a real research bent.** It pre-allocates a research supervisor — a significant advantage for PhD applications later.
3. **Use HKU JS6212 as a flexible Band A1 if you are torn between Physics, Astronomy and Applied AI.** The internal major-declaration model gives you a full year before committing.
4. **Pair HKUST JS5102 with a clear Year-1 GPA target of 3.7+.** Internal competition for the Physics major in Group A is intense, particularly for students wanting the Quantum Technology track.
5. **Demonstrate physics interest in the Personal Statement and OEA.** Physics Olympiad participation, science fair work, independent research projects on arXiv-readable topics, or strong M2 results materially help borderline cases.

## Final word

Physics in Hong Kong in 2026 is in an unusually strong position: world-class research clusters, generous undergraduate research funding, dense industry demand from quant finance and semiconductor companies, and a clear pipeline to top global PhD programmes. For DSE candidates who genuinely love the subject — not just for its grading rigour but for the questions it asks — JUPAS Physics remains one of the highest-leverage degree choices available in the city.

Plan your Band A carefully, take M1 or M2, and treat Year 1 as a competitive runway for major declaration. Done well, a Hong Kong Physics BSc remains one of the most globally portable, intellectually serious undergraduate experiences in Asia.
