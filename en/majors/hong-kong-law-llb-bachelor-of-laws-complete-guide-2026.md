# Hong Kong Bachelor of Laws (LLB) Complete Guide 2026

Studying law in Hong Kong remains one of the most competitive undergraduate paths in the JUPAS system. Only three universities — **The University of Hong Kong (HKU)**, **The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK)** and **City University of Hong Kong (CityU)** — offer the Bachelor of Laws (LLB), the qualifying degree recognised for entry into the Postgraduate Certificate in Laws (PCLL) and ultimately the local solicitor or barrister profession. Combined intake across all three institutions sits at roughly **200 UGC-funded places** per cohort, against thousands of Band A applicants — making law one of the tightest gateways alongside Medicine, Dentistry and Global Business.

This guide walks through every LLB programme on offer in 2026, the JUPAS subject and weighting rules each institution applies, the post-LLB qualification route through PCLL, and the realistic salary and career picture for trainee solicitors, barristers and government legal officers in Hong Kong.

## The Three LLB-Granting Universities

### HKU — Bachelor of Laws (JS6406)

The HKU Faculty of Law is the oldest law school in Hong Kong, founded in 1969, and consistently ranks in the **global top 30 for Law** (QS subject rankings). The single-degree LLB under JUPAS code **JS6406** offers roughly **92 UGC-funded places** in 2026 (combined faculty figure, to be finalised). The four-year programme covers the foundational courses required by both the Hong Kong Bar Association and the Law Society — Constitutional Law, Contract, Tort, Land Law, Equity & Trusts, Criminal Law, Legal System, and Business Associations — followed by a wide range of electives including International Law, Arbitration, IP, Maritime Law and Human Rights.

First-year UGC tuition is **HK$47,000** in 2025/26. Admission is interview-selective: HKU shortlists candidates based on JUPAS Band A score, then invites top applicants to a structured interview testing analytical reasoning, English fluency and current affairs. HKU's published JUPAS scoring formula for LLB applies **best-6** subjects with English Language weighted heavier (the LLB is one of the few HKU programmes where English carries an explicit higher multiplier). Historically the median DSE score among admitted students has sat in the **5\*/5\*\* range across four core subjects plus two electives** — roughly competitive with HKU's BBA(Acc&Fin) and BBA(IBGM) flagships.

### HKU — Bachelor of Business Administration (Law) and Bachelor of Laws (JS6808)

The flagship **BBA(Law)&LLB double degree** under **JS6808** is a **five-year** programme delivered jointly by HKU's Business School and Faculty of Law. Intake is approximately **80 places** (combined figure). Graduates exit with **two degrees** — a BBA in Law plus the full LLB — making them PCLL-eligible while also carrying business credentials that translate directly into corporate, banking and M&A practice. Admission is significantly tighter than the standalone LLB; the median admit score in recent cycles has typically required at least one additional 5\*\* compared to JS6406, reflecting the dual-faculty selection.

### CUHK — Bachelor of Laws (JS4903)

The CUHK Faculty of Law, founded in 2004, is the youngest of the three but has climbed rapidly in international reputation. **JS4903** offers **68 UGC-funded places** in 2026 — the smallest LLB cohort of the three universities, which often pushes the entry bar higher per place than the headline number suggests. The four-year programme uses **best-5** subjects in admission scoring, with weighted English Language and Mathematics-Extended (M1/M2) treated as preferred electives. CUHK interviews shortlisted candidates and looks for civic awareness, articulate reasoning and reading habits beyond the school syllabus.

CUHK's curriculum places strong emphasis on **Chinese law, comparative law and ADR** (alternative dispute resolution), reflecting its geographic and intellectual orientation toward cross-boundary practice. Students benefit from exchange agreements with University College London, NYU, Sciences Po, Renmin University and several Australian Group of Eight law schools.

### CityU — Bachelor of Laws (JS1061)

CityU's School of Law operates under **JS1061** with **42 UGC-funded places** — the smallest of the three programmes by quota, but with a strong reputation in **arbitration, technology law and intellectual property**. The four-year LLB uses **best-5** subject scoring. CityU also offers a **JD (Juris Doctor)** as a graduate-entry alternative for non-law-degree holders aiming at PCLL — relevant for second-degree applicants but outside the JUPAS pathway.

CityU's tuition matches the UGC band at **HK$47,000** in first year. The school's strength in arbitration is reinforced by Hong Kong's role as a leading regional seat for the **HKIAC (Hong Kong International Arbitration Centre)**, where CityU graduates frequently intern and clerk.

## JUPAS Subject and Weighting Rules at a Glance

| Programme | Code | Places | Years | Best-N Subjects | Interview |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| HKU LLB | JS6406 | ~92 | 4 | Best-6 | Selective |
| HKU BBA(Law)&LLB | JS6808 | ~80 | 5 | Best-6 | Selective |
| CUHK LLB | JS4903 | ~68 | 4 | Best-5 | Selective |
| CityU LLB | JS1061 | ~42 | 4 | Best-5 | May require |

All four programmes require the four core subjects — **Chinese Language, English Language, Mathematics Compulsory Part, and Citizenship & Social Development (attained)** — plus two elective subjects. **English Language is weighted heavier** at every law school: a 5\*\* in English is materially more valuable than a 5\*\* in most electives for law admission, since legal study in Hong Kong is conducted in English and the entire common-law curriculum is English-language. Strong candidates typically present a transcript including **English 5\*\*, Chinese 5\*, Liberal Studies / CSD attained, Mathematics 5+, and two electives at 5\*\* or 5\*** — commonly History, Economics, BAFS or Literature.

Indicative competitive Band A median totals (best-5 weighted, on the 7-point scale where 5\*\*=7): **HKU LLB ~36–38**, **HKU BBA(Law)&LLB ~38–40**, **CUHK LLB ~35–37**, **CityU LLB ~32–35**. These are indicative cut-offs reconstructed from prior-year admissions data; the official JUPAS PDF for 2025 entry remains the most authoritative reference for verifying the current bar.

## What the LLB Curriculum Actually Covers

All three Hong Kong LLBs are designed to satisfy the **"qualifying degree"** requirement set by the Law Society of Hong Kong and the Hong Kong Bar Association. To meet that requirement, every LLB must cover the **core subjects** identified by the joint Standing Committee on Legal Education and Training:

- **Constitutional and Administrative Law** (including the Basic Law)
- **Contract Law**
- **Tort Law**
- **Criminal Law**
- **Land Law / Property**
- **Equity and Trusts**
- **Hong Kong Legal System**
- **Business Associations / Company Law**
- **Civil and Criminal Procedure** (basic level)
- **Legal Research and Writing**

Years 3 and 4 are heavier on electives, mooting, externships and clinical legal education. HKU and CUHK both run **clinical legal education programmes** where students assist in pro-bono advice clinics under supervising solicitors; CityU offers similar clinic placements with NGOs and Hong Kong's Legal Aid Department.

## The PCLL Bottleneck

The LLB by itself does **not** licence you to practise law in Hong Kong. To become a solicitor or barrister, every LLB graduate must complete the **Postgraduate Certificate in Laws (PCLL)** — a one-year (full-time) or two-year (part-time) vocational qualification offered by HKU, CUHK and CityU.

PCLL admission is **highly competitive** and not automatic for LLB graduates. Each university sets its own admissions standard; typical informal requirements include:

- A **2:1 (upper-second class honours)** or equivalent in the LLB. Many cohorts are increasingly first-class only.
- Strong performance in the **PCLL Conversion Examinations** for non-local degree holders (e.g., UK or Australian LLB).
- Submission of **IELTS Academic** results (all applicants, including native English speakers) — deadline **31 July 2026** for the 2026 entry cycle.
- Personal statement and (commonly) interview.

The PCLL application deadline for 2026/27 entry is **30 April 2026**. Online IELTS is not accepted; results must be sent directly by an authorised IELTS test centre.

## Routes to Practice After PCLL

### Solicitor Pathway

After PCLL, intending solicitors must complete a **two-year training contract** at a registered Hong Kong law firm before being admitted to the roll of solicitors. Training contracts rotate trainees through three or four practice areas — typically a mix of corporate, finance, litigation and a specialist seat such as IP, employment, or arbitration. International firms (the so-called **Magic Circle** — A&O Shearman, Clifford Chance, Freshfields, Linklaters, Slaughter and May — plus US elite firms like Sullivan & Cromwell, Davis Polk, Skadden and Latham) compete fiercely for the top tier of PCLL graduates; many secure training contracts in their **second year of LLB** through vacation schemes.

**Trainee solicitor monthly salaries in 2025/26**:

- **PRC firms / smaller Hong Kong outfits**: HK$25,000–35,000
- **Mid-tier and regional firms**: HK$35,000–55,000
- **International / Magic Circle / US firms**: **HK$65,000–95,000+** per month (often plus PCLL fee sponsorship and a maintenance grant during the PCLL year)
- **Average across the market** (Glassdoor 2025): ~HK$55,000/month for trainees

Newly qualified (NQ) solicitor base salaries at Magic Circle / US firms in Hong Kong commonly exceed **HK$1.6–2.0 million** per annum, lockstep-aligned with the firm's London or New York pay scale.

### Barrister Pathway

Intending barristers complete a **12-month pupillage** at a barristers' chambers, the latter six months of which carry a limited right of practice. After pupillage and admission, barristers begin tenancy as junior barristers, earning fee-based income that varies widely — strong commercial juniors at top sets such as **Des Voeux Chambers, Temple Chambers or Gilt Chambers** can match Magic Circle NQ pay within their first two to three years; criminal and family junior practice typically pays meaningfully less in the early years.

### Government Legal Officer — Department of Justice

The **Department of Justice Legal Trainee Scheme** recruits PCLL graduates directly into government legal service. After a one-year training period, trainees are appointed as **Government Counsel** (entry-level Counsel grade). DoJ salaries follow the Master Pay Scale: Counsel posts start at around **HK$77,000–85,000/month** depending on entry point, with strong job security, pension, housing benefits and a structured promotion pathway to Senior Government Counsel and Deputy Principal Government Counsel.

### In-House Counsel and Compliance

A significant minority of LLB graduates skip private practice and move directly into **in-house legal teams** at banks, asset managers, insurance firms, the HKEX, tech multinationals, and regulators (SFC, HKMA, ICAC). These roles often suit graduates who prefer commercial advisory work over the billable-hour culture of private practice; starting salaries in regulator or in-house counsel positions typically sit in the **HK$45,000–65,000/month** range for fresh graduates with PCLL.

## Choosing Between the Three LLBs

- **Choose HKU LLB (JS6406)** if you want the strongest brand recognition for international practice, the widest electives and exchange network, and the broadest alumni base in Hong Kong's top firms and the judiciary.
- **Choose HKU BBA(Law)&LLB (JS6808)** if you have an unambiguous interest in **corporate, banking, M&A or financial regulation** and are willing to commit to a five-year programme; this dual degree is the most efficient route to a Magic Circle training contract.
- **Choose CUHK LLB (JS4903)** if you have strong Chinese-language skills and orient toward **cross-boundary, PRC and ADR practice**; CUHK's clinical legal education and Greater Bay Area exposure are distinctive.
- **Choose CityU LLB (JS1061)** if you are competitive on Band A score but not the very top of the cohort; CityU's specialisations in **arbitration, technology law and IP** match well with Hong Kong's strategic positioning as an international dispute resolution hub.

## Key Dates and Action Items for DSE 2026 Applicants

1. **JUPAS Band A choice**: Place LLB programmes you genuinely target at Band A — interview shortlisting follows Band A weighting.
2. **English Language preparation**: Aim for 5\*\* — it is the single biggest lever in your admission score across all three law schools.
3. **Interview preparation**: Read at least one quality English newspaper daily (SCMP, FT, The Economist), follow major HKSAR judgments via the **HKLII** legal database, and be prepared to discuss current affairs in clear, structured English.
4. **Externships and exposure**: Spend a summer at a law firm, the Hong Kong Mediation Centre, or a Legal Aid clinic before Year 3 of your LLB — visible commitment to law materially helps PCLL admissions and training contract applications.
5. **PCLL forward planning**: Start tracking PCLL conversion exam requirements from Year 1 if you intend to study abroad and return for Hong Kong qualification.

The path from DSE to fully qualified Hong Kong lawyer is long — typically **four years LLB + one year PCLL + two years training contract = seven years** for a solicitor, or six for a barrister via the pupillage route. It is demanding, but the destination remains one of the most stable, prestigious and well-compensated professional careers available to a Hong Kong DSE graduate.
