# Hong Kong Anthropology Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

For DSE students, **Anthropology** is arguably Hong Kong's most underrated undergraduate major. It lacks the instant salary signal of Global Business or Quantitative Finance, and it does not come with the clean professional licence path of Medicine or Law. But in Hong Kong it has one unique structural fact: **only the Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK) runs an independent standalone Anthropology department, under JUPAS code JS4006**, with an actual intake of around 18 students per year. It is one of the smallest and most elite "micro-programmes" in the entire CUHK Faculty of Social Science.

For DSE candidates who genuinely enjoy *studying people, studying culture, and studying how societies actually work underneath*, anthropology is not "an impractical humanities degree." It is the **secret entrance** to careers in museums, international NGOs, private-bank family offices, Big Four cultural consulting, government cultural policy, UNESCO, and luxury market research. This guide unpacks the 2026 JUPAS profile of CUHK JS4006, the four-year curriculum architecture, the mandatory senior fieldwork project, museum partnerships with Cambridge and Sydney, and where graduates actually end up — including realistic starting salaries.

## Why Anthropology Exists at Only One Hong Kong University

To understand the scarcity of JS4006, start with the history. CUHK's Department of Anthropology was founded in **1980** as the first independent department in Greater China dedicated to socio-cultural anthropology, with a secondary specialisation in archaeology. HKU has no standalone anthropology department (anthropology courses occasionally appear under the Sociology department). HKUST has no anthropology. PolyU, CityU, HKBU, EdUHK, and LingnanU offer no anthropology majors either.

This means that if you want a "proper" anthropology bachelor in Hong Kong, **CUHK JS4006 is literally the only option**. That monopoly position produces three direct consequences:

1. **Small intake, very high faculty-to-student ratio** — roughly 18 students per cohort against 20+ faculty members, producing near one-to-one supervision.
2. **The `ANTH` course code is reserved exclusively for the department** — it does not share its curriculum budget with sociology or cultural studies.
3. **Fieldwork funding is concentrated** on the same 18 students — every undergraduate has a realistic chance of securing departmental funding for overseas ethnographic fieldwork.

## JS4006 2026 JUPAS Admission Profile

### Quota and basic requirements

- **JUPAS code**: JS4006
- **Degree title**: Bachelor of Social Science in Anthropology (BSSc Anthropology)
- **Indicative intake**: approximately 18 students (CUHK 2026 figure marked "to be confirmed")
- **Programme length**: 4-year normal track
- **Interview required**: no — JUPAS admission is score-based without interview

### CUHK "3322 + 2" baseline

All CUHK undergraduate programmes require a minimum DSE profile:

- Chinese Language: Level 3
- English Language: Level 3
- Mathematics (Compulsory Part): Level 2
- Citizenship and Social Development: Attained
- Two elective subjects: at least Level 2 each (Mathematics Extended Module M1/M2 counts as an elective for this purpose)

JS4006 itself imposes no additional subject prerequisites — unlike Medicine, Pharmacy, or Engineering, anthropology does not require Chemistry, Biology, Physics, M1, or M2. **Any DSE elective combination is admissible**, which makes it one of the few CUHK programmes accessible to candidates from a pure humanities track (e.g., Chinese History + Geography + Economics).

### Score calculation

CUHK Faculty of Social Science uses a **best-5 subjects** formula (1×Chinese + 1×English + 3×other best-scoring subjects). The scoring conversion under the new CUHK scale is:

- 5** = 8.5
- 5* = 7
- 5 = 5.5
- 4 = 4
- 3 = 3

Maximum theoretical score = 5 × 8.5 = **42.5**. Historically the JS4006 median admission score has clustered around **22–24**, equivalent to roughly **5/5/5/4/4** across five subjects. The lower quartile drops to about 20, which makes anthropology a **realistic Band A target for DSE 4-4-5-5-5 candidates** — a profile that would be uncompetitive for JS4202 Global Business or JS4458 Quantitative Finance.

### Strategic JUPAS placement

JS4006 is generally placed as a **Band A2 or A3 "safety net"** by candidates whose Band A1 is a more competitive CUHK Social Science programme (Government & Public Administration JS4810, Psychology JS4870, Sociology JS4886). It is also a credible **Band A1** for candidates who *positively want anthropology*, since the small intake means even modest under-scoring on a 5/5/5/4/4 profile usually still secures the offer.

## Four-Year Curriculum: Fieldwork From Year One

CUHK Anthropology runs on a four-year structure that introduces ethnographic fieldwork from Year 1 — unusual among Hong Kong social-science programmes, which typically reserve original research for the final-year capstone.

### Year 1 — Foundations

- `ANTH 1010 Introduction to Anthropology`
- `ANTH 1020 Hong Kong Culture and Society`
- `ANTH 1410 Introduction to Archaeology`
- CUHK General Education and language requirements
- Free electives across the Faculty of Social Science

Year 1 already includes one short **observational fieldwork exercise** in a Hong Kong neighbourhood (e.g., Sham Shui Po wet markets, Yuen Long walled villages, or Chungking Mansions), graded on a 2,000-word ethnographic memo.

### Year 2 — Theory and methods

- `ANTH 2110 Anthropological Theory`
- `ANTH 2210 Ethnographic Methods`
- `ANTH 2310 Material Culture and Museums`
- Regional electives: `ANTH 2510 Anthropology of China`, `ANTH 2520 Anthropology of Southeast Asia`, `ANTH 2540 Anthropology of Japan`

The **ANTH 2210 Ethnographic Methods** course is the gateway to senior research: students design and execute a six-week mini-ethnography (typically 5,000 words), often in a community of their own choosing — Indonesian domestic workers on Sundays in Central, eSports teams in Mong Kok, ultra-Orthodox congregations in Kowloon Tong, or queer support networks in Sai Ying Pun.

### Year 3 — Specialisation and exchange

- Advanced topical courses: `ANTH 3220 Medical Anthropology`, `ANTH 3240 Visual Anthropology`, `ANTH 3260 Anthropology of Money and Finance`, `ANTH 3280 Anthropology of Food`, `ANTH 3320 Religion and Ritual`, `ANTH 3380 Gender, Sexuality and Society`
- Optional **Term Abroad** at one of CUHK's partner institutions — heavily skewed toward anthropology powerhouses such as **University of Cambridge (Division of Social Anthropology)**, **University of Sydney**, **SOAS London**, **Australian National University**, **National Taiwan University**, and **Kyoto University**

CUHK Anthropology has standing collaborations with the **Museum of Archaeology and Anthropology (MAA) at Cambridge** and the **Chau Chak Wing Museum at Sydney**, including curatorial internships specifically reserved for JS4006 students.

### Year 4 — The senior research project

The signature of the CUHK anthropology degree is the **mandatory two-semester research sequence**:

- `ANTH 4300 Research Project I` (proposal, IRB approval, pilot fieldwork)
- `ANTH 4301 Research Project II` (full fieldwork, analysis, 10,000–15,000-word ethnographic thesis)

Students typically spend **6–12 weeks of intensive fieldwork** between Year 3 summer and Year 4. Past projects have included ethnographies of **Hong Kong night-market hawkers post-2019**, **Burmese migrant garment workers in Sham Shui Po**, **AI-art communities on Discord**, and **archaeological surveys of pre-colonial saltworks in Tai O**. Department funding of **HK$5,000–HK$25,000 per student** is the norm rather than the exception.

## Faculty and International Reputation

CUHK Anthropology is internationally ranked — appearing in the **QS World University Rankings by Subject 2025** top 100 for Anthropology, the only Hong Kong department on that list. Faculty interests cluster around four pillars:

1. **Chinese society** — kinship, religion, lineage villages, gentrification, post-handover identity
2. **Medical and psychological anthropology** — mental-health stigma, traditional medicine, ageing
3. **Material culture and museums** — heritage politics, intangible cultural heritage, Cantonese opera
4. **Archaeology** — South China prehistory, maritime archaeology in the Pearl River Delta

Department leadership publishes regularly in *American Anthropologist*, *Current Anthropology*, *American Ethnologist*, and *Journal of the Royal Anthropological Institute*. The flagship area journal **Asian Anthropology** is edited from CUHK.

## Graduate Destinations and Realistic Starting Salaries

The 18-student annual output distributes across a surprisingly wide career surface. Drawing on CUHK Anthropology alumni surveys and Career Planning and Development Centre data, the typical 12-month outcomes look like this:

### Museum, heritage, and cultural sector

- **Hong Kong Palace Museum** — assistant curator (entry-level): **HK$28,000–HK$34,000/month**
- **M+ Museum** — research assistant / programmer (entry-level): **HK$26,000–HK$32,000/month**
- **Leisure and Cultural Services Department (LCSD)** — Curator II civil-service grade: **HK$34,000+/month** with full pension and housing benefits
- **Tai Kwun Centre for Heritage and Arts** — programme officer: **HK$25,000–HK$30,000/month**

### NGO and international development

- **Oxfam Hong Kong / MSF / UNHCR Hong Kong office** — programme officer: **HK$24,000–HK$30,000/month**
- **The D. H. Chen Foundation / Lee Hysan Foundation** — grants officer: **HK$28,000–HK$35,000/month**
- **Hong Kong Red Cross** — cross-border programme coordinator: **HK$25,000–HK$32,000/month**

### Private banking and family offices

This is the **least obvious but highest-paying** anthropology route. Hong Kong family offices and private banks (HSBC Global Private Banking, UBS, Julius Baer, Pictet) increasingly hire anthropologists for *qualitative client research, cross-cultural relationship management, and next-generation wealth transition advisory*. Entry-level analyst roles run **HK$45,000–HK$60,000/month**, with anthropology graduates competing successfully against finance majors precisely because they can sustain a structured 90-minute conversation with an Indonesian-Chinese matriarch about lineage politics.

### Big Four cultural and behavioural consulting

- **Deloitte / PwC / EY / KPMG** — Customer Strategy / Human Capital / Workforce Transformation analyst tracks regularly recruit social-science graduates: **HK$28,000–HK$36,000/month** at entry
- Boutique cultural consultancies (ReD Associates, Stripe Partners, Quilt.AI) hire CUHK anthropology graduates for ethnographic consulting in luxury, tech, and FMCG markets — Hong Kong-based salaries typically **HK$32,000–HK$45,000/month**, with London/New York rotations available

### Postgraduate progression

Roughly **30–40% of each cohort** progresses to a research master's or PhD. CUHK Anthropology BSSc graduates have a strong track record placing into:

- **University of Oxford** — MSc Social Anthropology (Institute of Social and Cultural Anthropology)
- **University of Cambridge** — MPhil Social Anthropology
- **Yale University** — PhD Anthropology (full funding)
- **LSE** — MSc Anthropology and Development
- **SOAS London** — MA Anthropology of Media / MA Anthropology of Food
- **UC Berkeley, Chicago, Michigan** — PhD with stipend

Department staff write strong letters of recommendation, and the small cohort size means students typically have **multiple faculty members** who know their fieldwork personally — a significant advantage in the postgraduate application process.

## Who Should — and Should Not — Apply

JS4006 is a strong fit if you:

- Enjoy long-form qualitative research, conversation, and writing
- Are comfortable spending weeks in unfamiliar communities asking awkward questions
- Want a degree that explicitly trains cross-cultural fluency for a globalised career
- Are willing to defer immediate salary gratification in exchange for unusual career optionality
- Have a 5/5/5/4/4 or stronger DSE profile but no Chemistry/Biology/M1/M2

JS4006 is **probably not** the right choice if you:

- Want a clean, licensed professional path with predictable salary progression
- Dislike open-ended assignments and prefer right/wrong answer structures
- Have no tolerance for fieldwork involving public transport, irregular hours, or rejection
- Want to maximise starting salary above HK$35,000/month directly out of undergraduate

## Practical Application Tips for DSE 2026 Candidates

1. **Band A1 strategy**: if you have ≥22 in the CUHK best-5 formula and you genuinely want anthropology, place JS4006 in Band A1 — the small intake means the median admit profile is *not* especially competitive and you preserve psychological commitment.
2. **Band A2/A3 strategy**: if your Band A1 is JS4870 Psychology or JS4886 Sociology, JS4006 is the strongest single safety-net at CUHK Social Science — the score requirement is meaningfully lower than the larger programmes.
3. **Non-JUPAS applicants** (IB, A-Level, mainland Gaokao, overseas) should apply directly through CUHK Office of Admissions and Financial Aid before **15 November 2026**. Indicative thresholds: IB 36, A-Level AAA, Gaokao 一本线 + 80, SAT 1450+.
4. **Personal background statements** are not required for JUPAS but *are* used by CUHK Anthropology informally when deciding borderline offers. A 300-word personal statement attached to your CUHK supplementary form — describing one ethnographic observation you have already made about Hong Kong — costs you nothing and visibly distinguishes you from candidates who placed JS4006 only as a fallback.

## Conclusion

CUHK Anthropology JS4006 is Hong Kong's only standalone anthropology bachelor, with an annual intake of approximately 18 students, no subject prerequisites, and a median admission score that is realistic for DSE candidates around the 5/5/5/4/4 profile. The four-year curriculum delivers structured fieldwork from Year 1, a mandatory two-semester senior research project, and well-established overseas partnerships with Cambridge, Sydney, and SOAS. Graduates land in museums, NGOs, private banking, and cultural consulting — with the private banking and Big Four routes producing starting salaries that rival or exceed many business programmes. For DSE 2026 candidates who genuinely enjoy understanding how people and societies work, JS4006 is one of the most strategically underpriced Band A options in the entire JUPAS catalogue.
