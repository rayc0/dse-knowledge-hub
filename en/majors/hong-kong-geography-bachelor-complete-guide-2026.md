# Hong Kong Geography Bachelor Complete Guide 2026: HKU JS6713, CUHK JS4836, HKBU JS2030

Geography is one of the most consistently underrated bachelor programmes in Hong Kong. It is the bridge discipline between the natural and social sciences — and the skill stack it produces (spatial analysis, GIS, remote sensing, fieldwork, policy evaluation) maps cleanly onto today's fastest-growing employment areas: ESG and sustainability, climate tech, smart cities, PropTech, urban regeneration, and public-sector planning.

This guide is written for 2026 DSE candidates and parents. It compares the three core JUPAS Geography programmes — HKU (JS6713), CUHK (JS4836), and HKBU (JS2030) — across JUPAS scoring, curriculum design, GIS depth, exchange networks, and graduate outcomes, with a practical "which one should I rank first?" decision framework at the end.

## 1. Why Geography in 2026? Three Underrated Facts

**Fact 1: Geography is no longer about "reading maps" — it is spatial data science.** HKU and CUHK now embed GIS (Geographic Information Systems), remote sensing, Python/R spatial analytics, drone surveying, and 3D modelling deep into the curriculum. What a Geography graduate sells to the labour market is the ability to turn geographic data into decisions, not just a humanistic understanding of place.

**Fact 2: Stable demand from the Hong Kong government and infrastructure sector.** The Planning Department, Civil Engineering and Development Department, Drainage Services Department, Lands Department, Environmental Protection Department, and Transport Department all maintain dedicated positions for spatial information and planning evaluation — Town Planner, GIS Officer, Environmental Protection Officer, Assistant Geotechnical Engineer. These civil-service tracks come with internal progression ladders and pension security, which a finance-only degree cannot match.

**Fact 3: Geography is "interdisciplinary insurance."** Because the bachelor cuts across natural and social sciences, graduates can pivot into Town Planning (PolyU MSc), Environmental Management (HKU MSc), GIS (CUHK MSc Geoinformation Science), or Sustainable Tourism via a one-year taught master's. The "broad bachelor + specialised master's" pathway hedges career risk better than a narrow undergraduate specialisation.

## 2. The Three JUPAS Geography Programmes at a Glance (2026 Intake)

### 1. The University of Hong Kong (HKU) — BSocSc in Geography (JS6713)

HKU's Department of Geography is one of the oldest in Hong Kong and sits within the Faculty of Social Sciences. Its centre of gravity is the "human–environment system" with a strong global-issues lens.

- **Structure**: 4-year programme. Year 1 covers Social Sciences common core (you pick one of Economics, Psychology, Politics, or Sociology as a second exposure). Geography major declared from Year 2. Minors available in GIS, Environmental Management, and Urban Studies.
- **Core areas**: Atmospheric environment and global climate, transport geography, urban planning, water resources, environmental hazards, China studies, biogeography, tourism, human and urban geography, GIS and spatial analysis.
- **Mandatory fieldwork**: Local and overseas (past destinations include Hualien Taiwan, Yunnan China, Southeast Asia), 1–2 trips per academic year.
- **Exchange**: Faculty of Social Sciences partners with 350+ universities worldwide. Popular Geography destinations include UCL, Bristol, Edinburgh (UK); UBC (Canada); UNSW (Australia); NUS (Singapore).
- **2025 admission reference (median)**: ~28–30 points (Best 5), mid-tier among HKU Social Sciences offers.

### 2. The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK) — BSSc in Geography and Resource Management (JS4836)

CUHK fuses geography with resource management — a strong signal that the programme prioritises sustainability, land and water resources, and ecological conservation. It is one of the few departments in South China running BSSc, MPhil, PhD, MSc Geoinformation Science, and MSSc Sustainable Tourism under the same roof.

- **Structure**: 4-year programme run by the Department of Geography and Resource Management (GRM). Double majors and minors permitted.
- **Core areas**: Sustainable resource management, climate and hydrology, land use planning, ecosystem services, sustainable tourism, geoinformation science, China and Asian regional studies.
- **Fieldwork and internships**: Partnerships with HK government departments (EPD, AFCD) and NGOs (WWF Hong Kong, Green Power). Routine field sites include the New Territories north, Mai Po wetlands, and Lantau.
- **GIS strength**: Full GIS and remote-sensing laboratory with ESRI ArcGIS, QGIS, ENVI, and Google Earth Engine training — directly aligned with the Hong Kong land, surveying, and planning consultancy market.
- **2025 admission reference (median)**: ~25–27 points (Best 5), suitable for mid- to upper-Band A candidates.

### 3. Hong Kong Baptist University (HKBU) — BSocSc (Hons) in Geography (JS2030)

HKBU runs a smaller but clearly positioned programme that prioritises rigorous geographic methodology and critical analysis of human–environment interaction. A flexible alternative entry is JS2610 (a combined Social Sciences code letting you choose between European Studies, Geography, Global and China Studies, Government and International Studies, History, and Sociology after Year 1).

- **Structure**: 4-year programme. Year 1 is Social Sciences common; major declared from Year 2. Double majors with Global and China Studies, Sociology, or Environmental Studies are possible.
- **Core areas**: Geographic theory and methods, human geography, urban and regional studies, GIS, sustainable development, cultural geography, East Asian studies.
- **Class size advantage**: Approximately 30–40 Geography majors per cohort. Tight student-to-faculty ratio means undergraduates can secure research assistantships (RA) more easily than at HKU or CUHK.
- **Exchange**: ~50 partner institutions across Japan, Korea, Europe, and North America.
- **2025 admission reference (median)**: ~22–24 points (Best 5), a sensible Band A safety pick for upper-Band B candidates.

> ⚠️ Admission scores are indicative. JUPAS medians fluctuate year-on-year and HKU/CUHK use proprietary scoring (5\*\* = 8.5) while HKBU uses the standard scale (5\*\* = 7). Do not compare across universities at face value — use a [JUPAS Score Calculator](https://jupascalculator.com/) and feed in your predicted grades.

## 3. JUPAS Scoring and Subject Strategy

### How the three universities count points

- **HKU**: 4 Core + 2 Electives = Best 6, proprietary scoring (5\*\* = 8.5, 5\* = 7, 5 = 5.5). Geography programme applies no subject weighting.
- **CUHK**: Geography and Resource Management uses 4C + 2X = Best 6, with some core subject weighting. M1 or M2 counts as a second elective.
- **HKBU**: Best 5 calculation, standard scoring (5\*\* = 7). Friendly to candidates with stable Top-5 grades but one weak subject.

### DSE Subject Recommendations for Geography Applicants

1. **Take DSE Geography**: None of the three universities make it strictly mandatory, but a Level 4+ in DSE Geography materially strengthens your case at interview and helps you self-test whether you actually enjoy the discipline.
2. **One science subject**: Physics, Chemistry, Biology, or Combined Science — any one strengthens your ability to handle physical geography (climate, hydrology, ecology). CUHK GRM values science backgrounds.
3. **M1 or M2**: If you are drawn to GIS and spatial statistics, M2 (calculus and algebra) will pay off heavily in geostatistics and spatial-analysis electives.
4. **Citizenship & Social Development**: Attain is sufficient. CSD performance does not influence Geography admission.

### Band A Strategy by Predicted Score

- **Solid HKU (30+)**: Band A1 HKU JS6713, A2 CUHK JS4836, A3 HKBU JS2030 as safety.
- **Stretching for HKU, secure CUHK (27–29)**: A1 HKU JS6713, A2 CUHK JS4836, B1–B3 PolyU adjacent programmes (e.g. Land Surveying & Geo-Informatics) or HKBU JS2030.
- **Secure CUHK, HKBU as floor (24–26)**: A1 CUHK JS4836, A2 HKBU JS2030, A3 EdUHK or HKMU interdisciplinary options.

## 4. What You Actually Study Across Four Years

Most prospective students underestimate how technical a modern Geography curriculum is. A typical year-by-year structure looks like this:

**Year 1**: Common Social Sciences core (sociology, economics, basic statistics), Introduction to Physical Geography, Introduction to Human Geography, Map Reading & Spatial Thinking, plus university-wide language and general education requirements.

**Year 2**: Climatology, Geomorphology, Urban and Regional Geography, Population & Migration, Introduction to GIS, Research Methods (qualitative and quantitative). Field methods training begins.

**Year 3**: Specialised electives — Remote Sensing, Spatial Statistics, Sustainable Tourism, Transport Geography, Environmental Impact Assessment, China's Regional Development, Climate Change & Society. Most students complete a major fieldwork trip (local or overseas). Some take a credit-bearing internship.

**Year 4**: Capstone project or Honours dissertation (often built around an original GIS or fieldwork dataset), advanced topical electives, internship (optional), exchange semester (optional).

**Toolchain you will leave with**: ESRI ArcGIS Pro, QGIS, R (sf, terra packages), Python (geopandas, rasterio), Google Earth Engine, basic SQL, drone-based mapping, statistical software (SPSS or Stata), and academic-writing fluency for policy reports.

## 5. Internships, Exchange, and Fieldwork

**Internship channels** typically include:

- HK government attachments: Planning Department, Lands Department, Environmental Protection Department, AFCD.
- Planning consultancies: AECOM, Arup, Atkins (SNC-Lavalin), Mott MacDonald, MVA, Ove Arup.
- ESG and sustainability roles: HSBC ESG, Swire Sustainability, MTR Environment Office, CLP Power sustainability division.
- NGOs: WWF HK, Green Power, Civic Exchange, Friends of the Earth (HK).

**Exchange**: All three universities offer one semester to one full year abroad. Tier-one Geography exchange destinations include UCL, LSE, Bristol, Edinburgh, Manchester (UK); UBC, McGill, Toronto (Canada); UNSW, Sydney, Melbourne (Australia); NUS, NTU (Singapore); Tokyo, Kyoto (Japan).

**Fieldwork**: Expect at least one mandatory overseas field trip across the four years, plus regular local fieldwork in places like Mai Po, Tai O, Sai Kung, Lantau, and the New Territories north. Out-of-pocket cost ranges from HK$3,000 to HK$15,000 per overseas trip — most universities offer subsidies for students with financial need.

## 6. Graduate Outcomes and Salary Ranges

Geography graduate destinations are wider than most candidates assume. Based on graduate employment surveys from the three universities (2023–2024 cohorts):

**Public sector (about 25–35% of graduates)**:

- Town Planner / Assistant Town Planner (HK Planning Department): starting MPS Point 16, around HK$32,000–35,000/month.
- Geotechnical / Land Surveyor / GIS Officer (CEDD, Lands Department, HKO): MPS Point 14–16, HK$28,000–34,000/month.
- Environmental Protection Officer (EPD): MPS Point 22+ after qualification, HK$40,000+/month.

**Planning, engineering, and ESG consulting (about 20–25%)**:

- Graduate Planner / Assistant Environmental Consultant at AECOM, Arup, Atkins, Mott MacDonald: HK$22,000–28,000/month, rising to HK$35,000–45,000 after 3–5 years and chartered status (HKIP, IEMA).
- ESG Analyst at banks, MTR, CLP, Swire: HK$25,000–32,000/month entry, with rapid escalation as ESG reporting requirements expand.

**Technology and PropTech (about 10–15%)**:

- GIS Developer / Geospatial Analyst at ESRI China HK, Cathay Pacific, JLL, CBRE, Knight Frank: HK$25,000–35,000/month.
- Data analyst roles at logistics and e-commerce platforms applying spatial analytics (HKTV Mall, SF Express, J&T Express).

**Education and academia (about 10–15%)**:

- Secondary school Geography teacher (post-PGDE): NSS pay scale, starting around HK$33,000–35,000/month.
- Research assistant or PhD pipeline at HK, mainland, or overseas universities.

**Other directions (about 15–20%)**: Tourism management (HKDMC, Cathay Pacific, hotel chains), NGO and policy think tanks, banking management trainees, civil service generalist tracks, graduate study overseas.

Median first-year salary across all Geography graduates from the three universities sits around **HK$22,000–28,000/month**, with the public-sector and consultancy tracks reaching HK$35,000+ within 3–5 years.

## 7. Which Programme Should You Rank First? Decision Framework

Strip the marketing away, and the choice between HKU, CUHK, and HKBU Geography reduces to four practical questions:

**Question 1 — Do you want global brand, or domain depth?**

- HKU JS6713 wins on international brand and overseas postgraduate placement. If you intend to apply for a master's at Oxbridge, UCL, or top US schools, HKU's Social Sciences pedigree carries weight.
- CUHK JS4836 wins on depth — the GRM department's tight integration of GIS, resource management, and sustainable tourism is unmatched locally. If you want to go deep on environmental planning or geoinformation, CUHK is the stronger pick.

**Question 2 — Do you want science or social science emphasis?**

- HKU: leans more global, theoretical, social-science-flavoured.
- CUHK: more balanced between physical and human geography, with explicit resource management framing.
- HKBU: most social-science-oriented, with humanities and critical theory weight.

**Question 3 — How important is class size?**

- HKU and CUHK cohorts are 80–120 majors; lectures can feel large.
- HKBU runs 30–40 majors with much closer faculty contact. If you want RA opportunities and personal mentorship, HKBU is genuinely stronger here.

**Question 4 — Career sector preference?**

- Civil service and consultancy: CUHK and HKU tie, with CUHK marginally ahead in surveying and land-related tracks.
- Academia and overseas postgraduate: HKU.
- Education sector and NGO: HKBU (good NGO and Buddhist Studies / NGO sector connections through HKBU networks).

## 8. Common Misconceptions to Drop Before You Apply

**Misconception 1: "Geography is for people who can't get into Engineering or Business."** Wrong. Geography intersects with planning, surveying, climate science, and PropTech — fields with strong wage growth. The cohort quality at HKU and CUHK Geography is comparable to mid-tier Engineering or Social Science programmes.

**Misconception 2: "There are no jobs in Geography."** Public sector and planning consultancies hire steadily every year. The misperception comes from the absence of "Geography Department" job listings — graduates fan out into Planning, Surveying, ESG, GIS, and Education roles instead.

**Misconception 3: "GIS will be replaced by AI."** AI augments GIS, it does not replace the spatial-thinking layer. Geographers who add Python, machine learning, and Google Earth Engine to their toolkit are precisely the people building the AI-spatial pipelines, not being displaced by them.

**Misconception 4: "I need to be good at DSE Geography to study Geography."** Helpful but not mandatory. Many CUHK GRM and HKBU students come from science backgrounds without DSE Geography. The university curriculum rebuilds the foundations in Year 1.

- ✅ Confirm DSE elective combination: keep DSE Geography if you've taken it; add M2 if GIS interests you.
- ✅ Attend HKU, CUHK, and HKBU Info Days (typically October–November 2026).
- ✅ Read at least one academic paper from each department's faculty page — it sharpens interview answers and signals genuine interest.
- ✅ If your predicted score is 27+: rank HKU JS6713 in Band A1, CUHK JS4836 in A2.
- ✅ If your predicted score is 24–26: rank CUHK JS4836 in A1, HKBU JS2030 in A2, with a Band B safety in PolyU surveying or EdUHK Liberal Studies.
- ✅ Start building a basic GIS portfolio now — free QGIS tutorials and Google Earth Engine tutorials are widely available and show universities you are serious.

Geography in 2026 is not the "fallback humanities subject" the previous generation imagined. It is the discipline that turns climate, cities, and data into decisions — and the Hong Kong programmes at HKU, CUHK, and HKBU are well-resourced, exchange-connected, and career-credible. The key is to choose by fit, not by reputation halo, and to enter the programme with a clear plan to graduate with the toolchain (GIS, remote sensing, fieldwork, policy writing) that the job market actually pays for.
