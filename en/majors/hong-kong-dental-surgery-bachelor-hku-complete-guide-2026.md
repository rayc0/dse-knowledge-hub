# Hong Kong Dental Surgery Bachelor (HKU JS6107) Complete Guide 2026: Six-Year BDS, Dental Council Registration, One-Year Internship, Department of Health Salary Scales

The Bachelor of Dental Surgery (BDS) is the only locally-trained dental degree in Hong Kong that leads directly to registration with the Dental Council of Hong Kong (DCHK). It is offered exclusively by the HKU Faculty of Dentistry under JUPAS code JS6107, with an annual intake of just 90 students - making it one of the most competitive and highest-scoring programmes in the entire JUPAS catalogue.

The HKU Faculty of Dentistry was ranked No. 1 in the world by QS World University Rankings by Subject for three consecutive years (2016, 2017, 2018) and has consistently sat within the global top ten - the only Asian dental school to do so. Teaching takes place primarily at the Prince Philip Dental Hospital (PPDH) in Wan Chai, Hong Kong's only dedicated dental teaching hospital.

This guide is written for 2026 DSE candidates and their parents. It covers BDS curriculum structure, JUPAS scoring, the registration pathway, the new mandatory one-year internship introduced under the 2024 Dentists Registration (Amendment) Ordinance, and post-graduation salary and career outlook in the Department of Health, the Hospital Authority, and private practice.

## 2. Minimum JUPAS Requirements and Scoring

### 2.1 Subject thresholds (2026 intake)

The non-negotiable minimums for HKU BDS in DSE are:

- **Chinese Language**: Level 3
- **English Language**: Level 4
- **Mathematics (Compulsory Part)**: Level 3
- **Citizenship and Social Development**: Attained
- **Elective 1**: Chemistry, Biology, or Mathematics Extended Module M1/M2 (any one, Level 3)
- **Elective 2**: Biology or Chemistry (Level 3)
- **One additional subject**: Any subject excluding Applied Learning, Level 3

Although M1/M2 can technically substitute for one of the science electives, **Chemistry is effectively mandatory**: the Faculty's interview and personal-statement evaluators consistently favour candidates with a Chemistry background. **Biology** is similarly indispensable for understanding oral anatomy, histology, and pharmacology. The recommended elective combination for serious BDS aspirants is **Chemistry + Biology + (M1/M2 or Physics)**.

### 2.2 JUPAS scoring formula

For 2026 admissions, HKU BDS uses **Best 6** scoring - the four core subjects (Chinese, English, Mathematics, Citizenship and Social Development) plus two electives - converted as follows:

| DSE level | Score |
| --- | --- |
| 5** | 8.5 |
| 5* | 7 |
| 5 | 5.5 |
| 4 | 4 |
| 3 | 3 |
| 2 | 2 |

Citizenship and Social Development is graded only as Attained / Not Attained and carries no score. **English Language and Chemistry/Biology are weighted up** under the Faculty's formula; the exact multipliers are adjusted yearly and should be verified on the HKU Admissions Office website.

### 2.3 Historical cut-offs

According to HKU admissions statistics and JUPAS-tracking platforms, the **Best 6 median for BDS (JS6107) sits at around 38-40 points** in recent years - on par with MBBS (Medicine). The lower quartile is approximately 36-37 points. In practical terms, a serious Band A 1A applicant should target **five 5* grades and one 5\*\* (or better)**.

Data from the 2025 admissions cycle shows that JS6107 received **994 applications across all bands** and issued **54 unconditional offers, all of them to Band A applicants**. BDS effectively does not pick up any Band B candidates - putting JS6107 in Band A 1A is a baseline requirement.

## 3. Interview and Personal Statement

Shortlisted applicants undergo a **Multiple Mini Interview (MMI)** format - typically six to eight stations, each lasting six to eight minutes. Stations cover:

- Ethical dilemmas (e.g. a patient demanding extraction of a tooth that can still be saved)
- Communication simulations (e.g. explaining anaesthesia to an anxious child)
- Group teamwork tasks
- Current-affairs discussion (healthcare policy, public-private imbalance, dentist shortage)
- Manual dexterity tests (origami, jigsaw puzzles, carving soft material)

The Faculty places exceptional weight on **manual dexterity** and **empathy**. Candidates with a background in fine arts, model-making, surgical observation, sewing, or sculpture have a measurable advantage in the dexterity stations.

A strong Personal Statement should address:

1. Why dentistry rather than medicine - candidates must articulate a substantive distinction between the two professions
2. Awareness of Hong Kong's dental landscape (public-private imbalance, long waiting times, gaps in elderly dental services)
3. Hospital/clinic shadowing or community oral-health volunteer experience
4. How personal traits align with PBL (problem-based learning) pedagogy

## 4. Six-Year Curriculum (2025/26 Revamped Structure)

HKU BDS centres on **Problem-Based Learning (PBL)** within an integrated, competency-based curriculum, divided into twelve semesters plus six clinical skills periods.

### Year 1: Healthy Person and Disease Awareness

Foundation year. Focus on the healthy human body and basic biomedical sciences (anatomy, physiology, biochemistry, histology), alongside HKU Common Core, English and Chinese enhancement, AI literacy, and Constitution and Basic Law modules. Students enter the pre-clinical simulation laboratory for early manual-skills training.

### Year 2: Common Oral Diseases

Focus on the common oral diseases - dental caries, periodontal disease, periapical lesions, malocclusion. Students begin **simple clinical observation and basic chairside assistance** with real patients under supervision. The integrated capstone experience is launched in this year.

### Year 3: Management of Oral Diseases at Discipline Level

Discipline-level deep dives into endodontics, periodontology, prosthodontics, oral and maxillofacial surgery, paediatric dentistry, and orthodontics. Clinical hours scale up sharply, with weekly polyclinic sessions at PPDH.

### Year 4: Advanced Clinical Care

Advanced clinical care including multi-disciplinary case management, dental implants, root-canal retreatment, and child behaviour management. Students concurrently take Professional English in Dentistry.

### Year 5: Integrated Patient Care across Disciplines and into Communities

Integrated, cross-disciplinary patient care extending into the community. Students participate in **outreach** programmes, geriatric dentistry, special-needs patient services, and school oral-health education. The **capstone research project** is completed in this year.

### Year 6: The Dentist into Practice

The "dentist-ready-to-practise" year. Coursework focuses on professional practice, risk management, medico-legal issues, infection control, clinic management, and inter-professional collaboration - simulating the full working environment of a registered dentist in preparation for the mandatory internship.

## 5. Registration Pathway: The 2024 Mandatory Internship Reform

This is the single most important change for **2026 entrants** to understand.

### 5.1 The 2024 Dentists Registration (Amendment) Ordinance

The Legislative Council passed the Dentists Registration (Amendment) Ordinance 2024, which began phased commencement on 1 January 2025. Key changes:

- Introduced **Limited Registration** and **Special Registration** mechanisms allowing non-locally-trained dentists employed by the Department of Health, Hospital Authority, HKU, or PPDH to practise in Hong Kong.
- Introduced **Temporary Registration** for overseas dentists visiting for clinical teaching or research stints of up to 14 days.
- **Local BDS graduates must now complete a one-year internship** before they qualify for full DCHK registration.

### 5.2 Internship structure

Internship placements are concentrated at:

- **Prince Philip Dental Hospital** (the largest cohort, around 60-70 places)
- **Department of Health dental clinics** (around 20-30 places)
- **Hospital Authority designated specialist units** (small numbers, mostly oral and maxillofacial surgery)
- A handful of accredited **community or NGO** dental services

Intern salaries follow the Civil Service Master Pay Scale entry point for Dental Officers - approximately **HK$71,010 to HK$93,255 per month**, depending on placement and host institution.

### 5.3 Comparison with the pre-2024 system

Under the old system, local BDS graduates received **full registration immediately upon graduation** and could practise independently. The new system requires a supervised internship year first - aligning Hong Kong with international norms (the UK GDC requires Dental Foundation Training; the Australian ADC requires supervised practice) and ensuring graduates have substantial clinical exposure before independent practice.

## 6. Career Outcomes and Salary

### 6.1 Public sector

**Department of Health Dental Officer (DO)**

- Entry pay point: around MPS Point 27 (HK$71,010 starting)
- Top pay point: up to HK$93,255 (around GDS Point 35) under the global recruitment scheme
- Patient base: civil servants, CSSA recipients, the School Dental Care Service, elderly dental services
- Locations: government dental clinics across Hong Kong - Kwun Tong, Kowloon Bay, Tuen Mun, Tsuen Wan, Tai Po, etc.

**Promotion ladder:**

| Rank | Monthly salary range |
| --- | --- |
| Dental Officer | from HK$71,010 |
| Senior Dental Officer | HK$120,000+ |
| Consultant Dental Surgeon | HK$180,000+ |

**Hospital Authority oral and maxillofacial surgery units**

The Hospital Authority is not the primary employer of general Dental Officers, but its Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery units at Queen Mary, Queen Elizabeth, Kwong Wah, and Tuen Mun Hospitals employ surgically-trained dentists at salaries comparable to DH Dental Officers.

### 6.2 Private practice

Income in Hong Kong private dental practice varies enormously, but new graduates working as **associate dentists** in their first year or two typically earn **HK$80,000 to HK$150,000 per month**, depending on clinic location, seniority, and personal productivity. Established practice owners with specialist qualifications (MOrth, MFDS, FCDSHK) can earn well in excess of HK$300,000 per month.

### 6.3 Specialist training

After full registration, graduates can apply for specialist training under the **College of Dental Surgeons of Hong Kong (CDSHK)**. Recognised specialties include:

- Periodontology
- Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery
- Prosthodontics
- Paediatric Dentistry
- Orthodontics
- Endodontics
- Restorative Dentistry
- Community Dentistry

After six years of specialist training and passing the Fellowship examination (FCDSHK / FHKAM), graduates become specialist dentists listed on the DCHK Specialist Register.

### 6.4 Overseas registration

HKU BDS is recognised in many jurisdictions:

- **United Kingdom**: Eligible to apply to the General Dental Council (GDC); in some cases the Overseas Registration Examination (ORE) may be required.
- **Australia**: Must clear the Australian Dental Council (ADC) assessment.
- **Singapore**: HKU BDS is on the Singapore Dental Council (SDC) approved list and can apply directly.
- **Canada**: Must pass the National Dental Examining Board (NDEB) equivalency process.

Overall, HKU BDS is one of very few Asian dental degrees with strong recognition across multiple English-speaking jurisdictions - offering far greater overseas mobility than mainland China stomatology degrees.

## 7. Practical Tips for DSE Candidates

1. **Subject combination**: Chemistry + Biology is the gold standard; add M1/M2 or Physics. BAFS and Economics offer little leverage for dentistry applications.
2. **Co-curricular activities**: Hospital or clinic shadowing, elderly-care centre service, and community health events - ideally sustained over more than a year.
3. **Manual dexterity training**: Pottery, model-making, drawing, sculpture, sewing, and surgical-knot tying all pay off in the MMI dexterity stations and in the personal statement.
4. **Track local dental policy**: Health Care Voucher coverage of dental services, School Dental Care Service reform, public dental waiting lists - all are perennial MMI topics.
5. **Band A 1A is the minimum**: BDS does not realistically pick up Band B applicants. JS6107 must sit in Band A 1A for an interview to be feasible.
6. **Plan B**: If the first round produces no interview or offer, alternatives include applying to HKU Biomedical Sciences or Pharmacy and pursuing internal transfer, or the non-locally-trained route via UK BDS / Australian BDSc returning to Hong Kong under Limited Registration.

## 8. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Which has the higher cut-off, BDS or MBBS?**

In recent years HKU MBBS (JS6456) and CUHK MBChB have run about 0.5-1 point higher than BDS at the median, but year-on-year volatility is significant. Interview and personal-statement criteria differ sharply - medicine emphasises broad knowledge and social-policy fluency, whereas dentistry weights manual dexterity and oral-health focus.

**Q: Can I apply to HKU BDS and HKU MBBS together?**

Yes. Both are six-year UGC-funded programmes and can sit at different positions within Band A. Note that interview dates may overlap, and the faculties review your band ordering as a signal of commitment. If dentistry is genuinely your top choice, JS6107 should be at Band A 1A.

**Q: Can mainland Chinese students apply to BDS?**

Yes. HKU admits non-local students through the non-JUPAS Mainland Gaokao admissions and the international student route, with limited places (around 20% non-local quota). Non-local tuition is significantly higher (HK$182,000 per year, with the 2026 intake expected to rise close to HK$200,000).

**Q: Do interns get paid during the mandatory internship year?**

Yes. Interns are paid at the Civil Service Dental Officer entry point - HK$71,010 per month and up.

**Q: My DSE score is below the BDS threshold but I really want to study dentistry. What are my options?**

Possibilities include:

- Read a related HKU/CUHK undergraduate degree (Biology, Biomedical Sciences, Pharmacy) and apply for graduate-entry BDS overseas (the University of Queensland and the University of Sydney offer four-year graduate dentistry programmes).
- Read an associate degree or self-financed programme in oral health (e.g. Tung Wah College Dental Hygiene), then articulate to a UK or Australian BDS programme.
- Apply directly to UK BDS through UCAS - five years to qualify, then return to Hong Kong via the Limited Registration route.

## 9. Conclusion

HKU BDS is Hong Kong's only direct route to local dental registration, supported by a globally top-ranked faculty, the Prince Philip Dental Hospital clinical platform, and - from 2025 onwards - a regularised one-year internship that aligns Hong Kong with international standards. For 2026 DSE candidates, BDS remains one of the most selective Band A choices in JUPAS, demanding at least 38 Best-6 points, a solid Chemistry and Biology foundation, a sustained co-curricular record, and demonstrable empathy and manual dexterity in the MMI.

If your goal is to become a dentist, HKU BDS is not just a credential - it is a six-year clinical, ethical, and leadership apprenticeship. Start planning early, ideally from Form 4: lock in your elective combination, accumulate volunteer hours, train your hands, and follow Hong Kong's dental policy debates. These investments compound into a substantially higher chance of securing that Band A 1A offer.
