# Hong Kong Music Bachelor Complete Guide 2026: HKU JS6054, CUHK JS4082, HKBU JS2060, HKAPA, EdUHK JS8801 — JUPAS Codes, Auditions, and Music Careers

For DSE students who have practised an instrument since primary school, sung in a choir for a decade, or composed since their first GarageBand experiment, the question by Form 6 becomes serious: can a music degree in Hong Kong actually lead somewhere? The short answer is yes — but the pathway is unusual. Music is one of the few subjects where DSE marks alone do not get you in. Every Hong Kong music bachelor programme requires an audition, a portfolio, or both, and the academic offer is contingent on what the panel hears, not just what JUPAS calculates.

This guide walks through every undergraduate music pathway available to a 2026 DSE cohort in Hong Kong: HKU's Bachelor of Arts with a Music major (JS6054), CUHK's Bachelor of Arts in Music (JS4082), HKBU's Bachelor of Arts / Bachelor of Music in Music or Creative Industries (JS2060), the Hong Kong Academy for Performing Arts (HKAPA, non-JUPAS audition entry), and the Education University of Hong Kong's co-terminal double degrees combining music with teacher training (JS8801, JS8001, JS8636, JS8685). For each, we cover JUPAS code, audition expectations, curriculum structure, and the career outcomes graduates actually pursue — from orchestral playing and composing to school music teaching, arts administration, and music technology.

## Why study music in Hong Kong

Music is unusual among DSE subjects in that fewer than 1,500 candidates sit it each year — Music is a Category A elective only some schools even offer. That small candidate pool obscures a much larger group of musically active DSE students who learned outside school: ABRSM Grade 8 or diploma graduates, choir veterans, jazz combo members, Cantopop songwriters, and Chinese music ensemble players. For these students, a music bachelor is the legitimate professional pathway into orchestras, conservatoires for postgraduate study, school teaching with full Education Bureau (EDB) registration, and the arts management track that increasingly shapes Hong Kong's cultural ecosystem (West Kowloon Cultural District, Hong Kong Phil, City Chamber Orchestra, Tai Kwun, and the new East Kowloon Cultural Centre).

The other reason to study music locally rather than overseas is cost and proximity. A four-year UK conservatoire pathway (Royal College of Music, Royal Academy, Trinity Laban, Royal Northern, Birmingham) runs roughly GBP 35,000–50,000 per year inclusive of accommodation; a US conservatoire (Juilliard, Curtis, NEC, Eastman, Manhattan School) easily exceeds USD 80,000 per year. The UGC-funded Hong Kong programmes charge HK$42,100 per year and HKAPA charges the same rate for local students. For families who do not have liquidity for overseas conservatoire tuition, the Hong Kong route is genuinely the only realistic option — and it is not a downgrade. HKU, CUHK, HKBU, and HKAPA all send graduates to top conservatoires for masters and doctoral study, and the senior music faculty at all four institutions includes performers and scholars who teach internationally.

## HKU — Bachelor of Arts (Music major), JUPAS JS6054

HKU does not run a standalone music BA; instead, music is offered as a major (and minor) inside the Faculty of Arts' Bachelor of Arts (JS6054). Students apply to the BA umbrella, take introductory music courses in Year 1, and declare Music as a major by Year 2.

For 2026 JUPAS applicants who want the Music major, the Department of Music holds interviews and auditions in June of the admission year. During the audition, applicants discuss their musical interests, perform on their principal instrument or voice, present compositions, and may show any other music-based projects. Non-local applicants who cannot attend in person should contact the music faculty for video-audition arrangements.

A separate stream worth knowing about is HKU's Music Scholarship Scheme, which guarantees a place in JS6054 to selected applicants who commit to majoring in Music; Music Scholarship Scheme applications close on May 29, 2026 for the 2026 entry. This scheme is how HKU competes for the most musically accomplished Form 6 students — if your principal instrument is at LRSM, ARSM, or international competition level, this is the pathway to investigate first.

The curriculum spans historical musicology, ethnomusicology (with strong Cantonese opera and Chinese instrumental music tracks), composition, music technology, and performance. Most graduates either continue to a masters at HKU, a postgraduate diploma in education (PGDE) to qualify as a music teacher, or pursue conservatoire training overseas. A non-trivial number end up in cultural management, arts journalism, or music PR — the BA umbrella means double majors with English Studies, Comparative Literature, or History are common and very employable.

## CUHK — Bachelor of Arts in Music, JUPAS JS4082

CUHK runs the most "conservatoire-style" of the UGC-funded BA Music programmes. The JUPAS code is JS4082 (a common point of confusion is that older articles cite JS4012, which is incorrect — verify the current JUPAS catalogue for 2026 entry).

The minimum musical requirement is ABRSM or Trinity Grade 8 (or equivalent) on the principal instrument or voice. The audition is multi-component:

- **Video audition**: applicants submit a recorded performance.
- **Written entrance test**: covering harmony, ear training, and music theory; this is more demanding than DSE Music Paper 1.
- **Supporting documents**: letters of recommendation from music teachers or professionals, certificates from graded examinations, a repertoire list covering the last two years of study, and a composition portfolio if available.

Once admitted, students enter a four-year curriculum covering Western music history, Chinese music (the Department maintains a Chinese orchestra and a serious gugu / pipa / erhu performance pathway), composition, music theory and analysis, jazz, early music, and new music. Performance is integrated through ensembles: the CUHK Symphony Orchestra, Wind Orchestra, Choir, Chinese Music Ensemble, Jazz Ensemble, and Early Music Ensemble. CUHK has historically been the strongest of the local universities for ethnomusicology and Chinese music research at the doctoral level.

The graduate outcomes split roughly into thirds: masters/doctoral study overseas (predominantly UK, US, and Germany), school music teaching (after PGDE), and the arts management / music journalism / publishing track. A smaller but visible cohort enters full-time performing careers in the Hong Kong Philharmonic, Hong Kong Sinfonietta, Hong Kong Chinese Orchestra, or freelance orchestral playing.

## HKBU — Bachelor of Arts (Hons) / Bachelor of Music (Hons) (Music / Creative Industries), JUPAS JS2060

HKBU's Academy of Music runs the most flexible programme: under a single JUPAS code (JS2060), students enter and then declare a concentration in Year 2 from four options:

1. **Composition** — original creation, orchestration, and contemporary techniques.
2. **Directed Studies** — research-track musicology, theory, ethnomusicology, and music education studies.
3. **Music Education** — pedagogy and methods for school music teaching (NB: this does not by itself qualify graduates as registered teachers; a PGDE is still required).
4. **Performance** — instrumental or vocal performance major.

A distinctive HKBU feature is a strong music technology and recording emphasis across all concentrations. The Academy maintains a substantial array of electronic instruments, MIDI workstations, and recording facilities — students leave fluent in DAWs (Logic Pro, Pro Tools, Ableton), notation software, and basic studio engineering, which feeds directly into Hong Kong's commercial music and post-production industries.

Admission requires a portfolio submission and an audition, interview, or examination — the exact format depends on the concentration the applicant indicates. Music qualifications are evaluated by the Academy itself rather than purely by DSE Music marks, which means strong external achievement (ABRSM diplomas, international competitions, original composition portfolios) can compensate for not having taken DSE Music as an elective.

Graduates work across the same channels as CUHK and HKU alumni, with a noticeably stronger pipeline into the creative industries — film and TV scoring, Cantopop production, music supervision, and tech-enabled music startups.

## HKAPA — School of Music (non-JUPAS, audition-based admission)

The Hong Kong Academy for Performing Arts is structurally different from the universities above. It is not a UGC-funded research university — it is a dedicated conservatoire under its own ordinance, modelled on institutions like the Royal Academy of Music or Juilliard. **HKAPA does not participate in JUPAS.** Applications go through HKAPA's own Online Application System, with the 2026 admission window running 8 September 2025 to 8 December 2025.

Admission is determined primarily by audition. Selected applicants are invited to a written test, an interview, and a final audition at the Academy in Wan Chai. For instrumentalists, the audition expects contrasting works of the applicant's own choice, with scales, arpeggios, and sight-reading possible. Vocalists must submit a video of contrasting classical repertoire and bring their own competent accompanist for the live audition — this is non-trivial and applicants should plan accompanist rehearsals weeks in advance.

The School of Music's specialisations include Western Classical Music (all orchestral instruments, piano, voice, conducting), Chinese Music (full erhu, pipa, dizi, sheng, suona, ruan, yangqin, guzheng, and percussion lineup), Composition and Electronic Music, and Music, Culture and Industry (the music business and arts management track). HKAPA's Bachelor of Music is four years, with performance students preparing extensive recital programmes and Chinese music students rotating through ensembles that perform widely.

The HKAPA pathway is the right choice if performance is the primary career intention rather than scholarship or teaching. Graduates feed disproportionately into professional orchestras (HK Phil, Sinfonietta, HKCO, Macau Orchestra, mainland and Taiwanese orchestras), conservatoire postgraduate study, and freelance performing careers. A growing arts management and creative industries cohort emerges from the Music, Culture and Industry stream.

## EdUHK — Music with Teacher Training (JS8801, JS8001, JS8636, JS8685)

For DSE students who already know they want to be school music teachers, EdUHK is structurally the most efficient pathway because the programmes are co-terminal: students earn both a music-content degree and a Bachelor of Education in five years, with EDB teacher registration upon graduation (no separate PGDE required).

Four music-related JUPAS codes are active for 2026 entry:

- **JS8801**: BA(Hons) in Creative Arts and Culture and BEd(Hons) (Music) — the flagship five-year co-terminal double degree. Strong general music + teaching track.
- **JS8001**: BA(Hons) in Creative and Digital Arts and BEd(Hons) (Music) — cross-sectoral double degree integrating Western and non-Western music (including Chinese music), digital arts practice, and STEAM education in music. Best for students who want school music teaching combined with digital media and creative-tech literacy.
- **JS8636**: BA(Hons) in Creative Arts and Culture (Music) — standalone four-year BA without the BEd.
- **JS8685**: BA(Hons) in Creative and Digital Arts (Music) — standalone four-year BA without the BEd.

Audition expectations: a satisfactory performance in an audition is required, except that applicants who obtain Level 4 or above in DSE Music are exempted from the audition — a notable concession that effectively rewards students for taking DSE Music as a Category A elective.

Graduates from the double-degree streams (JS8801, JS8001) enter Hong Kong primary and secondary schools as registered music teachers, with starting salaries on the EDB graduate teacher pay scale. Aided-school music teaching is a competitive but stable career — the master pay scale graduate point begins at roughly HK$32,000 monthly and rises substantially with experience and promotion. Career data on music teachers across the broader Hong Kong market shows averages of HK$398,440 annual gross, with senior music teachers (8+ years) averaging HK$489,491.

## Audition preparation: what panels actually look for

The single biggest predictor of admission across all four institutions is not DSE academic profile but audition quality. Practical preparation should start at least 12 months before the audition window:

- **Repertoire**: Two to four contrasting pieces, covering Baroque, Classical/Romantic, and 20th-/21st-century or Chinese music as relevant to your instrument. Panels want to hear stylistic range and technical command, not three Romantic warhorses.
- **Sight-reading and ear training**: All four institutions test these; weekly drilling with a tutor for the final six months pays disproportionate returns.
- **Theory and harmony**: CUHK, HKU, and HKAPA written tests assume comfort beyond DSE Music Paper 1 — ABRSM Grade 6 or 8 Theory is a reasonable baseline.
- **Composition portfolio**: For composition applicants, three to five original works in different genres, neatly notated in Sibelius / Dorico / MuseScore, with audio recordings (live or MIDI) for each.
- **Interview**: Be ready to articulate why you want to study music academically rather than just continue private lessons — panels distinguish strongly between "good young player" and "future scholar / professional musician".

## Career pathways for Hong Kong music graduates

Music bachelor graduates in Hong Kong cluster into five main career tracks:

1. **School music teaching** — the largest single destination, requires PGDE for non-EdUHK graduates. Stable EDB pay scale career.
2. **Performance** — orchestral playing (HK Phil, HKCO, HK Sinfonietta), chamber music, opera (Opera Hong Kong), freelance ensemble work, and Chinese opera (Cantonese opera with the new Sunbeam Theatre cohort).
3. **Composition and music production** — film and TV scoring (Hong Kong has a steady commercial scoring market), Cantopop production, video game scoring, advertising music, and concert composition.
4. **Arts administration and cultural management** — West Kowloon Cultural District (the Hong Kong Palace Museum, M+, Xiqu Centre), Leisure and Cultural Services Department (LCSD), Hong Kong Arts Festival, and the orchestral administrative offices. Many CUHK and HKU music graduates move into these roles within five years.
5. **Music technology, recording engineering, and creative industries** — disproportionately HKBU and HKAPA Music, Culture and Industry graduates. Roles include audio engineer, mixing engineer, sound designer for theatre, music supervisor, and music-tech product roles.

Postgraduate study overseas is also extremely common: roughly 30–40 percent of music bachelor graduates pursue a masters within three years, predominantly at UK conservatoires (Royal Academy, Royal College, Trinity Laban, Royal Northern), US conservatoires (Juilliard, NEC, Manhattan School, Eastman), and German Hochschulen.

## Which programme should you choose?

A short heuristic:

- **You want a conservatoire-style performance career** → HKAPA School of Music (non-JUPAS audition).
- **You want academic depth, ethnomusicology, or research postgraduate ambitions** → CUHK JS4082.
- **You want flexibility, double major options, or a BA umbrella** → HKU JS6054 (Music major, possibly via the Music Scholarship Scheme).
- **You want music technology, creative industries, or Cantopop production** → HKBU JS2060.
- **You want to be a registered school music teacher with the shortest pathway** → EdUHK JS8801 or JS8001.

Whichever route you choose, the audition is the gatekeeper. DSE results matter, but they are typically the second filter, not the first. Practice plans, repertoire choices, and theory drilling for the audition months should begin no later than Form 5 — and if you are serious about HKAPA or HKU's Music Scholarship Scheme, ideally earlier than that.
