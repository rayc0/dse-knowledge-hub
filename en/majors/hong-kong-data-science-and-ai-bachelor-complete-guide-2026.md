# Hong Kong Data Science & Artificial Intelligence Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

Artificial intelligence has moved from a research niche to the single fastest-growing hiring category in Hong Kong's economy. Banks, insurers, logistics groups, the Hong Kong Monetary Authority, the Hospital Authority and a wave of Greater Bay Area technology firms are all competing for graduates who can build models, engineer data pipelines and deploy AI responsibly. The eight UGC-funded universities have responded by launching or rebranding more than a dozen dedicated undergraduate programmes in data science and AI. This guide explains every major route for 2026 entry — what each programme actually teaches, indicative JUPAS admission scores, requirements for Mainland and international applicants, and where graduates end up.

## Why Data Science & AI Now

Three forces make this the standout major of the 2026 cycle. First, demand: Hong Kong's "I&T" (Innovation and Technology) blueprint, the InnoHK research clusters and the Northern Metropolis tech corridor have created structural, not cyclical, hiring. Second, salary: data science and machine-learning roles consistently sit in the top tier of fresh-graduate pay, alongside medicine, law and quantitative finance. Third, optionality: the skill set — Python, statistics, optimisation, large language models — transfers cleanly into finance, biotech, government and consulting, so a DS/AI degree rarely locks you into a single industry.

The trade-off is intensity. These are mathematics-heavy degrees. If you dislike calculus, linear algebra and probability, the marketing gloss of "AI" will not carry you through the second year. The strongest applicants typically take **Mathematics Extended Module M1 or M2** in the HKDSE, and Mainland applicants should expect a strong Gaokao mathematics score.

## The University of Hong Kong (HKU)

HKU offers the broadest menu, and choosing between its three routes matters more than the university brand.

- **JS6298 — BASc & BEng in Applied Computing and Artificial Intelligence / AI&DS double degree.** HKU's flagship interdisciplinary route pairs rigorous engineering and computer-science training with humanities and ethics. Some configurations run as a five-year double degree; note that for local students the fifth year is on a self-financing basis, so the final-year fee is higher than the standard UGC tuition. This is the most prestigious and most competitive AI entry point at HKU.
- **JS6999 — Bachelor of Engineering in Computing and Data Science.** A broad-based engineering admission that lets students stream into data science, AI or software engineering after a common first year. Best for applicants who want flexibility and are not yet certain whether they prefer the data-engineering or the modelling side.
- **JS6224 — Bachelor of Arts and Sciences in Applied Artificial Intelligence (BASc AAI).** A liberal-arts-flavoured AI degree aimed at students who want to apply AI to a domain (business, social science, design) rather than build core algorithms. Lower mathematics load than JS6298, but still quantitative.

**Indicative JUPAS scores:** the AI&DS double degree (JS6298) typically sits among HKU's most selective engineering programmes — broadly comparable to top computer science admissions, often in the high-30s on a best-five basis with mathematics and a science elective weighted. JS6999 and JS6224 tend to admit a point or two below the flagship. Treat all figures as indicative only: HKU re-weights subjects each year and published "admission scores" are not comparable across programmes or cohorts.

## The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK)

CUHK's signature programme is **JS4468 — BEng in Artificial Intelligence: Systems and Technologies (AIST)**, run by the Department of Computer Science and Engineering. AIST is deliberately foundations-first: heavy mathematics, data structures, statistics, algorithms and distributed computing before students build production AI systems. Graduates are positioned for AI engineering and research rather than light "AI-adjacent" analytics roles.

CUHK also offers adjacent quantitative routes worth shortlisting:

- **BSc in Data Science** and the data-science track within Statistics — strong for students leaning toward the statistical and inference side of the field.
- **BSc in Mathematics and Information Engineering (MIE)** — a long-established hybrid that doubles as a serious AI feeder.

AIST admission scores are competitive with CUHK's top engineering and business-analytics offers. Applicants with M1/M2 and Physics or ICT have an edge because of subject weighting.

## The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST)

HKUST historically dominates engineering admissions and has two principal routes:

- **BSc in Data Science and Technology (DSCT)** — jointly delivered across the Schools of Science and Engineering, blending statistics, computer science and machine learning. One of the most established standalone data-science degrees in the territory.
- **Engineering with an Extended Major in Artificial Intelligence (BEng, Hons)** — students enter through broad engineering admission and then add the AI extended major, graduating with a recognised AI specialisation on top of a home engineering discipline.

HKUST also runs a dual-degree in Technology and Management and a quantitative "Decision Analytics" stream for students who want AI plus business. HKUST applies programme-specific score formulae that weight mathematics and science heavily; its School of Science publishes an online score calculator each cycle, which serious applicants should use rather than relying on generic best-five estimates.

## PolyU, CityU and the Specialist Routes

Beyond the traditional "top three", several programmes are excellent value and often less brutally competitive:

- **PolyU — BSc in Artificial Intelligence and Data Analytics (AIDA).** Application-oriented, with strong industry placement and PolyU's signature work-integrated learning. Good fit for students who prefer building deployable systems over theory.
- **CityU — Bachelor of Artificial Intelligence, and BSc in Data Science.** CityU's data-science school is research-active in AI and offers clear streaming. CityU's "Discovery-enriched" curriculum and gateway-major admission give students a year to confirm fit before committing.
- **Hong Kong Metropolitan University (HKMU) — JSSU70 BSc (Hons) in Data Science and Artificial Intelligence.** A self-financing route with Year 1 entry via JUPAS and senior-year entry via direct application — a realistic pathway for sub-degree holders and associate-degree articulants who want an AI qualification.

Lingnan, HKBU (which runs AI-in-business and data-analytics offerings) and EdUHK (educational data science) round out the field for applicants whose interests sit at the intersection of AI and a specific domain.

## Admission Requirements at a Glance

**HKDSE (JUPAS / local).** All programmes require the "3-3-2-2" core minimum (English 3, Chinese 3, Mathematics 2, Citizenship & Social Development Attained). In practice, competitive AI/DS applicants present Mathematics at level 5 or above, an M1/M2 module, and a quantitative elective such as Physics, ICT, Economics or Chemistry. Universities weight Mathematics and the M1/M2 module upward in their score formulae — this is the single biggest lever you control.

**Mainland Gaokao applicants.** All eight universities admit Mainland students directly via their independent Gaokao admission schemes (not through JUPAS). Expect required scores well above the first-tier line, strong English (often gaokao English plus an interview), and excellent mathematics. HKU, CUHK and HKUST AI/DS programmes are among the most competitive Mainland admissions of all; budget for around HKD 145,000–185,000 per year in tuition plus living costs.

**International / non-local applicants.** Accepted credentials include IB (typically 38–42+ for the flagship programmes), GCE A-Levels (A*AA–AAA with Mathematics), Advanced Placement and recognised national high-school diplomas, plus English evidence (IELTS 6.5+ or TOEFL iBT 93+). Apply through each university's international admissions portal, not JUPAS.

## What You Actually Study

A typical four-year DS/AI degree follows this arc:

1. **Year 1 — foundations:** calculus, linear algebra, probability and statistics, programming (Python and often C++), discrete mathematics.
2. **Year 2 — core methods:** data structures and algorithms, database systems, statistical inference, introductory machine learning, data visualisation.
3. **Year 3 — specialisation:** deep learning, natural language processing and large language models, computer vision, big-data systems, optimisation, plus AI ethics and governance (now a near-universal compulsory component).
4. **Year 4 — capstone and electives:** a substantial final-year project or industry capstone, an internship, and electives in reinforcement learning, MLOps, quantitative finance or bioinformatics.

Mathematical maturity, not coding speed, is the usual filter for who thrives.

## Internships, Careers and Salaries

Hong Kong's AI graduate market is genuinely strong. Common destinations include:

- **Financial services:** HSBC, Standard Chartered, JPMorgan, the HKMA and the major insurers hire data scientists and ML engineers for fraud detection, risk modelling and algorithmic operations.
- **Technology and platforms:** Tencent, Alibaba, ByteDance and GBA start-ups recruit aggressively, often with Shenzhen-based rotations.
- **Consulting and Big Four:** McKinsey QuantumBlack, BCG X, and Deloitte/PwC/EY/KPMG analytics practices.
- **Public sector and health:** the Hospital Authority, government data labs and InnoHK research centres.

Fresh-graduate monthly salaries for strong DS/AI hires typically start around HKD 22,000–35,000, with quantitative-finance and top-tech offers materially higher. The IANG visa lets non-local graduates stay and work in Hong Kong after graduation, and AI is on every shortage-skills radar — making this one of the safest majors for international students planning to build a career locally or in the Greater Bay Area.

## How to Choose

- Want the most prestigious, research-oriented route and you love mathematics → **HKU JS6298** or **CUHK AIST JS4468**.
- Want flexibility to decide your stream after a common year → **HKU JS6999**, **HKUST broad engineering + AI extended major**, or **CityU gateway admission**.
- Want applied, industry-facing training over theory → **PolyU AIDA** or **HKU JS6224 Applied AI**.
- Articulating from a sub-degree or need a senior-year entry → **HKMU JSSU70**.

Whatever you choose, lock in strong Mathematics and an M1/M2 module early, build a small portfolio (a Kaggle project or a deployed model speaks louder than any personal statement), and use each university's official score calculator and admissions page before finalising your JUPAS band order.
