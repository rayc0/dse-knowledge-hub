# Hong Kong Logistics & Supply Chain Management Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

Hong Kong is one of the most logistics-intensive economies on earth. It runs the world's busiest international air cargo hub at Chek Lap Kok, sits among the top ten container ports globally, and serves as the procurement, financing and trans-shipment gateway for the Greater Bay Area's manufacturing base. That structural reality has kept "supply chain professionals" on the Hong Kong Government's Talent List and made logistics and supply chain management (SCM) one of the few business-adjacent disciplines where graduates can move straight into operations, analytics, freight, aviation or maritime roles without a postgraduate detour.

For 2026 entry, the strongest undergraduate pathways sit at three of the eight UGC-funded universities: PolyU's BBA (Hons) Scheme in Global Business and Logistics, Aviation, Maritime, and Supply Chain Management (JS3070), HKUST's BEng in Industrial Engineering and Engineering Management within the Department of Industrial Engineering and Decision Analytics (JS5260), and CUHK's logistics and decision-analytics tracks inside Systems Engineering and Engineering Management. This guide maps every route — admission scores, curriculum design, professional accreditation and graduate outcomes.

## Why Logistics and Supply Chain Management in Hong Kong

Three factors make Hong Kong distinctive for this discipline.

First, the city is a living case study. Trade, logistics and the four pillar industries account for a large share of GDP, and the SAR functions as the control tower for regional supply chains rather than the factory floor. Students intern and graduate into roles that actually exist locally: ocean and air freight forwarding, third-party logistics (3PL), procurement, demand planning, customs and trade compliance, and the supply-chain functions of luxury, electronics and FMCG multinationals headquartered in Hong Kong.

Second, the field has bifurcated into a *business* stream and an *engineering/analytics* stream. The business stream (PolyU's Faculty of Business, CUHK Business School) emphasises global trade, maritime and aviation management, sourcing and operations strategy. The engineering stream (HKUST IEDA, CUHK Systems Engineering) emphasises optimisation, statistics, machine learning, simulation and operations research. Both lead to supply-chain careers, but they suit different DSE profiles — a point most applicants underestimate when ranking JUPAS choices.

Third, professional accreditation is unusually accessible. Programmes carry recognition from the Chartered Institute of Logistics and Transport in Hong Kong (CILT) and, for the engineering routes, the Hong Kong Institution of Engineers (HKIE), giving graduates a structured route to chartered status.

## PolyU JS3070 — The Flagship Business Route

PolyU's Department of Logistics and Maritime Studies (LMS) is the single best-known undergraduate home for this subject in Hong Kong, and its research in transportation and logistics is consistently rated among the strongest in the world. For 2026, the relevant JUPAS programme is **JS3070 — BBA (Hons) Scheme in Global Business and Logistics, Aviation, Maritime, and Supply Chain Management**.

**Structure.** JS3070 is a *scheme*, not a single locked degree. All admitted students take a Common Year One within the Faculty of Business, then specialise from Year Two into majors such as Global Supply Chain Management, Aviation Management, International Shipping and Transport Logistics, and related business disciplines. After the first year, students may also switch to another Faculty of Business choice through a ranking assessment based on Year One GPA, which gives strong performers internal mobility but means the entry route does not guarantee a specific major.

**Admission.** PolyU's full-time BBA programmes require the University's general entrance requirements plus an overall HKDSE best-five score of 25 or above as a baseline; competitive applicants for the logistics scheme typically present materially higher. The minimum subject profile is Chinese Language and English Language at the required levels, Mathematics Compulsory Part, "Attained" in Citizenship and Social Development, plus electives. Selection uses a weighted best-five (or best-six) score with bonus weighting on English and Mathematics — the LMS scheme is competitive within the Faculty, and selective interviews may apply.

**Intake.** The combined Faculty scheme intake is in the order of 168 places across JUPAS and Non-JUPAS, with JS3070 itself receiving roughly the low-40s in places — small enough that it sits firmly in the Band A territory for serious applicants.

**Non-JUPAS and articulation.** PolyU also runs a senior-year intake — BBA (Hons) in Supply Chain Management and Analytics — designed for Associate Degree and Higher Diploma holders articulating into Year Three, which is a realistic route for sub-degree students and an important fallback for Mainland and international applicants who do not enter via JUPAS.

## HKUST JS5260 — The Engineering and Analytics Route

HKUST's **Department of Industrial Engineering and Decision Analytics (IEDA)** offers the BEng in Industrial Engineering and Engineering Management (IEEM) under JUPAS code **JS5260**. This is the analytics-heavy counterpart to PolyU's business route, and it is the better choice for students who are strong in Mathematics (especially M1/M2) and want quantitative depth.

**Curriculum.** IEEM equips students with predictive and prescriptive analytical tools — statistical models, optimisation, machine-learning algorithms, simulation and operations research — applied to supply chain management, operations planning and scheduling, transportation systems, quality control and technology management. The programme is engineering-accredited: graduates can pursue HKIE chartered engineer status, and the curriculum is recognised by the Chartered Institute of Logistics and Transport, giving a dual professional pathway in both engineering and logistics.

**Admission.** HKUST publishes simulated admission scores derived from prior-year intakes under the current score formula. For the 2026 cycle, the indicative simulated score for the IEDA programme (JS5260) sits in the low-80s on HKUST's weighted scale — broadly mid-range for the School of Engineering, more accessible than computer science or the dual-degree programmes but still requiring a solid best-five with strong Mathematics. HKUST assesses the whole profile, not the raw score alone.

**Broad admission alternative.** Students who are unsure between engineering disciplines can also enter through the School of Engineering's broad-based JUPAS route and select IEDA at the end of Year One, which de-risks the choice for applicants who like the analytics angle but want to compare it against other engineering majors first.

## CUHK — Systems Engineering and Decision Analytics

CUHK does not brand an undergraduate degree as "Logistics and Supply Chain Management," but its **Department of Systems Engineering and Engineering Management (SEEM)** carries a dedicated research and teaching cluster in logistics and supply chain management, embedded within its broader systems-engineering and decision-analytics curriculum. Students gain optimisation, data-analytics and operations-research training directly applicable to supply-chain roles. On the business side, CUHK Business School's Decision Sciences and Managerial Economics offerings also expose students to operations and supply-chain modelling. CUHK is the right pick for applicants who want supply-chain capability inside a more general systems-engineering or quantitative-business degree rather than a named SCM programme.

For completeness: HKU does not run a dedicated undergraduate SCM degree, though operations management appears within HKU Business School's BBA, and HKU SPACE offers a suite of self-financing supply-chain qualifications useful for top-up and conversion students.

## How to Choose Your Route

The decision hinges on your DSE profile and career intent:

- **Strong in Mathematics / M1 / M2, enjoy modelling and data** → HKUST IEDA (JS5260) or CUHK SEEM. You will graduate as a supply-chain *analyst/engineer* with optimisation and machine-learning fluency, well-positioned for tech-driven logistics, e-commerce operations and quantitative planning roles.
- **Stronger in business reasoning, language and commercial subjects (BAFS/Economics)** → PolyU JS3070. You will graduate with global trade, maritime, aviation and sourcing expertise, suited to freight, shipping lines, airlines, trading houses and multinational procurement.
- **Sub-degree holder or non-JUPAS applicant** → PolyU's senior-year SCM and Analytics intake or HKU SPACE top-up programmes are the realistic articulation routes.

A practical JUPAS tactic: because both PolyU's JS3070 and HKUST's engineering route use scheme/broad-based admission with Year-One internal selection, applicants can hedge by ranking the business scheme and the engineering broad route in adjacent Band A slots and deciding the precise major after experiencing the common first year.

## Curriculum Core — What You Actually Study

Across all three universities, the SCM core converges on a recognisable backbone: operations management, procurement and sourcing, inventory and demand planning, transportation and distribution, supply-chain analytics and information systems (ERP/SAP exposure), international trade and logistics law, and increasingly sustainability and supply-chain risk. The engineering routes layer on linear and integer programming, stochastic modelling, simulation and machine learning; the business routes layer on maritime economics, aviation management, global sourcing strategy and trade finance. Capstone projects, industry consultancy modules and credit-bearing internships are standard, and Hong Kong's density of logistics employers makes placements genuinely accessible.

## Professional Accreditation

This is a strong selling point of the Hong Kong route. The Chartered Institute of Logistics and Transport in Hong Kong (CILT) recognises the major programmes, allowing graduates an accelerated path to Chartered Member status. The HKUST engineering route additionally feeds the Hong Kong Institution of Engineers (HKIE) chartered engineer scheme. Some programmes also align with APICS/ASCM (CPIM, CSCP) certification content, which is the global benchmark for supply-chain practitioners and a meaningful CV differentiator for both local and Mainland graduates.

## Graduate Careers and Salaries

Demand is structural rather than cyclical. Typical entry roles include supply-chain analyst, demand/supply planner, procurement officer, logistics coordinator, freight forwarding executive, ocean/air export-import operations, customs and trade compliance, and graduate-scheme placements in 3PL firms (DHL, Kerry, DSV), shipping lines, airlines and the supply-chain functions of FMCG, electronics and luxury multinationals.

On compensation, Hong Kong's overall first-degree graduate average sits around HK$300,000 per year, with engineering and analytics-heavy graduates from the strongest programmes reporting monthly starting figures in the mid-HK$30,000s — comfortably above the cross-discipline median. Supply-chain graduates from PolyU LMS and HKUST IEDA cluster toward the upper half of that range because the skill set is in chronic short supply, and analytics-capable graduates command a premium as logistics digitises. Career progression is fast for those who move into supply-chain consulting, regional planning leadership or trade/freight commercial roles, and the GBA's manufacturing-to-market corridor is expanding the regional employer base year on year.

## Action Checklist for 2026 Applicants

1. **Diagnose your profile.** Mathematics-strong and modelling-inclined → engineering/analytics route (HKUST IEDA / CUHK SEEM). Business and language-strong → PolyU JS3070.
2. **Verify 2026 figures on official pages.** Admission scores, intake numbers and subject weightings are updated annually — confirm against PolyU LMS, HKUST IEDA and CUHK SEEM admissions pages before locking your JUPAS rank order.
3. **Use scheme admission to hedge.** Rank PolyU's JS3070 scheme and HKUST's broad engineering route in adjacent Band A slots; commit to the specific major after Year One.
4. **Plan the non-JUPAS fallback early.** Associate Degree and Higher Diploma holders should target PolyU's senior-year SCM and Analytics intake or HKU SPACE top-up routes.
5. **Bank an accreditation.** Map your electives toward CILT/HKIE eligibility and consider APICS/ASCM (CPIM) study before graduation to differentiate in the hiring market.

Logistics and supply chain management is one of the highest signal-to-noise undergraduate choices in Hong Kong: a discipline with structural local demand, professional accreditation, fast progression and a city that is, quite literally, the working laboratory for the subject.
