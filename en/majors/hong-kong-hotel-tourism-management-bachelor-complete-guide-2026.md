# Hong Kong Hotel and Tourism Management Bachelor's Programmes Complete Guide 2026

Hong Kong is one of the world's densest hospitality markets — five-star hotel towers in Tsim Sha Tsui, the Greater Bay Area MICE corridor, two theme-park resorts and a tourism economy that anchors a meaningful slice of GDP. That density has produced two hospitality schools that consistently rank among the global elite: **PolyU's School of Hotel and Tourism Management (SHTM)** and **CUHK's Department of Hotel and Tourism Management** (now expanded into the School of Hotel and Real Estate). For DSE candidates and non-JUPAS applicants who want a hospitality, tourism or events career — or a quantitative real-estate route built on top of hotel operations — these two programmes are the centre of gravity.

This guide compares both schemes side by side, walks through the JUPAS codes and DSE thresholds for 2026 entry, explains the specialisation tracks (Hotel Management, Smart Tourism and Hospitality, Event and Experience Management, Hospitality and Real Estate), and lays out tuition, internship infrastructure and graduate destinations.

## Why Hong Kong is a global capital for hospitality education

Two structural facts shape this discipline in Hong Kong:

- **PolyU SHTM is ranked 1st in Hong Kong and 15th globally for "Hospitality and Leisure Management" in the QS World University Rankings by Subject 2026.** Among comprehensive universities, SHTM is positioned at the very top of Asia.
- **CUHK's Hotel and Tourism Management programme is ranked 2nd in Asia and among the top globally** in the same subject area, and the school has now broadened its scope into hospitality + real estate, reflecting how property and hospitality have fused at the management level.

Beyond rankings, both schools own the supporting infrastructure that hospitality education needs — teaching hotels, industry-funded scholarships, mandatory paid internships, and direct lines into Hong Kong's hotel groups (Mandarin Oriental, Shangri-La, Marriott, Hyatt, IHG), Disney, Hong Kong Tourism Board, and Greater Bay Area integrated resorts.

## PolyU SHTM — JS3310 BSc (Hons) Scheme in Hotel and Tourism Management

PolyU's SHTM is the single most-applied hospitality programme in Hong Kong. From 2026 entry it is offered through one JUPAS scheme code with three specialisation paths.

### JUPAS code, structure and specialisations

- **JUPAS code:** JS3310
- **Award:** BSc (Hons) Scheme in Hotel and Tourism Management
- **Duration:** 4 years
- **Specialisations:**
  1. Hotel Management
  2. Smart Tourism and Hospitality
  3. Event and Experience Management

Students are admitted into the broad scheme, take a common Year 1 covering hospitality operations, tourism economics, marketing, finance and IT, then declare a specialisation from Year 2. The "Smart Tourism and Hospitality" stream is the newest of the three and reflects the industry's shift toward data analytics, revenue management software, and AI-assisted guest services.

### DSE entry requirements

PolyU has set the scheme up to be flexible on subject choice — the goal is to widen the applicant pool to students from arts, business or science streams.

- **No compulsory subject requirement.** Performance in "preferred subjects" (e.g. BAFS, Economics, Geography, Tourism and Hospitality Studies, ICT) earns weighting in selection but is not a hard gate.
- **University minimum:** 4 core subjects + 2 electives reaching the standard JUPAS gate.
- **Fast-track admission:** for JUPAS local applicants, **at least Level 5\*\* in 2 subjects and a Best 5 score of 26 or above** triggers a fast-track offer with an entrance scholarship, typically released in early Round.
- **Median Best 5 score for JS3310 admissions** has historically clustered in the **22–25** range — competitive but reachable for strong Band A students.

Non-JUPAS applicants (Mainland Gaokao, IB, A-Level, AP, US/Canadian high-school) apply through PolyU's direct admissions portal under code 24446-SY. Mainland Gaokao applicants typically need to clear their province's First-Tier (一本) line plus an English subscore around 120/150.

### Tuition and scholarships

- **HKDSE / local applicants (UGC-funded seat):** **HKD 44,500 per year** for 2026/27.
- **Non-local applicants:** **HKD 175,000 per year** for 2026/27.
- **Entrance scholarships:** SHTM grants automatic entry scholarships to high-scoring JUPAS admits; named scholarships from Mandarin Oriental, Shangri-La, Hong Kong Disneyland and the Cathay Group cover tuition + stipend for top admits.

### Hotel ICON, ICON in the SKY and the SHTM building

The single biggest differentiator at PolyU is **Hotel ICON** — a 262-room four-star hotel in Tsim Sha Tsui East, owned and operated by the university as a teaching hotel. Every SHTM undergraduate completes structured rotations there as part of Work-Integrated Education (WIE). The **Elite Management Programme** is a flagship management trainee track that selects high-performing students for accelerated rotations across Hotel ICON's front office, F&B, housekeeping and revenue management functions, leading directly into supervisory offers at PolyU partner hotels on graduation.

The new **ICON in the SKY** facility on top of the SHTM building adds a second teaching hotel layer focused on luxury service training. Combined with the dedicated SHTM building (the only purpose-built hospitality school building among Hong Kong universities), this gives SHTM a level of operational integration with industry no other Hong Kong school can match.

### Internship and exchange

- **Mandatory internship:** approximately **800 hours** (a full semester or summer block) in industry, often paid.
- **Global placements:** a quarter or more of each cohort completes internships overseas — Disney World Florida (Cultural Representative Programme), Switzerland (École hôtelière de Lausanne exchange), Marriott graduate trainee tracks in Singapore, IHG management trainees in mainland China.
- **Exchange semesters:** SHTM has 70+ partner schools, including Cornell, Lausanne, Bocconi, NUS, KAIST.

## CUHK Hospitality and Real Estate — JS4226

CUHK's response to the changing industry has been to fuse hotel and real-estate management. From entry 2024, the programme rebranded from "Hotel and Tourism Management" to **"Hospitality and Real Estate (HRE)"**, recognising that the senior management of major hotel groups in Asia now sits inside REIT-style property structures, and graduates who can underwrite a hotel deal as well as run rooms and F&B are in short supply.

### JUPAS code, structure and specialisations

- **JUPAS code:** JS4226
- **Award:** Bachelor of Business Administration in Hospitality and Real Estate
- **Duration:** 4 years
- **Streams:** Hospitality Management; Real Estate (corporate real estate, asset management, REIT analysis); a combined track is available from Year 3.

CUHK admits HRE students directly into a Business School curriculum — this means BBA-level finance, accounting, marketing, organisational behaviour and strategy are core, alongside hospitality-specific courses. The mathematical demands are higher than PolyU's BSc scheme.

### DSE entry requirements

- **Core minimum (programme-specific):** Chinese **Level 3**, English **Level 3**, Maths **Level 2**, Citizenship and Social Development **"Attained"** (replacing the old Liberal Studies Level 2).
- **Two electives at Level 3 or above** required.
- **Best 5 calculation** with bonus weighting on extra electives. Historical median Best 5 for HRE admits sits in the **26–30** range, a noticeable step above PolyU JS3310.
- **Interview:** shortlisted candidates are called for a **group interview** with faculty + industry executives — common in CUHK Business School admissions.
- **Maths preferred:** while not formally compulsory beyond Level 2, candidates with M1/M2 (Extended Modules) and Level 5 in core Maths are heavily favoured because of the real-estate finance content.

Non-JUPAS pathways: Mainland Gaokao admission to CUHK's Business School is one of the most competitive in Hong Kong — typical admits are well above the province's First-Tier line and feature English Gaokao scores at 130/150 or above.

### Tuition

- **Local students:** **HKD 44,500 per year** (UGC-funded).
- **Non-local students:** **HKD 165,000 per year** for 2026/27 (CUHK's standard non-local rate; subject to annual revision).

### Resources, internship and exchange

- CUHK Business School operates internships through **Career and Placement Office (CPO)** and partner hotel groups, including Mandarin Oriental, Peninsula, and the Hong Kong Tourism Board.
- HRE has structured exchange agreements with **Cornell SHA, ESSEC, NUS and Bocconi**.
- Real-estate-focused electives connect to **CUHK's Centre for Real Estate and Urban Economics** and to industry partners across REIT analysts and developers (Sun Hung Kai, Henderson, Swire).
- No equivalent to Hotel ICON — operational training is delivered through partner hotels rather than a university-owned hotel.

## Programme comparison: PolyU SHTM vs CUHK HRE

| Factor | PolyU SHTM (JS3310) | CUHK HRE (JS4226) |

A one-line summary: **choose SHTM if you want to run a hotel; choose HRE if you want to own one.**

## Other Hong Kong pathways into hospitality

Three other routes are worth knowing about:

- **HKBU School of Business — BBA in International Hospitality Management:** smaller cohort, strong on luxury service and Mandarin-Chinese-language training; lower JUPAS Best 5 (around 21–24).
- **HKMU (Hong Kong Metropolitan University) BBA Hons in Hospitality and Tourism Management** under the SSSDP subsidy scheme — a lower-cost self-financing route with subsidised tuition; suited to students whose Best 5 score is 17–20.
- **PolyU SHTM Higher Diploma → top-up degree:** for sub-degree students, SHTM's HD in Hospitality Management offers an articulation route into JS3310 Year 2 (subject to GPA), making it the strongest associate-degree → bachelor pathway in this subject.

For Mainland-route students who can't take JUPAS, both PolyU SHTM and CUHK HRE accept Mainland Gaokao scores; **PolyU's Mainland Gaokao route to JS3310** is among the more accessible of Hong Kong's UGC-funded hospitality places, while CUHK HRE remains highly selective.

## What you actually study

Despite the BSc / BBA distinction, the curriculum overlap is significant. Expect to take:

- **Operations:** Front office, housekeeping, F&B operations, kitchen tours, beverage management.
- **Tourism economics and consumer behaviour:** Demand modelling, destination marketing, tourism geography.
- **Hospitality finance:** Revenue management, hotel asset valuation, USALI accounting (the industry's uniform reporting standard).
- **Marketing and digital:** OTA dynamics, channel management, social-media-driven destination marketing.
- **Smart tourism and analytics (PolyU specialism):** PMS data, sentiment analysis, AI guest services, smart-city tourism platforms.
- **Real estate (CUHK specialism):** Real-estate finance, REIT structures, property law, asset management.
- **Events / MICE (PolyU specialism):** Event design, sponsorship, mega-event management (Olympics-level case studies).
- **Capstone / industry project:** Each programme ends with an industry-paired final-year project — common topics include hotel concept design, market entry studies for new brands, and post-pandemic recovery analytics for destinations.

## Career outcomes — where do graduates go?

Hong Kong hospitality bachelor's graduates fall into four main destinations:

1. **Hotel management trainee programmes:** Marriott "Voyage", Hyatt LDP, IHG iGrad, Mandarin Oriental, Peninsula, Shangri-La. Starting salary in Hong Kong typically ranges from **HKD 18,000–24,000 per month** for management trainees, climbing into the high HKD 30,000s by year three for high performers.
2. **Theme parks and integrated resorts:** Hong Kong Disneyland, Ocean Park, and (increasingly) Macau and Hengqin integrated resorts are large recruiters — Disneyland's Cast Member and management-trainee channels are well-established at SHTM.
3. **Adjacent industries:** Airlines (Cathay Pacific, Cathay Cargo), tourism boards (HKTB), luxury retail (LVMH, Richemont), consulting (HVS, JLL Hotels & Hospitality), banking client-facing roles. CUHK HRE graduates increasingly head into REIT analyst seats and hotel-asset M&A teams.
4. **Postgraduate study:** Cornell SHA, École hôtelière de Lausanne, ESSEC, NUS, and the SHTM master's programmes themselves.

PolyU SHTM consistently reports a graduate employment rate above 95% within six months of graduation, with the bulk in hospitality, tourism, retail and aviation. CUHK Business School graduates achieve broadly similar employment rates with a higher proportion entering finance-adjacent roles.

## How to choose: a decision checklist

Use these questions to decide which programme to put higher in your JUPAS Band A:

- **Do you want hands-on hotel operations exposure as part of the degree?** PolyU's Hotel ICON is a decisive advantage.
- **Are you stronger at maths and finance than at services / people skills?** CUHK HRE's real-estate stream rewards quantitative students; SHTM's Smart Tourism stream is the equivalent quantitative track at PolyU.
- **Is your DSE Best 5 in the 22–25 range?** PolyU SHTM is the realistic Band A choice.
- **Is your DSE Best 5 in the 27+ range, and you want a BBA brand on your CV?** CUHK HRE is the stronger fit.
- **Do you want a global theme-park or events career?** PolyU SHTM Event and Experience Management stream is unmatched in Hong Kong.
- **Are you targeting hotel-asset finance, REIT analysis or hospitality M&A?** CUHK HRE.
- **Are you a non-JUPAS applicant from the Mainland with a high Gaokao score and English subscore?** Both schools accept you, but expect interviews in both cases.

## Common mistakes to avoid

- **Treating it as "easy business":** Hospitality is operationally dense and has unsocial hours during internships. Burnout among students who chose the major because they thought it would be light is a real attrition pattern.
- **Skipping the Tourism and Hospitality Studies (THS) DSE elective:** It is not compulsory at PolyU but is a strong signal in admissions, especially for Event and Experience Management.
- **Underestimating the maths content at CUHK HRE:** Real-estate finance modules use IRR, NPV, DCF and stochastic property pricing models. Students without M1/M2 sometimes struggle.
- **Ignoring language preparation:** Mandarin and a third Asian language (Japanese, Korean) materially improve internship and graduate offers in luxury hospitality.

## Final word

If you want to enter Asia's hospitality industry from a top-tier programme, Hong Kong's two flagship schools — **PolyU SHTM** and **CUHK HRE** — sit at the global frontier and are accessible through both JUPAS and non-JUPAS routes. Pick PolyU SHTM if your career instinct is operational and customer-facing; pick CUHK HRE if it is financial and asset-driven. Either way, prepare for a degree that is half classroom, half hotel — and a graduate market that, post-pandemic, is hiring aggressively again.
