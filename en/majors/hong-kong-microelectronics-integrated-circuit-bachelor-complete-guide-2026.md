# Hong Kong Microelectronics and Integrated Circuit Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

In 2024 the Hong Kong SAR Government formally established the **Hong Kong Microelectronics Research and Development Institute (MRDI)**, signalling that — after FinTech and BioTech — **third-generation semiconductors** are now the city's next strategic emerging industry. Combined with Phase II of InnoHK, the Lok Ma Chau Loop / Hetao Shenzhen-Hong Kong cross-boundary co-operation zone, and the "new industrialisation" policy, all three local engineering faculties have launched dedicated Microelectronics / IC undergraduate majors. This is the single biggest structural change to Hong Kong engineering curricula in a decade. This guide focuses on the 2026 JUPAS entry into Microelectronics and Integrated Circuits (MEIC) at HKUST, CityU, CUHK and PolyU — comparing curriculum positioning, 2025 JUPAS admission scores, cleanroom tape-out resources, and graduate destinations — so HKDSE and Gaokao candidates can decide whether this track is right for them.

## 1. Why 2026 is the Most Important Year to Enter Microelectronics

- **Policy tailwind**: the January 2024 Policy Address listed "microelectronics" as one of four primary technology industries. MRDI is building an 8-inch third-generation semiconductor pilot line in the Hetao zone with Hong Kong Science Park, with SiC and GaN power-device pilot production starting from end-2025.
- **Greater Bay Area extension**: SMIC's 28 nm Shenzhen fab, Hua Hong's 12-inch Wuxi line and GTA Semiconductor's Guangzhou Huangpu site all recruit Hong Kong-trained IC engineers. Hong Kong undergraduates qualify for the Qianhai Young Talents Internship Scheme with subsidised housing.
- **Local champions are hiring**: HKEX-listed **ASMPT (522.HK)** is the world's leading advanced packaging-equipment vendor and runs steady-state graduate Application Engineer hiring in Hong Kong. HiSilicon, Apple HK, Nvidia HK, AMD, Intel and TSMC's Hong Kong office have all expanded local IC design and verification teams.
- **Curriculum maturity**: HKUST formally elevated MEIC to a stand-alone BEng major in 2023; CityU set up the **BEng Microelectronics Engineering** major inside JS1205 Electrical Engineering in 2024; CUHK has reinforced VLSI tracks across Electronic Engineering and Computer Engineering.

## 2. The Four Undergraduate Entry Points

### 2.1 HKUST — BEng in Microelectronics and Integrated Circuits (MEIC)

- **JUPAS code**: JS5250 (admitted through the Department of Electronic and Computer Engineering), or via the JS5200 School of Engineering broad-based entry with major declaration in Year 2.
- **2025 JUPAS admission profile**: highest simulated score 80.33, median 44, lower quartile 42 under the new score formula. Internal selection into MEIC is expected to be competitive owing to limited quota.
- **Positioning**: the **only BEng in Hong Kong named "Microelectronics and Integrated Circuits"**, emphasising the full flow from cleanroom wafer fabrication and IC layout through to **tape-out**.
- **Core courses**:
  - ELEC 2400 Electronic Circuits
  - ELEC 3100 Signals and Systems
  - ELEC 3200 Semiconductor Devices
  - ELEC 4130 Analog IC Design
  - ELEC 4140 Digital VLSI Design
  - ELEC 4150 RF IC Design
  - ELEC 4900 / 4910 Capstone Design Project (tape-out mini-project)
- **Signature resources**: the Clear Water Bay campus houses the **Nanosystem Fabrication Facility (NFF)** and the Materials Characterization and Preparation Facility (MCPF), supporting a full 6-inch wafer process. Students can complete a 65 nm CMOS analog circuit tape-out through TSMC University Shuttle in the ELEC 4860 elective.
- **Recommended HKDSE profile**: English 5 + Chinese 4 + Mathematics 5 + M2 5\* + Physics 5\* + Chemistry 5\* — comfortably clears the median band.

### 2.2 CityU — BEng Microelectronics Engineering (within JS1205)

- **JUPAS code**: JS1205 Department of Electrical Engineering, which umbrellas four BEng majors (Computer & Data Engineering, Electronic & Electrical, Information, **Microelectronics**). Students declare the major at the end of Semester B in Year 1.
- **2025 JUPAS admission scores**: 39 (first list) and 37 (second list) under the best-5 formula — English / Mathematics / ICT / M1 / M2 / Physics weighted 2.0; Biology / Chemistry weighted 1.5.
- **Major selection**: the **top 40 % of cohort** by GPA freely choose their major; the remaining 60 % are allocated subject to availability — so a strong Year-1 GPA is essential if Microelectronics is the target.
- **Indicative intake**: 163 local seats at the JS1205 department level.
- **Core courses**:
  - EE 3008 Semiconductor Materials and Devices
  - EE 3110 Analog Electronic Circuit Design
  - EE 4109 VLSI Design
  - EE 4140 Microelectronics Fabrication Technology
  - EE 4900 Final Year Project (jointly supervised with ASTRI)
- **Accreditation**: the Microelectronics Engineering curriculum is on track for HKIE accreditation, which means Washington Accord recognition in Australia, Canada, Mainland China and others.
- **Why pick CityU over HKUST**: roughly the same VLSI and fab content with a lower admissions bar — a sensible Band A choice for candidates locked at 5 in Maths / Physics rather than 5\*.

### 2.3 CUHK — VLSI Streams within Electronic Engineering and Computer Engineering

- **JUPAS codes**: **JS4434 Electronic Engineering (ELE)** and **JS4407 Computer Engineering (CENG)**.
- **2025 JUPAS bands**: ELE median 24–26; CENG slightly higher at 25–27 (UE Best-5 formula).
- **Positioning**: CUHK does not run a stand-alone "Microelectronics" name on its degree certificate, but its VLSI Laboratory has historically been Hong Kong's strongest in **digital VLSI**, **CAD / EDA** and **memory design**. Graduates feed steadily into TSMC HK, Samsung Austin and Nvidia.
- **Key courses**: ENGG 3070 / 3210 Circuits and Systems; ELEG 3110 Signals and Systems; ELEG 4701 Integrated Circuits; ELEG 4702 Analogue IC Design; CENG 3420 Computer Organisation; CENG 4480 Embedded System Design.
- **Research springboard**: the strongest performers can join a Joint Bachelor-Master programme with CUHK Shenzhen's School of Science and Engineering, with a co-supervised project on TSMC N3 / N5 testchips.

### 2.4 PolyU — BEng (Hons) Electronic and Information Engineering (EIE), Microelectronics Specialism

- **JUPAS code**: JS3868.
- **2025 admission band**: roughly Best-5 score 22–24.
- **Positioning**: PolyU's EIE department offers a **Microelectronics specialism** in the final two years, oriented towards mixed-signal IC design and IoT chipsets. Its strength is the close link to ASMPT (back-end packaging equipment) and ASTRI — students routinely complete 6-month industry placements via the Work-Integrated Education (WIE) scheme.
- **Distinctive course**: EIE4434 Integrated Circuit Design and Manufacture combines layout / DRC training with a real foundry shuttle.

## 4. Cleanroom and Tape-Out — the Three Capabilities You Must Build

1. **Layout and DRC sign-off**: master Cadence Virtuoso and Mentor Calibre. Most local courses ship with TSMC 28 nm or 65 nm PDKs.
2. **Wafer process literacy**: complete at least one cleanroom lab module — photolithography, etching, ion implantation, CMP. The NFF (HKUST) and University Research Facility in Nanotechnology (PolyU) are the most accessible.
3. **Tape-out and post-silicon validation**: an FYP with **TSMC University Shuttle** or **MOSIS** measurably differentiates you for MSc and graduate-engineer applications. Bring back wafer-level test data, not just simulation plots.

## 5. MRDI, InnoHK and the New Local Internship Lattice

The **Hong Kong Microelectronics Research and Development Institute (MRDI)**, established by the SAR Government in 2024, anchors a new layer of local microelectronics internship opportunities:

- **MRDI Yuen Long FAB** — pilot 8-inch SiC / GaN third-generation semiconductor line, expected to complete commissioning by end-2025. MRDI publishes summer intern and Graduate Trainee positions on its official site (mrdi.org.hk).
- **InnoHK Phase II clusters** — among the 2,500-plus researchers in the InnoHK network, **AI Chip Centre for Emerging Smart Systems (ACCESS)** and **Hong Kong Centre for Logistics Robotics (HKCLR)** both employ undergraduate research assistants on chip-level projects.
- **ASTRI** (Hong Kong Applied Science and Technology Research Institute) operates the Integrated Circuit Design Centre and has hosted DSE graduate IC interns since 2019.
- **Cyberport** and **HKSTP** maintain dedicated semiconductor-startup tracks (e.g. the IC Park) that recruit undergrad interns through the Innovation and Technology Commission's STEM Internship Scheme.

For overseas exposure, two reliable channels run from Hong Kong:

- **TSMC University Programme** (Hsinchu): summer internships open to MEIC undergraduates, particularly via HKUST and CUHK referrals.
- **Imec Summer Internship** (Leuven, Belgium): the world's leading semiconductor R&D institute; previous candidates from HKUST and CityU have placed in EUV lithography and 3D-IC packaging projects.

## 7. Postgraduate Articulation

Microelectronics is one of the few engineering disciplines where a **Master's degree is a near-default**, because advanced nodes demand a working command of process physics and EDA scripting that a 4-year BEng can only introduce.

- **Stanford MS EE — Hardware / Circuits track**: HKUST MEIC and CUHK ELE both feed several students per year.
- **UC Berkeley MEng EECS — Integrated Circuits concentration**: course-based, ideal as an industry on-ramp into the Bay Area.
- **MIT Course 6-3 / 6-2 SM**: research-track; a published tape-out paper is a strong differentiator.
- **ETH Zurich MSc Electrical Engineering and Information Technology — Integrated Systems major**: long-running pipeline from HKUST; Imec internship history is highly valued.
- **National Tsing Hua / National Yang Ming Chiao Tung (Taiwan)** and **Tsinghua / Peking / Fudan (Mainland)**: cost-effective options with direct foundry access.

For research-minded students, the **Hong Kong PhD Fellowship Scheme (HKPFS)** offers a stipend above HK$331,200 per year over three years — microelectronics is one of the funded priority areas under the 2026 cycle.

## 8. Should You Choose This Major? A Self-Assessment

Pick microelectronics if **at least three** of the following are true:

- You enjoyed DSE Physics — specifically the electricity and magnetism papers — more than mechanics.
- You can spend three hours debugging a circuit simulation without losing patience.
- You're comfortable with hands-on lab work in long sessions, including bunny-suit cleanroom protocols.
- You see the Greater Bay Area or Taiwan / US semiconductor industry as your medium-term workplace.
- You're willing to commit to a Master's degree after graduation.

Re-consider if **two or more** of the following apply:

- Your DSE Mathematics (Core) is below 4 and you have no M1 / M2.
- You strongly prefer software-only careers — Computer Science or Data Science fits better.
- You're optimising for short payback before postgraduate study — finance or actuarial science give faster first-year ROI.

## 9. 2026 Application Checklist

- **By August 2025**: visit HKUST NFF, CityU Department of Electrical Engineering and CUHK VLSI Lab open days; build at least one Arduino / FPGA personal project to discuss in JUPAS interviews.
- **By October 2025**: confirm your M1 / M2 choice (M2 is preferred by HKUST and CityU microelectronics admissions tutors).
- **By December 2025**: submit JUPAS Band A applications — recommended ordering for a strong candidate is JS5250 → JS5200 → JS4434 → JS1205 → JS3868.
- **March 2026**: prepare for HKUST and CUHK SLP (Student Learning Profile) interviews; expect questions on Moore's Law, EUV lithography and US export controls.
- **August 2026**: JUPAS result release; Band C self-financing options at HKMU and HSUHK are limited in this discipline, so plan a contingency around Associate Degree → Senior Year articulation if needed.

Microelectronics is the most policy-supported and most capital-intensive engineering track in Hong Kong right now. For a candidate with the right HKDSE profile and tolerance for an extra two-year MSc, the 2026 entry window is the most generous it has ever been.
