# Hong Kong FinTech (Financial Technology) Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

Financial Technology — "FinTech" — is one of the fastest-growing university disciplines in Hong Kong. Driven by the HKMA's FinTech 2025 strategy, the launch of the Cyberport FinTech Hub, the SFC's virtual asset licensing regime, and Hong Kong's positioning as Asia's premier Web3 and digital asset jurisdiction, demand for graduates who can write code AND price a swap has exploded. The number of FinTech firms in Hong Kong rose from 138 in 2016 to over 1,000 by 2025, covering payments, blockchain, insurtech, regtech, wealth management and AI-driven advisory.

This guide compares every major JUPAS FinTech bachelor's programme for 2026 entry — CUHK, PolyU, CityU, HKUST, HKBU and HKMU — and walks through admission scores, curriculum, internship pipelines, graduate salaries, and the regulatory career path through the HKMA, SFC and the Insurance Authority.

## What is a FinTech Bachelor's Degree?

FinTech degrees sit at the intersection of three traditional disciplines: computer science, finance, and statistics/data science. Unlike pure Computer Science (which is hardware/systems heavy) or pure Quantitative Finance (which is math/derivatives heavy), FinTech programmes teach students to **build the financial infrastructure of the future**:

- **Payments & digital banking**: APIs, mobile wallets, Faster Payment System (FPS) integration
- **Blockchain & digital assets**: smart contracts (Solidity), DeFi protocols, custody, stablecoins
- **AI in finance**: credit scoring, fraud detection, algorithmic trading, robo-advisory, LLM-based research
- **RegTech & cybersecurity**: KYC/AML automation, real-time transaction monitoring, regulatory reporting
- **InsurTech**: parametric insurance, telematics, underwriting automation

Programmes typically award a BSc or BBA (Hons) over four years, with all of them now offering substantive coursework in machine learning and at least one elective sequence on blockchain or distributed ledger technology.

## The Five Main JUPAS FinTech Programmes for 2026

### 1. CUHK — Financial Technology (JS4428)

Hosted jointly by the Faculty of Engineering and the Business School, CUHK's FinTech programme is the most prestigious dedicated FinTech offering in Hong Kong. It is a full BEng/BSc track (not a BBA) with rigorous quantitative core in calculus, linear algebra, probability, and stochastic processes.

- **Award**: BSc in Financial Technology
- **Duration**: 4 years
- **Intake**: ~30 JUPAS places
- **Indicative JUPAS median (best 5 + bonus)**: 27–30 (highly competitive, comparable to CUHK Quantitative Finance)
- **Core courses**: Financial Technology Foundations, Blockchain & Cryptocurrency, FinTech Crime & Cybersecurity, Big Data Finance, Mathematical Tools for FinTech
- **Distinct feature**: Compulsory FinTech internship + Final Year Project that can be conducted with an industry partner (HSBC Lab, Cyberport tenant, etc.)

### 2. PolyU — BSc(Hons) Scheme in Data Science & AI: Financial Technology and AI stream (JS3223)

PolyU does not admit students directly into FinTech; instead it offers a **broad-base Year 1 scheme** in the Faculty of Computer and Mathematical Sciences. At the end of Year 1, students choose between Data Science and Analytics (DSA) or **Financial Technology and Artificial Intelligence (FinTechAI)**.

- **Award**: BSc(Hons) Financial Technology and Artificial Intelligence
- **Duration**: 4 years (Year 1 common)
- **Indicative JUPAS median**: 25–27
- **Selection requirement**: Best 5 subjects + bonus for any 6th subject at Level 3 or above
- **Strengths**: Heavy AI/ML emphasis (deep learning, NLP for finance), close ties to PolyU's AIFinTech Lab and Hong Kong-Shenzhen Innovation Park
- **Caveat**: Stream choice in Year 1 can be competitive — students wanting FinTechAI should aim for strong Year 1 GPA

### 3. CityU — BBA Financial Technology (within JS1004 / College of Business)

CityU's College of Business offers Financial Technology as a major within the **BBA Scheme (JS1004)** — students enter the business school and declare a FinTech major at the end of Year 1. CityU also offers an interdisciplinary BSc in Computational Finance and Financial Technology in some intakes.

- **Award**: BBA (Hons) Financial Technology
- **Duration**: 4 years
- **Indicative JUPAS median (BBA Scheme)**: 23–26
- **Distinct feature**: BBA-flavoured — more accounting, corporate finance and management coursework alongside Python, SQL and blockchain electives. Strong for students who want banking/consulting careers rather than pure quant roles
- **Exchange**: CityU Business has one of the most active overseas exchange networks in HK

### 4. HKUST — Quantitative Finance & FinTech routes (JS5104 / JS5200)

HKUST does not currently brand a standalone "FinTech" JUPAS programme but offers two strong routes:

- **JS5104** (School of Business): BBA in Quantitative Finance, with FinTech and Risk Management electives
- **JS5200** (School of Science): BSc in Mathematics with options including Financial Mathematics & Statistics — pair with a CSE minor to build a FinTech profile
- **JS5201/5300** schemes also allow Computer Science majors to take FinTech electives via the IFB (Innovation, Policy & Entrepreneurship) program

HKUST FinTech-track graduates routinely place into top-tier investment banks (Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan, Morgan Stanley) and prop firms (Jane Street, Optiver). JUPAS medians for JS5104 typically sit in the **28–32 range** — the highest in this comparison.

### 5. HKBU and HKMU — Alternative pathways

- **HKBU** offers **BBA(Hons) Applied Economics with a FinTech concentration** and a **BSc in Financial Mathematics** with FinTech electives. JUPAS medians: 20–23.
- **HKMU (Hong Kong Metropolitan University)** offers **BSc(Hons) FinTech and Innovation (JS9760-series)** through self-financing places, providing a strong alternative for students with DSE scores around 18–21 (best 5).

### Subject Combinations That Work

For all FinTech programmes, the optimal HKDSE elective combination is:

- **Mathematics Compulsory + M1 or M2** (M2 is preferred for HKUST QFin and CUHK FinTech)
- **Economics or BAFS** (helpful but not required)
- **Physics or ICT** (signals quantitative/technical aptitude)

Students without M1/M2 are not excluded but should aim for Level 5* in Mathematics Compulsory and pair with strong English (Level 5+) and a science elective.

## Curriculum Deep Dive

A representative four-year FinTech curriculum at CUHK or PolyU follows this arc:

**Year 1** — Foundations
- Calculus I & II, Linear Algebra, Probability & Statistics
- Introduction to Programming (Python)
- Principles of Economics & Financial Accounting
- Discrete Mathematics / Data Structures

**Year 2** — Core FinTech and Finance
- Object-Oriented Programming (Java/C++)
- Corporate Finance, Investment Analysis
- Databases & SQL
- Stochastic Processes for Finance
- Introduction to Blockchain & Cryptocurrencies

**Year 3** — Advanced Track + Internship
- Machine Learning, Deep Learning for Finance
- Smart Contract Development (Solidity / Move)
- Algorithmic Trading & Market Microstructure
- Cybersecurity & RegTech
- **Summer Internship** (mandatory at CUHK, strongly encouraged elsewhere)

**Year 4** — Capstone
- Final Year Project (often industry-partnered)
- Electives: AI in Asset Management, DeFi Protocols, Quant Risk Management, Insurance Analytics
- Optional: Semester exchange (Wharton, Imperial, NUS, Tsinghua)

## Internship and Industry Exposure

Hong Kong's regulators actively fund FinTech talent pipelines:

- **HKMA FinTech Career Accelerator Scheme (FCAS)** — semester-based, full-time internships with banks like HSBC, Hang Seng, Standard Chartered, BOCHK. Interns work on real production projects for one year and receive technology + regulatory training.
- **Cyberport FinTech Internship** — placements with FinTech startups (Airwallex, WeLab, Micro Connect, etc.)
- **SFC Internship Programme** — exposure to virtual asset licensing, market surveillance, fund management regulation
- **InvestHK FinTech Summer Internship** — government-backed programme placing students with international FinTech firms setting up in Hong Kong

Most students complete **2–3 internships** before graduation. The first is often local retail banking IT; later ones target tier-1 investment banks, top-tier consulting (Accenture, McKinsey Digital), or specialised FinTech (Ant International, Tencent FiT, ZA Bank).

### Postgraduate and Further Study

A growing share of FinTech graduates pursue specialised masters:

- HKU MSc in Financial Technology and Data Analytics

- HKUST MSc in Financial Technology

## How to Decide Between Programmes

**Pick CUHK JS4428** if you want a dedicated, technically rigorous FinTech track with the strongest brand recognition in Greater China and active industry partners.

**Pick HKUST JS5104** if you are aiming for a quant trading or top-tier investment banking career — the maths bar is higher but exit options are unparalleled.

**Pick PolyU JS3223** if you are AI-first and may pivot between DS and FinTech — the broad Year 1 gives you flexibility, and PolyU's AIFinTech Lab is well-funded.

**Pick CityU BBA FinTech (JS1004)** if you want a business school environment, broad management coursework, and consulting/banking front-office orientation rather than pure engineering.

**Pick HKBU / HKMU pathways** if your DSE result lands below the top-tier band — these programmes still feed into legitimate FinTech careers, especially in operations, BA, and regulatory roles.

## Common Concerns and Misconceptions

**"Is FinTech just CS with finance electives?"** No. A proper FinTech programme includes meaningful finance theory (CAPM, Black-Scholes, fixed-income mathematics, market microstructure) plus regulatory content (HKMA, SFC, Basel III) that a CS degree never touches.

**"Will AI replace FinTech jobs?"** AI is reshaping the field, but the graduates building AI systems in finance — not those merely using them — remain in extreme demand. Every major HK bank is hiring LLM and ML engineers in 2026.

**"Do I need to be a maths genius?"** For HKUST QFin or CUHK FinTech, yes — you should be comfortable with calculus and probability. For CityU BBA FinTech or HKBU pathways, strong mathematics is enough; you don't need M2.

**"What about non-JUPAS / international applicants?"** All five programmes accept non-JUPAS applications via each university's own admissions office, with IB scores typically 38–42 for the top programmes, A-Levels A*A*A–AAA.

## Final Word

FinTech is no longer a niche elective bolted onto a finance degree — it is one of the defining career tracks of the next decade in Hong Kong. The combination of regulatory tailwinds (virtual asset licensing, stablecoin framework, FPS, eHKD pilots), corporate investment (every major bank now runs a FinTech innovation arm), and government support (Cyberport, HKSTP, HKMA schemes) makes this an unusually safe bet for a 2026 JUPAS applicant.

Apply with breadth: place CUHK JS4428 and HKUST JS5104 at Band A if your DSE projection supports them, with PolyU JS3223 and CityU JS1004 as Band B safeties. Build a programming portfolio (Python data projects, a basic smart contract on a testnet, a finance-themed Kaggle entry) during Form 6 — this materially helps in admission interviews and your first internship application.
