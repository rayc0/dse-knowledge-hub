# Hong Kong Mechanical Engineering Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

Mechanical engineering is the oldest and broadest of the engineering disciplines — often called "the mother of engineering." Anything that moves, heats, cools, or carries a load has a mechanical engineer behind it: aircraft engines, high-speed rail bogies, the powertrain and battery-thermal systems of electric vehicles, robotic arms, semiconductor fabrication equipment, medical devices, and the HVAC systems that make a 50-storey tower habitable. The cross-training in mechanics, thermodynamics, materials, control and manufacturing gives graduates an unusually wide range of exits — and in the Greater Bay Area's advanced-manufacturing, robotics, new-energy and aerospace economy, that range is a real advantage.

This guide walks through every JUPAS-accessible mechanical engineering programme in Hong Kong for 2026 entry, what the curriculum contains, how admission works for both local DSE candidates and mainland students, and what the career — including the HKIE professional engineer ladder — looks like afterwards.

> Note: several faculties admit students through a **broad engineering code with second-year streaming**, so the exact programme code and scoring formula should always be confirmed against the official JUPAS catalogue and each university's admissions page before you finalise your choices.

## The programmes: JUPAS codes at a glance

Five of Hong Kong's eight UGC-funded universities are the serious mechanical engineering players, each with a distinct emphasis:

| University | JUPAS code | Programme | Positioning |
|---|---|---|---|
| HKU | **JS6963** (common engineering) | BEng — Mechanical Engineering major | Single engineering code; choose major after Year 1; design and energy focus |
| CUHK | **JS4408** | BEng Mechanical and Automation Engineering (MAE) | Mechanical + robotics/automation in one programme; research-led |
| HKUST | **JS5270** (Mechanical & Aerospace Engineering); or broad School of Engineering | BEng Mechanical Engineering / Aerospace Engineering | Aerospace, energy and smart-manufacturing tracks |
| PolyU | **JS3611** | BEng(Hons) Mechanical Engineering | Sits alongside aviation and product engineering; strong industry placement |
| CityU | **JS1207** | BEng Mechanical Engineering (majors: Mechanical / Aerospace / Nuclear & Risk Engineering) | Cross-links with biomedical and nano engineering |

Two things are worth flagging. **HKU admits through one common engineering code (JS6963)** covering seven BEng programmes; you take a common first year and formally select the Mechanical Engineering major from Year 2, which means you are not locked in on day one — but popular majors apply a GPA threshold at the streaming stage. **HKUST's School of Engineering** similarly offers broad first-year entry as well as the department-based JS5270 route for Mechanical & Aerospace Engineering. CUHK's **MAE** uniquely fuses mechanical engineering with automation and robotics, which makes it the natural pick for students who want to build robots rather than just machines.

## Why study mechanical engineering in Hong Kong

Three structural factors make the city strong for this subject:

1. **Washington Accord accreditation.** Mechanical programmes accredited by the Hong Kong Institution of Engineers (HKIE) are recognised internationally under the Washington Accord, so the degree travels well to the UK, Australia, Canada, Singapore and the US.
2. **A Greater Bay Area manufacturing engine.** Shenzhen and Dongguan host one of the densest advanced-manufacturing, robotics and new-energy clusters on earth. Hong Kong graduates with bilingual ability and an internationally accredited degree are well placed for engineering roles across the GBA, frequently at a salary premium.
3. **A clear professional ladder.** The route from graduate engineer to Registered Professional Engineer (RPE) and HKIE Corporate Membership is well-defined, employer-supported through structured Scheme "A" graduate training, and tied directly to pay progression.

## JUPAS admission requirements and scoring (local DSE candidates)

Mechanical engineering is a mid-to-upper-band engineering subject. Admission medians at the five universities for 2025 entry generally sat in the **22–30 (best five-to-six subjects)** range, with HKU and HKUST noticeably higher than PolyU and CityU. CUHK engineering medians rose roughly 4–10% versus 2024, so competition is firming.

**Baseline entry (4C+2X):**
- Chinese Language 3, English Language 3, Mathematics 2, Citizenship and Social Development "Attained" (some programmes require English 3)
- Two elective subjects at level 3 or above
- Mechanical engineering values **Mathematics** and **Physics** most heavily; **M1/M2** (Mathematics Extended Modules) is weighted or counted as an extra elective at most universities and is the single biggest score lever.

**How each university tilts the formula:**
- **HKU (JS6963):** the Faculty of Engineering typically counts best 6 subjects, with English and Mathematics as required subjects and Physics/M1/M2 weighted.
- **HKUST:** the School of Engineering applies subject weighting that rewards Mathematics plus a best science subject (Physics weighted ×2 in several engineering formulas); ICT can substitute for the science elective and M1/M2 counts.
- **CUHK (JS4408, MAE):** Mathematics and Physics matter most, best 5 subjects counted; a science-heavy combination is a clear edge.
- **PolyU (JS3611) and CityU (JS1207):** comparatively friendlier thresholds, but Physics plus M1/M2 remain the core uplift — a good fit for mid-band candidates who are genuinely strong in science.

Conversion-score reference (Category A subjects): 5\*\*=8.5, 5\*=7, 5=5.5, 4=4, 3=3. A candidate scoring level 5+ in Mathematics and Physics with M2 as the extension has a strong shot at PolyU or CityU; pushing for HKU or HKUST realistically needs several subjects at 5\* or above.

## Mainland students and non-JEE pathways

Mainland applicants have three main routes into Hong Kong mechanical engineering:

1. **HKU, HKUST, PolyU, CityU "Mainland Undergraduate Admissions Scheme"** (independent of the gaokao central allocation): apply with gaokao total plus the English subject score and an interview. As a popular engineering major, mechanical typically expects gaokao scores comfortably above the provincial first-tier / special-type control line, with English ideally 120/150+ (HKU and HKUST higher).
2. **CUHK Mainland scheme:** admitted within the gaokao early batch by total score; MAE competition is medium-to-high.
3. **International-curriculum route (IB / A-Level / AP):** A-Level Mathematics + Physics + Further Mathematics is the gold-standard combination; IB normally requires HL Mathematics + Physics with a total of 35–40+ depending on the university.

Non-JEE applicants should note that mechanical engineering is a lab- and design-intensive subject. Some interviews probe spatial reasoning, basic mechanics intuition and hands-on project history — a robotics-competition, 3D-printing or formula-student background is a meaningful boost.

## Tuition and costs (2026 entry, indicative)

- **Local students (UGC-funded places):** approximately **HK$42,100/year**.
- **Non-local students (mainland independent admission, international):** mechanical engineering tuition is approximately **HK$171,000–198,000/year**, varying slightly by university and adjusted annually.
- **Living costs:** budget roughly **HK$50,000–90,000/year** for accommodation, food and transport depending on whether you secure a hall place.

Over a four-year degree, a non-local student should plan for a total of roughly HK$900,000–1,150,000. Engineering scholarships, entrance awards and HKSAR scholarships for outstanding mainland and international students can offset a substantial portion — check each faculty's scholarship page early.

## What you actually study

The first two years are common across universities: engineering mathematics, statics and dynamics, thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, mechanics of materials, materials science, electrical fundamentals, engineering drawing/CAD and programming. Years 3–4 specialise. Typical streams and electives include:

- **Mechatronics and robotics** — control systems, sensors and actuators, embedded systems, industrial automation (CUHK MAE is especially strong here).
- **Thermofluids and energy** — internal combustion and gas turbines, HVAC, renewable energy systems, building services.
- **Aerospace engineering** — aerodynamics, propulsion, flight mechanics, structures (HKUST and CityU offer dedicated tracks).
- **Manufacturing and design** — CAD/CAE/CAM, additive manufacturing, finite element analysis, product design and reliability.
- **Solid mechanics and materials** — composites, fatigue and fracture, computational mechanics.

A **final-year design project or capstone** — often a working prototype, a competition vehicle, or an industry-sponsored brief — is universal and is what employers ask about most in interviews.

## Accreditation and the professional engineer ladder

All five programmes are accredited by the **HKIE** under the relevant engineering discipline and recognised under the **Washington Accord**. The progression after graduation is:

1. **Graduate Engineer / Graduate Member (GMHKIE):** join an HKIE Scheme "A" structured graduate-training programme with an approved employer.
2. **Corporate Member (MHKIE) / Registered Professional Engineer (RPE):** typically after ~3–4 years of approved training and experience plus the Professional Assessment.
3. **RPE registration** is the gateway to signing off works, leading project teams and the steepest part of the salary curve. It is also the basis for mutual recognition with bodies such as the UK Engineering Council and Engineers Australia.

If long-term professional standing matters to you, confirm the specific stream you intend to take is HKIE-accredited in the year you graduate — accreditation is granted per discipline and reviewed periodically.

## Careers and salaries

Mechanical engineering has one of the widest employment funnels of any degree. Common destinations for Hong Kong graduates:

- **Building services and MEP** consultancies and contractors (Hong Kong's largest single mechanical employer base, given the construction and infrastructure pipeline).
- **MTR, Airport Authority, utilities and government** (EMSD, Water Supplies, Drainage Services) — stable graduate-trainee schemes with clear RPE pathways.
- **GBA advanced manufacturing, robotics, EV and consumer-electronics** firms — strong demand for bilingual, internationally accredited engineers.
- **Aerospace and aviation** maintenance, design and operations.
- **Finance and consulting** — quantitative and operations roles routinely recruit engineering graduates for their analytical training.

Indicative starting salaries for fresh bachelor's graduates are roughly **HK$18,000–26,000/month**, rising materially once RPE status is achieved. Graduate-trainee schemes at the MTR, Airport Authority and large consultancies tend to sit at the upper end and front-load structured training that accelerates the route to professional registration.

## Which programme should you choose?

- **Want robotics and automation as the core, not an add-on:** CUHK MAE (JS4408).
- **Want maximum flexibility and a top research brand, deferring the major decision:** HKU JS6963 (common engineering) or HKUST broad School of Engineering entry.
- **Want a dedicated aerospace track:** HKUST (JS5270) or CityU (JS1207, Aerospace major).
- **Want strong industry placement and a more accessible threshold while staying Washington-Accord accredited:** PolyU (JS3611) or CityU (JS1207).

Rank by genuine interest first, accreditation second, and admission realism third — a slightly less famous programme you complete with a strong GPA and a good capstone beats a prestige programme you struggle through.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is M2 essential for mechanical engineering?** Not a formal requirement everywhere, but it is the most effective score lever and excellent preparation for university engineering mathematics. Take it if you can.

**Mechanical vs. aerospace — do I have to decide at application?** Usually no. HKU and HKUST stream after a common first year, and CityU's JS1207 selects the major (Mechanical / Aerospace / Nuclear & Risk) during the degree.

**Is the degree recognised outside Hong Kong?** Yes — HKIE accreditation under the Washington Accord gives broad international recognition for engineering practice and further study.

**Can mainland graduates work in Hong Kong afterwards?** Yes, via the IANG arrangement for non-local graduates, with strong demand across building services, MTR/utilities and GBA manufacturing.

**How hard is it to get in?** Mid-to-upper band. HKU and HKUST are competitive (multiple 5\* subjects realistically needed); PolyU and CityU are achievable for science-strong mid-band candidates, especially with Physics and M1/M2.
