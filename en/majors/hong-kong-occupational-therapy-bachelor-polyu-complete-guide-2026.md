# Hong Kong Occupational Therapy Bachelor (PolyU JS3624) Complete Guide 2026

The Bachelor of Science (Honours) in Occupational Therapy at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU) - JUPAS code **JS3624** - is the only undergraduate programme in Hong Kong that leads to direct registration as a Registered Occupational Therapist (Part I) with the Occupational Therapists Board. Together with Physiotherapy (JS3636) and Optometry (JS3290), it forms the so-called "PolyU Three Treasures" (理大三寶) - a trio of clinical degrees that combine guaranteed registration, six-figure starting salaries in the public sector, and statutory practice rights nowhere else available in the territory.

This guide walks through the 2026 admission picture: how the score is calculated, what the curriculum looks like across four years, how the 1,000+ hours of clinical placements are structured, what the Hospital Authority pays new graduates, and how the World Federation of Occupational Therapists (WFOT) recognition opens doors to overseas licensure. It also covers the Non-JUPAS routes that international and mainland applicants increasingly use, given that the JUPAS quota is fixed at roughly 70 places out of an annual intake of 100.

## What Occupational Therapists Actually Do

Occupational therapy (OT) is one of the four statutory allied health professions in Hong Kong, alongside physiotherapy, medical laboratory technology, and radiography. Where physiotherapists focus on movement, strength, and pain, occupational therapists work on **purposeful activity** - the everyday "occupations" of self-care, productivity, and leisure that give a person's life meaning. A stroke survivor relearning to button a shirt, a child with autism developing fine-motor skills for handwriting, an elderly resident adapting a kitchen for safe cooking after a hip fracture, a worker returning to a factory line after a hand injury - all of these fall within the OT scope.

In Hong Kong's clinical landscape, OTs are deployed across the Hospital Authority's seven clusters (acute hospitals, rehabilitation hospitals, infirmaries, psychiatric units, child assessment centres), the Department of Health, special schools and integrated programmes under the Education Bureau, elderly day-care centres and integrated rehabilitation centres operated by NGOs such as the Hong Kong Society for Rehabilitation, and a fast-growing private sector that includes paediatric clinics, ergonomic consultancies, and post-acute home rehabilitation services for the ageing population.

The profession is governed by the Occupational Therapists Board under the Supplementary Medical Professions Ordinance (Cap. 359). Practising OT in Hong Kong without registration is a criminal offence, which is why the supply of new practitioners is tightly controlled by PolyU's annual intake.

## 2026 JUPAS Admission Requirements

### Minimum entrance requirements

- **Chinese Language**: Level 3
- **English Language**: Level 3
- **Mathematics Compulsory Part**: Level 2
- **Citizenship and Social Development**: Attained
- **Two elective subjects** at Level 3, **OR** one elective at Level 3 plus Mathematics Extended Module (M1 or M2) at Level 3

There is **no compulsory elective subject**, but the programme strongly favours science background. Applicants without Biology, Chemistry or Physics among their electives compete at a structural disadvantage in the weighted score and will face harder questioning in any interview.

### Subject weightings (admission score calculation)

PolyU calculates the JS3624 admission score using **Best 5 subjects with subject weighting**. Under the published weighting scheme:

- **English Language**: weight ×1.5
- **Biology / Chemistry / Physics / Combined Science (with Biology, Chemistry or Physics)**: weight ×1.5 (one science subject is given the bonus)
- **Chinese Language, Mathematics Compulsory Part, M1, M2, and other electives**: weight ×1.0

Citizenship and Social Development is **not** counted in the score - it only needs to be Attained to satisfy the entrance bar.

### Recent admission scores

| Intake year | Average admission score (Best 5 with weighting) |
|-------------|------------------------------------------------|
| 2024/25 | 281.8 |
| 2025/26 | 285.6 |

For context, on the standard "Best 5 unweighted" basis these scores correspond to roughly **5*-level performance across four core subjects plus a 5 in a fifth**. The lower 25th percentile typically lands around 27 (in unweighted Best 5 terms), while the top end of the cohort regularly exceeds 32. JS3624 is not as competitive as Medicine or Physiotherapy, but it is comfortably above the average band cut-off for non-clinical PolyU programmes.

### Interview and OEA

The programme reserves the right to require an interview or test, and in practice interviews are conducted for borderline applicants and those whose Other Experiences and Achievements (OEA) profile or Student Learning Profile (SLP) signals strong motivation. Useful OEA evidence includes volunteering with persons with disabilities, hospital or elderly-home shadowing, sports-coaching of children with special educational needs, and any independent reading or community projects related to rehabilitation. The interview, when held, is typically a structured panel format of 15-20 minutes, sometimes with a short scenario task.

## Four-Year Curriculum Structure

The programme follows a "scheme" model under the Department of Rehabilitation Sciences. Year 1 is shared with Physiotherapy (JS3636) for the foundation science modules, with branching into OT-specific content from Year 2.

### Year 1 - Foundation Sciences

Anatomy, physiology, kinesiology, biomechanics, psychology, sociology, plus PolyU's General University Requirements (GUR): English, Chinese, service learning, and a leadership and intra-personal development cluster. Students earn around 30 credits and take a single-credit "Introduction to Occupational Therapy" module that includes hospital observation visits.

### Year 2 - Conditions and Assessment

Pathology, neurology, orthopaedics, paediatric development, mental health and psychiatry, occupational science theory, and the first OT-specific assessment courses (activity analysis, occupational performance assessment). Year 2 ends with the **first clinical placement (Level I, ~3 weeks)** in a supervised acute or rehabilitation setting.

### Year 3 - Intervention and Specialty Practice

Adult physical rehabilitation, mental health practice, paediatric intervention, hand therapy, splinting and orthotics, assistive technology, environmental modification, vocational rehabilitation. Year 3 contains the largest single placement block - typically **two Level II placements of 8-10 weeks each**, rotating through different clinical specialties (e.g., one acute physical, one psychiatric or paediatric).

### Year 4 - Capstone and Independent Practice

Honours research project (year-long), advanced practice electives (e.g., community-based rehabilitation, hand therapy specialisation, dementia care), professional ethics and law, healthcare management. The final clinical block is a **Level III "role-emerging" placement** of 8-10 weeks where students function with substantial autonomy under remote supervision, often in non-traditional settings such as schools, NGO community centres, or workplace ergonomics consultancies.

Across the four years students accumulate **at least 1,000 hours of clinical fieldwork**, the WFOT minimum. PolyU consistently exceeds this benchmark, which is part of why its degree is recognised internationally without bridging requirements in most jurisdictions.

## Tuition, Costs and Scholarships

For local UGC-funded students the 2025/26 first-year tuition is **HK$47,000**. Total degree cost (tuition only) over four years is in the order of HK$188,000 - identical to other UGC programmes and significantly less than self-financed alternatives.

Indirect costs to budget for include:

- Hostel fees if applicable: HK$10,000-18,000 per academic year for PolyU halls
- Textbooks, lab coats, professional shoes, and a clinical placement uniform: roughly HK$3,000-5,000 in Year 1, less thereafter
- Transport to placement sites across all 18 districts of Hong Kong during clinical blocks
- Optional WFOT student membership (~US$30/year) for international networking and conference discounts
- Professional indemnity insurance during Level III placement (often covered by PolyU)

PolyU offers an extensive range of scholarships including the Talent Development Scholarship (renewable, HK$30,000+), entrance scholarships for students with 5** in three or more subjects, and need-based bursaries through the Student Affairs Office. Mainland students who enter via Non-JUPAS at full overseas tuition (HK$145,000 in 2025/26) are eligible for the PolyU Mainland Scholarship, which can cover up to 100% of tuition for top-scoring Gaokao admittees.

## Clinical Placements - What to Expect

Placements are coordinated through the Department of Rehabilitation Sciences in partnership with all seven Hospital Authority clusters, the Department of Health's Child Assessment Service, the Education Bureau's special schools, and over 50 NGO and private-sector partners. Students typically complete:

- **One Level I placement** (Year 2): exposure-style, 3 weeks, focus on observation and basic assessment
- **Two Level II placements** (Year 3): full immersion, 8-10 weeks each, structured caseload progression
- **One Level III placement** (Year 4): role-emerging or advanced practice, 8-10 weeks, near-autonomous practice

Placements are unpaid (in line with the global norm for clinical training) and students are responsible for their own commute. A common rite of passage is being assigned a Tuen Mun Hospital or Castle Peak Hospital block while living in Hung Hom - the daily MTR commute becomes part of the learning curve. Performance is graded by the on-site clinical educator (a registered OT with at least three years' experience) using a national fieldwork assessment instrument; failure of any single placement requires repeating the block, which can delay graduation by a semester.

## Career Prospects and Salary

### Hospital Authority

The single largest employer of new graduates is the Hospital Authority, which absorbs roughly 60-70% of each cohort into its allied health workforce. New graduates enter as **Occupational Therapist II** on the Master Pay Scale (MPS) Point 16. After the April 2024 adjustment the OT II band runs from approximately MPS 16 to MPS 33, with monthly salaries starting around **HK$36,000** and reaching HK$60,000+ within the band over 6-8 years. Promotion to **Occupational Therapist I** (MPS 34-44, roughly HK$67,000-91,000/month) typically follows after another 3-5 years of solid performance, with senior and managerial grades stretching beyond MPS 45.

Allied health staff in the HA receive 13 months of salary, civil-service-style medical and dental coverage, generous leave entitlements (22+ days, rising with service), and a Mandatory Provident Fund top-up scheme. Total first-year compensation for a new OT II is comfortably in the **HK$470,000-500,000** range.

### Government, NGOs and private sector

Beyond the HA, OTs are recruited by:

- **Department of Health** - Child Assessment Service, Elderly Health Service, Centre for Health Protection
- **Education Bureau** - special schools, integrated education support teams
- **Social Welfare Department** and subsidised NGO operators - Hong Kong Society for Rehabilitation, Heep Hong Society, Caritas, Tung Wah Group of Hospitals, Po Leung Kuk
- **Private hospitals and clinics** - Hong Kong Sanatorium, Adventist, Matilda, plus the rapidly growing paediatric OT clinic sector in Causeway Bay, Tsim Sha Tsui and Sha Tin
- **Workplace ergonomics consultancies** serving banks, airlines and construction companies
- **Self-employment** as a registered OT, particularly in private paediatric practice and home-based geriatric care

Private-sector salaries for new graduates often start slightly above HA scales (HK$38,000-45,000/month) and can rise faster, but lack the long-service progression and pension benefits of the public sector.

## WFOT Recognition and Overseas Practice

PolyU's BSc (Hons) in Occupational Therapy has been continuously accredited by the **World Federation of Occupational Therapists (WFOT)** since the programme's founding. WFOT accreditation means the curriculum meets international minimum standards, which translates into materially easier overseas registration in three of the most popular destination markets:

- **Australia**: Eligible to apply for registration with the Occupational Therapy Board of Australia (AHPRA) without bridging coursework, subject to English-language proof and a competency assessment
- **United Kingdom**: Eligible for HCPC registration via the international application route, typically with a streamlined review of curriculum and clinical hours
- **Canada**: Eligible to sit the National Occupational Therapy Certification Examination (NOTCE) administered by CAOT and provincial regulators after credential review
- **United States**: Requires NBCOT examination plus state-by-state licensure; PolyU graduates have a strong track record of passing NBCOT, though additional documentation is needed
- **Singapore**: Direct recognition by the Allied Health Professions Council for restricted registration leading to full registration after supervised practice

This international portability is one of the strongest selling points of JS3624. Graduates who later emigrate, study overseas, or take a working holiday are not stranded in a non-transferable qualification.

## Non-JUPAS and International Pathways

Of the 100 first-year places, roughly 70 are filled through JUPAS and the remaining 30 through Non-JUPAS - a category that includes Hong Kong students with non-DSE qualifications (IB, GCE A-Level, SAT/AP, Gaokao), mainland Gaokao applicants, and international students. The most common entry profiles are:

- **GCE A-Level**: AAA in three subjects including at least one science
- **IB Diploma**: 38-40 points overall with HL 6/6/6 including HL Biology or Chemistry
- **Gaokao**: well above the first-tier line (一本線) for the applicant's province, typically 80+ points above the cut-off
- **Associate Degree / Higher Diploma in related fields**: rare entry into Year 1 (PolyU does not typically offer senior-year articulation into JS3624 due to clinical sequencing)

Non-JUPAS applicants apply directly through PolyU's eAdmission system, with rounds opening in September and closing typically in mid-November (early round) and January (main round). Interviews for Non-JUPAS shortlistees are essentially universal and are taken seriously - PolyU is checking for English fluency, ethical reasoning, and authentic interest in the profession.

International (non-local) tuition is **HK$145,000 per year** in 2025/26, with the same hostel and living-cost picture as local students.

## Comparison with the Master in Occupational Therapy (MOT)

PolyU also offers a **Master in Occupational Therapy** (programme code 51068), a 2.5-year postgraduate-entry programme for graduates who already hold a bachelor's degree in another discipline. The MOT also leads to registration with the Occupational Therapists Board and is WFOT-accredited, but:

- Tuition is much higher (self-financed, around HK$340,000+ for the full programme)
- Intake is smaller (typically 30-40 places per year)
- Competition includes mature applicants with biology, psychology or healthcare backgrounds

For school leavers the BSc route remains by far the more cost-effective path. The MOT is best viewed as a career-change option for graduates in their mid-20s onwards.

OT typically draws applicants who are interested in **holistic, person-centred rehabilitation** rather than the more biomechanical focus of PT or the more procedural focus of optometry. The work is intellectually broad - one week may involve hand therapy splinting, the next a cognitive rehabilitation group for early dementia, the next ergonomic redesign of an office workstation.

## Should You Apply?

Apply to JS3624 if:

- You are drawn to long-term, relationship-based care rather than acute or surgical work
- You are comfortable with both physical (anatomy, kinesiology) and psychosocial (mental health, group therapy) aspects of healthcare
- You want a guaranteed registration pathway and a clear public-sector salary ladder
- You value international portability of your qualification
- You can sustain four years of an academically demanding curriculum with substantial unpaid placement time

Think carefully before applying if:

- You are primarily attracted by the salary - PT and OT pay similarly, but Pharmacy or Medicine pay more if the financial calculus is dominant
- You dislike documentation - OT clinical work is paperwork-heavy
- You are uncertain about working with elderly, paediatric or mental-health populations - all three are unavoidable across the four-year placement schedule

For applicants who fit the profile, JS3624 remains one of Hong Kong's best-value undergraduate degrees: guaranteed professional registration, an internationally portable credential, a stable public-sector employer ready to absorb the cohort, and a starting salary in the territory's top quintile. For the 2026 JUPAS cycle it deserves a serious place in any health-sciences-focused Band A.
