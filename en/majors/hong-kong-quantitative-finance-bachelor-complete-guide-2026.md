# Hong Kong Quantitative Finance Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

Quantitative Finance (QF) sits at the intersection of mathematics, statistics, computer science and financial markets. It is one of the most competitive — and most lucrative — undergraduate pathways in Hong Kong, feeding directly into trading desks, hedge funds, investment banks and risk divisions. If you are a strong mathematics student weighing JUPAS choices for the 2026 cycle, this guide explains exactly which programmes exist, how hard they are to get into, what you will actually study, and where graduates end up.

## What Is Quantitative Finance — and Who Is It For?

A pure finance or business degree teaches you how markets and corporations work. A quantitative finance degree teaches you how to *model* them. Expect heavy doses of stochastic calculus, linear algebra, probability theory, time-series econometrics and programming (Python, R, C++) layered on top of derivatives pricing, portfolio theory and risk management.

QF is the right fit if you genuinely enjoy mathematics and coding — not merely tolerate them. Students who pick QF only because of salary headlines tend to struggle in second-year courses like stochastic processes and numerical methods. The strongest applicants typically take **Mathematics Extended Module M1 or M2**, score well in core Mathematics, and have some programming exposure (even self-taught).

## The Main JUPAS Programmes for 2026

Hong Kong's research universities each offer a distinct flavour of quantitative finance. Programme codes change periodically, so always confirm against the official JUPAS website before submitting.

### HKUST — BSc in Quantitative Finance (JS5332)

HKUST's School of Business and Management runs the flagship **BSc in Quantitative Finance (QFIN)** under code **JS5332**. A defining feature: the Year 1 curriculum of JS5332 is *identical* to the broad **JS5300 Global Business and Management** intake. JS5332 is a direct-entry route for students certain about QFIN, while JS5300 students can later apply into QFIN through major-selection exercises held at the end of the first three terms.

- **General requirement:** four core subjects plus two electives at minimum level "332A33" (i.e., Chinese 3, English 3, Mathematics 2, Citizenship & Social Development "Attained", plus two electives at level 3).
- **Score formula:** either 4 core + 2 electives (4C + 2X), or 4 core + 1 elective + M1/M2 (4C + 1X + M1/M2). Results of Liberal Studies (Level 2+) from previous sittings are also considered.
- **Interview:** **compulsory.** Band A applicants showing strong talent and potential are invited to engagement activities and interviews between May and July 2026.
- **NCS applicants:** non-Chinese-speaking students may satisfy the Chinese requirement with an alternative Chinese qualification, an HKDSE Other Language subject, or an Applied Learning Chinese subject.

### CUHK — Quantitative Finance (JS4252) and Quantitative Finance & Risk Management Science (JS4276)

CUHK offers two closely related routes. **Quantitative Finance (JS4252)**, run through the Business School, is the standard QF degree. **Quantitative Finance and Risk Management Science (JS4276)** is a double-major collaboration with the Department of Statistics, giving deeper grounding in risk modelling and statistical theory — attractive for students targeting risk, actuarial-adjacent or data-heavy roles.

CUHK publishes a programme-specific score calculator. For both programmes, admission chances rise materially when the subjects used in the score formula average **level 5\* or above**. CUHK weights Mathematics and a quantitative elective heavily, so M1/M2 is a strong asset.

### HKU — Bachelor of Science in Quantitative Finance (JS6884)

HKU's **BSc in Quantitative Finance (JS6884)** is delivered jointly by the Faculty of Science and HKU Business School, blending rigorous mathematical training with finance applications. As with CUHK, HKU advises that competitive applicants achieve an average of **level 5\* or above** across score-formula subjects. HKU also offers broader admission into Science (JS6901) and Business (JS6781) from which students can specialise, but JS6884 is the dedicated direct-entry code.

### Related and Adjacent Programmes

If straight QF proves out of reach, consider adjacent quantitative pathways: **CityU's BSc in Quantitative Finance and Risk Analysis**, **PolyU's Investment Science / Financial Engineering**, **CUHK's Financial Technology (JS4428)**, and the various **Actuarial Science** and **Data Science** degrees. These share substantial mathematical DNA and overlapping career destinations. ## How Competitive Is Admission?

Quantitative finance is consistently among the highest-scoring business-stream programmes in Hong Kong. Realistic targets for 2026:

- **HKUST JS5332, HKU JS6884, CUHK JS4252:** competitive applicants typically present an average around **level 5 to 5\*** across the relevant subjects, with strong Mathematics and M1/M2 performance. Top-tier admits often hold multiple 5\*\* grades.
- **CUHK JS4276 and CityU/PolyU equivalents:** somewhat more accessible but still demanding, generally a notch below the three flagship codes.

A few practical notes on the 2026 cycle:

1. **Score calculators update late.** The official JUPAS and university score calculators for the 2026 cycle are typically refreshed between May and June 2026. Treat any earlier figure as indicative only and re-check before finalising band choices after results day.
2. **Mathematics is non-negotiable.** Every QF programme leans on Mathematics and the Extended Module. A level 5 in core Maths with M2 is far stronger than a 5\* in core Maths alone for these programmes.
3. **Interviews matter at HKUST.** Because the QFIN interview is compulsory, your DSE score gets you *invited*, but performance in the engagement activities and interview can be decisive at the margin.

## What You Will Actually Study

While details differ by university, a typical four-year QF curriculum follows this arc:

**Year 1 — Foundations.** Calculus and linear algebra, introductory probability and statistics, microeconomics and macroeconomics, accounting fundamentals, and introductory programming (usually Python). At HKUST the first year mirrors the general business intake.

**Year 2 — Core quantitative methods.** Stochastic processes, mathematical statistics, regression and econometrics, corporate finance, investments and portfolio theory, and data structures. This is the year that filters out students who chose QF for the wrong reasons.

**Year 3 — Financial engineering.** Derivatives and options pricing (Black–Scholes and beyond), fixed income and interest-rate models, risk management (VaR, Expected Shortfall), numerical methods and Monte Carlo simulation, time-series analysis, and often a machine-learning-in-finance elective. Many students complete summer internships between Years 3 and 4.

**Year 4 — Specialisation and capstone.** Advanced electives (algorithmic trading, credit risk, FinTech, quantitative asset management), a capstone project or thesis, and frequently a study-abroad or exchange term.

Programming is woven throughout. Graduates are expected to be fluent in Python and comfortable with at least one of R, MATLAB, C++ or SQL. Increasingly, employers also expect familiarity with machine-learning libraries and version control.

## Careers and Earnings

Quantitative finance graduates target some of the best-paid roles available to Hong Kong undergraduates. Common destinations:

- **Quantitative trader / quant trader** — designing and executing systematic trading strategies. Among the highest-paid roles; total compensation in Hong Kong commonly spans roughly HK$1.2M to HK$2.5M+ for experienced practitioners, with strong bonus leverage.
- **Quantitative analyst / quant researcher** — building pricing, risk and signal models at banks, hedge funds and asset managers. Average total pay clusters around the HK$0.9M–1.1M mark, with a wide range depending on firm type.
- **Quant developer** — engineering the low-latency systems and pricing libraries that traders and researchers rely on.
- **Market / model risk analyst** — validating models and measuring exposure inside banks and regulators.
- **Asset management, structuring and FinTech** — portfolio analytics, derivatives structuring, and increasingly data-science roles at FinTech firms.

Entry-level financial quantitative analyst roles in Hong Kong commonly start in the **HK$400,000+ per year** region, rising sharply with experience and firm prestige. Hedge funds and proprietary trading firms (the buy side) typically pay well above bank graduate programmes, but they also recruit far fewer people and screen extremely hard on mathematics and coding.

Two qualifications frequently complement the degree: the **CFA** charter (valued in asset management and research) and the **FRM** (valued in risk). Neither is required to start, but many graduates pursue them early. ## Application Strategy: Practical Advice

1. **Take M1 or M2.** It is the single highest-leverage decision a prospective QF applicant can make. M2 is generally preferred for its calculus and algebra depth.
2. **Build a coding portfolio.** Even a small GitHub repository with a backtested trading strategy or a data-analysis project signals genuine interest — useful for HKUST's interview and for internship applications later.
3. **Band wisely.** Place your single most-wanted QF code in Band A, pair it with a realistic second QF or adjacent quantitative programme, and keep a safety choice (e.g., a broad business or science intake from which you can later transfer into QF). HKUST's JS5300 → QFIN internal route is a legitimate back-door for strong students.
4. **Prepare for the interview.** Expect questions probing mathematical reasoning, basic probability puzzles, motivation, and awareness of markets. Practise explaining a quantitative concept clearly out loud. See our [JUPAS interview preparation guide](../admission/jupas-interview-preparation-complete-guide.md).
5. **Verify everything late.** Programme codes, score formulae and calculators are finalised close to the 2026 cycle. Confirm against official HKUST, CUHK, HKU and JUPAS pages before locking choices.

## Is Quantitative Finance Right for You?

Choose QF if you love mathematics, enjoy programming, and are energised by markets — and if you are realistic that the path is demanding and the elite buy-side seats are scarce. Even outside top trading roles, the degree's blend of statistics, coding and finance is highly transferable into data science, FinTech, consulting and actuarial work. For students who like finance but not heavy mathematics, a standard [business and economics](business-and-economics-hk.md) degree or [accountancy](hong-kong-accountancy-bachelor-complete-guide-2026.md) may be a better fit.

Done well, a Hong Kong quantitative finance degree is one of the strongest launchpads in Asia into the global financial industry. The barrier to entry is high — but for the right student, the return is among the best available.
