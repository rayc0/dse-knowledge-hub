# Hong Kong Chemistry Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

Chemistry sits quietly at the centre of almost every applied science you can name — pharmaceuticals, semiconductors, batteries, food safety, environmental remediation, forensic investigation, cosmetics. For DSE candidates considering a BSc in Chemistry, Hong Kong offers an unusually wide spread of options: a pure-science track at the three research universities, a chemical technology track at PolyU, a streamed programme at CityU with specialisations like forensic and cosmetic chemistry, and a strong biochemistry-flavoured programme at HKBU. This guide walks through JUPAS codes, admission scores, curriculum, labs, internships, postgraduate routes and career outcomes for the 2026 intake.

## Why study Chemistry in Hong Kong

Three things make Hong Kong worth considering for a chemistry degree.

First, **research density**. HKU, CUHK and HKUST consistently rank inside the global top 50 for chemistry on QS and THES subject tables, with strong groups in materials, catalysis, supramolecular and computational chemistry. CityU's chemistry department is a relatively young but fast-growing player, and PolyU's chemical technology offering plugs directly into industry. Final-year projects often feed into ongoing PhD or postdoctoral work, which means undergraduate students get exposure to publishable research.

Second, **the chemistry job market in the Greater Bay Area**. The Hong Kong Innovation and Technology Bureau, plus mainland-side investment in Shenzhen and Dongguan, has created sustained demand for analytical chemists, formulation scientists, QA/QC chemists in pharmaceuticals and electronics, and battery/materials chemists for the new-energy supply chain. The combination of a Hong Kong BSc and a working ability in Cantonese, English and Mandarin is unusually portable.

Third, **postgraduate pipelines**. The Hong Kong PhD Fellowship Scheme (HKPFS), MPhil routes at all the publicly funded universities, and clear master's offerings in analytical chemistry, environmental science, food science, materials and chemical biology give undergraduates a realistic step from a four-year BSc into a research career — without leaving the city.

## DSE admission requirements and 2025 reference scores

All publicly funded universities use the **332A33** general baseline — Chinese 3, English 3, Mathematics (Compulsory Part) 2, Citizenship & Social Development "Attained", plus two elective subjects at Level 3. For chemistry-related programmes, **Chemistry or Combined Science (with Chemistry component)** is strongly preferred as one elective, and many programmes will weight it. **Mathematics M1/M2 and Physics** are also valued, particularly at HKUST.

Indicative 2025 JUPAS median admission scores (best-five formula, varies by school):

- HKU JS6901 (BSc, intending Chemistry): around 27–30
- CUHK JS4604 Science: around 26–30 depending on stream
- HKUST JS5102 Science: around 26–30
- PolyU JS3011 Biotech & Chemical Technology Scheme: around 22–25
- CityU JS1202 BSc Chemistry: around 21–24
- HKBU JS2510 Faculty of Science: around 20–23
- EduHK JS8714 BEd & BSc Chemistry: around 22–25

Use these only as a rough guide — the actual cut-off depends on the year, the elective subject weights, and bonuses for higher levels in 5* and 5**. For 2026 entry, the Category A conversion is 5**=8.5, 5*=7, 5=5.5, 4=4, 3=3, with most chemistry-related programmes applying additional weight (typically 1.5× or 2×) to Chemistry, Mathematics Extended Module (M1/M2), Biology or Physics in the best-five calculation.

## Programme deep dives

### HKU — BSc (JS6901) with Chemistry major

HKU's Faculty of Science admits through one broad-based BSc scheme. In Year 1 students complete foundation courses in chemistry, mathematics, physics and biology, plus common-core requirements, and then declare a major from Year 2. The chemistry major offers two concentrations — **Chemistry** and **Materials Chemistry** — with elective clusters in chemical biology, energy and nanomaterials. The capstone is a year-long laboratory research project under a faculty member, often resulting in conference posters or co-authorship.

Notable points: HKU's chemistry teaching laboratories were refurbished in 2023, with new instrumentation in spectroscopy and chromatography; the Faculty of Science runs a Summer Research Fellowship that pays selected undergraduates to do lab work over the summer between Year 2 and Year 3. Graduates often progress to MPhil/PhD at HKU itself, or to top US and UK chemistry programmes (Cambridge, Oxford, ETH Zürich, MIT, Caltech have all been destinations).

### CUHK — BSc Science Programme, Chemistry major (JS4604)

CUHK's Faculty of Science uses a similar broad-based admission. The Chemistry department offers a **Chemistry major**, a **Materials Science** stream and an interdisciplinary **Risk Management Science** option for students with an analytical chemistry interest applied to industry. CUHK is particularly strong in supramolecular and host-guest chemistry, polymer chemistry and chemical biology. The four-year structure gives time for double-major or minor combinations — common pairings are Chemistry with Computer Science, or Chemistry with Cell & Molecular Biology.

CUHK chemistry graduates have one of the strongest postgraduate placement records in Hong Kong, in part because of the college system: small tutorial groups, a residential community of researchers, and well-funded summer programmes for undergraduates.

### HKUST — BSc Science Programme, Chemistry major (JS5102)

HKUST admits through the **School of Science** scheme; students decide their major (Chemistry, Biochemistry & Cell Biology, Mathematics, Physics, etc.) at the end of Year 1 or Year 2. The Department of Chemistry is research-intensive, with strong groups in catalysis, computational chemistry, energy materials and biomolecular chemistry. Curriculum is mathematically rigorous; expect a heavier load of physical chemistry and quantum mechanics than at other universities. The compulsory research-track project (CHEM4980/4981) is taken in the final year.

HKUST is particularly attractive if you intend to apply for the HKPFS or go on to a PhD at a top North American or European school — placement rates are high, and the department supports overseas exchange to MIT, Caltech, ETH and the National University of Singapore.

### PolyU — BSc (Hons) Scheme in Biotechnology and Chemical Technology (JS3011)

PolyU's offering is more **applied** than the three research universities. JS3011 is a Year-1-entry broad scheme; students choose between **BSc (Hons) in Chemical Technology** and **BSc (Hons) in Applied Biology with Biotechnology** at the end of Year 1 (subject to GPA and capacity). The Chemical Technology pathway emphasises industrial chemistry, process technology, environmental and analytical chemistry. Most graduates head into pharmaceuticals, electronics QA/QC, environmental consulting, the Government Laboratory and the Hospital Authority.

PolyU runs a **Work-Integrated Education (WIE)** programme — students complete an industry placement (4-12 months) as part of degree requirements. This is the single biggest practical differentiator: a PolyU chemical technology graduate typically arrives at their first interview with a real industrial reference, not just lab demonstrations.

### CityU — BSc Chemistry (JS1202) with four streams

CityU's BSc Chemistry has the **most differentiated curriculum** in Hong Kong. After a common Year 1 foundation, students choose one of:

- **Comprehensive Chemistry** (accredited by the Royal Society of Chemistry — useful for UK chartered status)
- **Cosmetic Chemistry** — formulation, regulation, dermatology-relevant chemistry; well-suited to careers in the personal-care and cosmetics industry, which is a strong sector in the Greater Bay Area
- **Forensic Chemistry** — taught with input from the Hong Kong Police's Forensic Science Division; covers controlled drugs, trace analysis, document examination, and crime-scene chemistry
- **GREAT (Global Research Enrichment and Technopreneurship)** — for students aiming to commercialise research; combines chemistry with entrepreneurship modules

CityU also runs strong programmes in materials chemistry and chemical sensors — a natural feeder into MPhil/PhD work in the Department of Materials Science and Engineering, which is regularly top-30 globally for materials.

### HKBU — BSc (Hons) Chemistry major (JS2510)

HKBU's chemistry department has a **biochemistry-leaning** identity, with strong groups in chemical biology, computational chemistry and analytical chemistry applied to traditional Chinese medicine. The BSc (Hons) in Chemistry offers a final-year research project, exchange opportunities with US, Canadian and European partners, and internships at the Hong Kong Government Laboratory, the HKBU Mr. Simon Suen and Mrs. Mary Suen Sino-Humanitas Institute and SME partners.

HKBU is a strong choice if you want a smaller-cohort, more teaching-oriented department with reliable progression to UK or Hong Kong master's programmes, or to teaching-track careers.

### EduHK — BEd & BSc (Hons) Chemistry (JS8714)

EduHK's five-year co-terminal degree is the standard route to teaching chemistry in Hong Kong secondary schools. The first two years are mostly subject-knowledge in chemistry; from Year 3 students take education-theory modules, pedagogy and progressively longer teaching practica. Graduates are eligible for the Teacher Registration Number and full secondary teacher status in Hong Kong.

If your goal is to teach DSE Chemistry, JS8714 is more direct than a chemistry BSc followed by a PGDE — you save a year, and you graduate with a guaranteed pedagogy track record.

## Curriculum: what you actually study

A typical four-year BSc in Chemistry in Hong Kong contains:

**Year 1 (foundation)** — General Chemistry I and II, Mathematics for Sciences (calculus and linear algebra), General Physics, Introductory Biology (at some schools), University English, Chinese Language and a Common Core or General Education cluster.

**Year 2 (core)** — Analytical Chemistry, Organic Chemistry I and II, Inorganic Chemistry, Physical Chemistry I (thermodynamics & kinetics), instrumental analysis laboratory. Some programmes add Biochemistry I.

**Year 3 (advanced)** — Physical Chemistry II (quantum chemistry and spectroscopy), Inorganic Chemistry (transition metals, organometallics), Organic Spectroscopy, Polymer/Materials Chemistry, an environmental or industrial chemistry option, and a major laboratory module that combines synthesis with characterisation by NMR, IR, UV-Vis, MS and HPLC.

**Year 4 (specialisation and capstone)** — Stream-specific electives (e.g. medicinal chemistry, computational chemistry, electrochemistry, energy materials, forensic analysis, food chemistry) plus a **two-semester research project** in a faculty member's lab. The research project is the single most important element for postgraduate applications — pick your supervisor carefully, in Year 3.

Across all six chemistry departments, the **lab portion** is around 30–40% of contact hours. Expect long lab afternoons, scientific report writing, and progressive escalation from "follow the protocol" exercises in Year 1 to independent project design by Year 4.

## Internships, exchange and industry exposure

Hong Kong's chemistry industry is smaller than its finance or logistics sectors, but more diverse than students sometimes assume. Common internship destinations include:

- **Government Laboratory of Hong Kong** — public-sector analytical chemistry: food safety, controlled drugs, environmental forensics
- **Hospital Authority** clinical biochemistry labs and pathology services
- **Chinese medicine quality control labs** — both at universities (HKBU, CUHK ICMS) and the Hong Kong Department of Health
- **Pharmaceutical and biotech** — Roche, Pfizer regional offices, Wuxi AppTec, BGI (Shenzhen)
- **Materials and electronics** — ASMPT, Foxconn (Shenzhen), CATL, BYD's R&D labs
- **Cosmetics and personal care** — L'Oréal, Watsons own-brand, P&G regional R&D
- **Environmental consultancies** — ALS, AECOM, ERM, the Environmental Protection Department

Most BSc programmes encourage at least one summer internship before Year 4; PolyU's chemical technology pathway makes a multi-month placement a degree requirement.

Exchange to overseas partners (one semester or one year) is open to high-GPA students in their second or third year. Common chemistry destinations are the UK (Imperial, Edinburgh, Manchester), the US (UC system, University of Washington, Penn State), Germany (RWTH Aachen, TU München), Switzerland (ETH Zürich, EPFL) and Japan (Osaka University, Kyoto University).

## Postgraduate pathways

Roughly **30–40%** of chemistry BSc graduates in Hong Kong proceed directly to postgraduate study. Typical routes:

- **MPhil (2-year research master's)** — at HKU, CUHK, HKUST, PolyU, CityU and HKBU. Funded with a postgraduate studentship (around HK\$18,500/month in 2025); a near-universal stepping stone to PhD.
- **PhD (3-4 years)** — domestic universities or, with strong GPA and research output, top US/UK/EU institutions. The Hong Kong PhD Fellowship Scheme (HKPFS) pays around HK\$331,200/year plus a conference allowance for top international PhD applicants at any of the eight publicly funded universities.
- **Taught master's** — analytical chemistry, food science, environmental science, chemical biology, materials science, drug discovery and development. PolyU, CityU and HKBU all run year-long taught chemistry master's degrees.
- **Conversion master's overseas** — Hong Kong chemistry graduates routinely enter US PhD programmes directly (US PhDs do not require a separate master's), or 1-year UK taught master's degrees in specific areas (medicinal chemistry, materials, computational chemistry).

If a research career is the goal, the single biggest predictor of success is the Year 4 project: pick a productive lab, contribute meaningfully, get your name on a paper if you can, and ask your supervisor for a strong reference.

## Careers and starting salaries

The starting-salary picture for Hong Kong chemistry BSc graduates in 2025 (based on the JoinUST, HKU and Government Statistics outcomes):

- Entry-level QA/QC chemist (consumer goods, food, electronics): HK\$18,000–24,000/month
- Analytical chemist, government lab or Hospital Authority: HK\$25,000–30,000/month (with civil service pension scale beyond MPS Point 15)
- R&D chemist, pharmaceutical or chemical company: HK\$22,000–32,000/month
- Cosmetic formulation chemist: HK\$20,000–28,000/month
- Forensic chemistry (police, Government Laboratory): HK\$28,000+/month at entry
- Patent examiner / IP analyst (chemistry-trained): HK\$28,000–35,000/month
- Secondary school chemistry teacher (after BEd or PGDE): HK\$33,000–37,000/month starting on the Master Pay Scale

For Greater Bay Area roles, salaries in Shenzhen at major pharma/materials employers are converging with Hong Kong's, especially in the new-energy and semiconductor supply chain, and the IANG (Immigration Arrangements for Non-local Graduates) visa makes it straightforward for non-local students to stay in Hong Kong and access GBA roles.

## Choosing between the six programmes

A practical decision tree:

- **You want a research career and may go on to a PhD overseas** — HKU, CUHK or HKUST. The differences are stylistic: HKUST is more mathematical, CUHK has the strongest postgraduate placement record, HKU has the most flexibility in Year 1.
- **You want a job in industry straight after graduation** — PolyU (Chemical Technology) for the WIE work placement, or CityU's GREAT stream.
- **You want a specialist stream that's hard to find anywhere else in Hong Kong** — CityU's Cosmetic Chemistry or Forensic Chemistry streams.
- **You want a smaller, teaching-oriented department with a biology/biochemistry leaning** — HKBU.
- **You want to teach chemistry in a Hong Kong secondary school** — EduHK JS8714 (BEd & BSc Chemistry).

## Application strategy: putting Chemistry on your JUPAS list

Practical tips for the 2026 cycle:

1. **Take Chemistry as an elective** — virtually all programmes weight it. Mathematics Extended Part M1 or M2 is also weighted at most schools; taking M2 is particularly valuable if you're applying to HKUST or for a more theoretical chemistry pathway.
2. **Bandwidth your choices**. Putting HKU/CUHK/HKUST chemistry in Band A makes sense if your DSE prediction is around 27+. If you're tracking 23–26, CityU JS1202 and HKBU JS2510 belong in Band A; HKU/HKUST as aspirational Band A only if you're confident on M1/M2 and Chemistry.
3. **PolyU JS3011 is a flexible safety**. The scheme entry gives you Year 1 to choose between chemical technology and biotech — and the cut-off is more forgiving than the JS5102/JS6901 schemes.
4. **Don't overlook EduHK JS8714** if teaching is in your plan. The five-year integrated programme is a smoother path to qualified teacher status than BSc + PGDE.
5. **Personal statement and SLP**. Chemistry departments value evidence of independent lab interest — a school-based investigation, a research-themed extended essay, participation in the HKU/CUHK chemistry Olympiad or the Hong Kong Chemistry Olympiad, science-fair work or relevant volunteer work all help.

## Final word

Chemistry is one of the most portable bachelor's degrees you can take in Hong Kong: it leads into research, into a wide spread of industry roles, into teaching and into specialist niches like forensic and cosmetic chemistry that are difficult to reach through any other degree. The six chemistry-offering universities each occupy a clear niche, and the JUPAS cut-offs reward students who choose **based on programme fit**, not on prestige alone. Read each university's individual programme page on the JUPAS portal, sit in on an open-day lab demo if you can, and pick the lab culture you would actually want to spend four years in.
