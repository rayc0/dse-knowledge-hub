# Hong Kong Biotechnology & Biomedical Sciences Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

Biotechnology and biomedical sciences sit at the centre of Hong Kong's push to become a regional life-sciences and innovation hub. The Northern Metropolis InnoLife Healthtech Hub, the InnoHK research clusters at Science Park, and the new School of Medicine at HKUST have all reshaped what a biotech or biomedical sciences degree looks like in 2026. For DSE students who like biology, chemistry and quantitative thinking — but do not necessarily want clinical medicine — this family of programmes is now one of the most strategically positioned routes in JUPAS.

This guide walks through the full 2026 landscape: HKU's restructured Bachelor of Biomedical Sciences (JS6949), CUHK's Biomedical Sciences (JS4550), HKUST's Biotechnology (BIOT, under JS5100) and BSc in Biotechnology and Business (JS5811), PolyU's Biotechnology and Chemical Technology Scheme (JS3011) and Applied Biology with Biotechnology, and CityU's BSc in Biomedical Sciences. We compare admission scores, curriculum design, lab and internship structure, and what graduates actually do.

## Biotechnology vs Biomedical Sciences: What's the Difference

The two labels are often confused, but the underlying philosophy diverges sharply by the second year of study.

**Biomedical sciences** centres on the human body — molecular biology, physiology, genetics, pharmacology, anatomy and pathology — with the aim of understanding disease mechanisms and translating discoveries into diagnostics and therapeutics. Graduates often progress to MPhil/PhD research, MBBS through second-degree entry, dentistry, or roles in pharmaceutical R&D, regulatory affairs and clinical trials.

**Biotechnology** uses living organisms or molecular tools to make products and processes: vaccines, monoclonal antibodies, fermentation, agritech, synthetic biology, CRISPR-edited cell lines, biomanufacturing. The lens is engineering and applied — chemical engineering, process scale-up, quality assurance and intellectual property are taught alongside molecular biology.

In practice the curricula overlap significantly in years one and two, then specialise. A student wanting medical school as a backup should lean to biomedical sciences. A student attracted by start-ups, bio-manufacturing or the biotech investment side should lean to biotechnology — especially the BIBU model at HKUST.

## The Headline JUPAS Programmes for 2026

### HKU Bachelor of Biomedical Sciences (JS6949)

The University of Hong Kong's BBiomedSc is the flagship biomedical sciences degree in the city, housed within the LKS Faculty of Medicine (HKUMed) and routinely sitting in the top admission band after MBBS, BDS and Pharmacy.

From the 2025/26 intake onwards, BBiomedSc switched to a four-major structure within the same four-year, 240-credit programme. Students take 96 credits of prescribed major courses, 36 credits of Common Core, 18 credits of Language Enhancement, plus electives. The four majors are:

- **Biomedical Research** — molecular biology, genetics, bioinformatics, pharmacology; classic feeder into MPhil/PhD.
- **Bioinformatics** — genomics, precision medicine, AI in medical imaging, digital health.
- **Clinical Sciences** — designed for future healthcare professionals, healthcare management and public health pathways.
- **Health Technology** — synthetic biology, gene editing and AI in medicine, oriented to the biotech/medtech industry.

Admission is competitive: indicative best-five scores cluster in the high 30s to low 40s, with biology and chemistry essentially mandatory in the elective slots and Mathematics Extended Module M1 or M2 frequently counted as a bonus in the HKU score calculator.

### CUHK Biomedical Sciences (JS4550)

CUHK's Biomedical Sciences programme runs from the School of Biomedical Sciences inside the Faculty of Medicine. It offers a three-track design after a common foundation year — Cell and Molecular Biology, Drug Discovery, and Cell, Developmental and Stem Cell Biology — with strong feeders into CUHK's MBChB second-track and into PhD programmes at the Li Ka Shing Institute of Health Sciences. Students typically take an Honours Project in year four, often joint with the Prince of Wales Hospital research labs.

The programme requires Mathematics (compulsory part) and uses the standard CUHK 4C+2X formula. Biology and Chemistry are the conventional pairing; one of them is usually treated as a required elective by score weighting. Best-five medians for recent intakes sit in the 35–38 range, though the highest-scoring offers go well above 40 because the cohort heavily overlaps with MBChB applicants who use it as a JUPAS Band A backup.

### HKUST Biotechnology (within JS5100, School of Science) and BIBU (JS5811)

HKUST runs two distinct biotech routes:

The **BSc in Biotechnology (BIOT)** sits inside the broad-based School of Science admission (JS5100). Students declare the BIOT major from year two and choose one of two tracks introduced from the 2023 intake: an **Applied Bioscience Track** (cell biology, biomanufacturing, molecular diagnostics) or an **Entrepreneurship Track** (start-up incubation, IP, biotech finance). The curriculum is built on biochemistry, cell biology, molecular biology, microbiology and genetics, then layered with applied modules.

The **BSc in Biotechnology and Business (BIBU, JS5811)** is jointly offered by the School of Science and the School of Business and Management. It is one of the only formal dual-discipline biotech degrees in the region, combining life-science fundamentals with accounting, finance, economics, marketing and operations management. JS5811 has a small intake — well under 50 — and admission medians regularly exceed 35, since the programme attracts students aiming at biotech venture capital, equity research or biotech management consulting.

### PolyU Biotechnology and Chemical Technology Scheme (JS3011) and BSc Hons Applied Biology with Biotechnology

PolyU's **JS3011** is a broad scheme that, after the first year, splits into two BSc (Hons) degrees: **Applied Biology with Biotechnology** and **Chemical Technology**. The combined intake is around 55 places. The biotech specialism is run out of the Department of Applied Biology and Chemical Technology (ABCT) and is the most chemistry-heavy of the local biotech programmes — fermentation, bioprocess engineering, food biotechnology and Chinese medicinal product analysis feature strongly.

PolyU also offers a Senior-Year Entry (SYE) Bachelor of Science (Honours) in Applied Biology with Biotechnology for sub-degree articulation, which is relevant for HD/AD students rather than direct DSE applicants.

### CityU BSc in Biomedical Sciences and BSc in Biotechnology

CityU runs both a BSc in Biomedical Sciences (within the College of Science) and biotech-oriented BSc programmes through its Department of Biomedical Sciences and Department of Chemistry. The programmes are typically a step lower in admission score than HKU/CUHK but offer strong access to InnoHK research at the AIM HK and CHFS centres. They are a sensible Band B or C choice for students with mid-30s best-five scores.

## 2026 JUPAS Admission Score Expectations

Exact medians shift every cycle; the 2025 JUPAS admission scores published by each institution are the most reliable proxy for 2026 planning. Indicative best-five (or programme-specific formula) ranges for recent intakes:

- **HKU JS6949 BBiomedSc** — median around 39–41; lower-quartile typically not below 36.
- **CUHK JS4550 Biomedical Sciences** — median around 35–38; lower-quartile around 33–34.
- **HKUST JS5811 BIBU** — median around 33–35; small intake so volatility is high.
- **HKUST JS5100 (with BIOT major target)** — broad-based admission, median around 30–33.
- **PolyU JS3011** — median around 27–30 depending on the year.
- **CityU Biomedical Sciences** — median around 27–30.

DSE subject expectations are remarkably consistent: a clean **5 in Chemistry**, **5 in Biology**, level 4 or above in English, level 4 in Maths (compulsory part), and either Physics, Maths M1/M2, or another science as the fifth weighted subject. Citizenship and Social Development is pass/fail and does not contribute weighted points.

A practical rule of thumb for 2026: aim for **two 5* in science electives** if HKU JS6949 is the realistic dream programme; aim for **one 5** and **one 5*** in Bio/Chem if CUHK JS4550 is the target; mid-30s with a strong 5 in Biology is enough to be competitive at HKUST BIBU and most PolyU/CityU programmes.

## Curriculum: What You Actually Study Across Four Years

Most local programmes follow a 120-credit (HKUST/CUHK) or 240-credit (HKU) four-year structure. A representative trajectory:

**Year 1 (foundation).** Cell biology, general chemistry, organic chemistry, an introductory biostatistics or quantitative biology course, plus university Common Core / GE breadth requirements.

**Year 2 (core sciences).** Molecular biology, biochemistry, genetics, microbiology, immunology, physiology. Wet-lab work intensifies — pipetting, PCR, gel electrophoresis, basic cell culture.

**Year 3 (specialisation).** Track-specific modules: pharmacology and pathology for biomedical sciences; bioprocess engineering, fermentation and synthetic biology for biotech; bioinformatics and biostatistics for the data tracks. Many students complete a summer internship at InnoHK, Hong Kong Science Park tenants (e.g. Sanwa Biotech, Insilico Medicine, Prenetics), or government labs at the Department of Health.

**Year 4 (capstone).** Final-year project or Honours research thesis — typically a one-year wet-lab project in a faculty supervisor's lab — plus business/IP electives for BIBU, regulatory affairs or clinical trial design electives for biomedical tracks.

Lab safety certifications (BSL-2 work, animal-handling permits) are integrated. Several programmes — particularly HKUST BIBU and HKU's Health Technology major — embed mandatory innovation/entrepreneurship modules where teams pitch biotech business cases to industry mentors.

## Postgraduate and Professional Pathways

A biotech or biomedical sciences bachelor's is rarely a terminal degree. The dominant pathways after graduation:

**Research routes.** MPhil or direct-entry PhD at HKU, CUHK, HKUST, PolyU, CityU; the local UGC funds full RPg scholarships at competitive stipend levels. Overseas options are strong — Imperial, UCL, ETH, Karolinska, UCSD and Oxbridge regularly admit Hong Kong BBiomedSc graduates, especially those with publications from Honours projects.

**Medical second-entry.** A non-trivial share of CUHK JS4550 and HKU JS6949 students apply for MBBS/MBChB as graduate-entry students, locally or in Australia/UK/Ireland. The biomedical sciences degree is a structurally favoured pre-med background.

**Allied health conversion.** Two-year fast-track Masters or postgraduate diplomas in Genetic Counselling, Clinical Embryology, Medical Physics, Public Health, or Health Informatics are increasingly popular.

**Direct industry employment.** Roles in QA/QC at pharmaceutical and food biotech companies (Lee's Pharmaceutical, Hong Kong Genome Institute, Avalon GloboCare), in regulatory affairs at the Department of Health, or in clinical research associate (CRA) positions at CROs (Parexel, IQVIA, Labcorp Hong Kong). The Hong Kong Science Park biomedical cluster now hosts well over 250 tenant companies, dramatically improving on-shore industry hiring compared to a decade ago.

**Biotech investment, consulting and journalism.** BIBU and HKU Health Technology graduates particularly target equity research at Goldman Sachs/Morgan Stanley healthcare desks, biotech VC (Morningside, Sequoia Capital), strategy consulting healthcare practice (McKinsey, BCG, L.E.K.), and increasingly the policy side at HKSAR's Health Bureau and Innovation, Technology and Industry Bureau.

## Industry Salary and Outlook in 2026

Entry-level wet-lab research assistant roles at universities and InnoHK clusters pay around HK\$18,000–22,000 per month. Industry roles at pharma/biotech companies typically start HK\$22,000–28,000, with QA/regulatory tracks pushing HK\$30,000+ within two to three years. The biotech-business hybrid roles — biotech equity research analyst, biotech VC associate, healthcare strategy consultant — start considerably higher at HK\$45,000–70,000 monthly base, comparable to top finance fresh-grad bands, but admission to these tracks is fiercely competitive and typically requires a BIBU, HKU Health Technology, or BBA/BSc dual track.

The structural tailwind is real: Hong Kong's pharmaceutical and biotechnology export and re-export trade, the IPO pipeline of biotech firms under HKEX Chapter 18A, and Mainland integration via the Greater Bay Area Drug and Medical Device Connect scheme are all pushing demand upward. Realistic graduates who plan early — Honours research, internships, a strong final-year project and either an MPhil or a healthcare-finance pivot — are well placed.

## Choosing Between the Programmes: A Practical Framework

Use these heuristics rather than rank alone:

- **You want maximum optionality and might still pursue medicine** → HKU JS6949 (Clinical Sciences major) or CUHK JS4550.
- **You're attracted to AI, genomics and computational biology** → HKU JS6949 Bioinformatics major, or HKUST BIOT with electives from the Department of Computer Science and Engineering.
- **You want biotech but also a finance/consulting exit option** → HKUST JS5811 BIBU.
- **You like chemistry, food biotech, fermentation, traditional Chinese medicine product science** → PolyU JS3011 with the Applied Biology with Biotechnology stream.
- **Your DSE is mid-30s and you want a strong biomedical pathway with InnoHK access** → CityU BSc Biomedical Sciences.
- **You want to start a biotech company** → HKUST BIBU or HKU Health Technology major.

## Application Tips and Timeline

Portfolio is not formally required for any of these programmes, but personal statements, interview performance and OEA (Other Experiences and Achievements) all matter materially. Strong applicants typically have:

- A summer research attachment at a university lab, a Hong Kong Science Park company, or a hospital research office.
- Participation in the Hong Kong Biology Olympiad, Hong Kong Chemistry Olympiad, or the iGEM (International Genetically Engineered Machine) competition.
- A clear narrative about a specific disease area or biotech application that motivates the choice — admissions tutors at HKU and CUHK specifically test for this in interviews.

The 2026 JUPAS cycle follows the standard timeline: application opens December 2025, programme choice amendment deadline late May 2026, DSE results release on 16 July 2026, programme choice finalisation by 19–20 July, main round offers on 6–7 August 2026.

## The Bottom Line

For 2026 DSE students, the biotechnology and biomedical sciences family is a strategically smart pick. The local industry, regulatory and research infrastructure has grown materially in the past five years, postgraduate funding remains generous, and the cross-over routes — to medicine, to healthcare finance, to biotech entrepreneurship, to AI in medicine — are wider than they were even three years ago. Choosing among HKU, CUHK, HKUST, PolyU and CityU should hinge on the specific track you want, not just rankings.
