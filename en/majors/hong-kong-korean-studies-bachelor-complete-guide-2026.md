# Hong Kong Korean Studies Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

If your screen time for the last year has been split between BTS comebacks, K-dramas on Netflix, and the occasional Samsung product launch, you are not alone — and you are also, statistically, sitting in the largest cohort of Korean-language learners Hong Kong has ever produced. The Korean wave (Hallyu) is no longer a niche interest. It is a HKD 200 billion+ global content economy, and Hong Kong sits squarely on its regional distribution path. For DSE candidates choosing a major in 2026, "Korean Studies" has moved from a hobbyist elective to a credible humanities pathway with concrete career outcomes in Korean conglomerates, consulates, content distribution, and K-beauty trading.

This guide walks through the only standalone BA Korean Studies major in Hong Kong (HKU), the four other UGC universities that offer Korean as a minor or elective, the TOPIK certification ladder that employers actually care about, exchange options to Seoul, and the salary bands Korean-speaking graduates can realistically expect in their first three years of work.

## 1. The Korean Studies Landscape in Hong Kong

Unlike Japanese Studies — where HKU and CUHK both run full majors — Korean Studies is more concentrated. Only HKU offers Korean Studies as a **full major** at undergraduate level. The other four UGC universities (CUHK, CityU, HKBU, PolyU) treat Korean as a **minor programme**, language stream, or free elective. EdUHK and Lingnan offer Korean as a credit-bearing language course but do not formalise it as a minor.

This means JUPAS candidates who are firm about Korean Studies as a primary discipline almost always end up applying to HKU JS6054 (Bachelor of Arts), then declaring the Korean Studies major in Year 1 or Year 2. Candidates from PolyU, CityU, HKBU, or CUHK typically pair Korean with a primary degree in Business, Translation, Communication, or Linguistics.

### Why a "Korean major" rather than self-study

The honest question every DSE student should ask: with Talk To Me In Korean podcasts, Duolingo, and YouTube tutors available for free, why pay HKD 168,000 in tuition over four years to study Korean formally?

Three answers that hold up in 2026:

1. **TOPIK Level 5–6 is the employer threshold.** Korean conglomerates (Samsung Hong Kong, LG, Hyundai, Lotte) and Korean SME branches that hire bilingual staff in Hong Kong almost universally require TOPIK Level 5 or 6 — the top two bands of the six-level Test of Proficiency in Korean. Self-learners rarely reach this level without immersion. University major routes structure the immersion (compulsory or strongly encouraged exchange to Seoul) and the assessment cadence (Year 3 TOPIK sittings) that get students there.
2. **Area studies content matters.** Korean Studies is not just language drills. It is Korean history (Joseon dynasty to colonial period to democratisation), contemporary politics (inter-Korean relations, chaebol economy), film and media (Korean New Wave, K-drama production systems), and gender / society modules. Employers in K-content distribution and Korea-Hong Kong trade desks value graduates who understand the regulatory and cultural context, not just the language.
3. **University-brokered exchange.** HKU and CUHK have direct exchange MOUs with Seoul National University (SNU), Yonsei, Korea University ("SKY"), Sogang, Ewha, and Hanyang. A semester or year on these campuses, with tuition waived through exchange, is structurally cheaper than self-funded study abroad and looks materially different on a CV.

## 2. HKU JS6054 BA — Korean Studies Major Route

The University of Hong Kong's Bachelor of Arts (JUPAS code **JS6054**) is the gateway. JS6054 is a wide-portal BA admitting roughly 140–180 students per year across all Arts disciplines, and students declare a major during Year 1 (or end of Year 1). Korean Studies is one of the language-and-area-studies majors offered by the **School of Modern Languages and Cultures (SMLC)** within the Faculty of Arts.

### JUPAS admission scores (best 5 / best 6, indicative)

HKU does not publish a separate cutoff for the Korean Studies major because students enter via the general BA. Indicative HKU BA best-5 scores for 2024 and 2025 intake sat around **22–24** at the median, with the top 25% around **25–27**. For a strong shot at JS6054 in 2026, candidates should plan for:

- Chinese Language: Level 4 or above
- English Language: Level 4 or above (Level 5 strongly preferred for Arts)
- Mathematics: Level 3 minimum
- Citizenship & Social Development: Attained
- Two electives: ideally one humanities (Literature in English, Chinese History, Geography, Economics) and one flexible

Korean Studies, like other SMLC majors, does **not** require any prior Korean language experience at DSE level. The expectation is that students start from Korean 1 (KORE 1011) in Year 1.

### Major structure: KORE course progression

The Korean Studies major requires 60 credits, typically structured as:

- **Year 1**: KORE 1011 Korean 1.1 (6 credits) + KORE 1012 Korean 1.2 (6 credits) — beginner grammar, Hangul, basic conversation. Target: TOPIK Level 1–2 by end of Year 1.
- **Year 2**: KORE 2011 Korean 2.1 + KORE 2012 Korean 2.2 (12 credits total) plus an introductory area studies module on Korean culture, history, or media. Target: TOPIK Level 3 by end of Year 2.
- **Year 3**: Semester or full year on exchange in Korea (typically SNU, Yonsei, Korea University, Sogang, or Hanyang). KORE 3011 / 3012 Korean 3.1 / 3.2 are taken on exchange or at HKU on return. Target: TOPIK Level 4 by end of Year 3.
- **Year 4**: KORE 4011 / 4012 advanced Korean, capstone seminar in Korean Studies (research essay in Korean or English), and area studies electives. Target: TOPIK Level 5 (employer threshold) by graduation.

### Faculty of Arts entrance scholarships

HKU Faculty of Arts offers entrance scholarships covering up to four years of full tuition (HKD 42,100 × 4 = HKD 168,400) for students with outstanding DSE results. The bar is informally around best-5 of 28+ with a substantive Arts portfolio. Students with high scores who choose a less-competitive Arts pathway (like Korean Studies versus Law and Literature) sometimes find these scholarships more accessible than at the BBA or LLB tier.

## 3. Korean as a Minor at CUHK, CityU, HKBU, PolyU

For candidates whose primary discipline is Business, Translation, or Communication — but who want Korean as a serious second skill — the minor route is the practical choice.

### CUHK Korean Minor — Department of Linguistics and Modern Languages

CUHK's Korean minor is the most academically rigorous of the minors and explicitly targets **TOPIK Level 3 – 4 ("independent user")** by completion. The minor sits within the Department of Linguistics and Modern Languages and is open to students from any CUHK major. Five courses (15 units) of Korean language are required, typically Korean I through Korean V, with one cultural-studies elective. Students often pair the Korean minor with majors in Translation, Linguistics, Global Studies, or Business Administration.

### CityU Korean Studies Minor

City University of Hong Kong offers **MNR-KORS Korean Studies** as a 15-credit minor under the Department of Linguistics and Translation. Required language courses (Korean I, II, III) plus area studies electives in Korean society, popular culture, or business communication. CityU's K-content offer is strong because the College of Liberal Arts and Social Sciences runs occasional electives on Korean media industries that complement the language sequence.

### HKBU Korean Language Programme

HKBU offers Korean as a language course series (KORE 1110, 2110, 3110) under the Language Centre and Faculty of Arts. It is not formally branded as a "Korean Studies minor" in the same way as CUHK or CityU, but students can accumulate 12–15 credits of Korean to fulfil the university's language proficiency requirements. HKBU's strength is the integration of Korean with Communication and Film majors — useful for students targeting K-content production or PR roles.

### PolyU Korean Minor (CBS / LST)

PolyU offers a Korean minor jointly through the **Department of Chinese and Bilingual Studies (CBS)** and the **Department of Language Science and Technology (LST)**. It is a 15-credit minor combining Korean language (4 courses) with one Korean culture / society / business communication module. The PolyU minor is popular with students in Hotel and Tourism Management (SHTM), Translation, and Marketing — three disciplines where Korean fluency directly maps to employer demand.

## 4. TOPIK Certification Ladder

Korean-language employers do not assess applicants by GPA or transcript. They look at the **TOPIK certificate**, the six-level Test of Proficiency in Korean run by the Korean Ministry of Education's NIIED. Understanding the ladder is essential for any Hong Kong student planning a Korean career.

- **TOPIK I (Levels 1–2)**: Beginner. Basic survival Korean. End of Year 1 for HKU majors, end of Year 2 for minors. Not an employable threshold.
- **TOPIK II (Levels 3–4)**: Intermediate. Can hold professional conversations on familiar topics. End of Year 2 (HKU major) or end of minor (CUHK / CityU / PolyU). Threshold for some entry-level customer service or tourism roles.
- **TOPIK II (Levels 5–6)**: Advanced. Near-native business and academic Korean. Required for corporate roles at Samsung, LG, Hyundai, Lotte, KOTRA, and the Consulate-General of the Republic of Korea in Hong Kong. Typically reached only after a year of exchange in Korea plus continued study.

The TOPIK is held twice a year in Hong Kong, usually April and October, administered through the Consulate-General. Registration fee is around HKD 350 per attempt. Most HKU Korean Studies majors aim for one TOPIK sitting per year from Year 2 onward.

## 5. Exchange to Seoul: SNU, Yonsei, Korea University

The HKU SMLC Korean Studies major **strongly encourages** (in practice, near-mandates) a semester or year-long exchange to a Korean university in Year 3. HKU's existing MOUs cover:

- **Seoul National University (SNU)** — Korea's flagship national research university, the "S" in SKY.
- **Yonsei University** — established 1885 as Korea's first Western higher education institution; HKU's free Yonsei Online Korean Course Basic 1 is also available to all HKU students as a primer.
- **Korea University** — the "K" in SKY; College of International Studies and the Department of Korean Language and Literature host most exchange students.
- **Sogang University** — known for its Korean Language Education Centre (KLEC), arguably the most respected Korean-as-a-foreign-language programme in Korea.
- **Hanyang University**, **Ewha Womans University**, **Sungkyunkwan University**, and **Korea Foundation for Advanced Studies** partner programmes.

Exchange tuition is waived under bilateral agreements — students continue paying HKD 42,100 per year to HKU and pay no SNU / Yonsei / KU tuition. Out-of-pocket costs are accommodation (KRW 600,000 – 900,000 / month in Sinchon or Anam dormitories ≈ HKD 3,400 – 5,200) plus living expenses of roughly HKD 5,000 – 7,000 per month. Several Korean Foundation and HKU Arts overseas-experience scholarships (HKD 20,000 – 40,000) help offset this.

CUHK runs parallel exchanges with SNU and Yonsei through the Office of Academic Links, accessible to Korean minor students who apply competitively against students from other majors. CityU and PolyU exchanges to Korea are smaller in volume but available through institution-wide exchange schemes.

## 6. Career Pathways and Starting Salaries

The Hong Kong job market for Korean-speaking graduates has expanded significantly over the past five years on the back of Korean conglomerate expansion in Asia and K-content distribution. As of February 2026, there are approximately 130–150 active Hong Kong-based job postings explicitly requiring Korean language skills on Glassdoor and JobsDB.

### Korean conglomerates (Samsung, LG, Hyundai, Lotte)

The Hong Kong branches of major Korean firms hire Korean-speaking graduates into:

- **Marketing and Brand Communications** — Samsung Hong Kong, LG, Lotte Duty Free. Starting salary around **HKD 22,000 – 28,000 per month** for fresh graduates with TOPIK 5+. Career path leads to Assistant Brand Manager (HKD 35,000+) by Year 3–4.
- **Trade / Distribution / Logistics** — Hyundai Motor Hong Kong, Hyundai Glovis, Hyundai Merchant Marine. Starting salary HKD 23,000 – 30,000. Strong overlap with logistics and supply chain backgrounds.
- **Sales and Account Management** — particularly in B2B electronics, automotive parts, and FMCG. HKD 20,000 – 26,000 base plus commission.

### KOTRA Hong Kong Branch Office

**KOTRA** (Korea Trade-Investment Promotion Agency) operates a Hong Kong office that supports Korean SMEs entering the Hong Kong and Greater Bay Area markets. KOTRA hires locally engaged staff for market research, trade fair coordination, and matchmaking. Starting salary around HKD 24,000 – 30,000 with strong networking exposure to Korean exporters. Many KOTRA local staff move on to Korean SME branch positions after 2–3 years.

### Consulate-General of the Republic of Korea in Hong Kong

The Korean Consulate hires local staff for visa processing, cultural affairs, and political reporting. Starting salaries are stable but unspectacular (HKD 22,000 – 27,000) but the prestige and embassy benefits (extended annual leave, training in Korea) make it a sought-after first job. TOPIK Level 5 effectively required.

### K-Content, IP Licensing, and Entertainment

This is the fastest-growing segment. Hong Kong is a regional distribution hub for K-pop concert tours, K-drama licensing to mainland streamers (where direct distribution is restricted), and K-beauty brands entering Asia. Roles include:

- **K-pop / K-drama licensing executive** — companies like SM Entertainment Asia, JYP, HYBE Asia, Studio Dragon distribution arms. Starting salary HKD 24,000 – 32,000.
- **Concert and event promotion** — Live Nation, AEG Presents, and local Korean event agencies. Project-based plus base salary; HKD 22,000 – 30,000.
- **K-beauty / fashion brand management** — Amorepacific, LG H&H, Olive Young, JM Solution. Strong overlap with Marketing graduates who speak Korean. HKD 23,000 – 30,000.

### Higher pathways: Korea-based employment

A meaningful subset of HKU Korean Studies graduates with TOPIK Level 6 take Korea-based positions on the **E-7 specialist work visa**. Typical starting compensation for foreign IT or business graduates in Seoul ranges KRW 38,000,000 – 90,000,000 per year (≈ HKD 220,000 – 520,000), with engineering specialists at Hyundai and SK earning KRW 55,000,000 – 75,000,000 (≈ HKD 320,000 – 435,000) starting. Cost of living in Seoul is roughly 70–80% of Hong Kong, so disposable income is competitive.

## 7. Honest Trade-offs Before You Commit

Korean Studies is a high-passion, moderate-employability major. To be candid:

1. **Smaller absolute job market than Japanese or Chinese.** Hong Kong has thousands of Japanese-speaking roles and tens of thousands of Putonghua roles. Korean-speaking roles number in the low hundreds. The pool is growing, but it is still niche.
2. **TOPIK Level 5 is hard.** Achieving Level 5 in four years requires consistent daily practice, a successful exchange year, and active extracurricular immersion (Korean drama club, language tandem partners). Students who treat the major as a hobby rarely cross the employer threshold.
3. **Salary ceiling lower than STEM in the medium term.** Korean specialists at HKD 30,000 starting are competitive at graduation, but salary growth tracks language-and-area-studies professionals (HKD 50,000 – 70,000 mid-career) rather than tech or finance (HKD 80,000+). Pairing Korean with a STEM or business primary degree is the highest-ceiling combination.

If you are comfortable with these trade-offs and genuinely passionate about Korean language, history, and culture — not just K-pop fan engagement — Hong Kong's Korean Studies pathways in 2026 are more accessible, better-resourced, and better-connected to employer pipelines than at any point in the past decade.
