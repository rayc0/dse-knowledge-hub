# Hong Kong Surveying Bachelor Complete Guide 2026 - HKU JS6016, PolyU JS3789, HKIS Six Divisions, RICS Dual Membership, APC Assessment of Professional Competence and Government Quantity Surveyor Starting Salary

Surveyors are the unsung professionals behind every square foot of Hong Kong's real estate, construction and infrastructure economy. Together with architects and engineers they form the so-called "three pillars" of the built environment - and yet most DSE candidates have never heard of them. From valuing Grade A office towers in Central, controlling project costs at the Kai Tak development, to setting boundaries for the Northern Metropolis in the New Territories, surveyors are the professionals whose stamps are required by law on virtually every land or property transaction. The Hong Kong Institute of Surveyors (HKIS), incorporated in 1984 and granted statutory status under Cap. 1148 of the Hong Kong Institute of Surveyors Ordinance in 1990, today has more than 11,000 members spread across six specialist divisions, each with its own scope of practice and career ladder.

What makes surveying particularly attractive for Hong Kong school leavers is its dual qualification advantage. Graduates of an accredited bachelor programme who pass the HKIS Assessment of Professional Competence (APC) automatically qualify for direct entry to the Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors (RICS). One licence then opens the door to practise in roughly 150 RICS-recognised jurisdictions including the United Kingdom, Australia, Singapore, Canada, the United Arab Emirates and Malaysia. This guide unpacks the two JUPAS-admitted surveying degrees for 2026 entry - the University of Hong Kong's BSc(Surveying) (JS6016) and the Hong Kong Polytechnic University's BSc(Hons) in Surveying (JS3789) - covering curriculum, entrance requirements, division pathways, APC training, government and private market starting salaries, and overseas mobility.

## What surveyors actually do in Hong Kong

Land in Hong Kong is scarce and prices are stratospheric, so every parcel of ground involves enormous capital and complicated bundles of rights. Surveyors are involved at every stage of the property life cycle: land disposal and demarcation, design and feasibility, construction and cost control, valuation, management, and finally transactions and disputes.

- **Land disposal and demarcation**: Land surveyors at the Lands Department fix boundaries and prepare lease conditions
- **Construction cost control**: Quantity surveyors prepare bills of quantities, run tenders and certify interim payments for developers and contractors
- **Property valuation**: General practice surveyors value real estate for banks, the Inland Revenue Department and listed companies, with the Companies Ordinance and the Banking Ordinance both requiring valuations to be signed by an authorised valuer
- **Building inspection and repair**: Building surveyors run statutory inspection schemes such as the Mandatory Building Inspection Scheme (MBIS) and issue legal notices to building owners
- **Planning and development**: Planning and development surveyors prepare feasibility studies and negotiate land premiums with the government
- **Property and facility management**: PFM surveyors run shopping malls, large estates and commercial buildings

Put simply, almost any transaction, development or management activity involving land or buildings in Hong Kong needs a registered HKIS surveyor's professional sign-off.

## The six HKIS divisions

HKIS members must elect one of six specialist divisions. Each division has its own APC syllabus and a separate examination panel, and once admitted, members work within that scope of practice for their entire career.

| HKIS Division | Abbreviation | Scope of Practice | Typical Employers |
|---|---|---|---|
| **General Practice** | GP | Property valuation, leasing, sales agency | JLL, Cushman & Wakefield, Savills, Rating and Valuation Department |
| **Quantity Surveying** | QS | Construction estimation, contracts, claims | Developers, contractors, consulting firms, Housing Authority, Airport Authority |
| **Building Surveying** | BS | Building maintenance, MBIS, unauthorised building works | Buildings Department, URA, private consultancies |
| **Land Surveying** | LS | Boundary, mapping, GIS | Lands Department, Planning Department, CEDD |
| **Planning & Development** | P&D | Urban planning, land premium negotiation, development studies | Developers' planning departments, planning consultancies |
| **Property & Facility Management** | PFM | Estate management, facilities engineering | CK Property, Swire Properties, Link REIT, Airport Authority |

Graduates from the four-year BSc(Surveying) programmes can usually choose freely among the GP, QS, BS and P&D divisions for their APC training. Land surveying (LS) requires an LS-specific degree such as PolyU's JS3130 BSc(Hons) in Spatial Data Science and Smart Cities (Land Surveying and Geo-Informatics), and PFM has its own dedicated specialism.

## JUPAS surveying programmes compared

### 1. HKU BSc(Surveying) (JS6016)

HKU's surveying degree is run by the Department of Real Estate and Construction (REC) within the Faculty of Architecture. It is the world's first complete six-division undergraduate programme, and remains one of the very few programmes globally to hold simultaneous accreditation from HKIS, RICS, the Chartered Institute of Building (CIOB) and the Pacific Association of Quantity Surveyors (PAQS).

- **Length**: 4 years
- **Intake**: Approximately 60 students per year (JUPAS plus non-JUPAS)
- **JUPAS scoring**: Best 6 (4C+2X), with English and Mathematics compulsory
- **2025 minimum JUPAS Best 6 score**: Around 22 (median around 27)
- **Entrance requirements**: HKDSE 4 core + 2 elective subjects at "3322 + 33", with English Level 4 commonly required
- **Year 1 foundation**: Built environment, economics, statistics, law, design and communication
- **Division streaming begins Year 2**: All six divisions are open, with 80 credits of core and elective courses needed before graduation
- **Year 3 summer placement**: Mandatory 8-12 week internship, with most students placed at JLL, Cushman & Wakefield, Rider Levett Bucknall, Arup or the Lands Department
- **Overseas exchange**: Partnerships with UCL, the University of Reading, the National University of Singapore and RMIT in Melbourne

### 2. PolyU BSc(Hons) in Surveying (JS3789)

PolyU's BSc(Hons) Surveying is run by the Department of Building and Real Estate (BRE). The programme was spun off from the umbrella JS3100 "Building and Real Estate Programme" in 2024 to become a standalone JS3789 degree, focused on the GP, QS, BS and P&D divisions. PolyU has the longest-running surveying programme in Hong Kong, and its alumni dominate the senior ranks of the Housing Authority, Airport Authority and Urban Renewal Authority.

- **Length**: 4 years (with an optional 6-month paid Work-Integrated Education placement in Year 3)
- **Intake**: Approximately 70 students per year
- **2025 minimum JUPAS Best 5 score**: Around 19
- **Entrance requirements**: HKDSE "3322 + 33", with Mathematics Level 3 or above
- **Year 1 common core**: Construction technology, built environment law, principles of economics, sustainability
- **Year 2**: Economics and finance, law, construction engineering, real estate, property management
- **Years 3-4**: Streaming into GP, QS, BS or P&D, capped by a final-year capstone project
- **Industry network**: PolyU is the largest single source of HKIS, CIOB and RICS-accredited graduates in Hong Kong

### HKU JS6016 vs PolyU JS3789: how to choose

| Comparison | HKU JS6016 | PolyU JS3789 |
|---|---|---|
| **International accreditation** | HKIS (all 6 divisions) + RICS + CIOB + PAQS | HKIS (4 divisions) + RICS + CIOB |
| **Year 1 flexibility** | All 6 divisions open | Mainly 4 divisions (GP/QS/BS/P&D) |
| **Campus and culture** | Comprehensive university, strong overseas exchange | Engineering-heavy, tight industry links |
| **Average JUPAS intake** | Best 6 around 27 | Best 5 around 19 |
| **Industry ties** | Stronger in valuation and planning consultancy | Stronger in QS and government departments |

For prospective land surveyors (LS), the recommended pathway is **PolyU JS3130 BSc(Hons) in Spatial Data Science and Smart Cities (Land Surveying and Geo-Informatics)**, which holds the only HKIS Land Surveying Division accreditation in Hong Kong.

## DSE subject choices and admissions strategy

Surveying is not a "god-tier" programme like medicine, dentistry or pharmacy, but starting salaries have risen sharply in the last five years and competition has tightened. DSE candidates should think about subject choice carefully:

1. **Mathematics is compulsory**: Surveying involves area calculation, valuation modelling and quantity take-off; Level 3 is the floor, but PolyU recruits often score Level 4 or higher
2. **Physics or Economics**: Physics helps in building structures and building surveying; Economics is essential for general practice and planning surveying
3. **BAFS**: Direct relevance for QS and valuation work
4. **English Level 4 or above**: HKU's surveying programme is taught entirely in English, and the APC examination is also in English
5. **M1/M2 extended modules**: HKU's REC welcomes M1/M2 candidates because of the cost modelling and real estate finance content

For JUPAS Band A strategy, given the gap between HKU JS6016 and PolyU JS3789 cut-off scores:

- **A1**: HKU JS6016 BSc(Surveying)
- **A2**: PolyU JS3789 BSc(Hons) Surveying
- **A3**: PolyU JS3791 BSc(Hons) Building Engineering and Management

## HKIS APC: the road to becoming a chartered surveyor

A bachelor's degree alone is not enough to practise. Graduates must complete the HKIS Assessment of Professional Competence (APC) to become a Member of HKIS (MHKIS).

### APC pathway (QS, GP, BS divisions)

1. **Structured Training**: After graduation, candidates join an HKIS-recognised employer where a Counsellor (a senior surveyor) supervises their training. The minimum structured training period is 24 months for QS, GP and BS, and 36 months for LS.
2. **Diary**: Candidates keep a daily log book mapping their work to the published HKIS Core Competencies. The log book must be signed by the candidate and Supervisor, and counter-signed by the Counsellor every three months.
3. **Final Assessment**: Three components -
   - **Critical Analysis Report**: A 5,000-word English-language professional report on a real project, focusing on the candidate's professional judgment
   - **Written Assessment**: A 3-hour examination covering surveying theory, Hong Kong law and professional conduct
   - **Interview Assessment**: An approximately 60-minute oral examination by a panel of three senior HKIS assessors
4. **Pre-Qualification Structured Learning**: For QS candidates, no less than 20 hours of CPD-style learning is required in the 12 months immediately preceding the assessment, with records submitted at least 7 days before the assessment date.
5. **Membership**: Successful candidates become MHKIS, pay annual fees and complete 60 hours of Continuing Professional Development (CPD) every year to maintain status.

According to the HKIS Rules and Guide to APC (June 2025 edition), **from 1 April 2026 all Building Surveying APC submissions must be made through the APC Digital System**, with paper submissions no longer accepted. Other divisions are following on a rolling basis.

### RICS dual membership

Because both HKU JS6016 and PolyU JS3789 are RICS-accredited, MHKIS members can apply for RICS Direct Entry without further examination, becoming MRICS (Member of RICS). With one MRICS designation, surveyors can then practise in around 150 RICS-recognised jurisdictions including the UK, Australia, New Zealand, Singapore, Canada and the UAE. This is a major advantage over architects (HKIA) and engineers (HKIE), whose international mutual recognition is much narrower.

## Government and private sector salaries

Hong Kong surveyors typically follow one of three career tracks: civil servant (Surveyor grade), statutory body (Housing Authority, Airport Authority, URA, MTR), or private practice (the Big Five international firms, Hong Kong developers, QS consultancies).

### 1. Government Surveyor grade

The government's Surveyor grade (which encompasses Surveyor, Land Surveyor and Estate Surveyor) sits in the Professional and Technical class. The entry rank is Surveyor II.

- **Surveyor II**: Master Pay Scale (MPS) Points 25-33, around HKD 50,665 - 81,890 per month in 2025
- Most fresh graduates enter at HKD 50,665 - 60,925 depending on APC status and seniority
- After APC, promotion to **Surveyor I** lifts pay to MPS 34-44 (HKD 85,090 - 130,720 per month)
- Senior surveyors can rise to **Chief Surveyor** at the Directorate Pay Scale D1 (around HKD 187,500 per month)

The main employing departments are the Lands Department, Buildings Department, Rating and Valuation Department, Planning Department, Civil Engineering and Development Department, and Housing Department.

### 2. Statutory and semi-government bodies

- **Housing Authority** (Housing Department): A Term Quantity Surveyor was advertised at **HKD 72,180 per month** in the 2024 Housing Department recruitment circular
- **Urban Renewal Authority, Airport Authority, MTR Corporation, Hospital Authority**: Starting salaries are typically 5-15% higher than the equivalent civil service grade
- **MPF Schemes Authority, Legislative Council Secretariat**: Surveyors are involved in property and facility management

### 3. Private sector

- **Big Five international firms** (JLL, Cushman & Wakefield, Savills, CBRE, Knight Frank): Fresh BSc graduates start at around HKD 25,000 - 32,000 per month, jumping to HKD 45,000 - 60,000 after passing APC
- **Major developers** (Cheung Kong, Sun Hung Kai, New World, Henderson, Sino, Swire, Kerry): Surveyors in development and cost control departments start at around HKD 28,000 - 38,000
- **QS consultancies** (Rider Levett Bucknall, Currie & Brown, Arcadis, AECOM): Fresh QS graduates start at around HKD 24,000 - 30,000
- **Senior partners**: Partners with 15+ years of experience typically earn HKD 2 - 6 million per year

According to PayScale 2025 data, the average annual salary for Hong Kong quantity surveyors across all experience levels is approximately **HKD 357,099**; mid-career surveyors (5-9 years) average around HKD 360,659; senior surveyors (10+ years) typically exceed HKD 600,000 per year.

## Overseas practice and migration pathways

A surveying degree is one of the most migration-friendly Hong Kong qualifications:

- **United Kingdom**: After HKIS APC and RICS Direct Entry, graduates can apply for the Skilled Worker Visa under Standard Occupational Classification (SOC) Code 2434 Chartered Surveyors. The 2025 minimum salary threshold of GBP 38,700 is comfortably met by most UK employers
- **Australia**: With RICS, AIQS or API membership, surveyors can apply for skilled migration. **Quantity Surveyor (ANZSCO 233213)** and **Valuer (224512)** are both on the Medium and Long-term Strategic Skills List (MLTSSL)
- **Canada**: RICS holds mutual recognition with the Appraisal Institute of Canada (AIC), which translates into Express Entry NOC TEER 1 points
- **Singapore**: The Singapore Institute of Surveyors and Valuers (SISV) has a Mutual Recognition Agreement with HKIS, so Hong Kong GP and QS surveyors can apply for SISV Direct Membership
- **New Zealand, UAE, Malaysia**: All accept RICS qualifications for direct practice

For non-local students who want to stay in Hong Kong after graduation, the Surveyor grade benefits from the **Immigration Arrangements for Non-local Graduates (IANG)** and the **Quality Migrant Admission Scheme**. The HKSAR Government has also placed surveyors on the Talent List, fast-tracking residency applications.

**Interview preparation**: HKU REC does not routinely interview, but borderline candidates may be invited. Typical questions cover Hong Kong real estate news (recent land premium cases, MBIS reform, Northern Metropolis progress), professional ethics scenarios, and global RICS trends.

## Industry outlook: the Northern Metropolis and Lantau Tomorrow Vision

The Northern Metropolis Development Strategy plans to build a new town for 2.5 million residents in the northern New Territories between 2027 and 2042, requiring at least 600 hectares of new developable land, 1.5 million residential units and 65 million square feet of commercial floor space. The **Lantau Tomorrow Vision Kau Yi Chau Artificial Islands project** is scheduled to begin reclamation in phases in the 2030s. Together these mega-projects will drive 20 years of strong demand for quantity surveyors, general practice surveyors, land surveyors and planning and development surveyors.

The HKIS Manpower Survey 2024 estimates a shortfall of around 2,000 surveyors over the next five years, the most severe supply-demand imbalance in three decades. That means the 2026 cohort (graduating in 2030) will hit the job market at exactly the right moment in the salary cycle.

## Conclusion

Surveying is a low-profile profession with high returns. For DSE candidates, HKU JS6016 and PolyU JS3789 offer two equally strong entry routes that suit different academic profiles and JUPAS scores. After four years of study and two years of APC training, graduates hold both an HKIS registration and an internationally portable RICS designation that opens around 150 jurisdictions worldwide. In government, junior Surveyors earn over HKD 50,000 a month; the Housing Authority pays its Term Quantity Surveyors HKD 72,180; and senior partners at developers and the Big Five can earn millions per year. With the Northern Metropolis and Lantau Tomorrow infrastructure cycles ahead, the next twenty years of Hong Kong surveying look unusually attractive.

If you are interested in real estate, construction or urban planning, and want a professional licence that travels with you, surveying deserves a Band A spot on your JUPAS list.
