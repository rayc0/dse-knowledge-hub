# Hong Kong Social Work Bachelor Complete Guide 2026: HKU JS6731, CUHK JS4874, PolyU JS3250, HKBU JS2660, CityU JS1113, SWRB Registration, ASWO Starting Salary HKD 36,850

Five UGC-funded universities in Hong Kong offer an undergraduate Bachelor of Social Work (BSW) degree that is recognised by the Social Workers Registration Board (SWRB) for direct registration as a Registered Social Worker (RSW): **The University of Hong Kong (HKU) JS6731**, **The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK) JS4874**, **The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU) JS3250**, **Hong Kong Baptist University (HKBU) JS2660**, and **City University of Hong Kong (CityU) JS1113**. All five run for four years full-time, all five embed the SWRB-prescribed 800 hours of fieldwork practicum, and all five graduates can apply for RSW registration on the strength of the degree alone — without any further examination.

Social work is one of the few professions in Hong Kong where the labour market is structurally short of practitioners. The Social Welfare Department (SWD), the Hospital Authority's medical social services, the 165+ subvented NGOs that contract with SWD, the Lump Sum Grant (LSG) sector, and an expanding private and school-counselling market all compete for the same pool of new graduates. The 2023 Mental Health Review Report and the rollout of the District Services and Community Care Teams have added further demand. This guide breaks down all five JUPAS programmes in detail — admissions, curriculum, the RSW registration pathway, civil service and NGO pay, and overseas mobility — for 2026 DSE candidates choosing Band A.

## 1. Why social work is a structurally protected profession in Hong Kong

### 1.1 The Social Workers Registration Ordinance (Cap. 505)

Under **Cap. 505 of the Laws of Hong Kong (Social Workers Registration Ordinance)**, in force since 6 June 1997, only persons whose names appear on the SWRB register may use the title "Social Worker" or "社會工作者" or hold a position designated as a social worker post. The Board, established 16 January 1998, maintains the register, sets the Code of Practice, accredits qualifying programmes, and handles disciplinary inquiries.

To be registered, an applicant must:

1. Hold a SWRB-recognised social work qualification (one of the local BSW programmes listed in section 2, or an SWRB-assessed overseas qualification);
2. Be ordinarily resident in Hong Kong (the Board does not consider an RSW ordinarily resident if absent from Hong Kong for two years or more);
3. Satisfy fit-and-proper checks and pay the registration fee.

Unlike pharmacists, dentists, and physiotherapists, social workers do **not** sit a separate licensing examination — the SWRB-recognised degree itself is the qualifying credential. This makes the local BSW the single cleanest entry route to the profession.

### 1.2 Statutory job market protection

Because Cap. 505 reserves the title and the post designation, every job advertisement that names "Social Worker", "Assistant Social Work Officer", "Social Work Officer" or "Social Work Assistant" by SWD or any subvented NGO must be filled by an RSW. This produces the stable graduate employment rate (95%+ across all five universities) that distinguishes social work from many cognate humanities and social-science majors.

### 1.3 Two parallel pay tracks — civil service and NGO

A BSW graduate can be employed either:

- **Government civil service** as an Assistant Social Work Officer (ASWO) on the **Master Pay Scale (MPS) Point 16, currently HKD 36,850/month** (2024-2025 figure, reviewed annually by the Civil Service Bureau). Promotion to Social Work Officer (SWO), MPS 33-44, follows after several years of frontline experience and is a competitive internal promotion.
- **Subvented NGO** as a Social Worker on a "Social Worker pay scale" pegged to the SWD Lump Sum Grant model. Most large NGOs (Caritas, Tung Wah, HKSKH, Po Leung Kuk, Christian Family Service Centre, Yang Memorial, Methodist Centre etc.) place a fresh BSW on a starting point around MPS 14-16, broadly comparable to ASWO but with NGO-specific allowances, contract length, and welfare benefits.

Graduates also work in school social work, integrated family service centres (IFSCs), integrated children & youth service centres (ICYSCs), elderly homes, hospital medical social services, probation, drug rehabilitation, mental health residences, and a growing private counselling sector.

All five programmes appear on the SWRB's Lists of Recognized Qualifications under the heading "Hong Kong" and confer direct RSW eligibility. CityU and HKBU also operate a senior-year-entry (Year 3) BSW route for sub-degree holders, but the JUPAS DSE intake is to Year 1.

### 2.1 HKU JS6731 — Bachelor of Social Work

Offered by the Department of Social Work and Social Administration in the Faculty of Social Sciences. Curriculum integrates social policy, sociology, psychology, and social administration with social work methods (casework, group work, community work). Students complete two blocks of fieldwork (a 50-day Concurrent Field Practicum in Year 3 and a 80-day Block Field Practicum in Year 4), totalling the SWRB minimum of 800 supervised hours. HKU also offers a stream allowing dual focus on social work and Chinese medicine social services or mental health. Graduates frequently progress to Master of Social Sciences in Mental Health, MSW, or PhD.

### 2.2 CUHK JS4874 — Bachelor of Social Work (Honours)

Run by the Department of Social Work, Faculty of Social Science. Strong tradition in family therapy, community organisation, and Chinese cultural perspectives in social work. Year-1 admission combined with the College system (New Asia, Chung Chi, United, Shaw, Morningside, S.H. Ho, C.W. Chu, Wu Yee Sun, Lee Woo Sing). The Department was the first in Hong Kong to launch postgraduate clinical training and offers a strong articulation to its MSocSc in Family Counselling and the Master of Social Work. JUPAS interviews after DSE results in late July are mandatory for shortlisted Band A candidates.

### 2.3 PolyU JS3250 — Bachelor of Arts (Honours) in Social Work

Run by the Department of Applied Social Sciences. Largest single intake of the five, with the most diverse fieldwork placement network across rehabilitation, gerontology, school social work, youth at risk, and family service. PolyU's WIE (Work-Integrated Education) framework integrates the SWRB practicum hours with additional 80-hour service learning. Strong industrial reputation in the NGO sector — PolyU graduates are heavily represented in subvented agency middle management.

### 2.4 HKBU JS2660 — Bachelor of Social Work (Honours)

Run by the Department of Social Work, Faculty of Social Sciences. HKBU emphasises a Christian liberal-arts tradition (though admission is non-denominational) and has a notable specialism in family-life education, marriage and family enrichment, and community engagement. The four-year curriculum includes the SWRB-prescribed 800 hours of supervised fieldwork in Years 3 and 4. HKBU's Whole Person Education general-education core gives the BSW a strong humanities foundation.

### 2.5 CityU JS1113 — Bachelor of Social Sciences (Honours) in Social Work

Run by the Department of Social and Behavioural Sciences. Curriculum balances social work methods with quantitative social-research training (statistics, research methods) — useful for graduates aiming at programme evaluation, policy analysis, or graduate research. CityU also offers minors in Counselling, Public Policy, and Asian and International Studies that pair well with the BSW major. The 800-hour practicum is split between a Year-3 concurrent placement and a Year-4 block placement.

## 3. JUPAS admission — DSE scoring and interviews

### 3.1 Minimum admission requirements

All five programmes require the standard "**3-3-2-2-2**" or "**4C32**" entry profile under the New Senior Secondary academic structure: Chinese Language Level 3, English Language Level 3, Mathematics Compulsory Part Level 2, Citizenship and Social Development "Attained", plus two electives at Level 2 or above. Additional programme-specific weightings vary:

- **HKU JS6731** weights the best 6 subjects, with English typically given a x1.5 multiplier.
- **CUHK JS4874** uses best 5, English x1.5.
- **PolyU JS3250** uses best 5, English Level 3 minimum.
- **HKBU JS2660** uses best 6 with weighted English.
- **CityU JS1113** uses best 5, with bonuses for relevant electives.

Indicative median DSE scores in recent years sit in the range 23-29 depending on the school, with HKU and CUHK at the upper end. Liberal Studies/Citizenship and Social Development is now scored on Attained/Not Attained only.

### 3.2 The JUPAS interview

All five programmes interview shortlisted Band A applicants in late July, after DSE results are released. The format varies — HKU and CUHK use group discussion plus individual interview; PolyU and CityU use group interview; HKBU uses a combination. Common themes include:

- Why social work and not psychology/counselling/sociology?
- A current Hong Kong social issue (e.g., subdivided flats, sandwich-class poverty, elderly suicide, youth mental health, ethnic minority integration) — what is the social-work response?
- A short ethical scenario (e.g., a colleague breaches confidentiality; a service user expresses suicidal ideation; a mandatory reporting trigger).
- The applicant's volunteering or community service history.

Interviews are scored on motivation, communication, ethical reasoning, and Cantonese/English proficiency. There is no formal pre-reading list, but candidates should be familiar with the SWD Five-Year Plan, the major NGO families, and the most recent Policy Address sections on welfare.

## 4. The four-year curriculum and the 800-hour fieldwork requirement

All SWRB-recognised BSW programmes share a common skeleton dictated by the Board's **Principles, Criteria and Standards** (8th Edition, March 2022). The minimum requirements include:

1. **Generic foundation** — psychology (lifespan development, abnormal), sociology, social policy, research methods, Hong Kong social welfare history.
2. **Social work methods** — casework, group work, community work, integrated method, and at least one specialised method (family therapy, narrative, CBT, solution-focused).
3. **Fields of practice** — children & youth, family, gerontology, mental health, rehabilitation, medical, school, probation/correctional, addiction, ethnic minorities.
4. **Law and ethics** — Cap. 505 ordinance, SWRB Code of Practice, Mental Health Ordinance Cap. 136, Mental Capacity Bill, Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance, child protection legislation.
5. **Fieldwork practicum** — minimum **800 supervised hours** in two or more SWRB-registered placement agencies, supervised by a Field Instructor who is an RSW with at least three years of post-qualification experience. Most universities split this into a concurrent (part-time, alongside lectures) and a block (full-time) placement.

Failing the practicum prevents graduation regardless of academic GPA, and a serious ethical breach during practicum can be referred to the SWRB.

## 5. Becoming a Registered Social Worker (RSW)

The post-graduation registration process is straightforward but must be completed before any "Social Worker" job offer can be confirmed:

1. **Submit Form A** to the SWRB with the BSW transcript, degree certificate, three passport photos, HKID copy, and the registration fee.
2. The Board verifies the qualification against its recognised list (all five JUPAS programmes are pre-listed, so verification is administrative rather than substantive).
3. The Board issues an RSW certificate and adds the applicant to the public register (searchable at swrb.org.hk).
4. The RSW must thereafter renew **annually** and notify the Board of any change of address within one month.
5. Failure to reside in Hong Kong for two consecutive years ends "ordinarily resident" status and triggers removal from the register; re-registration on return is possible but requires fresh application.

Disciplinary jurisdiction includes professional misconduct, criminal convictions, and breaches of the Code. Sanctions range from reprimand to deregistration.

## 6. Civil service and NGO salary in 2026

### 6.1 Assistant Social Work Officer (ASWO) — civil service

ASWO is the entry-level civil service post for BSW graduates. As of the latest Civil Service Bureau Master Pay Scale (effective 1 April 2024 and rolled forward in 2025-2026 unless revised):

| Rank | MPS Points | Approximate monthly salary |
|---|---|---|
| ASWO (entry) | MPS 16 | **HKD 36,850** |
| ASWO (mid) | MPS 17-22 | HKD 38,755 – HKD 53,055 |
| Social Work Officer | MPS 33-44 | HKD 79,140 – HKD 124,180 |
| Senior Social Work Officer | MPS 45-49 | HKD 130,500 – HKD 156,420 |

Civil service ASWO posts are advertised by the Civil Service Bureau several rounds a year and are extremely competitive — typical entry ratios of 30:1 to 50:1 applicants per post. Successful candidates take a Common Recruitment Examination (English, Chinese, Aptitude) and a structured interview.

### 6.2 NGO Social Worker

The 165+ subvented NGOs employ the majority of BSW graduates. Pay is broadly pegged to MPS but specific to each NGO's HR scheme. Indicative starting monthly salaries in 2026 range from **HKD 33,000 to HKD 38,500**, generally rising to HKD 45,000-55,000 after five years of frontline experience and to HKD 65,000+ for unit-in-charge or senior worker roles. Shift allowances apply for residential care and emergency 24-hour services.

### 6.3 Specialist routes

Hospital Authority Medical Social Workers, court-attached probation officers (Social Welfare Department), school social workers under the SWD/Education Bureau scheme, and Correctional Services Department welfare officers all map to government MPS scales similar to ASWO. Private practice (counselling, EAP, family mediation) and corporate social responsibility roles tend to be paid by hourly or contract rates.

## 7. Overseas recognition and mobility

A SWRB-recognised Hong Kong BSW does **not** automatically qualify in other jurisdictions, but most major systems accept it through assessment:

- **United Kingdom — Social Work England**: Hong Kong BSW graduates apply via the International Application for Registration. Most are required to complete an Overseas Social Work Qualification assessment, an English language test (IELTS 7.0 generally), and may be asked to complete a Test of Competence or supervised return-to-practice.
- **Australia — AASW**: graduates apply for Overseas Qualifications Assessment with the Australian Association of Social Workers. Hong Kong BSWs have been routinely recognised at "Eligible for AASW membership" subject to documentation; some applicants with shorter practicums are required to complete additional supervised hours.
- **Canada — CASW / provincial colleges**: each province has its own College of Social Workers (OCSWSSW in Ontario, BCCSW in BC, etc.). Hong Kong BSWs typically need a Canadian Council on Social Work Regulators credential assessment and may need to sit the Association of Social Work Boards (ASWB) examination at Bachelor or Master level.
- **Mainland China**: the Ministry of Civil Affairs Social Worker Vocational Qualification Examination (社工師) is a separate national exam unrelated to Cap. 505; Hong Kong RSWs interested in the Greater Bay Area must sit the Mainland exam and meet residence requirements.
- **Singapore — SASW / Allied Health Professions Council**: post-2021, Singapore moved to statutory registration for social workers; Hong Kong BSWs apply through SASW credentialing.

For graduates considering postgraduate study, the **Master of Social Work (MSW)** at HKU, CUHK, PolyU, HKBU, or CityU is the natural progression for those without a BSW (a one-to-two-year qualifying degree); for those who already hold the BSW, a coursework or research master in **family therapy, mental health, gerontology, or counselling** adds specialism. PhD pathways through the same departments lead to academic, policy-research, and senior NGO management roles.

## 8. How to choose between the five programmes

**Choose HKU JS6731** if you want the smallest cohort, the strongest research culture, and a broad social-administration foundation.

**Choose CUHK JS4874** if you want the family-therapy specialism, the College system, and a strong articulation to MSW or counselling masters.

**Choose PolyU JS3250** if you want the largest placement network, the most diverse fieldwork, and a clear NGO career trajectory.

**Choose HKBU JS2660** if you want a humanities-rich liberal-arts foundation, family-life education focus, and a smaller-group teaching style.

**Choose CityU JS1113** if you want strong quantitative research training alongside social work practice, useful for policy and evaluation careers.

All five lead to the same RSW credential and broadly the same starting salary. The differences lie in cohort culture, research focus, placement networks, and elective options. 2026 DSE candidates should rank all five in JUPAS Band A or B according to predicted score and personal fit, and prepare thoroughly for the interview round in late July.
