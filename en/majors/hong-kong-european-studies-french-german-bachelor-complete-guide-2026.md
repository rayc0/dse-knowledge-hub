# Hong Kong European Studies / French / German Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

European Studies is one of the most underrated humanities tracks in Hong Kong. While Korean and Japanese majors dominate undergraduate language interest, French and German graduates from HKU and HKBU consistently land in luxury houses (LVMH, Richemont, Kering), German industrial groups (BMW, Siemens, Lufthansa), European banks (BNP Paribas, Société Générale, Deutsche Bank), and EU/consulate-affiliated bodies — often at starting salaries 15–25% above generic BA averages because a B2-level European language is a scarce skill in the Greater Bay Area.

This guide covers every undergraduate route into European Studies and modern European languages in Hong Kong for 2026 entry — JUPAS scores, programme structure, the mandatory continental exchange, the DELF/Goethe/CILS certification ladder, and where graduates actually end up.

## The two real flagships: HKU and HKBU

Only two Hong Kong universities offer European Studies as a full undergraduate major with a structured language progression. Everything else is a minor or a free elective.

### HKU — BA European Studies (School of Modern Languages and Cultures, SMLC)

HKU's European Studies sits inside the School of Modern Languages and Cultures (SMLC) and is delivered through the Faculty of Arts. It is not a single JS-code programme — students enter through **JS6054 BA (School of Modern Languages and Cultures)** or the broader BA, then declare European Studies as their major in Year 1 or Year 2 after taking a beginner language stream.

- **Streams offered**: French, German, Italian, Portuguese, Russian, Spanish, Swedish (French and German are the deepest, with 6 progression levels each)
- **2025 JUPAS reference mean**: ~26–28 for JS6054 (varies by language stream); upper quartile candidates typically present 5* English + 5 Chinese + 5 in two electives
- **Mandatory exchange**: One full semester (most students take a full year) at a partner institution in continental Europe — Sciences Po Paris, Heidelberg, Sorbonne, Freiburg, LMU München, Sciences Po Lyon, Bocconi, Roma La Sapienza
- **Language exit standard**: B2 (CEFR) by graduation, with most French/German majors reaching C1
- **HKU-Sciences Po Dual Degree**: A separate, highly selective four-year track (2 years in Paris, 2 years in HKU) — admits roughly 12–18 students annually with mean scores around 32–35

The SMLC structure also lets students who entered HKU through other JS codes (JS6901 BBA, JS6456 BSocSc, even Engineering) take French or German as a **minor** — six courses, no exchange requirement, exits at B1.

### HKBU — JS2610 BA (Hons) / BSocSc (Hons) European Studies (French Stream / German Stream)

HKBU runs the single most accessible European Studies programme in the city. **JS2610** is a flexible BA/BSocSc that admits students to six possible majors — European Studies (French Stream), European Studies (German Stream), Geography, Global and China Studies, Government and International Studies, History, or Sociology — with the major declared at the end of Year 1.

- **2025 JUPAS admission score (JS2610)**: Mean 24.88, median profile 4-3-4-Attained-5\*\*-4, lower quartile 4-4-3-Attained-5-4
- **Why the mean is mid-20s**: JS2610 bundles humanities and social science majors, so the score reflects the whole cluster — students who actually want French/German typically present 4-4-4-Attained-5-4 or better, and the European Studies streams are not the most competitive sub-major inside JS2610
- **Mandatory year abroad**: Year 3 is spent in France (French Stream — partners include Université de Strasbourg, Sciences Po Bordeaux, ESCE Paris) or Germany (German Stream — partners include Universität Tübingen, Freie Universität Berlin, Universität Mannheim)
- **Language exit standard**: B2 (most students score DELF B2 or Goethe-Zertifikat B2 by Year 4)
- **Programme size**: ~30–40 students per language stream per cohort, very intimate seminar-style teaching

HKBU is structurally the best route for a student whose DSE result is in the high 4-5 zone but who is committed to mastering French or German — the mandatory year abroad is non-negotiable and embeds you in a European university for two full semesters, not just an exchange semester.

## Curriculum structure: what you actually study

European Studies is **not** a literature degree. The modern programmes at HKU and HKBU split roughly:

- **40% language acquisition** — six progression levels (Elementary → Intermediate → Advanced → Pre-Mastery), spoken/written, with the final two semesters typically conducted entirely in French/German
- **30% area studies content** — EU institutions, European political economy, Franco-German relations, post-1989 Europe, immigration and integration, Brexit, European Green Deal
- **20% cultural / historical / literary content** — French Revolution and République, German philosophical tradition (Kant, Hegel, Habermas), European cinema, contemporary literature
- **10% capstone / dissertation / professional skills** — translation seminar, business French/German, EU-China policy paper

Typical HKBU French Stream four-year sequence:

- **Year 1**: French Elementary I & II + European Civilization + Introduction to European Union + General Education
- **Year 2**: French Intermediate I & II + History of Modern Europe + Franco-Chinese Relations + Major elective
- **Year 3**: **One full year in France** — direct enrolment at a partner university (Strasbourg, Bordeaux), all courses in French, typically 8–10 courses credit-transferred back
- **Year 4**: French Advanced I & II + Business French + Translation Seminar + Capstone Project (often in French)

HKU's structure is similar but the exchange is one semester (some students extend to full year), with extra electives in European literature and a more flexible cultural-studies pathway.

## The DELF / Goethe / CILS / DELE certification ladder

External certifications are non-negotiable for graduates targeting European employers. The standard ladder:

| Level (CEFR) | French (DELF/DALF) | German (Goethe) | Italian (CILS) | Spanish (DELE) | Where you'll be |
|--------------|--------------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|------------------|
| A1 | DELF A1 | Goethe A1 Start Deutsch 1 | CILS A1 | DELE A1 | End of Year 1 (most students) |
| A2 | DELF A2 | Goethe A2 | CILS A2 | DELE A2 | End of Year 2 |
| B1 | DELF B1 | Goethe-Zertifikat B1 | CILS UNO-B1 | DELE B1 | End of Year 2 / before exchange |
| **B2** | **DELF B2** | **Goethe-Zertifikat B2** | **CILS DUE-B2** | **DELE B2** | **Programme exit standard** |
| C1 | DALF C1 | Goethe-Zertifikat C1 | CILS TRE-C1 | DELE C1 | Top 20–30% of graduates |
| C2 | DALF C2 | Goethe-Zertifikat C2 GDS | CILS QUATTRO-C2 | DELE C2 | Postgraduate / professional |

For postgraduate study in continental Europe taught in the local language, B2 is the **minimum** and C1 is increasingly the de facto requirement (Sciences Po Master, Bocconi taught-in-Italian programmes, German Masters at Mannheim or Heidelberg in non-English tracks).

The Alliance Française de Hong Kong (Jordan campus) and Goethe-Institut Hongkong (Wan Chai) are the only authorised exam centres in the city. Test fees in 2026 are roughly HK$1,800–2,400 per level.

## Mandatory continental exchange — what to expect

This is the single biggest reason European Studies graduates command salary premiums: they have actually lived in Europe and produced academic work in the target language.

**HKBU JS2610** — the year abroad is part of tuition and counted as Year 3. Partner placement is decided in Year 2 Semester 2 based on CGPA and language proficiency. Top students get Strasbourg, Sciences Po Bordeaux, or Tübingen; others get regional universities (Université de Lorraine, Universität Trier).

**HKU European Studies** — one semester is mandatory under the SMLC structure, but the majority extend to a full year. HKU's bilateral exchanges include:
- **France**: Sciences Po Paris, Sciences Po Lyon, Université Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne, Sciences Po Bordeaux, ESSEC, EM Lyon
- **Germany**: Universität Heidelberg, LMU München, Universität Freiburg, Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin
- **Italy**: Università Bocconi, Università di Bologna, Sapienza Roma
- **Spain**: Universidad de Salamanca, Universidad Complutense Madrid
- **Switzerland**: Universität Zürich, Université de Genève

The **HKU-Sciences Po Dual Degree** (a separate JS code admission) gives you a full Sciences Po Bachelor + HKU BA in four years — the strongest Europe-facing undergraduate credential available in Hong Kong.

Living costs in continental Europe for a Hong Kong student in 2026 run roughly €900–1,400/month (Strasbourg, Tübingen, Bordeaux) and €1,300–1,900/month (Paris, Munich, Milan). HKU and HKBU both offer exchange grants of HK$15,000–40,000, and the HKSAR Government's Reaching Out Award covers up to HK$20,000 for outbound students at qualifying institutions.

## Career outcomes and starting salaries (2024/25 graduate survey data)

Hong Kong European Studies and French/German graduates fall into roughly six employment buckets:

### 1. Luxury and fashion houses — LVMH, Kering, Richemont, Hermès, Chanel
Asia-Pacific HQs in Hong Kong actively recruit French-speaking graduates for management trainee tracks. LVMH's Inside LVMH and Future of Luxury graduate programmes typically open to candidates with B2+ French.
- **Starting salary (2025)**: HK$22,000–28,000/month base + bonus
- **Typical roles**: Buyer trainee, marketing executive, retail operations management trainee

### 2. German industrial groups — BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Siemens, Bosch, BASF, Lufthansa, DHL
German-speaking graduates are scarce in Hong Kong and Shenzhen. Most German firms run regional graduate programmes that explicitly favour Goethe B2+ holders.
- **Starting salary (2025)**: HK$24,000–32,000/month base
- **Typical roles**: Supply chain analyst, sales engineering trainee, Mandarin-German market liaison, technical translation

### 3. European banks and asset managers — BNP Paribas, Société Générale, Crédit Agricole, Deutsche Bank, Allianz, AXA
French and German banks recruit a small but consistent number of European Studies graduates into operations, corporate banking, and private banking client management.
- **Starting salary (2025)**: HK$28,000–38,000/month base + bonus (operations) up to HK$45,000–60,000 (private banking analyst with European-language client books)

### 4. Diplomatic missions, consulates, EU bodies, chambers of commerce
The Consulate General of France in Hong Kong and Macau, German Consulate General, Italian Consulate, EU Office to Hong Kong and Macao, French Chamber of Commerce (CCIFHK), German Industry and Commerce (AHK), and Camera di Commercio Italiana all hire locally.
- **Starting salary (2025)**: HK$22,000–30,000/month (locally engaged staff); EU/consulate ranks have additional Brussels/Paris-set scales for specific positions
- **Typical roles**: Cultural attaché assistant, trade promotion officer, visa officer, programme coordinator

### 5. Hospitality and tourism — Accor, AccorHotels Asia-Pacific, Air France/KLM, Lufthansa Group, Swiss International
- **Starting salary (2025)**: HK$18,000–24,000/month + service charge; airlines premium higher

### 6. Postgraduate study (~30% of graduates)
European Studies graduates are uniquely positioned for Master programmes in continental Europe at heavily discounted (often zero) tuition:
- **Sciences Po Paris** — Master in International Affairs, European Affairs (€14,000–18,000/year, with Hong Kong-eligible scholarships)
- **Universität Heidelberg, LMU München** — German-taught Masters typically €350/semester semester fee only (taught-in-German requires C1)
- **Bocconi, Bologna** — Italian-taught Masters under €4,000/year
- **KU Leuven, Sciences Po Lille** — European Studies / European Politics Masters
- **College of Europe (Bruges / Natolin)** — the gold standard for EU-track careers, ~€26,000 (heavy scholarship availability)

## Postgraduate routes — what European Studies graduates actually do next

For students considering the long arc, European Studies opens disproportionately strong postgraduate doors:

1. **Sciences Po Master programmes** — direct entry from HKU/HKBU, B2/C1 French depending on track. International Affairs and European Affairs streams.
2. **College of Europe** (Bruges) — selective postgraduate institution for EU-track careers (Commission, EEAS, Council). 10-month intensive.
3. **MBA via European language route** — INSEAD (Singapore/Fontainebleau), HEC Paris, ESCP Europe — language proficiency is a tiebreaker
4. **Translation/Interpretation Masters** — ESIT Paris, FTI Geneva, University of Westminster, CUHK Translation MA — Conference Interpreter route requires DALF C2 / Goethe C2
5. **Law conversion in Europe** — Hong Kong LLB conversion routes to French/German/Italian legal systems are rare but exist for civil-law-curious humanities graduates

| Decision factor | HKU European Studies | HKBU JS2610 French/German Stream |
|-----------------|----------------------|-----------------------------------|

| Class size | Medium (60–90/cohort across streams) | Small (~30–40/stream) |
| Exchange length | 1 semester mandatory, most extend to year | **Full year mandatory** (Year 3) |
| Language exit standard | B2, many reach C1 | B2 consistently |
| Postgraduate-Europe placement | Sciences Po, LMU, Heidelberg very strong | Strasbourg, Tübingen, Bordeaux network |

If your DSE result is in the upper band and you are pre-decided on Europe-track careers, HKU European Studies (or the Sciences Po Dual Degree) is the higher-ceiling choice. If your DSE result is in the realistic 22–26 zone and you want guaranteed, structured immersion, HKBU JS2610 is the more deliberate language-acquisition machine.

## Common myths and clarifications

**"European Studies is a soft major with no employability."** False — graduates with documented B2 French or German routinely out-earn generic business/humanities majors because the language scarcity is real in Hong Kong and the Greater Bay Area.

**"You need to start French/German before university."** Most students at HKU and HKBU start from absolute beginner (A0) in Year 1. The programmes are designed for zero prior knowledge.

**"You have to be from Band 1 to get in."** JS2610 admits students from 24-mean profiles routinely. HKU SMLC is more competitive but not at HKU Law/Business levels.

**"German is harder than French so the major is rarer."** True on both counts — and that's exactly why German-majoring graduates command the highest premiums in the city.

## Application timeline for 2026 entry

- **September 2025**: JUPAS account creation
- **November 2025**: Submit JS2610 (HKBU) and JS6054 (HKU SMLC) in Band A if these are your top choices
- **December 2025**: For HKU-Sciences Po Dual Degree, submit separate JUPAS code and prepare Sciences Po online questionnaire (in English and French/your second language)
- **April–May 2026**: HKU and HKBU interviews — expect questions on motivation for Europe, basic knowledge of EU institutions, and (occasionally) a short written response
- **July 2026**: DSE results release, Band A modification deadline
- **August 2026**: Offers, registration, language placement test in Week 1 of Year 1

## Final word

If you have any interest in European culture, languages, business, or politics, the European Studies / French / German route in Hong Kong is structurally undervalued by JUPAS applicants. The mandatory continental exchange, the language scarcity premium, and the postgraduate-Europe pipeline make this one of the cleanest "humanities major with strong career outcomes" tracks the city offers. Two universities — HKU and HKBU — do this seriously; everyone else delivers the language as a minor.

Choose HKU SMLC if you have the DSE score and the appetite for a less prescriptive curriculum. Choose HKBU JS2610 if you want a full year embedded in France or Germany as a non-negotiable part of your degree. Either way, the DELF B2 or Goethe-Zertifikat B2 in your hand at graduation will open doors that English-only humanities graduates simply cannot reach.
