# Hong Kong International Business and Global Business Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

International Business — sometimes branded as Global Business, Global Management, or Integrated BBA — is one of the most fiercely contested business sub-tracks among Hong Kong's eight UGC universities. It is not "general management with extra travel." A proper International Business (IB) degree bundles finance, marketing, supply chain, cross-cultural negotiation, and foreign-language training into a four-year "multinational manager starter kit," and the elite versions in Hong Kong make an overseas exchange semester (or year) a graduation requirement rather than a bonus.

Among local programmes, only four to five truly behave like flagship IB degrees: HKU's BBA(IBGM) International Business and Global Management (JS6896), CUHK's Integrated BBA (IBBA, JS4202), HKUST's BBA in International Business Management (IBM, declared inside JS5300), CityU's BBA Global Business Systems Management (GBSM, inside JS1011), and PolyU's BBA in Global Supply Chain Management (inside JS3070). This guide walks DSE students through the JUPAS scores, curriculum, exchange requirements, starting salaries, and which programme realistically fits which profile.

## 1. BBA vs IBBA vs BBA in International Business — What's the Difference?

DSE candidates often confuse these three labels. They are not interchangeable.

| Degree label | What it means | Hong Kong examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **BBA (general)** | Standard Bachelor of Business Administration; choose major in Year 2 (Accounting, Finance, Marketing, etc.) | HKUST JS5300, HKU JS6781, CUHK JS4204, CityU JS1011 |
| **BBA in International Business** | Standard BBA with IB declared as the major from Year 2; exchange semester usually compulsory | HKUST IBM major, CityU GBSM major, PolyU GSCM |
| **IBBA / Integrated BBA** | A standalone, English-medium, small-cohort programme — exchange is mandatory and entry standards are noticeably higher | CUHK JS4202 IBBA, HKU JS6896 BBA(IBGM) |
| **BBA in Global Business** | Functionally identical to IB; CityU and HSUHK prefer this wording | CityU GBSM, HSUHK BBA in Global Business |

Plain English: **IBBA and BBA(IBGM) are elite "integrated" degrees** — small cohorts, full English instruction, mandatory exchange, higher cut-offs. The "International Business major inside a normal BBA" is more flexible and lets you stream in Year 2, with a noticeably lower entry bar.

## 2. The Four Flagship International Business Programmes

### a. HKU Business School — BBA(IBGM), JS6896

HKU's BBA(IBGM) International Business and Global Management is the city's original integrated IB degree. The cohort is tiny — roughly 30 students per year — and it sits in the same tier as BBA(Law) and BBA(IS) as one of HKU Business School's three "elite integrated" programmes.

- **JUPAS code:** JS6896
- **Length:** 4 years (a full year of mandatory overseas exchange, usually Year 3)
- **Minimum:** Core 332A33, English Level 5, Mathematics Level 4; interview is decisive
- **2025 admissions:** Best-5 DSE median around 30 (roughly 5\*\* + 5\*\*5\*5\*5\*); 25th percentile around 27
- **Exchange partners:** LSE, Wharton, HEC Paris, Bocconi, IE Business School, NYU Stern, UVA Darden
- **Typical destinations:** investment banking (IBD) and elite consulting

### b. CUHK Business School — IBBA, JS4202

CUHK's Integrated BBA was launched in 1996 and shares the top tier with HKU IBGM. The cohort is a little larger at about 60 students, but the selectivity is comparable.

- **JUPAS code:** JS4202
- **Length:** 4 years (mandatory one-year exchange)
- **Minimum:** Core 33232, with English/Mathematics weighted heavier in best-5 formula
- **2025 admissions:** Best-5 median around 29; 25th percentile around 26
- **Exchange partners:** LBS, HEC Paris, NYU Stern, UC Berkeley Haas, Cornell SC Johnson, Bocconi, SMU, Copenhagen Business School
- **Sister flagships:** CUHK Quantitative Finance (JS4204) and Global Economics and Finance (JS4222) form the school's three "pre-IB" integrated tracks

### c. HKUST School of Business — IBM major, JS5300

HKUST admits all business students through one broad JS5300 Business and Management code. Students stream into majors in Year 2: Accounting, Economics, Finance, Global Business, Information Systems, Management, Marketing, Operations Management, or International Business Management (IBM).

- **JUPAS code:** JS5300 (school-wide)
- **Length:** 4 years (IBM and Global Business majors require at least one exchange semester)
- **Selection:** HKUST's bespoke weighted best-5 formula, with English and Mathematics boosted
- **2025 admissions:** JS5300 weighted best-5 median around 31; 25th percentile around 29.5
- **IBM requirement:** at least 12 credits of cross-cultural business management, including the exchange semester
- **Distinguishing feature:** HKUST has the highest international-student ratio of any Hong Kong university (roughly half the campus comes from overseas); its dual-degree BBA in Global Business (GBUS) splits four years between HKUST and McGill Desautels, NUS, or Copenhagen Business School

### d. CityU College of Business — BBA Global Business Systems Management (GBSM), JS1011

CityU's College of Business uses a single JS1011 code, with majors declared in Year 2. GBSM blends international business with supply-chain management — a deliberately practical alternative to the more finance-tilted IBGM/IBBA.

- **JUPAS code:** JS1011 (school-wide)
- **2025 admissions:** Best-5 median around 23–25; weighted DSE around 27
- **Exchange:** strongly encouraged, not mandatory; roughly 70% of GBSM students go abroad
- **Position:** lower entry bar than HKUST IBM, but graduates land Big Four and shipping/logistics multinationals at comparable rates

### e. PolyU Faculty of Business — BBA in Global Supply Chain Management, JS3070

PolyU's JS3070 Scheme in Global Business funnels students into Global Supply Chain Management and International Shipping and Transport Logistics in Year 2.

- **JUPAS code:** JS3070
- **2025 admissions:** Best-5 median around 21–22
- **Edge:** sits next to PolyU's flagship logistics department; graduates feed Maersk, DHL, Kuehne+Nagel, Cathay Pacific, and the Hong Kong Airport Authority

## 4. Required Courses and a Typical Four-Year Map

The core finance-marketing-accounting backbone resembles a normal BBA, but IB degrees layer on cross-cultural management, global strategy, and a second language. Using CUHK IBBA as a representative example:

**Year 1 — Business foundations and language**
- IBBA 1110 Business Foundations
- IBBA 1120 Accounting for Business
- ECON 1011 Introduction to Microeconomics
- College English I + a second foreign language (French, Spanish, Japanese, or Korean)
- CHLT 1100 / UGEC General Education

**Year 2 — Functional business and global perspective**
- IBBA 2210 Marketing Management
- IBBA 2220 Operations and Supply Chain Management
- IBBA 2230 Financial Management
- IBBA 2240 Business Law
- IBBA 2250 Cross-Cultural Management

**Year 3 — Mandatory full-year overseas exchange**
- Credits transferred back from LBS, HEC, Wharton, NYU Stern, or another partner

**Year 4 — Capstone and strategy**
- IBBA 4410 Strategic Management
- IBBA 4420 Business Ethics
- IBBA 4430 Capstone International Business Project
- Electives: International Trade Finance, Global Marketing, China Business Strategy

## 5. Overseas Exchange — From "Nice to Have" to "Graduation Requirement"

The defining feature of a flagship IB degree is that **exchange is compulsory**, not optional. The three "integrated" programmes (IBGM, IBBA, IBM) share heavily overlapping partner rosters:

- **Americas:** Wharton, MIT Sloan, NYU Stern, UC Berkeley Haas, Cornell SC Johnson, UVA Darden, McGill Desautels
- **Europe:** LBS, LSE, HEC Paris, ESSEC, Bocconi, IE Business School, Copenhagen Business School, Stockholm School of Economics
- **Asia-Pacific:** NUS, SMU, Seoul National University, University of Tokyo, Keio Business School, Tsinghua SEM, Peking Guanghua

Money: HKU and CUHK both offer additional exchange bursaries in the HK\$25,000–60,000 range. Students continue paying HKU/CUHK tuition (about HK\$42,100/year) instead of partner-school tuition. Living expenses are separate — budget HK\$80,000–120,000 per semester for Europe and HK\$100,000–150,000 for the United States.

## 7. Interviews and Personal Statements — The Real Selection Layer

JUPAS scores alone do not get you into the flagship integrated degrees. Both HKU IBGM and CUHK IBBA run a **second-round selection** on top of DSE results.

- **HKU IBGM:** group interview plus individual interview. The group case is usually a cross-border strategy question ("Should Tesla enter the Indian market?") with 20 minutes of discussion and a group presentation. Common individual prompts: "Why IBGM rather than BBA(Law) or BBA(IS)?" and "What does global mindset mean to you?"
- **CUHK IBBA:** English individual interview, plus a short mental-arithmetic exercise to test composure under pressure (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division — fast).
- **HKUST IBM:** declared in Year 2 by GPA. To stream into IBM you need roughly CGA 3.5+ and a successful group-project record from first-year core courses.

For the OEA and personal statement, three categories of evidence move the needle:
1. **International exposure:** prior exchange, Model UN, overseas volunteering, international competitions (HSBC International Junior Golf, F1 in Schools world finals, etc.)
2. **Cross-cultural language ability:** JLPT N2, TOPIK Level 3, DELF B2, or similar
3. **Real business work:** YDC Young Entrepreneurs Scheme, Yet 2 Be, Y20 Hong Kong delegate, accredited social-enterprise contests

## 8. Minors and Long-Term Pathways

Most-picked minors among IB undergraduates:
- **Finance / Quantitative Finance** — adds the quant layer required to break into IBD or quant strategy
- **Computer Science / Business Analytics** — necessary for the digital-transformation hiring track at consulting firms and tech companies
- **Mandarin for Business / French / Spanish** — adds another commercial language
- **Law (HKU and CityU only offer this as a minor)** — eases later transition into JD or PCLL programmes

Typical five-year trajectories:
1. Two to three years at an IB / consulting firm / Big Four, then a top-tier MBA (Harvard, Wharton, INSEAD).
2. Stay in FMCG and reach Brand Manager in four to five years.
3. Pivot into tech-company strategy or Strategy & Operations (Google, Meta, TikTok APAC) after two to three years in consulting.
4. Join the family business or start your own venture.

## 9. Realistic Advice for DSE Candidates

- **Weighted DSE 28+ with a stable English 5\*\*:** push hard for HKU IBGM or CUHK IBBA. The network compounding effect of an integrated degree is significantly above a general BBA.
- **Weighted DSE 26–28:** HKUST Business JS5300 is the best risk-adjusted choice — secure the broad admission first, then stream into IBM or Global Business in Year 2. The campus is the most international in the city anyway.
- **DSE 24–26:** CityU JS1011 with the GBSM major, or PolyU JS3070, are both solid. CityU GBSM graduates enter Big Four at rates comparable to CUHK BBA's general majors.
- **Below 22 but committed:** consider HSUHK's self-funded BBA in Global Business or EdUHK's BBA, and stack the CV with language certifications and a self-arranged exchange semester to compensate.

International Business is not "the major for people whose English is decent." What it actually tests is your ability to negotiate, coordinate, and ship outcomes inside organisations where every meeting is bilingual or trilingual. Hong Kong's flagship integrated degrees retain their premium because they compress three things — compulsory exchange, full English environment, and a high-bar peer cohort — into the same four-year window. If you already know you want to be a multinational manager working out of London or New York, this is the most direct path the local system offers.
