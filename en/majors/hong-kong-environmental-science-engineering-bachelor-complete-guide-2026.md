# Hong Kong Environmental Science & Engineering Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

Climate change, carbon neutrality, air and water quality, waste management and the green economy have moved from the margins of policy to the centre of Hong Kong's development agenda. The government's commitment to carbon neutrality before 2050, the rapid growth of ESG roles in finance and the expansion of environmental impact assessment work have made environmental graduates more employable than the field's quiet reputation suggests. This guide walks DSE and international students through every major undergraduate route into environmental science and environmental engineering in Hong Kong for 2026 entry — the JUPAS codes, the realistic admission scores, what each programme actually teaches, and where graduates end up.

## Environmental Science vs Environmental Engineering — Choose the Right Track First

Before comparing programmes, understand the split. The two tracks lead to different careers and have different DSE subject leanings.

**Environmental science** is the analytical, investigative side: atmospheric science, ecology, water and soil chemistry, climate modelling, biodiversity, pollution monitoring and environmental management. It is closer to a pure science degree. Graduates work as environmental officers, EIA (Environmental Impact Assessment) consultants, conservation officers, sustainability analysts and researchers. Biology, Chemistry and Geography are the natural feeder subjects.

**Environmental engineering** is the design-and-build side: water and wastewater treatment, air pollution control systems, solid waste and resource recovery, contaminated land remediation, and green building services. It is an accredited engineering discipline. Graduates can progress toward Chartered Engineer status through the Hong Kong Institution of Engineers (HKIE). Mathematics, Physics and Chemistry matter more here, and the salary ceiling for chartered engineers is generally higher.

If you are unsure, the broad first-year-entry programmes (CityU's School of Energy and Environment, HKUST's Science admission groups, HKU's broad Science/Engineering entry) let you defer the choice for a year.

## The Main JUPAS Programmes for 2026 Entry

### PolyU — JS3375: BEng (Hons) in Environmental Engineering and Sustainable Development

This is arguably the most directly vocational option. PolyU's EESD programme blends environmental engineering, environmental science and carbon-neutrality technologies, covering air, water, soil and waste problems alongside circular-economy policy and environmental impact assessment. It is **fully accredited by the HKIE**, which means graduates can enter the professional engineer track without bridging requirements.

- **Duration:** 4 years full-time
- **DSE requirements:** PolyU general entrance requirements; no compulsory elective. Preferred subjects (carrying the highest weighting) include Mathematics, the M1/M2 Extended Modules, Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Economics, Geography and Combined/Integrated Science.
- **Scoring:** Best 5 subjects with subject weighting, plus a bonus for a 6th subject at Level 3+.
- **2025/26 average admission score:** approximately **225 (weighted)** — a mid-band score that makes this one of the more accessible engineering routes at PolyU.
- **Careers:** government departments (EPD, DSD, WSD), engineering consultancies and construction contractors; many graduates progress to professional engineer status.

### CUHK — JS4648: Earth and Environmental Sciences (EESC)

CUHK merged its former Earth System Science and Environmental Science programmes into a single integrated **Earth and Environmental Sciences** degree. It is science-oriented and flexible, with specialisation streams in **Atmospheric Science, Geology and Geophysics, and Environmental Science and Technology**. This is the strongest choice for students drawn to climate science, geohazards, meteorology or research, and a natural feeder to postgraduate study and roles at the Hong Kong Observatory or in conservation.

- **Feeder subjects:** Biology, Chemistry, Physics and Geography.
- **Profile:** broad first two years, then stream specialisation; strong fieldwork and laboratory component.

### CUHK — JS4462: Energy and Environmental Engineering

The engineering counterpart at CUHK. It trains students to tackle combined energy and environmental challenges with three streams: **Sustainable Energy Technology, Green Building Technology, and Environmental Engineering**. This suits students interested in the energy transition, building services and decarbonisation technology rather than ecology or atmospheric monitoring.

### CityU — JS1051: Energy and Environment (School of Energy and Environment)

CityU admits to the **School of Energy and Environment** as a whole, with students choosing a major after the first year. The current major options are **Energy Science and Engineering** and **Environmental Science and Engineering**. This deferred-choice structure is ideal if you are committed to the sustainability field but undecided between the energy and environmental sides. CityU's SEE has a strong research reputation in air quality and atmospheric science.

### HKUST — Broad Science/Engineering Admission

HKUST does not run a single narrow "environmental" JUPAS code; instead environmental study is reached through its broad **Science** admission groups and the **Environmental Management and Technology** offering, as well as Civil and Environmental Engineering within the School of Engineering. HKUST's general JUPAS requirement is typically expressed as **"332A33"** across four core and two elective subjects, with school-specific subject weightings. The advantage is interdisciplinary flexibility; the trade-off is that admission scores for the broad science/engineering groups run higher than the dedicated programmes at PolyU or CityU.

### HKU — Faculty of Science / Engineering

HKU offers environmental science as a major within its broad **Bachelor of Science** entry, and environmental/civil engineering within the Faculty of Engineering's flexible first-year admission. Like HKUST, the broad entry means a higher score requirement but maximum flexibility to switch majors after exploring the first year.

### EdUHK — JS8702: BSc (Hons) in Integrated Environmental Management

The Education University's **Integrated Environmental Management** degree is more policy-, education- and management-oriented than the research-heavy CUHK route or the engineering-heavy PolyU route. It is well suited to students who want careers in environmental education, NGO work, corporate sustainability and policy rather than laboratory science or chartered engineering. Admission scores are typically the most accessible of all the options here.

## DSE Subject Strategy

There is no single compulsory elective across these programmes, but your elective choices materially affect both eligibility and weighted scores:

- **For the engineering track (PolyU JS3375, CUHK JS4462):** prioritise **Mathematics plus M1 or M2**, **Physics** and **Chemistry**. The Extended Module is heavily weighted at PolyU and CUHK engineering.
- **For the science track (CUHK JS4648, CityU environmental science, HKU/HKUST science):** **Biology and Chemistry** are the core pair, with **Geography** a strong third and Physics useful for atmospheric/geophysics streams.
- **English Language** is universally weighted and gate-keeps the broad-entry HKU/HKUST routes more strictly than the others.

A realistic competitive profile for the dedicated programmes (PolyU JS3375, CityU JS1051, EdUHK JS8702) is roughly Level 4-5 in core subjects with two strong science electives. The broad HKU/HKUST entries demand a noticeably higher overall score.

## Fees and Costs

All eight UGC-funded universities charge the standard government-subsidised tuition of **HK$42,100 per year** for local (including JUPAS) students. Non-local students pay substantially more — typically **HK$145,000-198,000 per year** depending on the institution, with engineering and science programmes at the higher end. Budget an additional HK$45,000-70,000 per year for accommodation and living costs in Hong Kong. Fieldwork-heavy programmes (CUHK EESC in particular) may carry modest field-trip levies.

## Career Prospects and Salary

Environmental graduates have a broader employment market than the field's image suggests. Typical destinations include:

- **Government:** the Environmental Protection Department, Drainage Services Department, Water Supplies Department, Agriculture, Fisheries and Conservation Department and the Hong Kong Observatory recruit environmental science and engineering graduates as Environmental Protection Officers, Engineers and Scientific Officers — stable, pensionable roles with starting salaries in the HK$32,000-40,000/month range for degree-entry grades.
- **Consultancies:** EIA and sustainability consultancies (AECOM, Arup, Mott MacDonald, Ramboll, ERM) hire heavily for environmental impact assessment, air/water modelling and ESG advisory work.
- **Engineering and construction:** contractors and MTR-type infrastructure operators employ environmental engineers for compliance, water treatment and waste systems.
- **Corporate ESG:** banks, property developers and listed companies have rapidly expanded sustainability and ESG-reporting teams — a fast-growing destination for science-track graduates with strong analytical and communication skills.
- **NGOs and research:** WWF, Friends of the Earth, university research labs and postgraduate study.

The chartered-engineer pathway (most accessible via PolyU JS3375 and CUHK JS4462 because of HKIE accreditation) typically lifts mid-career earnings well above the science-track average, while the science track offers stronger routes into research, government scientific grades and ESG analytics.

## How to Choose — A Quick Decision Guide

- **Want accredited engineering and the highest salary ceiling, with a mid-band score?** → **PolyU JS3375**.
- **Drawn to climate, weather, geology or research?** → **CUHK JS4648 (Earth and Environmental Sciences)**.
- **Interested in the energy transition and green buildings?** → **CUHK JS4462** or **CityU JS1051**.
- **Committed to sustainability but undecided between energy and environment?** → **CityU JS1051** (defer the choice a year).
- **Top scores and want maximum flexibility?** → **HKU or HKUST broad Science/Engineering entry**.
- **Aiming at environmental education, NGO or policy/management work?** → **EdUHK JS8702**.

## Final Word

Environmental study in Hong Kong is no longer a niche fallback. With carbon-neutrality targets driving public spending, an explosion of ESG hiring in the private sector, and stable government scientific and engineering grades, the field offers more breadth than students assume. The single most important early decision is the science-vs-engineering split — get that right, choose DSE electives to match, and the dedicated programmes (especially PolyU JS3375 and CityU JS1051) are achievable at mid-band scores rather than the elite thresholds that medicine or law demand. Always confirm the latest entrance requirements and subject weightings on the official JUPAS programme pages and each university's admissions website before finalising your band choices.
