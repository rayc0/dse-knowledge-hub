# Hong Kong Sociology Bachelor Complete Guide 2026: JUPAS Scores, Curriculum, Careers

Sociology is the systematic study of human society, social relationships, and social change. In Hong Kong — a hyper-urbanised, globally connected city deeply integrated with the Greater Bay Area — sociology is more than a traditional humanities and social science (HSS) option. It is one of the most powerful academic lenses for understanding population ageing, class stratification, cross-border families, platform labour, AI ethics, and the social impact of mainland integration. This guide systematically maps the 2026 sociology bachelor offerings at six Hong Kong universities — HKU, CUHK, HKUST, HKBU, LingnanU, and EdUHK — comparing JUPAS scoring, interview requirements, curriculum architecture, research tracks, and graduate destinations to help local DSE and non-local candidates make an informed decision.

## Why Study Sociology in Hong Kong?

Hong Kong is arguably the best living laboratory for the study of contemporary Chinese society. Under "one country, two systems," it presents a unique combination of social phenomena: hyper-ageing (35%+ of the population projected to be 65+ by 2046), stalled intergenerational mobility, cross-border marriage and family networks, dense informal labour markets, platform-economy disruption, and ongoing Greater Bay Area integration. Hong Kong's sociology departments — particularly CUHK Sociology, the oldest and largest in the city — publish regularly in top-tier journals including the *American Journal of Sociology*, *American Sociological Review*, and *Social Forces*. HKU, HKUST, and HKBU sociology faculty likewise enjoy strong international reputations in urban sociology, migration studies, medical sociology, and computational social science.

Undergraduate sociology training in Hong Kong centres on five core competencies: **social survey methods, quantitative and qualitative data analysis, critical thinking, cross-cultural comparison, and policy evaluation**. These translate readily into careers in UX research, market research, government policy units, NGOs, journalism, education, public health, human resources, and social-impact consulting.

## The Six Hong Kong Universities Offering Sociology

### 1. The University of Hong Kong (HKU) — JS6717 Bachelor of Social Sciences

HKU does not offer a standalone "Sociology" JUPAS degree. Instead, it adopts the **Faculty-based admission model**: JS6717 BSocSc is a 4-year broad-discipline programme with first-year intake of roughly 220 students across the whole faculty. **Sociology** is one of the available majors students elect at the end of Year 1, with approximately 25–35 sociology majors per cohort.

**Curriculum structure**: Year 1 students complete 24 credits of foundation coursework, including *Introduction to Social Science Research Methods*, *Introduction to Sociology*, *Introduction to Psychology*, and *Introduction to Politics & Public Administration*. From Year 2, sociology majors take core courses in **sociological theory, social research methods, and social statistics**. Advanced electives include urban sociology, sociology of gender, family sociology, sociology of religion, media and society, criminology, and medical sociology. Students can pair sociology with minors or second majors in psychology, politics, geography, or journalism.

**JUPAS scoring**: HKU BSocSc uses the **4C+2X** formula. 5** = 8.5; 5* = 7; 5 = 5.5; 4 = 4. The Faculty of Social Sciences requires **interviews for all programmes**, with interview and written test components weighted at around 30% of the overall admission score. The 2025 median admission score was approximately 31 (out of a maximum 51), with a lower quartile near 29.

### 2. The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK) — JS4886 Sociology / JS4801 Social Science

Founded in 1965, the CUHK Department of Sociology is the **oldest and largest** sociology department in Hong Kong. CUHK offers two JUPAS pathways into sociology study:

- **JS4886 Sociology** (direct major): 4-year programme, first-year intake of 37, students enter sociology directly. A **Capstone research project** is required for graduation.
- **JS4801 Social Science** (broad-based): 4-year programme, first-year intake of approximately 130. Students choose a major at the end of Year 1 from 14 departments including sociology, social work, journalism, government & public administration, psychology, geography, anthropology, and gender studies.

**Curriculum highlights**: The CUHK sociology curriculum is organised around **three concentrations**:
1. **Research Methods and Data Analytics** — emphasis on quantitative and computational sociology
2. **Sociology and Social Policy** — focus on healthcare, housing, welfare, and education policy
3. **Society, Sustainability and Technology** — AI ethics, platform economy, climate sociology

**JUPAS scoring**: CUHK uses **4C+2X** or **4C+1X / Best 5** depending on the programme. 5** = 7; 5* = 6; 5 = 5. JS4886 requires **no interview** and admits primarily on DSE results plus OEA; JS4801 sub-pathways may require interviews. 2025 median admission score: JS4886 ≈ 28 (out of 35); JS4801 ≈ 27.

### 3. Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST) — JS5411 BSc Global China Studies (Sociology Track)

HKUST does not have a standalone sociology department, but the **School of Humanities and Social Science (SHSS)** offers **JS5411 Global China Studies (GCS)**, an interdisciplinary BSc in which students may concentrate in **Sociology**. The programme analyses contemporary China through a comparative, globalised lens, training students to examine social structure, inequality, demographic change, and cultural transformation in China and Hong Kong.

**Curriculum structure**: 4-year BSc, first-year intake around 30. Core courses include *Introduction to Global China Studies*, *Social Research Methods*, and *Social Theory*. The sociology track covers **social structure, power and inequality, social change, demography, and everyday culture**. Final-year students complete an **original supervised research project**.

**JUPAS scoring**: HKUST SHSS uses **Best 5 subjects** (including Chinese, English, and Maths cores). 5** = 7; 5* = 6; 5 = 5. Typical entry requirement: 4 core + 2 elective subjects at 332A33 or above. SHSS programmes generally **require no interview**. 2025 median admission score for JS5411: approximately 22 (Best 5).

### 4. Hong Kong Baptist University (HKBU) — JS2310 BSocSc Sociology

HKBU's BSocSc (Hons) in Sociology is housed in the **Department of Sociology**, with strong faculty research in cultural sociology, urban studies, family and marriage, and qualitative methods. The programme is 4-year, first-year intake of approximately 25.

**Curriculum highlights**: The HKBU sociology curriculum is known for **strong qualitative methods training** — ethnography, in-depth interviews, life history, and visual sociology. Core courses include *Classical and Contemporary Sociological Theory*, *Quantitative and Qualitative Research Methods*, and *Social Statistics with SPSS/R*. Distinctive electives cover the sociology of food, the sociology of religion, popular culture in Asia, and gender in Chinese society. Students may participate in **field trips to mainland Chinese cities and Southeast Asia** as part of comparative research courses.

**JUPAS scoring**: HKBU uses **Best 5** with English weighting × 1.5. 5** = 7; 5* = 6; 5 = 5. **No interview required** for most applicants; admission is primarily DSE-based with OEA review. 2025 median admission score: approximately 22 (Best 5 with English weighting).

### 5. Lingnan University (LingnanU) — JS7304 Sociology

Lingnan University, Hong Kong's only liberal arts university, offers **JS7304 BSocSc (Hons) Sociology** through the Department of Sociology and Social Policy. The programme features small class sizes (first-year intake approximately 30) and a strong liberal-arts ethos.

**Curriculum highlights**: Lingnan's sociology programme emphasises **policy-oriented training** with substantial overlap with the Department's social policy stream. Core courses include *Sociological Theory*, *Research Methods in Sociology*, and *Social Statistics*. Notable specialisations include **gerontology and ageing studies** (Lingnan is Hong Kong's leading hub for ageing-policy research through the Asia-Pacific Institute of Ageing Studies), **social inequality and welfare**, and **urban and community studies**. Students complete a **mandatory internship** and a **final-year independent research project**.

**JUPAS scoring**: Lingnan uses **Best 5** with English × 1.0, Chinese × 1.0, and elective × 1.0. 5** = 7; 5* = 6; 5 = 5. **No interview required**. 2025 median admission score: approximately 19 (Best 5).

### 6. The Education University of Hong Kong (EdUHK) — Social Sciences Pathway

EdUHK does not offer a "Sociology" major directly, but its **BSocSc (Hons) in Greater China Studies (JS8108)** and **BSocSc (Hons) in Global and Hong Kong Studies (JS8120)** incorporate substantial sociological content, particularly in social inequality, urban sociology, and migration. These programmes suit students interested in policy work, NGO careers, or postgraduate study in sociology with a regional focus.

**JUPAS scoring**: EdUHK uses **Best 5**. 5** = 7; 5* = 6; 5 = 5. **No interview required**. 2025 median admission score: approximately 18–19 (Best 5).

## Curriculum Comparison: How the Six Programmes Differ

While all six programmes share a common backbone of sociological theory, research methods, and statistics, their distinctive emphases differ significantly:

- **HKU**: Broadest disciplinary exposure (BSocSc shared with politics, psychology, geography), highest research output, strongest path to overseas PhD admission.
- **CUHK**: Most comprehensive sociology curriculum in Hong Kong; explicit **data analytics** concentration is unique; deep faculty bench in stratification, family, and computational sociology.
- **HKUST**: Comparative and global-China focus; quantitative-heavy; strong for students who want sociology embedded in interdisciplinary social science.
- **HKBU**: Qualitative methods strength; cultural and media sociology; field-research orientation.
- **LingnanU**: Liberal-arts pedagogy; **ageing and policy** specialisation; mandatory internship; small class sizes.
- **EdUHK**: Regional studies (Greater China / Global HK) flavour; lighter on sociological theory but stronger on applied policy.

## Sociology vs. Adjacent Majors: How to Choose

Prospective applicants often weigh sociology against psychology, social work, anthropology, or government and public administration. The key distinctions:

- **vs. Psychology**: Sociology studies **social structures and groups**; psychology focuses on individuals and cognition. Pick sociology if you find inequality, institutions, and culture more compelling than mental processes.
- **vs. Social Work**: Social work is **practice-oriented and licensed** (RSW registration); sociology is **research/analysis-oriented**. Social work has clearer occupational pathways but less theoretical depth.
- **vs. Anthropology**: Anthropology emphasises **ethnography and culture**; sociology emphasises **quantitative analysis and structural theory**. Many HK departments combine both.
- **vs. Politics/Public Administration**: PPA focuses on **institutions, governance, and policy design**; sociology examines **social conditions that shape policy**. Sociology graduates are often stronger researchers; PPA graduates more often enter the civil service track directly.

## Graduate Destinations

Sociology graduates in Hong Kong typically pursue one of five pathways:

1. **Research and analytics**: UX researcher, market research analyst, data analyst (especially with quantitative concentration). Major employers include Nielsen, Kantar, Ipsos, internal research teams at HSBC, Hang Seng Bank, and tech firms like Tencent and ByteDance.
2. **Public sector**: Government Administrative Officer (AO) / Executive Officer (EO), Census and Statistics Department, Social Welfare Department, Equal Opportunities Commission, Hong Kong Council of Social Service.
3. **NGO and social enterprise**: Programme officer at Oxfam, Caritas, Save the Children, Hong Kong Red Cross. Sociology majors are particularly competitive for evaluation and impact-assessment roles.
4. **Media and communications**: Journalists, content strategists, documentary producers. Strong fit with newspapers like *Ming Pao*, *SCMP*, and broadcasters like RTHK.
5. **Postgraduate study**: Master's or PhD in sociology, social policy, public health, or urban studies. Top HK sociology departments routinely place graduates at Oxford, Cambridge, LSE, Chicago, Wisconsin–Madison, NUS, and Peking University.

**Starting salary range (2025 data)**: HKD 18,000–28,000/month for fresh graduates, with research and policy positions clustering around HKD 22,000–25,000. Sociology majors with strong quantitative skills (R, Python, SPSS, Stata) command a 15–25% premium and often move into tech-firm research roles paying HKD 30,000+ from the start.

## Application Strategy for 2026

1. **Verify JUPAS programme codes and weightings** on the JUPAS portal and individual university websites — these can shift year to year, especially for HKU (faculty-based admission) and CUHK (broad-based vs. direct entry).
2. **Strengthen English**: At HKBU, English carries a 1.5 multiplier; at HKU, Faculty of Social Sciences interviews are conducted in English. A Level 5 or above in DSE English meaningfully shifts your competitive position.
3. **Prepare OEA carefully**: Sociology departments value evidence of social engagement (community service, debate, journalism, research projects). Quantitative achievements (a maths or statistics-related competition) help signal research aptitude.
4. **Practice the HKU interview**: HKU BSocSc interviews assess critical reasoning on contemporary social issues. Prepare to discuss inequality, ageing, AI ethics, and the impact of mainland integration on Hong Kong society.
5. **Consider Band A vs. Band B placement**: If your predicted DSE is 26–29, JS4886 (CUHK Sociology) and JS5411 (HKUST GCS) are strong Band A choices; JS6717 should be reserved for candidates predicting 30+.

## Conclusion

Sociology in Hong Kong sits at a unique intellectual crossroads: a globally competitive research environment with direct empirical access to one of the world's most complex urban societies. The six programmes covered here span the full range from research-elite (HKU, CUHK, HKUST) to practice-oriented (LingnanU, EdUHK) and culturally rich (HKBU). For DSE candidates considering sociology in 2026, the choice should rest less on rankings and more on **methodological orientation** (quantitative vs. qualitative), **disciplinary breadth** (broad social science vs. direct sociology entry), and **post-graduation pathway** (research, policy, NGO, or industry). Whichever path you choose, a Hong Kong sociology degree opens doors across Asia and beyond — particularly for students who pair sociological training with strong data skills, fluent English, and a clear social purpose.
