# Hong Kong Politics, Public Administration & International Relations Bachelor Complete Guide 2026

For DSE students drawn to elections, geopolitics, civil service exams, NGO work, think-tanks, diplomacy or policy journalism, Hong Kong offers a surprisingly wide spectrum of undergraduate programmes — from the classical "Government and Public Administration" at CUHK to CityU's strongly internationalised "International Relations and Global Affairs" and HKBU's interdisciplinary GIS major. The labels overlap, but the curricula, JUPAS competition and career outcomes are very different. This guide unpacks each programme for **2026 entry**, with JUPAS codes, subject weighting, indicative scores, exchange and internship features, and what the degree realistically leads to.

## 1. Why study politics / public administration / IR in Hong Kong in 2026?

Hong Kong sits at the intersection of Chinese governance, common-law institutions, ASEAN trade flows and global finance — a near-unique laboratory for studying public policy and international affairs. Several structural factors make 2026 a strong year to enter the field:

- The **HKSAR Government** continues to recruit Administrative Officers (AO), Executive Officers (EO) and Labour Officers via the Civil Service Examination (CRE); social science graduates dominate the AO intake.
- The **Greater Bay Area** integration has expanded policy-research jobs at Hong Kong-based think-tanks (Bauhinia Foundation, Our Hong Kong Foundation, MWYO, Civic Exchange) and at consultancies that advise mainland firms entering ASEAN markets.
- International organisations with an Asia-Pacific footprint (UN ESCAP, ADB, WTO, ICC) continue to draw HK graduates with bilingual or trilingual skills.
- The professional skills overlap strongly with **law, journalism, management consulting and ESG advisory** — so the degree opens doors well beyond "politics" jobs in the narrow sense.

Critically, these programmes are also among the more *affordable* pathways into elite professional networks: tuition is HK$44,500/year for local students, and most departments have generous exchange and internship funding.

## 3. CUHK Government and Public Administration (JS4848)

### 3.1 What you study

CUHK's Department of Government and Public Administration (GPA) is the oldest dedicated politics department in Hong Kong, founded in 1969. The BSocSc covers **four pillars**:

1. Political theory and comparative politics
2. Public administration and public management
3. International relations and Chinese foreign policy
4. Hong Kong, China and Greater China politics

Core courses include *Introduction to Political Science*, *Public Administration*, *Hong Kong Government and Politics*, *Comparative Politics*, *International Relations* and *Research Methods*. Senior-year electives push into specialised areas — political economy, Chinese politics, public policy analysis, e-governance, and global governance.

### 3.2 Subject weighting & JUPAS competition

CUHK uses its standard "best 5" formula with English × 1.5 for most arts/social-science programmes. JS4848 does **not** require a specific elective — any HKDSE electives are accepted, though **History, Chinese History, Economics, BAFS or Geography** demonstrably help in interviews and essays. CUHK has not published a fixed score floor; competitive applicants typically achieve mid Level-5 across core subjects with at least one strong essay-based elective.

Tip: For JS4848, framing your school-based assessment portfolio around **public-policy or current-affairs themes** (Liberal Studies / Citizenship and Social Development projects, debating teams, Model UN) is more useful than yet another math-Olympiad line.

### 3.3 Exchange, internships and capstone

GPA students enjoy CUHK's college-based exchange system (United, New Asia, Chung Chi, Shaw, etc.), with partner universities including Sciences Po, LSE, Georgetown, NUS and Peking University. The department also runs a **Washington DC Internship Programme** and a **Capstone Research Project** that is widely respected by employers — many AO/EO recruits come out of this pipeline.

### 3.4 Career outcomes

The most-trodden paths: Administrative Officer, Executive Officer, policy researcher at think-tanks, legislative councillor's office assistant, NGO programme officer, and management trainee in regulated industries (MTR, Airport Authority, Hospital Authority). A meaningful minority continue to JD/PCLL conversion or MPP/MPA programmes at Oxford, Harvard Kennedy, LSE or NUS LKY School.

## 4. HKU Politics and Public Administration (BSocSc PPA)

### 4.1 Admission structure

HKU's Faculty of Social Sciences admits the bulk of its undergraduates through a **broad BSocSc programme** — students choose their major (Politics and Public Administration, Sociology, Psychology, Social Work, Geography, etc.) in their first or second year. PPA is one of HKU's flagship majors, with strong reputational weight inside the civil service and the financial-services policy world.

A small number of specialised double-degrees (e.g. **BSocSc (Government & Laws)**, the **Bachelor of Arts and Sciences (Social Data Science)** with a political-data slant) admit directly. The double-degree routes are highly competitive — typically requiring scores comparable to HKU Law.

### 4.2 Curriculum

The PPA major builds on three cores — *Public Administration*, *Comparative Politics* and *International Relations* — and offers tracks in Chinese politics, political economy, security studies and quantitative political methods. HKU's strength is its **research culture**: the department has a high concentration of internationally cited scholars, and undergraduates are encouraged into directed-study projects with faculty.

### 4.3 Subject weighting & JUPAS competition

HKU uses programme-specific formulas with English × 1.5 in many social-science admissions; the BSocSc broad route is competitive but more flexible than direct-entry programmes. Aim for **English Level 5 or above**; at least one strong essay-based elective (History, Economics, Chinese History, BAFS or Geography); and a clean DSE profile without sub-Level-3 results in any core.

### 4.4 Distinctive opportunities

- **HKU Worldwide Undergraduate Student Exchange Programme** — over 350 partner universities; PPA majors regularly secure exchanges at Oxford, Cambridge, Yale, UPenn and the Australian National University.
- **Common Core** courses pull in students from Law, Medicine, Engineering and Business — a useful network for later think-tank or consulting careers.
- HKU's location in **Pok Fu Lam** and proximity to consulates make in-term internships at diplomatic missions practical.

### 4.5 Careers

Civil service AO/EO, multinational consultancies (McKinsey, Bain, BCG actively recruit HKU social-science graduates), regulatory affairs, in-house government affairs at banks (HSBC, Standard Chartered, JPMorgan), and a sizeable pipeline into UK/US graduate schools.

## 5. CityU International Relations and Global Affairs (JS1102)

### 5.1 Streamed degree

JS1102 is the **BSocSc International Relations and Global Affairs**, with three declared streams:

1. **Asian Studies** — Chinese and East Asian politics, language electives
2. **International Studies** — global political economy, security studies, diplomacy
3. **Development Studies** — Global South, NGOs, sustainable development, aid

The streamed model is appealing if you already have a concrete career image — for example, a development-NGO trajectory benefits enormously from CityU's Development Studies stream, which is one of the few of its kind in Hong Kong.

### 5.2 Subject weighting & indicative scores

CityU's 2026 admission-score formula for **JS1108 Public Affairs and Management** (a useful comparator) is best of 3 cores + 2 electives with **English × 1.5** — **median score 23.5, lower quartile 23** in the most recent admissions cycle. JS1102 IRGA tends to land within a comparable band, with strong applicants typically holding Level 4–5 across core subjects.

### 5.3 Internationalisation

CityU is unusually international for a Hong Kong programme — it operates exchange partnerships with Columbia, KCL, Sciences Po and Waseda, and the **Global Studies Tracks** include language credit (Mandarin, Japanese, French, Korean). The Department of Asian and International Studies (AIS) also offers a **dedicated international-relations internship** at think-tanks and chambers of commerce.

### 5.4 Careers

Graduates trend slightly more towards **multinational corporates, consulates, journalism, and NGOs** than towards the local civil service — the inverse of CUHK JS4848's bias. A small but meaningful percentage pursue MA programmes in Security Studies, International Affairs or Development at SOAS, Sciences Po PSIA, Yale Jackson, or Georgetown SFS.

## 6. CityU Public Affairs and Management (JS1108)

### 6.1 Streamed degree

Two streams:

1. **Public Affairs and Governance** — political analysis, public administration, governance frameworks
2. **Public Policy and Management** — quantitative policy analysis, public-sector management, programme evaluation

This is the more **domestic-policy-flavoured** sibling of JS1102. If your ambition is the Hong Kong civil service or a policy-think-tank in the GBA, JS1108 is arguably better-positioned than the IR-heavy JS1102.

### 6.2 Admission

As above — median 23.5 / LQ 23 in the most recent intake. Applicants with strong **Mathematics and an essay-based elective** (Economics, History, Chinese History) are particularly well placed.

### 6.3 Distinctive opportunities

- **Public-sector internship placements** with bureaux and statutory bodies
- **Joint courses with CityU's School of Law and College of Business** — useful for students considering a JD or MBA conversion later
- Sizeable cohort of **mainland and international students**, providing comparative perspective

## 7. HKBU Government and International Studies (within JS2610)

### 7.1 The "broad-admit" model

HKBU admits Arts and Social Sciences students through the broad code **JS2610** (BA/BSocSc majors in European Studies, Geography, Global and China Studies, Government and International Studies, History, Sociology). Students choose **Government and International Studies (GIS)** as a major after a foundation semester.

### 7.2 Curriculum

GIS at HKBU is **interdisciplinary by design** — students take a structured core in political theory, comparative politics, international relations and research methods, then build minors across humanities and other social sciences. The department has unusually strong **European Studies** ties (it owns a European Studies major with French/German streams), so HKBU GIS students gain access to EU-focused electives that are rare elsewhere in Hong Kong.

### 7.3 Admission

JS2610 is generally **more accessible** than CUHK JS4848 or HKU PPA in terms of JUPAS competition, while still delivering a strong qualification. HKBU's weighting for the broad code uses standard best-5 with English × 1.5; applicants with strengths in **English, History, Chinese History, Economics, Geography or Liberal Studies / Citizenship & Social Development** should be in the conversation.

### 7.4 Career outcomes

HKBU GIS graduates trend towards **media, NGOs, education, public-sector and SME public-affairs roles**. The department also has a strong record in placing students into UK MA programmes (Edinburgh, KCL, SOAS).

## 9. JUPAS subject choice — what to take in S5/S6

For all five programmes, a few elective combinations dominate the admit pool:

- **History + Economics** — the classical politics combo; trains both narrative and analytical writing
- **Chinese History + Economics** — distinctive for China-track careers (CUHK JS4848, HKU PPA-China)
- **Geography + Economics** — strongest for development studies (CityU JS1102 Development stream)
- **BAFS + Economics** — preferred for IR/global-affairs careers eyeing consulting
- **M1/M2** — not required, but a Level-3+ in either adds quantitative credibility for HKU PPA and CityU JS1108

You do **not** need a science elective for any of these programmes. Two essay-based electives are typically more useful than mixing in Biology or Physics.

## 10. Beyond the headline: interviews, portfolios and "polish"

All five programmes invite borderline applicants to **interview** in Bands A and B. What the panels are testing:

- **Awareness of current affairs**, both Hong Kong (district council, LegCo, GBA integration) and international (US-China relations, Middle East, EU, ASEAN)
- **Argument construction** — they will hand you a policy proposition (e.g. "Should Hong Kong introduce a sugar tax?") and ask you to take a position and defend it for 5–10 minutes
- **Source literacy** — be ready to name books or analysts you've read; do not over-claim, but having read *one* credible book on HK politics (Lo Shiu-hing, Steve Tsang, Sonny Lo, Brian Fong) goes a long way
- **English fluency** — most interviews are conducted in English; some at CUHK include a Cantonese component

Useful preparation:

- Subscribe to *South China Morning Post* and *HKFP* daily; *The Economist* weekly
- Follow **HKU Centre for Civil Society and Governance**, **CUHK Hong Kong Institute of Asia-Pacific Studies**, **CityU School of Energy and Environment policy briefs**
- Join a debating or Model UN club in S5; the practice is closer to interview format than school discussion classes

## 11. Realistic career landscape after graduation

### 11.1 Civil service

The **Administrative Officer (AO)** grade is the most prestigious entry route — starting salary around **HK$60,000+/month** with very fast progression. Recruitment requires the **Common Recruitment Examination (CRE)** (Aptitude Test, English, Chinese, Basic Law & National Security Law tests). **Executive Officer (EO)** is the second tier — starting around HK$32,000–HK$36,000/month with steady progression. EO is realistic and stable; AO is selective and elite. Both grades draw heavily from politics/public administration graduates.

### 11.2 Statutory bodies and policy professions

The **Hong Kong Monetary Authority, Securities and Futures Commission, Insurance Authority, Hospital Authority, Equal Opportunities Commission, Ombudsman's Office, Mandatory Provident Fund Schemes Authority** all hire graduate-trainee cohorts that take politics, public administration, economics and law backgrounds.

### 11.3 Think-tanks, consultancies and government-affairs

Roles range from **policy researcher (HK$25,000–35,000 starting)** at smaller think-tanks to **management consulting analyst (HK$45,000–55,000+)** at MBB firms. Big Four advisory practices (Deloitte Government & Public Services, PwC Strategy&) also hire selectively from this pool.

### 11.4 NGOs, international organisations, media

NGO programme-officer roles tend to start lower (HK$18,000–25,000) but include significant international-deployment opportunities. United Nations Junior Professional Officer programmes are open after a master's degree and 2+ years of relevant experience.

### 11.5 Law and graduate school

Many graduates pursue **JD/PCLL conversion** for a legal career, or **MPA/MPP** programmes at Harvard Kennedy, Princeton Woodrow Wilson (now SPIA), LSE, Oxford Blavatnik, NUS LKY School, and Sciences Po PSIA. Scholarships from the HKSAR Government's **Hong Kong Scholarship for Excellence Scheme** and the **Hong Kong PhD Fellowship Scheme** are highly relevant.

## 12. Decision framework: which programme should you band?

- **You're aiming squarely at the HK civil service / Hong Kong policy think-tanks** → CUHK JS4848 or HKU PPA (Band A both); CityU JS1108 as a strong Band B.
- **You want international / development / NGO careers** → CityU JS1102 (IRGA) — particularly the Development or International Studies stream.
- **You're research-oriented, considering a PhD or top-tier MPP** → HKU PPA via BSocSc, with a deliberate plan to take quantitative methods electives.
- **You want flexibility — possible double-major in History, Sociology, European Studies** → HKBU JS2610 with a GIS major path.
- **You want the strongest Greater Bay Area / China-policy weighting** → CUHK JS4848 (the China-politics teaching is genuinely deeper here).

A pragmatic Band A/B/C might be: **A1 CUHK JS4848 → A2 HKU BSocSc → A3 CityU JS1102 → B1 CityU JS1108 → B2 HKBU JS2610 → C1 EdUHK or HKMU social-science fallback**.

## 13. Final notes for S5 students reading this in May 2026

You have 18 months to position yourself for these programmes. The single highest-leverage move is **structured current-affairs reading**, not extra tutorial sessions. Spend 30 minutes daily on SCMP and a weekly long-form policy publication. Join a debating, Model UN or mock-trial team. Pick one local policy area (housing, transport, ageing, climate adaptation) and write a short essay on it that you can revise into an interview talking-point. Strong English writing — clear, persuasive, succinct — is the single most predictive admissions signal across all five programmes.

Pair that with disciplined DSE preparation in English, Mathematics, and two essay-heavy electives, and your offer is well within reach.
