# Hong Kong Top Talent Pass Scheme (TTPS) Complete Guide 2026

The Top Talent Pass Scheme (TTPS, 高才通) is the flagship piece of Hong Kong's post-pandemic talent strategy. Launched in late 2022 and refined every year since, it has become the single most popular route for overseas-trained graduates, mid-career professionals and high earners to relocate to Hong Kong without first securing a job offer. By the start of 2026 the eligible universities list has grown to **200 institutions**, the renewal model has stabilised into a clear "2 + 3 + 3" pattern, and a new **Top-Tier Stream** lets high earners jump straight to a six-year extension.

This guide walks DSE-pathway readers and overseas Chinese students through TTPS in depth: who qualifies under Categories A, B and C, what changed in 2026, how renewal interacts with the seven-year permanent residence (PR) clock, and where TTPS sits relative to **IANG** (the post-study work visa for Hong Kong-trained graduates) and the **Quality Migrant Admission Scheme** (QMAS).

> **Quick orientation.** TTPS is a *talent admission* scheme, not a study visa. If you completed a Hong Kong undergraduate degree, you must apply for **IANG**, not TTPS — non-local graduates of Hong Kong undergraduate programmes are explicitly excluded from Category C. TTPS is the right scheme if your degree is from an eligible *non-Hong Kong* university (mainland China, UK, US, Australia, etc.) or if your annual income clears the Category A threshold.

## 1. The three categories at a glance

| Category | Who it targets | Core requirement | Initial stay | Quota |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | High-income earners | HK$2.5 million+ annual income in the year before application (employment, business, salaries plus stock options/profits) | 36 months | None |
| **B** | Experienced graduates | Bachelor's degree from an eligible university **and** at least 3 years of work experience in the past 5 years | 24 months | None |
| **C** | Recent graduates | Bachelor's degree from an eligible university obtained in the past 5 years, with less than 3 years of work experience | 24 months | **10,000 per year**, first-come first-served |

Category A is uncapped and aimed at senior executives, founders and dual-listed company employees. Category B is the workhorse for mid-career hires. Category C is the most contested: a fixed annual quota of 10,000 places allocated strictly on a first-come, first-served basis, which is why January and February applications close out far faster than later in the year.

A few qualifications apply across all three categories:

- No security objection and no record of serious crime.
- The applicant must be able to maintain themselves and any dependants without recourse to public funds.
- Online, distance learning, evening, self-taught and part-time bachelor's degrees from "eligible universities" do **not** count for Categories B or C. The qualifying degree must have been earned through full-time, in-person study.
- Holding a Hong Kong undergraduate degree as a non-local graduate disqualifies you from Category C — you should apply for IANG instead.
- TTPS is a one-time scheme. If your application is refused, or if you previously held TTPS and left, you cannot reapply.

## 2. What changed for 2026

Two updates dominate the 2026 cycle.

### 2.1 The aggregate list expands to 200 universities

Effective **1 January 2026**, the aggregate list of eligible universities was raised from 198 to 200 institutions. The list is built by combining the union of the top 100 from the four major global rankings (THE, QS, US News and Shanghai ARWU), with additional named institutions in hospitality, art and design, and a curated list of leading mainland Chinese universities. New entries are typically added when an institution rises into the global top 100 in any of the four rankings; entries are rarely removed mid-year, but applicants should always confirm against the **current** list published on the Immigration Department site, since the list is the version in force on the date your application is *received*.

The expansion mostly benefits applicants whose alma mater jumped into the top 100 of QS or THE in the 2025–26 cycle, including several mainland double first-class universities that previously sat just outside.

### 2.2 The Top-Tier Employment Stream

The biggest renewal change — introduced in the previous cycle and now fully operationalised — is the **Top-Tier Employment Stream**. Holders who have stayed under TTPS for at least 2 years and whose Hong Kong **assessable salaries-tax income** in the previous year of assessment is **HK$2 million or more** can apply for an extension of up to **6 years** in one go, instead of the usual 3-year renewal. This compresses the standard 2 + 3 + 3 path into 2 + 6, accelerating the run-up to permanent residence at year 7.

## 3. Eligible universities: how the list actually works

The "aggregate list" is the operative document. It contains roughly 200 institutions and is split into two major parts:

1. **The world top-100 union list** — any institution that has appeared in the top 100 of THE, QS, US News or Shanghai ARWU in the past five years.
2. **The leading mainland universities list** — Project 985, selected Project 211 and a small number of named research institutes.

Plus a handful of specialist additions in hospitality (e.g. EHL Lausanne) and art and design.

Two practical points are easy to miss:

- The applicable list is the one published **on the date the Immigration Department receives your application**. If your university was added on 1 January 2026 and you applied on 31 December 2025, your application will be assessed against the older list. Check carefully before submitting.
- Joint or transnational programmes (e.g. UNNC, XJTLU, NYU Shanghai, UIC, Duke Kunshan) generally count under the **awarding institution**'s entry on the list, not the campus location. So a UNNC graduate counts under University of Nottingham (UK), not Ningbo.

## 4. Application procedure and fees

The end-to-end TTPS workflow is unusually compact for a Hong Kong immigration scheme.

**Step 1 — Document set.** Prepare your passport, HKID/exit-entry permit (if applicable), full-time bachelor's degree certificate and transcript, employment / income proof, criminal record clearance and a completed ID 1026 application form. Category A applicants must provide tax returns, employment contracts, share allotment documents or business financials substantiating the HK$2.5 million figure. Category B applicants must demonstrate 3 years of qualifying work experience through employment letters, payslips and (where relevant) tax records. Category C applicants only need the degree and a minimal personal statement, since work history is intentionally not assessed.

**Step 2 — Online submission.** Submit through the e-Visa portal. Most TTPS applications are processed in **4 to 6 weeks** for straightforward Category C cases and 6 to 8 weeks for Categories A and B, where evidence verification takes longer. There is no requirement to land in Hong Kong before approval.

**Step 3 — Visa label and arrival.** Once approved you receive an e-Visa label, which you print and carry on first entry. You must register for a Hong Kong Identity Card within 30 days of arrival.

**Step 4 — Settle in.** TTPS holders are free to take up and change employment during the permitted stay **without prior approval**. You can also start a business or freelance, provided you can substantiate the activity at renewal time.

The application fee is modest — a non-refundable HK$190 for processing and HK$230 for the visa label on approval, with similar amounts for each dependant.

## 5. Renewal: the 2 + 3 + 3 path versus 2 + 6

The renewal architecture is what most candidates underestimate. TTPS approval is the easy part; staying long enough to qualify for permanent residence is where careful planning matters.

### 5.1 Standard 2 + 3 + 3 renewal

Categories B and C start with 24 months. To extend, you must show **stable employment** or an **established Hong Kong business** at the renewal date. The Immigration Department typically grants:

- **First extension:** up to 3 years (or the length of your employment contract if shorter).
- **Second extension:** up to 3 more years on the same basis.

So a typical Category C graduate progresses: 2 years initial → 3 years extension → 3 years second extension = 8 years total, which comfortably crosses the 7-year continuous residence line required for permanent residence under section 2AAA of the Immigration Ordinance.

Apply for renewal **within 3 months before expiry** and submit **at least 6 weeks** before your visa runs out. Processing usually takes 2 to 3 weeks. Crucial point: an unemployment gap at the renewal date is grounds for refusal. The Department wants to see a current contract or, at minimum, a signed offer letter for a degree-level role.

### 5.2 Top-Tier Stream (2 + 6)

If your assessable salaries-tax income in the previous year of assessment was HK$2 million or more, you can request an extension of up to **6 years** at first renewal — collapsing the path to 2 + 6 = 8 years total. This is now the default play for high earners in finance, tech and senior consulting roles, since it removes one renewal cycle entirely and decouples your status from a single employer for a longer window.

### 5.3 What "previous year of assessment" actually means

Hong Kong's tax year runs **1 April to 31 March**. The assessable income figure used by the Immigration Department is the one you reported on your salaries-tax return for the year ending the most recent 31 March before your renewal application. So a renewal lodged in October 2026 will be assessed against your 2025/26 income (1 April 2025 to 31 March 2026). Plan stock vesting and bonus timing accordingly if you are close to the HK$2 million line.

## 6. Dependants

Spouses and unmarried children under 18 may accompany the principal applicant. Dependant visas track the principal's stay length exactly. Importantly, dependant spouses are **allowed to take up employment or study** without further approval, and dependant children can attend any school in Hong Kong on the same basis as local children.

Sponsorship requires:

- Genuine relationship evidence (marriage certificate, birth certificates).
- No adverse immigration record.
- Financial sponsorship "well above the subsistence level" — there is no published income floor, but most successful sponsors show monthly income at least 3 to 4 times the household subsistence cost, with a stable address in Hong Kong.

Time spent in Hong Kong by dependants also counts towards their own 7-year PR clock. For dual-career couples, this means both partners can independently qualify for permanent residence around year 7, even if only one of them ever holds the principal TTPS status.

## 7. TTPS vs IANG vs QMAS — which scheme should you use?

For DSE-pathway and overseas Chinese readers, the choice usually comes down to three options.

| Scheme | Best for | Initial stay | Job needed up front? | Quota |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **TTPS** | Graduates of eligible non-HK universities | 24 or 36 months | No | Cat C: 10,000/year |
| **IANG** | Non-local graduates of HK universities (UGC-funded or eligible self-financing) | 24 months | No | None |
| **QMAS** | Talents with strong points-based profiles, including world champions and specialists | Varies | No | Annual quota |

If you completed your undergraduate or postgraduate degree in **Hong Kong**, IANG is faster, free and has no quota — TTPS Category C is closed to you anyway. If you completed your degree at a top-100 university **outside** Hong Kong, TTPS Category B or C is almost always the right answer. QMAS only makes sense if you have an exceptional non-degree profile (Olympic-level athlete, awarded scientist, etc.) and want to skip the employment requirement entirely.

## 8. Strategic timing for the PR clock

The seven-year continuous ordinary residence requirement under the Immigration Ordinance starts on the date of your **first lawful entry** under TTPS, not the visa approval date. The clock continues to run during business trips and short overseas absences, provided your "ordinary residence" remains Hong Kong (i.e. you keep a home, banking and tax footprint in the city).

For most Category C applicants, the realistic permanent residence timeline therefore looks like:

- **Year 0:** Apply, receive e-Visa, land and register HKID.
- **Years 0–2:** Initial stay. Find and hold a degree-level role.
- **Year 2:** First extension (3 years standard, or 6 years under Top-Tier Stream).
- **Year 5:** Second extension if needed (3 years).
- **Year 7:** Apply for permanent residence using form ROP145, with continuous residence evidence (employment, lease/property, MPF, salaries-tax records, utility bills).

Applicants on the 2 + 6 Top-Tier track only need to renew once before reaching the seven-year mark. Those on the 2 + 3 + 3 track will renew twice, with the second renewal often overlapping the PR application window.

## 9. Common pitfalls

A few recurring mistakes derail otherwise strong TTPS applicants.

- **Submitting before your university appears on the list.** The list version on receipt date governs eligibility. If you graduated from a university that just entered the world top 100, wait until the next aggregate list publication before submitting.
- **Counting part-time or online degrees.** Categories B and C explicitly require full-time, in-person bachelor's degrees. Distance, evening and self-taught degrees are excluded.
- **Letting employment lapse before renewal.** Even a one-month gap between contracts at the renewal date can trigger refusal. Time job changes so that there is overlap or at least a signed offer in hand on the day you submit.
- **Misreading Category A income.** Cat A income includes salary, allowances, stock options *that vested in the year* and profits from your own company — not paper net worth or real estate gains. Documentation must reconcile to tax filings in your home jurisdiction.
- **Confusing TTPS with QMAS scoring.** TTPS does not use a points test. You either meet a category threshold or you do not. There is no value in submitting "extra" qualifications beyond what each category requires.
- **Holding off on HKID registration.** You must register within 30 days of arrival. Late registration can complicate later renewal and PR applications.

## 10. Bottom line

TTPS in 2026 is the cleanest, fastest path to Hong Kong residence for graduates of an eligible overseas or mainland university. The 200-university list has widened the funnel, the Top-Tier Stream has compressed the renewal cycle, and Category C remains the most generous option ever offered to recent graduates anywhere in Asia — provided you secure your spot in the annual 10,000-person quota early in the year.

For DSE-pathway readers, the decision tree is simple. If you read this guide as a current Hong Kong undergraduate, plan around **IANG**, not TTPS. If you are studying overseas at a top-100 university and want a return route to Hong Kong, **TTPS Category C** is your primary tool — apply within five years of graduation, ideally in January or February to lock in quota, and plan your employment so that the 2 + 3 + 3 (or 2 + 6) renewal path lines up cleanly with the seven-year PR window.
