# City University of Hong Kong (CityU) Complete Guide 2026

Among Hong Kong's eight UGC-funded universities, City University of Hong Kong — CityU, or CityUHK as the institution itself now styles the name — sits in an interesting middle position. It is younger than HKU, CUHK, PolyU and the Education University. It is consistently ranked behind HKU, CUHK and HKUST on the global tables. And yet on a number of measures that matter to undergraduates — global internationalisation, the only veterinary medicine degree in the territory, the strongest school of creative media in Asia, and triple-accredited business education — CityU is genuinely first not just in Hong Kong but across Asia.

In QS World University Rankings 2026, CityU is placed #=63 globally and #7 in Asia. In the Times Higher Education World University Rankings, it has been named the world's most international university for two consecutive years (2024 and 2025). Five of its subjects sit in the QS global top 50 in 2026, and its Bachelor of Veterinary Medicine programme is the only RCVS- and AVBC-dual-accredited veterinary degree in Asia.

This guide walks through what CityU is in 2026, the JUPAS and non-JUPAS pathways, tuition and scholarships, the flagship colleges, accommodation, and the career trajectories that follow a CityU degree. If you are deciding between CityU and another UGC institution — or weighing CityU's veterinary, business, creative media or data science programmes against overseas offers — this is the context you need.

## 1. CityU's Identity in 2026

CityU was founded in 1984 as the City Polytechnic of Hong Kong, and was granted full university status in 1994. Its main campus sits in Kowloon Tong, directly above Festival Walk and a one-minute walk from Kowloon Tong MTR station — making it arguably the most accessible major university campus in the city.

Three features define CityU's identity in 2026:

1. **Genuine global reach.** When THE rates a university the world's most international, it is measuring the share of international students, the share of international staff, the share of international research collaborations, and the share of international research reputation. CityU has come first on this composite measure in both 2024 and 2025 — ahead of every university in Switzerland, the UK, Australia and Singapore. For an applicant who will spend three to four years in a single institution, this is not a marketing claim; it is the daily texture of student life.

2. **Strategic specialisations rather than across-the-board strength.** CityU does not try to compete with HKU on classical liberal arts, or with CUHK on Chinese studies, or with HKUST on engineering breadth. Instead it has invested heavily in a handful of areas where it can lead: veterinary medicine (where it is the only provider in Hong Kong), creative media (where it was Asia's first multimedia school), data science (one of Asia's first dedicated faculties), materials science, and business. Five subjects are in the global QS top 50 for 2026, including a Hong Kong number-one ranking in materials science.

3. **Application-led research culture.** CityU's research output skews towards engineering, applied science, business and creative practice rather than humanities. The institution's name change to "CityUHK" in 2024 was paired with a strategic plan emphasising innovation, technology transfer and industry partnership rather than basic research alone.

## 2. College Structure

CityU's undergraduate teaching is organised across ten colleges and schools:

- **College of Business (CB)** — Accounting, Economics & Finance, Information Systems, Management, Marketing, Management Sciences. Triple-accredited by AACSB, EQUIS and AMBA — only a small minority of business schools globally hold all three.
- **College of Engineering (CEng)** — Architecture & Civil Engineering, Computer Science, Electrical Engineering, Biomedical Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Systems Engineering.
- **College of Science (CSci)** — Chemistry, Mathematics, Physics, Biomedical Sciences.
- **College of Liberal Arts and Social Sciences (CLASS)** — Asian & International Studies, Applied Social Sciences, Chinese & History, English, Media & Communication, Linguistics & Translation, Public & International Affairs.
- **CityU Law School (SLW)** — JD, LLB and combined-degree pathways.
- **School of Creative Media (SCM)** — Asia's first multimedia school combining art and technology, founded 1998.
- **Jockey Club College of Veterinary Medicine and Life Sciences (JCC)** — home to the Bachelor of Veterinary Medicine, the only veterinary degree in Hong Kong.
- **School of Energy and Environment (SEE)** — interdisciplinary energy, environmental engineering and sustainability.
- **School of Data Science (SDS)** — established 2018, one of Asia's first dedicated data science faculties.
- **Chow Yei Ching School of Graduate Studies** — coordinates research postgraduate programmes.

## 3. Admission Routes

For 2026 entry CityU operates three principal undergraduate admission routes.

### 3.1 JUPAS (HKDSE)

For HKDSE candidates with permanent-resident or eligible-non-permanent-resident status, JUPAS is the only legitimate route into a UGC-funded place. CityU is one of the eight participating institutions and is well represented in the upper Band A choices for business, computer science, data science, creative media and law.

For 2026 entry, the main admission score calculation is based on one of three formulas, set programme by programme:

- **3 core + best 2 elective subjects** — the most common formula
- **Best 5 subjects** (may include specified subjects)
- **Best 4 subjects** (may include specified subjects)

CityU continues to apply admission flexibility in 2026: applicants who fall slightly short of stated minimum requirements may still be considered, particularly where strengths in specified subjects offset a weaker core score. This is a meaningful difference from the more rigid cut-off behaviour at some other institutions.

Typical Band A median admission scores in 2025 (calculated on the relevant formula) range from the high teens for general arts and social sciences programmes to the low- to mid-30s for the most competitive offerings — Veterinary Medicine, Computer Science (Data Science track), Business Information Systems and Quantitative Finance.

### 3.2 Non-JUPAS (International Qualifications)

All non-local applicants — regardless of whether they hold HKDSE results — must apply via the non-JUPAS route, not via JUPAS. This is a structural rule across all eight UGC institutions and CityU enforces it strictly.

CityU accepts non-JUPAS applications based on:

- **GCE A-Level / IAL** — typically AAB to A*A*A depending on programme
- **IB Diploma** — typically 32 to 40 points depending on programme, with subject-specific HL requirements
- **AP / SAT / ACT** — for applicants from US-system schools
- **Other recognised national qualifications** — Australian ATAR, Canadian provincial diplomas, etc.

English language proficiency is required: IELTS 6.5 (no band below 5.5) or TOEFL iBT 79 are the standard floor, with higher requirements for English, Translation, Law and certain business programmes.

Non-JUPAS applications open in early autumn for the following September intake. Early-round applicants generally have a stronger position than later-round candidates, particularly for popular programmes.

### 3.3 Mainland Gaokao Direct Recruitment

CityU does **not** participate in the unified Mainland recruitment system (统招). Instead it conducts an independent recruitment exercise in all Mainland provinces and municipalities. Applicants apply directly to CityU (not through provincial enrolment offices) on the basis of Gaokao results. This means CityU offers do not interfere with applicants' simultaneous Mainland 一本/二本 applications until acceptance.

Indicative Gaokao thresholds for 2026 entry sit at roughly 30–80 points above the local 一本 line for most provinces, with English performance — typically 120/150 minimum — a separate requirement. The most selective programmes (Veterinary Medicine, Quantitative Finance, Computer Science with Data Science) typically require 100+ points above the 一本 line.

## 4. Tuition and Fees (2026/27)

| Category | Tuition per annum |
|---|---|
| Local (UGC-funded) — most undergraduate programmes | HK$47,000 |
| Non-local — most undergraduate programmes | **HK$190,000** |
| Non-local — Bachelor of Veterinary Medicine | **HK$392,000** |

The non-local tuition is fixed at the rate set in the year of admission and remains at that level for the duration of the programme — a useful predictability for families budgeting four years ahead.

In addition to tuition, students should budget approximately HK$15,000–25,000 per year for hall residence (where allocated), HK$3,000–5,000 per month for off-campus rent (Kowloon Tong / Yau Yat Tsuen / Tai Wai are the typical commuter zones), and HK$3,000–5,000 per month for food, transport and personal expenses. A realistic total cost-of-attendance for a non-local undergraduate is in the range of HK$240,000–280,000 per academic year, before international travel.

## 5. Flagship Programmes

### 5.1 Bachelor of Veterinary Medicine — Asia's only RCVS/AVBC dual-accredited veterinary degree

The six-year BVM, hosted by the Jockey Club College of Veterinary Medicine and Life Sciences, is the only veterinary degree offered in Hong Kong. It was developed in partnership with Cornell University's College of Veterinary Medicine, and is the first and only veterinary programme in Asia to hold dual accreditation from the Royal College of Veterinary Surgeons (RCVS, UK) and the Australasian Veterinary Boards Council (AVBC). Graduates are eligible to register and practise in the UK, Australia, New Zealand, Hong Kong, Singapore and several other jurisdictions without further qualifying examinations.

Annual intake is small — roughly 20 to 30 students — and competition is intense. JUPAS Band A applicants typically present 32+ on the best-five formula plus strong science subjects (Biology and Chemistry are de facto requirements). Non-JUPAS applicants typically present IB 40+ or A-Level A*AA or above, with interview performance a major factor. Non-local tuition is HK$392,000 per annum, reflecting the clinical training cost and the small cohort size.

### 5.2 College of Business — triple-accredited, top-tier in Asia

CityU Business holds AACSB (US), EQUIS (Europe) and AMBA (UK) accreditations — the so-called "triple crown" held by fewer than 1% of business schools worldwide and only a handful in Asia. Its undergraduate flagship streams are Quantitative Finance, Business Analytics, Information Systems and Management, and Accountancy — all of which lead efficiently to investment banking, Big Four, and global consulting recruitment in Hong Kong. The College of Business is consistently ranked in the QS Subject top 50 globally for Business & Management and Accounting & Finance.

### 5.3 School of Creative Media — Asia's pioneer

Founded in 1998, the School of Creative Media (SCM) was the first school in Asia to combine fine art, computer science and film practice in a single degree structure. Its BA in Creative Media and BSc in Creative Media (Computational Track) are unusual in offering serious technical training — game engines, VFX, generative AI tools — alongside artist-led studio practice. SCM graduates are heavily represented in Hong Kong's animation, gaming and digital media industries and increasingly in Shenzhen and Singapore studios.

### 5.4 School of Data Science — Asia's first dedicated faculty

Established in 2018, the School of Data Science was one of the first standalone data science faculties in Asia (most institutions house data science within Computer Science or Statistics departments). Its BSc in Data Science integrates statistics, machine learning, optimisation and domain applications, and is one of the highest-scoring JUPAS programmes at CityU.

### 5.5 Computer Science and Engineering

Within the College of Engineering, the Department of Computer Science offers a BSc Computer Science with multiple concentration tracks — AI, Cybersecurity, Software Engineering, and Computational Intelligence. CityU CS is consistently ranked in the QS top 100 globally and admission is competitive, with Band A medians in the low 30s on the best-five formula.

### 5.6 Flagship Scholarship programmes

CityU's Flagship Programmes — including INSPIRE (International Sustainability Programme for Innovation, Research and Entrepreneurship) at the School of Energy and Environment, and GREAT (Global Research Enrichment and Technopreneurship) for sciences — offer Flagship Scholarships covering tuition and hall fees, a guaranteed berth, and a one-off exchange sponsorship of up to HK$500,000 for selected students. These are awarded competitively after admission and are among the most generous undergraduate awards in Hong Kong.

## 6. Scholarships and Financial Aid

Beyond the Flagship Scholarships, CityU offers several scholarship tiers:

- **Entrance scholarships** for top JUPAS and non-JUPAS admittees, ranging from partial tuition waivers to full coverage plus living stipend
- **Mainland Outstanding Student Scholarship** for top Gaokao admittees from Mainland China — typically full tuition for the duration of study, conditional on maintaining strong academic standing
- **HKSAR Government Scholarships** for outstanding non-local students under the Belt and Road or Targeted Scholarship schemes
- **In-course academic scholarships** awarded annually based on GPA performance

For local students, the standard government tuition assistance schemes (TSFS, NLS) apply; CityU's Student Development Services office administers these alongside emergency hardship grants.

## 7. Accommodation and Campus Life

CityU operates eleven student residence halls in the Student Residence complex on Tat Hong Avenue, immediately adjacent to the academic campus. Total bed capacity is approximately 3,300 — sufficient to guarantee at least one year of residence to non-local first-year undergraduates and to most local first-years from outside Kowloon. Beyond first year, residence is competitive and allocated on a points system that rewards co-curricular contribution.

For students who do not secure on-campus places, the surrounding Kowloon Tong / Yau Yat Tsuen area offers a mix of older walk-up flats and newer serviced studios. Average rents for a shared two-bedroom flat in the immediate area run HK$8,000–12,000 per bedroom; further out (Tai Wai, Sha Tin, Tsuen Wan) rents drop by 20–30%.

The Kowloon Tong location is one of CityU's quiet advantages: 25 minutes by MTR to Central, 15 minutes to Mong Kok, direct East Rail to the boundary at Lo Wu, and a single interchange to the airport via the Tung Chung line. For non-local students who want to explore both Hong Kong Island and the New Territories without committing to either, it is the most balanced campus location among the eight UGC universities.

## 8. Graduate Outcomes

CityU graduates compete strongly in Hong Kong's professional labour market. Recent graduate employment surveys show:

- Overall employment rate within six months of graduation consistently above 90%
- Median starting salary in the HK$22,000–28,000 per month range, with significant variation by discipline
- Quantitative Finance, Computer Science (Data Science track) and Business Information Systems graduates regularly reporting starting packages of HK$35,000–50,000 per month at investment banks, Big Four firms and global tech companies
- Veterinary Medicine graduates entering practice at HK$35,000–45,000 per month and benefiting from acute supply shortage in the Hong Kong, Singapore and UK markets
- Creative Media graduates clustering in the HK$18,000–25,000 range with substantial freelance and project-based variation

Approximately 25–30% of each graduating cohort proceeds to postgraduate study, divided roughly evenly between Hong Kong, the UK and the US, with a growing minority entering Mainland top-tier institutions for taught masters programmes.

## 10. Decision Factors — Is CityU Right For You?

CityU is the best UGC choice if any of the following apply:

- You want to study **veterinary medicine in Asia** at an internationally recognised institution
- You want a **triple-accredited business education** with direct access to Hong Kong's banking and consulting recruitment
- You are interested in **creative media** at a serious technical and artistic level
- You want a campus in the **most internationally diverse student body in the world** by THE's measure
- You value **the most accessible MTR-served campus** in the city
- You want **dedicated data science** rather than data science as a CS sub-track

CityU is probably not the best choice if:

- Your primary interest is classical humanities or Chinese studies (HKU and CUHK are deeper)
- You want the broadest possible engineering portfolio (HKUST is broader)
- You want professional accreditation across the largest range of disciplines (PolyU is denser)
- You require the absolute top global ranking for prestige reasons in your home country

For most other applicants, CityU offers a compelling combination of ranking, internationalisation, specialised excellence and a central campus location that few other Asian universities can match.
