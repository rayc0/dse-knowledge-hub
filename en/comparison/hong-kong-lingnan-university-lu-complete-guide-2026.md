# Lingnan University (LU) Complete Guide 2026

Among Hong Kong's eight UGC-funded universities, **Lingnan University** — LU, or simply *Lingnan* — is the one that does not try to play the comprehensive research-university game. Where HKU, CUHK and HKUST chase global league-table rankings on the back of giant labs and medical schools, Lingnan has spent the last quarter-century sharpening a single, very deliberate identity: **a small, residential, liberal-arts university where the ratio of students to faculty stays low, almost every undergraduate spends a term abroad, and the curriculum forces breadth across the humanities, business and the social sciences**.

Internationally, Lingnan is the only Hong Kong member of the **Global Liberal Arts Alliance** and is one of the very few institutions in Asia that the international press routinely lists among the region's top liberal-arts universities. If you are a DSE candidate, a mainland Gaokao student or an international applicant whose interests sit in language, literature, translation, cultural studies, economics, philosophy, politics, sociology, business or data science — and who values small classes, mandatory hostel life and exchange opportunities over giant lecture halls — Lingnan offers something its larger peers genuinely cannot.

This guide covers the 2026/27 admissions cycle: what kind of university Lingnan really is, where it sits in the 2026 rankings, the three faculties and the School of Interdisciplinary Studies, the JUPAS and non-JUPAS application routes, tuition fees and scholarships, the flagship programmes you should know about, and what graduates actually do after they leave the Tuen Mun campus.

---

## 1. What kind of university is Lingnan?

Lingnan traces its lineage to the **Christian College in China** (格致書院), founded in **Canton (Guangzhou) in 1888**. The institution moved to Hong Kong in **1967** as Lingnan College, and was granted full university status in **1999**. The main campus sits in **Tuen Mun**, New Territories, on Castle Peak Road, hugging the foothills of Castle Peak. It is reached most easily via the West Rail Line to Siu Hong station and then a short bus or taxi ride, or via the Light Rail. The campus is the most "liberal-arts-college" in feel of any in Hong Kong: low-rise red-brick buildings, courtyards, a single central plaza, and dormitories that house roughly 80% of full-time undergraduates.

The "Christian" origin reflects the institution's founding mission, but Lingnan today operates as a fully secular public university. The motto, **"Education for Service"** (作育英才・服務社會), has remained unchanged since the Canton era and is reflected in the university's strong emphasis on community engagement and a mandatory **Service-Learning** component for many programmes.

### Size and campus character

- Around **3,500 undergraduates** and **5,000 postgraduates**, total enrolment of roughly **8,500** — comfortably the smallest of the eight UGC-funded universities
- **Student-to-faculty ratio of approximately 17–18:1 overall, but small-class seminars of 20–35 are standard** for arts, languages and social sciences subjects
- **Hostel guarantee for all four years** for non-local students and at least three years for local students — almost no other Hong Kong public university offers this
- Around **270 exchange partner institutions in over 50 countries**, with **more than 80% of undergraduates** taking part in a credit-bearing exchange, summer programme or winter institute
- Compact campus: the entire ground can be crossed end-to-end in under ten minutes, which means students bump into their classmates and professors daily

For an applicant who wants to know what life on a Hong Kong public university actually feels like, Lingnan is the closest thing to a North American liberal-arts college.

## 2. Lingnan's place in 2026 rankings

The 2026 ranking cycle is genuinely strong for Lingnan, especially given that for many years the university opted out of submitting data to the major league tables.

- **QS World University Rankings 2026: 701–710** — the rise reflects Lingnan's deliberate decision in recent years to participate fully in the global rankings.
- **Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings 2026: 301–350** — Lingnan is one of only six Hong Kong institutions in the global top 350.
- **THE Asia University Rankings 2026: #84** — the university's first time participating, landing immediately in the Asia top 100.
- **THE International Outlook 2026: #47 globally** — one of the most internationally diverse universities in the world by faculty and student composition.
- **QS World University Rankings by Subject 2026: nine broad and individual subjects ranked**, more than half of which moved up versus 2025; Computer Science enters for the first time.

The headline takeaway: in pure overall research-volume rankings Lingnan will never catch HKU or CUHK, and applicants chasing a top-100 brand should not expect that here. But on the metrics where a liberal-arts university *should* shine — international outlook, teaching reputation, and named subject areas in the humanities and social sciences — Lingnan now performs very respectably indeed.

## 3. Faculties and academic structure

Lingnan organises its undergraduate teaching into **three faculties** plus a **School of Interdisciplinary Studies**. There are 16 academic departments and a handful of language and disciplinary centres. Across the four units, Lingnan offers roughly **29 honours bachelor's degree programmes**.

### Faculty of Arts
- Departments: Chinese, English, History, Philosophy, Translation, Visual Studies, Cultural Studies (joint with Social Sciences in some programmes)
- Flagship majors: **BA in Chinese**, **BA in Translation** (one of the longest-established translation programmes in Hong Kong), **BA in English**, **BA in Cultural Studies**, **BA in Visual Studies**, **BA in Philosophy**, **BA in History**

### Faculty of Business
- **AACSB-accredited** — Lingnan's business school sits in the top ~5% globally that hold this accreditation
- Departments: Accountancy, Finance, Management, Marketing & International Business, Operations & Supply Chain Management, Computing & Decision Sciences
- Programmes include the **BBA (Honours)** with majors and concentrations in Accounting, Finance, Marketing, HRM, Risk and Insurance Management; and the standalone **BSc in Risk and Insurance Management** and **BSc in Data Science**

### Faculty of Social Sciences
- Departments: Economics, Political Science, Sociology & Social Policy, Psychology, Health and Social Services Management
- Programmes include **BSocSc (Honours)** with concentrations in Economics, Political Science, Sociology and Psychology, plus the **BSocSc in Data Science** and the **BSocSc in Health and Social Services Management** offered in collaboration with health-services partners

### School of Interdisciplinary Studies
- Houses Lingnan's newer cross-faculty programmes such as **BA in Animation and Digital Arts**, **BSc in Data Science**, the joint major in **Philosophy, Politics and Economics (PPE)**, and the **BA in Interdisciplinary Studies in Humanities** which lets students design their own major from across the Faculty of Arts
- Closely linked to the Wong Bing Lai Music and Performing Arts Unit and to the Science Unit, which together provide quantitative literacy and arts electives across the entire university

The takeaway: **Lingnan is not the place for medicine, dentistry, civil engineering or pure science as a major** — those programmes simply do not exist here. It is the place for languages, humanities, business, social sciences, data science and the new interdisciplinary humanities-plus-tech mix.

## 4. JUPAS admission for HKDSE candidates

Lingnan participates fully in **JUPAS**, with all UGC-funded undergraduate programmes available through the system.

### Minimum entrance requirements
- **English Language**: Level **3**
- **Chinese Language**: Level **3**
- **Mathematics (Compulsory Part)**: Level **2**
- **Citizenship and Social Development**: **Attained**
- One elective at Level **3** (or two at Level 2 for some arts programmes)

### Score profiles in recent admissions
Lingnan's JUPAS median score for the **best five subjects** typically falls in the **18–22 range**, depending on the programme:
- Most BA Faculty of Arts majors: **median best-five around 18–20**
- BBA and BSc Data Science: **median best-five around 20–22**
- BSocSc programmes (Economics, Politics, Sociology, Psychology): **median best-five around 19–21**
- The most competitive concentrations (PPE, Data Science, Marketing & International Business): **median best-five around 22–24**

These are noticeably more accessible than HKU/CUHK/HKUST cut-offs and broadly comparable to HKBU and EdUHK. For DSE candidates whose final score lands in the **18–22 best-five band**, Lingnan is realistically a Band A–B target.

### JUPAS entrance scholarships
Lingnan operates a generous entrance scholarship scheme. Any JUPAS applicant achieving **22 or above in the best five subjects** who is admitted to a JUPAS-participating programme is awarded an entrance scholarship — single payment for one-time awards or renewable for the full four years for higher scoring bands. Top scorers (best five 30+) can receive full tuition plus a hostel and living allowance.

## 5. Non-JUPAS admission routes

Lingnan accepts non-JUPAS applicants through three main streams.

### Mainland Chinese students (Gaokao)
Lingnan participates in the **Mainland China Undergraduate Admission Scheme**, which is **independent of the Gaokao national enrolment system** — applicants apply directly to Lingnan and admission decisions are based on Gaokao score, English subject score and (for some programmes) interview. Indicative score floors:
- **Gaokao total**: above the **first-tier (一本)** line in the applicant's province, typically with a margin of 30–60 points
- **Gaokao English**: 120/150 or above (recommended)
- Direct application to Lingnan opens in **January–May 2026** for September 2026 entry

### International qualifications
- **A-Levels**: typical offer 3 A-Levels at grades **BBB to ABB**, with at least a B in any subject directly relevant to the chosen major
- **IB Diploma**: typical offer **28–32 points** total, with English at HL or SL Grade 5+
- **SAT / ACT**: SAT 1300+ (1600 scale) or ACT 28+ in addition to school transcripts
- **English requirement** for non-local applicants: IELTS **6.5** (no band below 5.5), or TOEFL **iBT 88+**, or equivalent

### Senior-year transfer
Lingnan accepts senior-year transfer applicants (Year 2 or Year 3 entry) holding an Associate Degree, Higher Diploma, or completed first/second year of a recognised bachelor's degree. Cumulative GPA of **3.0/4.0 or above** is recommended; transfer credits are assessed individually.

## 6. Tuition fees and cost of attendance (2026/27)

| Item | Local students | Non-local students |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Tuition (UGC-funded undergraduate, per year)** | **HK$47,000** | **HK$175,000** |
| Caution money (refundable) | HK$450 | HK$450 |
| Student union, hall and amenities fees | HK$1,000–1,500 | HK$1,000–1,500 |
| Hostel (compulsory residence, per year) | HK$13,000–18,000 | HK$13,000–18,000 |
| Estimated living costs (food, transport, books, personal) | HK$45,000–55,000 | HK$50,000–70,000 |

For a non-local student, the all-in budget for one academic year at Lingnan is roughly **HK$240,000–265,000** (≈ US$30,500–34,000 / RMB 220,000–245,000). That is **slightly below** the comparable budget at HKU, CUHK or HKUST, mostly because the Tuen Mun area is meaningfully cheaper than Pok Fu Lam, Sha Tin or Clear Water Bay.

### Scholarships beyond JUPAS
- **Entry scholarships for non-JUPAS and mainland applicants**: tiered scholarships covering tuition (full or partial) plus a hostel and living allowance for top applicants
- **Exchange scholarships** to subsidise the mandatory or recommended international experience
- **Need-based bursaries** through the HKSAR Government's Tertiary Student Finance Scheme for eligible local students

## 7. Flagship programmes worth knowing about

### BA in Chinese
One of the most respected Chinese-language and literature programmes in Hong Kong, with concentrations in Chinese literature, Chinese linguistics, and Chinese language teaching. Particularly strong choice for students considering careers in publishing, secondary teaching or graduate study in Chinese studies.

### BA in Translation
Lingnan was one of the first Hong Kong universities to offer a dedicated translation degree. The programme covers Chinese-English and English-Chinese translation across literary, legal, business and audiovisual contexts, with simultaneous and consecutive interpreting modules. Graduates routinely enter government, professional translation firms, publishing houses and the major broadcasters.

### BA in Cultural Studies
The first dedicated cultural studies undergraduate degree in Asia, founded in the 1990s. Combines critical theory, media studies, urban studies and political economy. Strong feeder for graduate study at top-tier UK, US and Asian institutions.

### BSocSc (Hons) in Economics
A small, rigorous economics programme with closer faculty access than at HKU or CUHK Economics. Recent graduates have moved into central-bank graduate schemes, financial-services research desks and top US/UK postgraduate economics programmes.

### BBA (Hons) — Marketing & International Business / Finance / Risk and Insurance Management
The AACSB-accredited business school punches above its weight, with the Risk and Insurance Management programme in particular feeding graduates into the major Hong Kong insurance and reinsurance firms. The Marketing & International Business concentration enjoys very high placement rates with consumer-goods, retail and digital firms across the Greater Bay Area.

### BSc in Data Science (Faculty of Business and the School of Interdisciplinary Studies)
Lingnan's newest flagship, designed to combine Python and R programming, statistics, and machine learning with humanities-side data interpretation. The programme deliberately positions graduates as "data translators" between technical teams and business or policy decision-makers.

### BA in Animation and Digital Arts
A relatively young School of Interdisciplinary Studies programme that has rapidly attracted attention. Combines storytelling, 2D and 3D animation, game design and immersive media. Studio-style teaching with industry mentors from the Hong Kong creative cluster.

## 8. Hostel and campus life

Lingnan operates **ten residential halls** clustered on the main Tuen Mun campus. Hostel life is genuinely central to the Lingnan experience:
- **All non-local undergraduates** are guaranteed a hostel place for the full four years
- **Local undergraduates** are guaranteed three years of residence
- Each hostel has its own master, tutors, hall culture, hall colours, hall song and an annual programme of academic, sports and community-service activities
- Mandatory **integrated learning programme** credits (community service, leadership, arts and sports) are tracked against hostel residency

The flip side: Tuen Mun is a 45–55 minute MTR ride from Central, and weeknights on campus are quiet. Students who want easy access to the bars, restaurants and gigs of Causeway Bay or Lan Kwai Fong should plan around it. Students who value a self-contained campus community where you actually know everyone in your year usually love it.

## 9. Graduate outcomes

Lingnan publishes annual graduate employment surveys. Recent figures show:
- Overall employment / further-study rate of **roughly 95%** within six months of graduation
- Median starting salary across all programmes of approximately **HK$20,000–22,000 per month**
- BBA and BSc Data Science graduates: median starting salary closer to **HK$22,000–25,000**
- Translation, Chinese and Cultural Studies graduates: strong placement into government, NGO, secondary-school teaching and publishing roles, with a meaningful share (15–20%) progressing to taught masters in Hong Kong, the UK or the US
- A growing share of mainland graduates use the **IANG visa** to remain in Hong Kong and work in the Greater Bay Area's media, marketing, insurance and education sectors

## 10. Who should apply to Lingnan?

**Lingnan is right for you if**:
- You want **small classes, close faculty contact and a true residential experience** more than you want a top-100 brand on your CV
- Your interests sit in **languages, humanities, cultural studies, social sciences, business or data science** rather than medicine or hard engineering
- You actively want to **spend a term or year abroad** as part of your degree
- You are a mainland or international student who wants to live in Hong Kong but cannot afford the rent of Pok Fu Lam, Sha Tin or Clear Water Bay
- You value an AACSB business school with an undergraduate-first culture rather than a giant MBA-driven research factory

**Lingnan is probably wrong for you if**:
- You want to study **medicine, dentistry, law (LLB), pharmacy, civil/mechanical engineering or architecture** — none of which are offered
- You measure a university entirely by its overall QS or THE world rank
- You strongly prefer an urban-core campus over a suburban residential one

For the right student, Lingnan can be one of the most rewarding undergraduate experiences in Hong Kong — and one of the most internationally connected. For the wrong one, the Tuen Mun location and the absence of professional schools will eventually grate. Knowing which student you are is the single most important question in any Lingnan application.
