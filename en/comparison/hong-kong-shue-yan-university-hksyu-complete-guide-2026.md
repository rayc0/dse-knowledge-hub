# Hong Kong Shue Yan University (HKSYU) Complete Guide 2026

Outside the halo of Hong Kong's eight UGC-funded universities sits an institution that has quietly walked its own road for more than half a century. **Hong Kong Shue Yan University (HKSYU)** was founded in 1971 by Dr Henry Hu Hung-lit and Dr Chung Chi-yung as a private college, became the first institution in Hong Kong to be granted full **private university** status by the Government in December 2006, and in 2026 is celebrating its 55th anniversary and 20 years as a chartered university.

For DSE candidates who want a small-class liberal-arts environment, for mainland students priced out of overseas options, and for international applicants looking for an English-medium Hong Kong degree without the brutal cut-offs of HKU or CUHK, Shue Yan is a serious option that often goes underestimated. This guide covers everything you need: the three faculties, the roughly 15 honours bachelor programmes, JUPAS and non-JUPAS admission, the two SSSDP-subsidised programmes that change the cost calculus, scholarships, accommodation, and graduate outcomes.

---

## 1. What HKSYU Is — and Why Its Status Matters

Shue Yan is unique among Hong Kong's degree-awarding bodies in three ways:

1. **It is Hong Kong's first private university.** When the Government granted university title under the Post Secondary Colleges Ordinance in December 2006, HKSYU became the first private institution in the city allowed to use the word "University" in its name. Other self-financing universities — Hang Seng University, Saint Francis University, the Hong Kong Metropolitan University — were chartered later.
2. **It has insisted on a four-year curriculum since 1971.** While the rest of Hong Kong's higher education sector ran on a three-year British model until the 334 reform in 2012, Shue Yan refused Government subsidy specifically so it could keep a four-year liberal-arts structure modelled on American colleges. That refusal — costly at the time — turned out to be visionary: when Hong Kong moved to a 3-3-4 secondary and four-year tertiary system in 2012, Shue Yan was the only institution that did not need to restructure at all.
3. **It is governed independently of the UGC.** As a self-financing private university, HKSYU is not funded through the University Grants Committee. The Hu family foundation and the university's own council set strategy. The trade-off is that tuition is paid by students (or, for SSSDP-listed programmes, by Government subsidy plus students), but the institution can move faster on programme design than UGC peers.

Crucially, HKSYU's degrees are accredited by the **Hong Kong Council for Accreditation of Academic and Vocational Qualifications (HKCAAVQ)** at Qualifications Framework Level 5, the same level as bachelor's degrees from the eight UGC universities. They are recognised by the Hong Kong Government, the Mainland Ministry of Education, and most overseas universities for postgraduate admission.

## 2. The Three Faculties and Around 15 Honours Bachelor Programmes

HKSYU is organised across three faculties and about 11 academic departments, offering approximately 15 honours bachelor programmes for the 2026/27 intake. The portfolio is deliberately humanities-and-social-science heavy, with two strategic STEM/business additions in the form of SSSDP-subsidised programmes.

### Faculty of Arts

- **Bachelor of Arts (Honours) in Chinese Language and Literature** — one of the few programmes in Hong Kong still teaching classical Chinese as a serious system rather than a survey topic.
- **Bachelor of Arts (Honours) in English Language and Literature** — small-class English major; entry requires DSE English Language Level 3 or above.
- **Bachelor of Arts (Honours) in History** — strong on modern Chinese and Hong Kong history.
- **Bachelor of Arts (Honours) in Journalism and Mass Communication** — includes Cantonese broadcasting, video and TV production studios, with placement pipelines into RTHK, TVB and digital media outlets.
- **Bachelor of Social Sciences (Honours) in Arts, Culture and Technology** — a newer interdisciplinary programme combining curatorial studies, digital media and cultural studies.

### Faculty of Commerce

- **Bachelor of Business Administration (Honours) in Accounting** — recognised by the Hong Kong Institute of Certified Public Accountants (HKICPA) for paper exemptions.
- **Bachelor of Business Administration (Honours) in Business Administration** — concentrations in marketing, human resources and international business.
- **Bachelor of Business Administration (Honours) in Economics and Finance** — incorporates CFA Programme curriculum content.
- **Bachelor of Commerce (Honours) in Financial Technology (BCom FinTech) — JUPAS code JSSY01** — listed on the **Study Subsidy Scheme for Designated Professions/Sectors (SSSDP)**. The 2026/27 first-year sticker tuition is HK$80,089; after the SSSDP subsidy of HK$46,780, the **net first-year tuition is approximately HK$33,309**, and the four-year net cost is around HK$133,236.
- **Bachelor of Arts (Honours) in Law and Business** — non-vocational legal studies that articulate to PCLL or overseas JD programmes.

### Faculty of Social Sciences

- **Bachelor of Social Sciences (Honours) in Counselling and Psychology** — accredited by the Hong Kong Psychological Society (HKPS) and arguably HKSYU's flagship programme; consistently the most competitive entry in JUPAS year on year.
- **Bachelor of Social Sciences (Honours) in Psychology with Forensic Investigation** — a Hong Kong-unique pairing of psychology with criminal justice and policing applications.
- **Bachelor of Social Work (Honours)** — accredited by the Hong Kong Social Workers Registration Board (SWRB); graduates qualify directly for Registered Social Worker status.
- **Bachelor of Social Sciences (Honours) in Sociology**.
- **Bachelor of Science (Honours) in Applied Data Science — JUPAS code JSSY02** — also listed on **SSSDP**. With the 2026/27 subsidy ceiling of HK$46,780 applied against tuition, students pay a substantially reduced net fee.
- **Bachelor of Science (Honours) in Digital Entertainment and Game Application** — partnerships with local game studios; covers Unity and Unreal Engine pipelines.

> The two SSSDP-subsidised programmes (FinTech, Applied Data Science) are HKSYU's strategic answer to Hong Kong's Innovation and Technology talent shortage. Because the subsidy is paid per student per year, net tuition for these two programmes is comparable to a UGC-funded place — which materially changes the value calculation for cost-sensitive applicants.

## 3. Admission Requirements

HKSYU runs three parallel admission tracks for undergraduate entry: JUPAS (for HKDSE candidates applying to the two SSSDP programmes), non-JUPAS Direct Application (for HKDSE candidates applying to non-SSSDP programmes, sub-degree holders, IB/A-Level/SAT candidates, and mainland Gaokao students), and articulation entry from associate degree or higher diploma.

### 3.1 JUPAS (HKDSE) Route

JUPAS is the route for the two SSSDP programmes, JSSY01 (BCom FinTech) and JSSY02 (BSc Applied Data Science). The general minimum entry requirement is the standard Hong Kong "**3-3-2-2**" matrix:

- Chinese Language: Level 3
- English Language: Level 3
- Mathematics Compulsory Part: Level 2
- Citizenship and Social Development: Attained
- Plus one elective at Level 2 or above (one elective subject at Level 3 may be required for individual programmes)

HKSYU accepts **combined results from multiple HKDSE sittings**, which is unusual and welcome — retake candidates can mix their best papers across two sittings.

For the two SSSDP programmes, real-world Band A intake medians have historically clustered around **score 14 on the best-five elective method** (reading the JUPAS programme catalogue figures), with applied data science slightly lower than FinTech. Counselling and Psychology, although offered through non-JUPAS, typically takes its strongest cohort at a similar effective score.

### 3.2 Non-JUPAS Direct Application

For all programmes outside SSSDP, **HKDSE candidates apply directly to HKSYU** rather than through JUPAS. Direct applications are also the route for:

- IB Diploma holders (typically 24 points or above, with English at HL 4 or SL 5)
- GCE A-Level / IAL candidates (typically two A-Level passes plus three GCSEs including English)
- Mainland Gaokao candidates (no fixed national line — HKSYU evaluates Gaokao English score, total score relative to provincial first-tier line, and a personal statement and interview)
- International applicants with equivalent qualifications such as SAT, IBE, or recognised national matriculation exams

Non-JUPAS applications open in November and run on a **rolling basis** until programme places fill. Applying earlier is materially advantageous, especially for Counselling and Psychology and Social Work.

### 3.3 Articulation Entry (Senior-Year Entry)

Hong Kong sub-degree holders (Associate Degree, Higher Diploma) can apply for **Year 2 or Year 3 senior entry** with credit transfer, on a programme-by-programme basis. Many Counselling and Psychology, Sociology and Business Administration cohorts include articulation students. This route is particularly important for Hong Kong Community College and HKBU CIE graduates.

### 3.4 English Language Requirement

For English-medium programmes, applicants whose qualifications are not in English typically need IELTS 6.0, TOEFL iBT 70, College English Test (CET) Band 6, or Gaokao English at 110+ depending on the programme. The English major requires higher minima.

## 4. Tuition and the SSSDP Subsidy in Detail

For 2026/27 entry, sticker tuition at HKSYU varies by programme but typically falls in the range **HK$73,000 to HK$96,000 per academic year** for full-time honours bachelor programmes. This is comparable to other Hong Kong self-financing universities and well below international undergraduate fees in the UK, US, Australia or Singapore.

The **SSSDP subsidy** materially changes the picture for two programmes. The 2026/27 SSSDP subsidy ceiling is **HK$46,780 per student per year** (announced by the Education Bureau in September 2025). Applied to BCom FinTech, that brings the net first-year tuition down to roughly HK$33,309 and the four-year net programme cost to around HK$133,236. Applied to BSc Applied Data Science, it produces a similarly large reduction. SSSDP funding is awarded automatically with the JUPAS offer — students do not apply for it separately.

For non-SSSDP programmes, scholarships and Government student finance schemes (the Non-means-tested Subsidy Scheme for Self-financing Undergraduate Studies, NMTSS) help eligible Hong Kong-domiciled students reduce the net cost.

## 5. Scholarships and Financial Aid

HKSYU operates a substantial entrance and continuing scholarship pool. Key items:

- **Entrance Scholarships for high-scoring DSE applicants** — full or partial tuition waivers for top JUPAS / non-JUPAS entrants. Typical thresholds: full waiver for DSE best-five score of 25 or above; half waiver for 22 or above; quarter waiver for 20 or above. Continuing eligibility requires maintaining a stipulated GPA.
- **Mainland Outstanding Student Scholarships** — separate awards for top Gaokao entrants; Gaokao scores at or above the provincial first-tier line plus competitive English typically secure consideration.
- **Faculty Dean's Scholarships** for second-year and beyond, awarded on cumulative GPA.
- **NMTSS / Non-means-tested Subsidy Scheme** (for eligible Hong Kong residents, around HK$36,000 per year; not applicable to Mainland or international students).
- **Government Tertiary Student Finance Scheme — Publicly-funded Programmes (TSFS) does not apply to HKSYU**; eligible Hong Kong students should look at the FASP / NMTSS schemes instead.

International and mainland students should also check HKSYU's hardship grants and named donor scholarships, several of which are reserved specifically for non-local students.

## 6. Campus, Accommodation and Student Life

HKSYU's campus sits at **6 Wai Tsui Crescent, Braemar Hill, North Point**, on Hong Kong Island, with adjoining academic buildings on Wai Man Road. The location is one of the most attractive in the Hong Kong higher-education sector — a hillside campus surrounded by greenery, looking across Victoria Harbour, on the same Braemar Hill ridge as the Chinese University of Hong Kong's Chung Chi College on the opposite side. It is a 15-minute bus ride from Tin Hau MTR station and around 25 minutes from Central.

The campus is compact (roughly 4,000 full-time undergraduates) and almost all classes are taught in groups of fewer than 30 students — a sharp contrast to the lecture-hall format common at the eight UGC universities. The library, Lady Hu Tin-yuet Library, holds around 300,000 print volumes plus digital resources.

**Student residence** is offered in two halls — the Lady Lily Shaw Hall and the Research Complex residences — with priority given to non-local (Mainland and overseas) and remote-domicile Hong Kong students. Residence is not guaranteed for all years; many local students commute. Off-campus rentals in North Point, Quarry Bay and Tin Hau typically run HK$5,000–9,000 per month for a shared room or studio.

Student societies are active across academic departments, with notable strength in journalism (the JMC Society runs a campus newspaper and broadcast unit) and counselling/psychology (peer-counselling and forensic-investigation clubs). The Christian heritage of the founding family is visible in chapel programmes, but the university is non-denominational and students of all backgrounds are welcome.

## 7. Graduate Outcomes

Because Shue Yan is small and humanities-heavy, its graduate destinations look different from the eight UGC universities, but they are well-defined:

- **Counselling and Psychology graduates** typically progress to a master's in clinical, counselling, or educational psychology — at HKSYU itself, at the eight UGC universities, or overseas — to qualify as registered psychologists. A meaningful share proceed directly into school counselling, NGO casework, and corporate HR roles.
- **Social Work graduates** qualify directly as Registered Social Workers and move into Social Welfare Department posts, NGO casework, and elderly/family service agencies. Pass rates and registration outcomes are consistently strong.
- **Journalism and Mass Communication graduates** are visible in RTHK, TVB, HK01, Now TV and digital-native outlets, including in production, news and corporate communications.
- **BCom FinTech and BSc Applied Data Science graduates** (the two SSSDP programmes) target Hong Kong's banking IT, insurtech and Greater Bay Area technology companies. As the first cohorts complete their degrees, the early-career signal is positive given the heavy compulsory internship loading.
- **Accounting graduates** with HKICPA paper exemptions feed into the Big Four and second-tier audit firms, particularly into private-company and SME assurance roles.
- **Postgraduate study** is a common path: HKSYU graduates regularly enrol in master's programmes at HKU, CUHK, HKUST, the National Taiwan University, and UK and Australian institutions.

The university's alumni include several public figures in journalism, broadcasting, social work and the Legislative Council, and Hu family alumni networks remain influential in Hong Kong civic life.

## 8. How HKSYU Compares — and Who It Suits

HKSYU is not trying to be a research-led UGC university, and prospective students should evaluate it on its own terms.

**Choose HKSYU if you want**:

- A small, four-year, English-medium liberal-arts environment in Hong Kong.
- A flagship Counselling and Psychology, Social Work, Journalism, or Chinese Language programme with professional accreditation and small classes.
- A Hong Kong undergraduate degree at a substantially lower sticker price than overseas options, with SSSDP-net tuition for the two designated programmes that approaches UGC levels.
- A non-JUPAS pathway with rolling admissions and acceptance of combined HKDSE sittings.

**Look elsewhere if you need**:

- Heavy laboratory-science programmes such as Medicine, Dentistry, Engineering or Pure Sciences — Shue Yan does not offer these.
- A large research university brand for a research-track career.
- A campus with a high proportion of international exchange students; HKSYU's exchange network is smaller than the UGC universities'.

## 10. Bottom Line

In a city defined by its "Group of Eight" UGC universities, Hong Kong Shue Yan University has built an enduring identity that does not depend on UGC validation. As Hong Kong's first private university, the institution that pioneered the four-year liberal-arts model long before the 334 reform, and the home of accredited Counselling and Psychology and Social Work programmes that consistently feed Hong Kong's helping professions, HKSYU deserves a serious look from any DSE, Gaokao or international applicant whose priorities are small classes, professional accreditation and a humanities-and-social-science focus.

The 2026/27 intake brings two SSSDP-subsidised programmes — BCom FinTech and BSc Applied Data Science — that bring net tuition close to UGC levels, a rolling non-JUPAS admissions process that rewards early applicants, and a 55-year-old institution celebrating two decades as a chartered university. For the right candidate, that is a strong combination.
