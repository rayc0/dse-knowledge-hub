# The Hang Seng University of Hong Kong (HSUHK) Complete Guide 2026

When Hong Kong students and parents talk about university choices, attention usually centres on the eight UGC-funded "Big 8" institutions. Yet outside that bracket sits a parallel track that is increasingly hard to ignore - the **self-financing universities**. And on that track, **The Hang Seng University of Hong Kong (HSUHK)** is widely regarded as the flagship: it is Hong Kong's largest self-financing private university, founded by The Hang Seng Bank Education Foundation, with an alumni network deeply embedded in the city's banking and financial services sector. In 2024 its School of Business secured **AACSB International accreditation** - the global gold standard for business education held by only **6% of business schools worldwide** and, among Hong Kong self-financing institutions, by **HSUHK alone**.

For HKDSE candidates whose results sit just below Big 8 cut-offs, for non-JUPAS applicants from international or mainland systems, and for those targeting business, communication, translation or supply chain careers, HSUHK is one of the strongest "second-track" options in the city. This guide walks through HSUHK's identity, its 2026 admission requirements (JUPAS, Non-JUPAS, and Gaokao), tuition and scholarships, flagship programmes, and graduate outcomes - so you can decide whether it deserves a place on your application list.

## 1. What kind of university is HSUHK?

### Origins and positioning

HSUHK traces its lineage to **Hang Seng School of Commerce (1980)**, founded by The Hang Seng Bank Education Foundation. It became **Hang Seng Management College in 2010**, and in **2018** received government approval to be retitled **The Hang Seng University of Hong Kong** - the second self-financing private institution in Hong Kong (after Shue Yan University) to attain full university status.

The campus sits on a hillside in **Siu Lek Yuen, Sha Tin**, a short walk from MTR Shek Mun station, with a true "one-stop campus" layout that few self-financing peers can match. HSUHK's positioning is "**liberal arts plus profession**": small-class teaching (staff-to-student ratio around 1:18), compulsory residential education for all undergraduates, and an applied, employer-aligned curriculum.

### Scale and faculty

- **Undergraduates**: roughly 6,000 - the largest private undergraduate body in Hong Kong outside the Big 8
- **Faculty**: around 350 full-time academics, **over 80% holding doctoral degrees**
- **Schools**: 6 (Business; Communication; Decision Sciences; Humanities and Social Science; Translation and Foreign Languages; and Transdisciplinary Studies)
- **Undergraduate programmes**: **27 four-year honours degrees**
- **Non-local students**: roughly 12% of the cohort

### Rankings and accreditation

- **AppliedHE ALL ASIA Private University Ranking 2026**: Greater China #7
- **QS Asia University Rankings 2026**: Greater China #157 band
- **THE Young University Rankings**: 351-400 globally
- **AACSB International** accreditation for the School of Business (2024) - one of only six AACSB-accredited business schools in Hong Kong
- **Professional accreditation** for individual programmes from ACCA, HKICPA, CIMA, ANZIIF, CILT and others

HSUHK does not feature in the QS World University Rankings main table, but among Hong Kong employers - especially in banking, accountancy, insurance and supply chain - its graduates carry significant brand recognition. HSBC, Hang Seng Bank, the Big Four accounting firms, and licensed local securities houses consistently hire HSUHK graduates each year.

## 2. The six schools and 27 undergraduate programmes

### School of Business (AACSB-accredited)

The flagship school, accounting for nearly half the undergraduate cohort. Programmes include:

- BBA (Hons) in Banking and Finance
- BBA (Hons) in Professional Accountancy (HKICPA / ACCA-accredited)
- BBA (Hons) in Management
- BBA (Hons) in Marketing
- BBA (Hons) in Human Resource Management
- BBA (Hons) in Economics
- BBA (Hons) in Supply Chain Management (CILT-accredited)
- BBA (Hons) in Convention and Event Management
- BBA (Hons) in Insurance and Risk Management (ANZIIF-accredited)
- BBA (Hons) in Financial Analysis

### School of Communication

A regional reputation in journalism and convergent media:

- BA (Hons) in Journalism and Communication
- BA (Hons) in Convergent Media and Communication Technology

The school is known for its dual-language newsroom training and a strong record placing graduates with TVB, Now TV, RTHK, Bloomberg and South China Morning Post.

### School of Decision Sciences

- BSc (Hons) in Computing and Decision Sciences
- BSc (Hons) in Data Science and Business Intelligence
- BSc (Hons) in Financial Analytics
- BSc (Hons) in Supply Chain Management and Analytics

### School of Humanities and Social Science

- BA (Hons) in English
- BSocSc (Hons) in Applied Sociology
- BSocSc (Hons) in Applied and Human-Centred Computing
- BA (Hons) in Cultural and Creative Industries

### School of Translation and Foreign Languages

A signature HSUHK strength, backed by a graduate Translation School:

### School of Transdisciplinary Studies

A newer school created to host interdisciplinary degrees combining humanities, technology and business.

## 3. JUPAS admission (HKDSE candidates)

HSUHK has participated in the **JUPAS Admissions Scheme** since the 2024-25 cycle for selected programmes, and accepts **direct application** through its own admission portal in parallel.

### Minimum HKDSE entry requirement

- **Chinese Language**: Level 3
- **English Language**: Level 3
- **Mathematics (Compulsory Part)**: Level 2
- **Citizenship and Social Development**: Attained
- **One elective subject**: Level 2

The **3-3-2-2-2** profile is the floor; competitive programmes (BBA Banking and Finance, BBA Professional Accountancy, BA Journalism, BA Translation with Business) typically admit students at **Level 4 in Chinese and English** with **3-4 in core subjects**.

### How HSUHK calculates JUPAS scores

HSUHK uses the **best 5 subjects** model - including Chinese, English, Mathematics (Compulsory Part), and the two best elective or M1/M2 results - converted to a 35-point scale. Unlike some Big 8 programmes that double-weight specific subjects, HSUHK applies a relatively flat weighting, which can favour balanced candidates.

### Key dates (JUPAS cycle 2026)

- **Programme choices submitted**: by early December 2025
- **Programme choice modification**: post-results, July 2026
- **Main round offers**: early August 2026 (after HKDSE release)

### Direct (Non-JUPAS) parallel application

HSUHK also accepts direct DSE applications outside JUPAS for the same programmes, enabling candidates to receive an **early conditional offer** based on predicted grades - a useful insurance policy if your JUPAS Band A choices are competitive.

## 4. Non-JUPAS admission (international and mainland qualifications)

HSUHK welcomes applicants holding:

- **GCE A-Level / IAL**: typically 3 A-Levels at **CCC or above** for general entry, **BBC** for competitive programmes
- **IB Diploma**: minimum **24 points** overall, **30+** for competitive programmes
- **SAT / ACT**: combined SAT 1100+ (EBRW + Math), with strong English subscores
- **Associate Degree / Higher Diploma**: senior-year entry available - HSUHK is one of the most active receivers of Hong Kong sub-degree articulation
- **Other recognised national qualifications** (Australian ATAR, Canadian provincial diplomas, etc.)

### English language requirement

For applicants whose senior secondary medium of instruction was not English:

- **IELTS**: 6.0 overall (no band below 5.5)
- **TOEFL iBT**: 70+
- **HKDSE English**: Level 3+
- **GCE / IGCSE English**: Grade C / Level 4+

### Application timeline (Non-JUPAS / International, 2026 entry)

- **Round 1 deadline**: 30 April 2026
- **Round 2 deadline**: 30 June 2026
- **Rolling decisions** typically issued within 4-6 weeks of complete submission

## 5. Mainland Gaokao admission (Independent Recruitment)

Beginning in the **2025-26 academic year**, HSUHK was approved by China's Ministry of Education to expand its **Independent Recruitment Scheme to all 31 provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities** (previously around 20). The 2026 intake quota has been increased from 450 to **750 students**, with **no per-province cap** - meaning candidates are not blocked because their province's quota is full.

### Gaokao score thresholds

| Item | Minimum requirement |
|---|---|
| Total Gaokao score | At or above the **first-tier line (一本线 / 本科线)** in the candidate's province; certain programmes accept second-tier line |
| English subject score | **100/150** (this is a hard cut-off, applied uniformly across all programmes) |
| Interview | Online English interview required for shortlisted candidates |

### Application window

- **Online application opens**: October 2025
- **Application deadline**: late June 2026 (typically 22 June)
- **Interviews**: rolling, June-July 2026
- **Offer release**: early-mid July 2026, ahead of the mainland regular admission round

A critical feature: HSUHK admission **does not occupy a slot in the mainland centralised college admission system (统招)**. Students who receive an HSUHK offer remain free to participate in mainland Gaokao admission and can choose between the two offers.

## 6. Tuition fees and total cost (2026-27)

### Tuition

- **Local (HKDSE / JUPAS) students**: HK$95,000 per year (most BBA / BA programmes); the Hong Kong SAR Government **Non-means-tested Subsidy Scheme for Self-financing Undergraduate Studies (NMTSS)** provides up to **HK$36,260** per year, bringing net tuition for eligible local students to roughly **HK$58,740**
- **Non-local students (mainland and international)**: approximately **HK$100,000-110,000 per year** (around USD 12,800)

### Living costs

For non-local students residing in Hong Kong, total annual cost (tuition plus accommodation, meals, transport, books, insurance) typically ranges **HK$160,000 - HK$200,000**. HSUHK guarantees first-year on-campus residence for non-local undergraduates; subsequent years can be on-campus (subject to ballot) or in nearby Sha Tin / Tai Wai private rentals (HK$5,000-9,000 per month for a shared flat).

## 7. Scholarships

HSUHK runs one of the more generous self-financing scholarship programmes:

- **Talent Admission Scheme**: **full tuition waiver** (renewable annually subject to GPA) for HKDSE students with five subjects at Level 5 or above, or Gaokao students 50+ points above the first-tier line
- **Entrance Scholarships**: HK$30,000 - HK$60,000 one-off awards for outstanding new entrants
- **President's Scholarships**: full tuition plus living stipend, for the highest-scoring entrants per school
- **Need-based bursaries**: for non-local students with documented financial need
- **Programme-specific awards** in Translation, Journalism, Supply Chain and Data Science

## 8. Flagship programmes - what HSUHK does best

### BBA in Professional Accountancy

The most established programme, holding **HKICPA, ACCA and CPA Australia** accreditation. Graduates routinely place into the Big Four (Deloitte, EY, KPMG, PwC) graduate intakes - in some recent years HSUHK was the second-largest source of Big Four campus hires in Hong Kong after CUHK Business School.

### BBA in Banking and Finance

Leverages the Hang Seng Bank heritage. Curriculum covers retail banking, wealth management, FinTech, and CFA Level I content. Internship pipelines into Hang Seng Bank, HSBC, Bank of China (Hong Kong) and Standard Chartered.

### BBA in Supply Chain Management

CILT-accredited; the **MSc in Global Supply Chain Management** at HSUHK ranked **84th globally in QS International Trade Rankings 2025**, indicating real research weight in this niche. The undergraduate programme runs two pathways - "academic / further study" and "professional practice" - allowing students to build either toward an MSc or directly into logistics employment with companies like DHL, Kerry Logistics and Cathay Cargo.

### BA in Journalism and Communication

Strong placement track at TVB, Now TV, RTHK, SCMP and Bloomberg. The school operates dual-language student newsrooms and a TV studio facility comparable in equipment to Big 8 journalism schools.

### BA in Translation with Business

HSUHK's translation programmes are ranked alongside CUHK and PolyU as the top three in Hong Kong by industry surveys. The "with Business" specialisation - combining commercial translation, business communication and bilingual editing - is genuinely distinctive and feeds directly into demand from law firms, banks, and the Hong Kong civil service.

## 9. Career outcomes

HSUHK publishes annual graduate employment surveys; recent cohorts show:

- **Overall employment rate (within 6 months)**: 95%+
- **Average starting monthly salary (2024 graduates)**: roughly **HK$22,000 - HK$25,000**, with BBA Banking and Finance and BBA Professional Accountancy graduates averaging **HK$26,000 - HK$28,000**
- **Top employers**: HSBC, Hang Seng Bank, Bank of China (HK), Big Four accounting firms, AIA, Prudential, Cathay Pacific, DHL, MTR Corporation, Hong Kong civil service
- **Postgraduate progression**: roughly 18-20% of graduates pursue a master's degree within two years, mostly at HKU, CUHK, HKUST, PolyU or overseas universities

For non-local graduates, the **IANG (Immigration Arrangements for Non-local Graduates)** visa applies in full - HSUHK degrees are recognised under the Qualifications Framework, and graduates can stay in Hong Kong for **24 months** (extendable) to work without first securing a job offer.

## 10. Should HSUHK be on your list?

HSUHK is the right choice if you:

- Are an HKDSE candidate scoring in the **18-24 (best 5)** range, and want a recognised, residential, English-medium degree pathway
- Target **business, accountancy, banking, communication, translation or supply chain** careers in Hong Kong
- Are a mainland Gaokao student at or just above the **first-tier line** with strong English (100+/150), and want a Hong Kong degree without the Big 8 cut-throat competition
- Hold international qualifications (A-Level / IB / SAT) and want a Hong Kong option that responds quickly with rolling decisions
- Value **AACSB-accredited business education** but are priced out of, or competitively shut out of, CUHK, HKU and HKUST business schools

It is less suitable if your target is research-intensive STEM, medicine / dentistry, law, or top-tier global rankings prestige - those remain Big 8 territory.

In a crowded Hong Kong undergraduate landscape, HSUHK has carved out a clear position: the **strongest self-financing brand**, the **only AACSB business school outside the Big 8**, and a **professional, employment-focused** degree experience on a genuine campus. For the right student, it is not a fallback - it is a deliberate choice.
