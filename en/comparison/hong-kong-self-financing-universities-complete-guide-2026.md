# Hong Kong Self-Financing Universities Complete Guide 2026

For most DSE candidates and international applicants, the eight UGC-funded universities (HKU, CUHK, HKUST, PolyU, CityU, HKBU, EdUHK, LingnanU) dominate the conversation. But Hong Kong has a parallel — and rapidly maturing — self-financing degree sector that admits thousands of students every year, including students whose JUPAS scores miss UGC cut-offs by two or three points, mainland Gaokao students who do not clear the UGC bar, and international students looking for English-medium bachelor's programmes at a lower price point than HKU or HKUST.

This guide explains how the self-financing system works in 2026, profiles the six most established degree-awarding institutions, walks through the SSSDP and NMTSS subsidies (and why they matter even when you are not eligible for them), and gives a decision framework for whether a self-financing degree is the right call.

## What "Self-Financing" Actually Means in Hong Kong

In Hong Kong, "self-financing" is a funding label, not a quality label. UGC-funded programmes receive a per-student block grant from the University Grants Committee, which keeps tuition for local students at the standard HK$42,100 per year. Self-financing programmes do not receive this block grant — instead, institutions cover their own costs from tuition, which means published tuition runs anywhere from HK$70,000 to HK$140,000 per year depending on the programme.

Three of these institutions have been granted full university title:

- **The Hang Seng University of Hong Kong (HSUHK)** — granted university title in 2018
- **Hong Kong Metropolitan University (HKMU)** — formerly OUHK, retitled in 2021, designated as Hong Kong's first University of Applied Sciences in 2024
- **Hong Kong Shue Yan University (HKSYU)** — Hong Kong's first private university (2006)

Three others are degree-awarding institutions that do not yet hold full university title but are accredited to award their own bachelor's degrees:

- **Chu Hai College of Higher Education**
- **Caritas Institute of Higher Education (CIHE)** (rebranding to Saint Francis University in some streams)
- **Tung Wah College (TWC)**

THEi (Technological and Higher Education Institute of Hong Kong, part of VTC) also operates a large self-financing degree portfolio, though it sits inside the VTC group structure rather than as a standalone university.

## How JUPAS Treats Self-Financing Programmes

Self-financing degrees do not appear on the standard JUPAS Band A–E list, but they are available through the JUPAS-linked **E-APP (Electronic Advance Application System for Post-Secondary Programmes)**, which lets DSE candidates apply to self-financing degrees and sub-degrees in parallel with their JUPAS choices. Conditional offers from E-APP institutions can be issued before DSE results day, which means candidates who are not confident of clearing UGC cut-offs can secure an insurance offer in advance.

For non-DSE applicants — IB, A-Level, Gaokao, IGCSE-only, mature students, sub-degree top-up — every self-financing institution operates its own direct-application portal, with deadlines staggered between October of the preceding year and August of the entry year. Several offer rolling admissions, and Shue Yan in particular is known for very fast turnaround on direct applications.

## Tuition and the Two Subsidy Schemes That Reshape the Real Cost

Headline tuition is misleading. For local Hong Kong students (including students with right of abode, dependant visa holders meeting residency rules, and certain visa categories) the government runs two subsidy schemes that effectively cut the cost of many self-financing degrees down to UGC levels or below.

### NMTSS (Non-means-tested Subsidy Scheme for Self-financing Undergraduate Studies)

For the 2026/27 cohort, eligible local students receive **HK$35,120 per academic year** (announced December 2025), automatically deducted from tuition. NMTSS applies broadly to most accredited locally-offered self-financing bachelor's programmes. There is no income test and no application — eligibility is determined at admission.

Practical impact: if HSUHK publishes a tuition of HK$95,000 for a non-laboratory programme, an eligible local student pays roughly HK$60,000 net.

### SSSDP (Study Subsidy Scheme for Designated Professions/Sectors)

For 2026/27, SSSDP provides a much larger subsidy — up to **HK$46,780 per year for non-laboratory programmes and HK$81,450 per year for laboratory-based programmes** — but only for designated programmes in sectors with manpower shortages: nursing, allied health (physiotherapy, occupational therapy, medical laboratory science, radiography), early childhood education, architecture and engineering technology, hospitality and tourism, testing and certification, applied sciences, computing, creative industries, and others.

A nursing degree at Tung Wah College or HSUHK with SSSDP can come out at roughly HK$35,000–55,000 net per year for an eligible local student — cheaper than UGC-funded tuition. This is the single most important fact that any local DSE candidate should understand before dismissing self-financing nursing or allied health programmes as expensive backups.

### Important: International Students Do Not Get These Subsidies

Both NMTSS and SSSDP are restricted to local students. International applicants — including most non-permanent-resident mainland and overseas students — pay the full published tuition, which typically ranges from HK$90,000 to HK$140,000 depending on programme and institution. Scholarships are the main route to bring this down, and every self-financing university operates entry, merit-based, and continuation scholarships that can cover 25%, 50%, or 100% of tuition for top entrants.

## Profiles of the Six Major Self-Financing Institutions

### Hang Seng University of Hong Kong (HSUHK)

Located in Siu Lek Yuen, HSUHK has become the most academically competitive self-financing university in Hong Kong. The QS Asia University Rankings 2026 placed HSUHK at 157th in Greater China and the institution has continued to rise in the broader QS Asia table.

HSUHK's flagship strength is its **School of Business**, which holds AACSB accreditation — a credential shared with HKU, CUHK, HKUST and PolyU and rare among self-financing institutions globally. Its BBA in Supply Chain Management, BBA in Financial Analysis, and BA in English are the highest-cut-off programmes; Best 5 of around 18–22 (3322 minimum) is typical of admitted students, though some specialised entry pathways admit lower scores.

Tuition for 2026/27 is approximately HK$95,000 per academic year for most undergraduate programmes. NMTSS-eligible local students pay roughly HK$60,000 net.

### Hong Kong Metropolitan University (HKMU)

HKMU is the largest of the self-financing institutions in headcount. It debuted in the QS World University Rankings in 2025 (781–790 band) and ranked 393rd in the QS Asia University Rankings 2026 — a jump of more than 140 places.

In 2024 HKMU was designated Hong Kong's first **University of Applied Sciences (UAS)**, a new track introduced by the government to formally recognise vocationally-oriented degree institutions. UAS designation matters because the government has signalled that UAS graduates will increasingly receive parity of recognition with UGC graduates in civil service and statutory body recruitment.

HKMU's School of Nursing & Health Studies is the dominant feeder of registered nurses outside the UGC sector, and its programmes in computing, data science, applied AI, and creative arts are heavily SSSDP-subsidised. The university also operates Hong Kong's longest-running and largest distance learning bachelor's programmes through the Li Ka Shing School of Professional and Continuing Education.

### Hong Kong Shue Yan University (HKSYU)

Established in 1971 and granted university title in 2006, Shue Yan is Hong Kong's oldest private institution and is known for its small-class, liberal-arts-style four-year curriculum. Strongest faculties are Counselling and Psychology (the BSocSc Counselling and Psychology programme is widely considered the strongest non-UGC counselling pathway in Hong Kong), Journalism and Mass Communication, Chinese Language and Literature, and Social Work.

Shue Yan's tuition is among the most accessible in the self-financing sector — typically HK$80,000–90,000 per year — and the university is unusual in retaining a four-year structure for all bachelor's programmes from the pre-2012 era, which gives students an extra year of subject depth compared to many self-financing competitors.

### Chu Hai College of Higher Education

Originally founded in 1947, Chu Hai gained degree-awarding status in 2012 and has built a reputation in **Architecture and the Built Environment**. Its BA (Hons) Architecture is one of the few Hong Kong undergraduate architecture programmes accredited by the Hong Kong Institute of Architects (HKIA), giving it a clear professional pathway. Engineering, Business and Communications round out the offering. Tuition runs approximately HK$80,000–95,000 per year.

### Caritas Institute of Higher Education (CIHE) / Saint Francis University

Operated by Caritas Hong Kong (the Catholic Church's social services arm), CIHE focuses on social work, nursing, gerontology, applied psychology, and digital humanities. Several programmes are SSSDP-subsidised, particularly in nursing and social work — fields where Hong Kong has documented manpower shortages. Some programme streams have been rebranded under the Saint Francis University banner as the institution works toward full university title.

### Tung Wah College (TWC)

Operated by the Tung Wah Group of Hospitals, one of Hong Kong's largest charitable healthcare providers, TWC is a specialist health-sciences institution. Its **Bachelor of Health Science (Honours) in Nursing** and BSc programmes in Medical Laboratory Science, Radiation Therapy, and Occupational Therapy are SSSDP-subsidised and lead directly into Tung Wah Group hospitals via clinical placement networks. For DSE candidates aiming at allied health professions whose JUPAS scores fall just short of PolyU or HKU SPACE quotas, TWC is the most direct alternative pathway.

## Rankings Reality Check

Self-financing universities have made unprecedented progress in international rankings in 2026 — HKMU's QS Asia leap and HSUHK's Greater China placement are real signals of institutional development. But ranking parity with HKU or HKUST is not the goal and is not realistic on a five-year horizon. The honest framing for applicants:

- HSUHK and HKMU are now visible in QS World/Asia rankings; the others are not yet
- Programme-level accreditations (AACSB for HSUHK Business, HKIA for Chu Hai Architecture, Nursing Council of Hong Kong registration for HKMU/TWC/CIHE nursing) carry far more weight in graduate hiring and licensure than overall institutional rank
- Employer perception still trails UGC institutions in finance, law and consulting recruitment but is approximately at parity in nursing, allied health, social work, early childhood education, and creative industries

## Decision Framework: When Should You Choose a Self-Financing Degree?

Choose a self-financing degree over a JUPAS Band C UGC offer or a year of retake when:

1. **The programme is SSSDP-subsidised and matches your career target** — particularly nursing, allied health, social work and early childhood education. These subsidised programmes lead to professional registration that is identical to UGC graduates.
2. **You hold a programme-specific advantage** — Counselling and Psychology at Shue Yan, Architecture at Chu Hai, Supply Chain at HSUHK, all of which match or exceed comparable UGC-sector programmes in employability data.
3. **You are an international student priced out of HKU/CUHK** — full-fee international tuition at HSUHK or HKMU runs HK$110,000–140,000, roughly 35–50% below HKU's international rate, and the visa pathway (student visa → IANG graduate visa → Hong Kong PR after seven years) is identical.
4. **You need an insurance E-APP offer before DSE results day** — securing an HSUHK or HKMU conditional offer in March before sitting your final papers reduces stress and gives you a confirmed pathway if your results come in below your JUPAS Band A.

Avoid a self-financing degree when:

- The same career goal can be reached via Associate Degree → UGC top-up at lower total cost (relevant for Business, Arts and Social Science streams)
- You are committed to graduate destinations — investment banking front office, magic-circle law, top-tier consulting — where UGC brand premium remains decisive
- The specific programme is not professionally accredited, in which case the degree functions as a generic bachelor's at premium cost

## Application Timeline for 2026 Entry

For 2026 entry, the typical timeline is:

- **Sep–Oct 2025** — direct admission portals open at all six institutions
- **Dec 2025** — E-APP opens for DSE candidates
- **Mar–Apr 2026** — first round of conditional offers issued
- **Jul 2026** — DSE results released, JUPAS main-round offers, self-financing institutions issue final unconditional offers
- **Aug 2026** — late-round and clearing-style admissions for unfilled places, particularly at Chu Hai, CIHE and TWC
- **Sep 2026** — semester begins

International applicants should add roughly two months for student visa processing through the Immigration Department and aim to submit their final application by April at the latest.

## Final Thoughts

Hong Kong's self-financing sector in 2026 is no longer a fallback option. With NMTSS and SSSDP subsidies bringing local-student net tuition to UGC parity or below in many subsidised fields, with HKMU's UAS designation and QS Asia rise, with HSUHK's AACSB business school visibility, and with established niches in nursing, allied health, architecture and counselling, the question for DSE candidates is no longer "is self-financing good enough?" but "which self-financing programme matches my professional goal?". For international students priced out of HKU or HKUST, it is one of the most under-appreciated value propositions in the Greater Bay Area's higher education landscape.
