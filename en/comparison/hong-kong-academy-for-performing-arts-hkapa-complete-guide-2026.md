# The Hong Kong Academy for Performing Arts (HKAPA) Complete Guide 2026

When Hong Kong students and parents discuss "the universities," conversation usually orbits the eight UGC-funded institutions - HKU, CUHK, HKUST, PolyU, CityU, HKBU, EdUHK and Lingnan. Sitting alongside the Big 8, however, is a publicly funded institution that does not appear in the JUPAS league table at all and yet ranks ahead of every Hong Kong university in its own discipline: **The Hong Kong Academy for Performing Arts (HKAPA / "the Academy" / 演藝學院)**. In the **2026 QS World University Rankings by Subject**, HKAPA placed **10th globally and 1st in Asia for Performing Arts** - a 10-place leap from its 2025 position and one of only five Hong Kong subject entries to break into the world top 10.

For HKDSE candidates whose talent and ambition lie in Chinese opera, dance, drama, film and television, music, or theatre and entertainment arts, HKAPA is in a league of its own. The application logic is also unlike any other Hong Kong university: HKAPA is **not part of JUPAS**, it does not consider Best 5 / Best 6 scores in the conventional way, and the deciding factor is almost always the **audition, portfolio or interview**, not the academic transcript. This guide walks DSE candidates and overseas applicants through HKAPA's identity, the six Schools, 2026/27 admission requirements, tuition and scholarships, flagship programmes and graduate outcomes - so you can decide whether the Academy belongs on your application list.

## 1. What kind of institution is HKAPA?

### Origins and positioning

HKAPA was established by Hong Kong government ordinance in **1984** to provide professional, practice-based training in the performing arts. It is a **statutory tertiary institution funded by the University Grants Committee (UGC)** through a separate funding arrangement, distinct from the eight UGC-funded universities. Its mandate is narrow but globally significant: to be the leading performing arts conservatoire in Asia and a feeder of professional artists, performers, designers and technicians to Hong Kong, the Greater Bay Area and international stages.

The main campus on **Gloucester Road, Wan Chai** - directly opposite the Hong Kong Convention and Exhibition Centre - is itself a working performing arts complex, housing the 1,181-seat Lyric Theatre, the Drama Theatre, the Concert Hall, the Studio Theatre, multiple recital halls, dance studios, sound stages, post-production suites and design workshops. A second campus, the **Béthanie** in Pok Fu Lam (a restored 19th-century Gothic Revival sanatorium), houses the School of Film and Television.

### Scale and faculty

- **Total student body**: approximately 1,000 across all programme levels (Diploma, Advanced Diploma, Bachelor's, Master's, MFA)
- **Schools**: six discipline-based Schools (see Section 2)
- **Faculty**: a mix of full-time academics and adjunct industry professionals - working choreographers, conductors, opera principals, film directors, lighting designers, stage managers - which is unusual even by international conservatoire standards
- **Languages of instruction**: English for most academic teaching; Cantonese, Putonghua and English in studio / rehearsal contexts depending on School
- **Performances per year**: HKAPA students stage over **150 public performances** annually, giving every undergraduate substantial public-stage experience before graduation

### Why HKAPA is "the ninth institution" people forget

Because HKAPA is outside JUPAS and does not award the kind of conventional academic degrees families compare on rankings tables, it is often overlooked by mainstream DSE candidates. Yet it is government-funded, awards UGC-recognised degrees (Bachelor of Fine Arts (Honours), Bachelor of Music (Honours)), and graduates qualify for the same post-study work and PR pathways (IANG visa, seven-year permanent residence) as Big 8 graduates. For the right candidate, it is the single most prestigious place to study performing arts anywhere east of London.

## 2. The six Schools

HKAPA's undergraduate offering is organised into six Schools, each with its own faculty, studios and audition panel.

### School of Chinese Opera

The only degree-awarding institution in the world dedicated to **Cantonese opera (粵劇)** at conservatoire level. Streams include performing (singing, acting, fighting), Cantonese opera music, and percussion. Cantonese opera is recognised by UNESCO as Intangible Cultural Heritage, and HKAPA is the principal pipeline for new generation principals at major Hong Kong opera houses.

### School of Dance

Three streams: **Ballet, Chinese Dance, and Contemporary Dance**. The School works with the Hong Kong Ballet, City Contemporary Dance Company and Hong Kong Dance Company on residencies and choreographic placements. Auditions test technique, improvisation and a solo in the chosen major.

### School of Drama

Streams in **Acting, Directing and Playwriting**. Teaching combines Stanislavski-rooted Western actor training with Cantonese-language dramatic practice. Productions run year-round at the Drama Theatre and Studio Theatre.

### School of Film and Television

Streams in **Directing, Cinematography, Producing, Screenwriting, Editing, Sound, and Production Design for Film**. Based at the Béthanie campus, with full sound stages, location vehicles, ARRI / RED camera packages and post-production suites. Graduates feed directly into Hong Kong's film and streaming industry and have placed strongly at the Hong Kong Film Awards and the Fresh Wave Festival.

### School of Music

Hong Kong's leading conservatoire-style music school. Streams across **strings, winds, brass, percussion, piano, voice, Chinese music (Erhu, Pipa, Zheng, Dizi, Sheng, etc.), composition and conducting**. Faculty includes principals from the Hong Kong Philharmonic, the Hong Kong Chinese Orchestra and the Hong Kong Sinfonietta. The Concert Hall hosts weekly student recitals.

### School of Theatre and Entertainment Arts

The "behind the curtain" School. Streams in **Stage and Event Management, Technical Direction, Set and Costume Design, Lighting Design, Sound Design, Theatre Design and Property Making, and Arts and Event Management**. The Bachelor of Fine Arts (Honours) in Theatre and Entertainment Arts is one of HKAPA's most internationally recruited programmes, and graduates work across theatre, film, theme parks, K-pop touring production, e-sports stagecraft and major event delivery (M+, West Kowloon, the Hong Kong Sevens, Disney parks).

## 3. 2026/27 admission pathways

HKAPA's admission system is fundamentally **audition-based**. Academic results matter, but only as a baseline; the panel decides on artistic potential.

### General Academic Requirement (HKDSE)

For the four-year Bachelor's programmes, HKDSE candidates need to meet HKAPA's General Academic Requirement, which is broadly:

- **Level 3** in English Language
- **Level 3** in Chinese Language
- **Level 2** in Mathematics (Compulsory Part)
- An **"Attained"** result in Citizenship and Social Development (CSD)
- **Level 2** in at least one elective subject

This is roughly equivalent to "**3322 + 2 elective**" expressed in HKAPA's own language - lower than the Big 8 minimum (typically 332233) but never the deciding factor. Even a perfect academic profile will not secure an offer without a successful audition.

### Non-JUPAS, mainland Gaokao and international applicants

HKAPA does not participate in JUPAS. **All applicants** - HKDSE, mainland Gaokao, IB, GCE A-Level, BTEC, overseas - apply through the **same single application portal** at hkapa.edu. There is no Band A / Band B logic and no mainland JEE-style score cut-off.

- **Mainland Gaokao** candidates need to meet the General Academic Requirement-equivalent (typically a benchmark above the Provincial First Tier line is treated as a positive academic indicator, but it is not a hard cut-off)
- **IB** applicants typically need IB scores meeting or exceeding the equivalent of HKAPA GAR (often 24+ with English at Standard Level 4 or Higher Level 3+)
- **A-Level** applicants typically need 2 A-Level passes plus GCSE-level English

### English language requirement

For applicants whose previous education was not in English, HKAPA accepts **IELTS Academic 5.5 overall** (or equivalent TOEFL / IB English / A-Level English). This is one of the lowest English thresholds among Hong Kong tertiary institutions, reflecting the conservatoire model where studio teaching often happens in Cantonese or Putonghua.

### Audition, portfolio and interview - the deciding factor

Every HKAPA applicant goes through a **discipline-specific selection process** decided by their target School:

| School | Typical selection format |
| --- | --- |
| Chinese Opera | Performance audition (singing, recitation, body movement, percussion piece) + interview |
| Dance | Technique class + solo in chosen major (Ballet / Chinese / Contemporary) + improvisation + interview |
| Drama | Two contrasting monologues (one classical, one contemporary), movement / voice work, group improvisation, interview |
| Film and Television | Portfolio submission (showreel, screenplay, photography, design folio per stream) + entry test + interview |
| Music | Pre-screening video + live audition on principal instrument / voice + sight-reading + ear test + theory test + interview |
| Theatre and Entertainment Arts | Portfolio (drawings, technical drafts, design boards, model boxes, photographs of past production work) + interview |

For 2026 entry, **non-local applicants** unable to travel may submit a high-quality digital recording / portfolio in lieu of in-person audition, but HKAPA strongly recommends attending in person where feasible. The **second-round Dance audition for 2026 entry** is scheduled for **24-25 January 2026**.

### Application timeline

- **Application opens**: typically September of the year before entry
- **Main round closing**: typically late November / early December
- **Late round (subject to programme availability)**: until June
- **Auditions / interviews**: November - April (some Schools run multiple rounds)
- **Offers**: rolling, with most offers by April / May

## 4. Tuition fees and scholarships (2026/27 reference)

### Tuition

HKAPA tuition is structured by funded vs non-funded seats:

- **Local (Hong Kong) students - UGC-funded seats**: **HK$44,500 per year**, the same as the Big 8 UGC undergraduate fee
- **Non-local students** (mainland China and overseas): typically **HK$140,000 - HK$180,000 per year** depending on programme (broadly USD 18,000 - 23,000), comparable to the lower end of Big 8 non-local fees

Postgraduate Diploma, Diploma and Advanced Diploma programmes have separate fee schedules - generally lower than the Bachelor's level.

### Living costs

A realistic Hong Kong undergraduate budget remains **HK$70,000 - HK$120,000 per year** for accommodation, food, transport and personal items. HKAPA does not operate a traditional residential hall system on the same scale as the Big 8; many non-local students rent private accommodation in Wan Chai, North Point or eastern Hong Kong Island, where the campus is highly accessible. HKAPA does maintain a non-local student hostel and provides housing assistance.

### Scholarships

- **HKSAR Government Scholarship Fund**: open to outstanding local and non-local students; awards range from partial tuition to full tuition coverage
- **The Society of APA Scholarships**: awarded annually to students of high artistic achievement
- **Belt and Road Scholarship**: for students from designated Belt and Road countries
- **Targeted Scholarships for non-local students** from mainland China and selected overseas territories
- **Hundreds of named scholarships and prizes** funded by donors and industry partners (Cathay, Swire, HSBC, Disney, Cha Family Foundation, Tung Foundation, etc.) - HKAPA disburses tens of millions of HKD in financial support each year, and high-talent admits routinely receive scholarship offers attached to their admission letter

## 5. Flagship programmes worth a closer look

### Bachelor of Fine Arts (Honours) in Theatre and Entertainment Arts (TEA)

Arguably HKAPA's most internationally recruited degree. Production stream graduates work as stage managers, lighting and sound designers, set and costume designers, technical directors, and event producers across Hong Kong, mainland China, Singapore, Macau (theme parks) and increasingly the Middle East live entertainment sector. Cohort sizes are small (15-25 per stream), and final-year capstone productions are mounted on HKAPA's professional stages with full crews.

### Bachelor of Music (Honours)

HKAPA's BMus is the leading conservatoire degree in Hong Kong. The Chinese Music stream is particularly distinctive - one of the few degree-level Chinese instrumental performance pathways in the world, with direct artistic links to the Hong Kong Chinese Orchestra. Western strings, woodwinds, brass and piano students benefit from masterclasses with visiting Hong Kong Philharmonic principals and international guest artists.

### Bachelor of Fine Arts (Honours) in Drama

Strong record placing alumni in Hong Kong's professional theatre scene (Hong Kong Repertory Theatre, Chung Ying Theatre, Tang Shu-wing Theatre Studio) and into Cantonese-language film and TVB drama. Acting alumni include household-name film and TV actors and stage directors.

### Bachelor of Fine Arts (Honours) in Cinema and Television

Practice-led training in directing, cinematography, producing, screenwriting, editing, sound and production design. Hong Kong's commercial film industry continues to recruit cinematographers, gaffers, focus pullers, editors and production designers directly from this programme. The Béthanie campus and HKAPA's industry placements are key advantages.

### Bachelor of Fine Arts (Honours) in Dance

Streams in Ballet, Chinese Dance and Contemporary Dance. Graduates feed into Hong Kong Ballet, City Contemporary Dance Company, Hong Kong Dance Company, and increasingly into mainland and overseas companies (Cloud Gate, Beijing Dance Theatre, contemporary ensembles in Europe).

### Bachelor of Fine Arts (Honours) in Chinese Opera

The only conservatoire-level Cantonese opera Bachelor's degree in the world. Funded by both HKAPA and Hong Kong's Cantonese Opera Development Fund. Graduates form the backbone of Hong Kong's opera principal pipeline.

## 6. Graduate outcomes

Because HKAPA's mission is professional vocational training, its employment outcomes are best read **by industry**, not by salary tables.

- **Theatre and Entertainment Arts**: very high industry employment (often >95%) within six months. Lighting, sound and stage management graduates are consistently in short supply in the Hong Kong, Macau, Singapore and Middle East live entertainment markets
- **Music**: graduates take orchestral seats, build studio teaching practices, take up positions with Hong Kong Philharmonic, Hong Kong Sinfonietta, Hong Kong Chinese Orchestra, the Hong Kong Academy for Performing Arts itself, or proceed to international postgraduate programmes (Royal Academy / Royal College / Juilliard / Curtis / Eastman / Berklee, etc.)
- **Drama**: a mix of professional theatre, TV, film and voiceover work; many alumni join Hong Kong Repertory Theatre and Chung Ying Theatre
- **Film and Television**: direct entry into Hong Kong film, drama, advertising and streaming production crews; alumni regularly nominated at the Hong Kong Film Awards, Golden Horse and Fresh Wave
- **Dance**: contracts with Hong Kong Ballet, CCDC, HKDC and overseas companies; many alumni teach in Hong Kong dance studios and international schools
- **Chinese Opera**: principal and supporting roles with Hong Kong's professional Cantonese opera houses

HKAPA graduates are also eligible for the Immigration Department's **IANG (Immigration Arrangements for Non-local Graduates)** scheme on the same terms as Big 8 graduates - 24 months of unconditional stay-and-work post-graduation - and may apply for **Hong Kong permanent residence after seven years** of ordinary residence.

## 7. Should HKAPA be on your list?

HKAPA is the right choice if **at least three** of the following apply to you:

1. Your strongest profile is artistic, performance-based or technical-craft, not academic
2. You can present a serious audition, portfolio or showreel demonstrating real promise in your chosen discipline
3. You want a small, hands-on conservatoire environment and direct mentorship from working industry professionals
4. You are willing to live and work in Cantonese, Putonghua and English studio environments
5. You see your career as a performer, designer, technician or producer in performing arts, film or live entertainment - not in finance, law, medicine or general business

If your priority is a broad, conventional university experience with classmates across many disciplines, the Big 8 will suit you better. If your priority is **becoming an artist or performing arts professional in Asia**, HKAPA is, on the strength of its 2026 QS performing arts ranking, faculty pedigree and direct industry pipeline, the strongest single choice on the Hong Kong map - and one of the strongest in the world.

## 8. Practical next steps

1. **Identify your target School and stream early.** Each School has its own audition requirement and timeline. Do not generalise.
2. **Build your audition / portfolio over months, not weeks.** Strong HKAPA admits typically prepare 6-12 months in advance with a teacher, mentor or coach.
3. **Watch HKAPA productions.** Public performances at the Wan Chai campus (most are open to the public, many free or low-cost) are the single best way to feel whether the Academy fits you.
4. **Submit early.** Applications open in September; early applicants get the widest audition slot choice and the best chance at scholarship consideration.
5. **Have an academic backup.** Even strong artistic candidates should keep a JUPAS application open for the Big 8, and consider arts management or related programmes at PolyU, BU, EdUHK or self-financing institutions as alternatives if the audition does not go your way.

For the most current 2026/27 deadlines, fee schedule and audition dates, always cross-check the official HKAPA admissions pages at **hkapa.edu** before submitting your application.
