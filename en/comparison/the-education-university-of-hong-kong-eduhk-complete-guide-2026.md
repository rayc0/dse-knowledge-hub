# The Education University of Hong Kong (EdUHK) Complete Guide 2026

The Education University of Hong Kong (EdUHK) is Hong Kong's only publicly-funded university devoted primarily to teacher education and education-related disciplines. Although it is the youngest of the eight UGC-funded universities to attain full university status (granted in 2016), it now occupies a position in global education-discipline rankings that few specialist institutions in Asia can match. For the 2026/27 admission cycle, EdUHK enters as the world's #7 university for Education in the QS World University Rankings by Subject 2026, alongside debut entries in the QS World University Rankings 2026 (#530), QS Asia 2026 (#152), and Times Higher Education World 2026 (#195) — placing it firmly in the global top 10%.

This guide explains everything DSE candidates, Mainland Gaokao students, and international applicants need to know to apply to EdUHK in 2026 — from ranking interpretation, JUPAS Best 5 scoring with interview weighting, Non-JUPAS routes, tuition and scholarships, to flagship programmes and graduate employment realities in Hong Kong's competitive teacher-recruitment market.

## 1. Why EdUHK in 2026: The Numbers Behind the "Education Specialist" Brand

For students considering teacher education or education-adjacent careers (counselling, special needs, early childhood, creative arts, sport science, language teaching, educational psychology), EdUHK is no longer a "regional teachers' college". The 2026 rankings tell a different story:

- **QS World University Rankings by Subject 2026 — Education: #7 globally** (up from #12 in 2025). EdUHK is ahead of NIE Singapore in some sub-indicators and behind only a handful of US/UK/Australian giants like UCL Institute of Education, Harvard, Stanford, Oxford, Toronto and Melbourne.
- **U.S. News & World Report 2026 — Education and Educational Research: #3 worldwide.**
- **QS World University Rankings 2026 (overall): #530**, EdUHK's debut entry.
- **QS Asia University Rankings 2026: #152** — top 10% in Asia, climbing 60 places year-on-year.
- **Times Higher Education World University Rankings 2026: #195** — debut, top 10% globally.
- **QS Sustainability 2026:** climbed over 600 places, entering the global top 30%.

For Mainland and international applicants, the practical reading is this: if your target career involves classroom teaching, education policy, special-needs support, applied psychology, or any role in Greater Bay Area schools, EdUHK gives you a higher subject-rank ceiling than any Hong Kong general-comprehensive university — at substantially lower JUPAS cut-off scores.

## 2. Faculties and Flagship Programmes

EdUHK is organised into three faculties plus the Graduate School. It offers about 45 HKDSE-eligible undergraduate programmes spanning education-major (BEd) tracks and a growing portfolio of non-education BA/BSc/BSocSc degrees.

### 2.1 Faculty of Education and Human Development (FEHD)

The historical core. Five-year Bachelor of Education (BEd) programmes give graduates dual qualification — academic degree plus the Education Bureau's Qualified Teacher status — eliminating the need for a separate PGDE.

Flagship BEd specialisations:
- BEd (Honours) Early Childhood Education — co-tier with NIE Singapore in early-years pedagogy.
- BEd (Honours) Primary Education with majors in Mathematics, General Studies, or English Language.
- BEd (Honours) Special Needs / Inclusive Education — Hong Kong's only full undergraduate programme of its kind.
- BEd (Honours) Physical Education — produces most of Hong Kong's secondary-school PE teachers.

### 2.2 Faculty of Liberal Arts and Social Sciences (FLASS)

The non-teacher-education growth area. Highlights:
- BSocSc (Honours) Psychology — increasingly competitive; useful springboard to clinical/educational psychology master's programmes.
- BA (Honours) Language Studies (English / Chinese / Bilingual).
- BSocSc (Honours) Global and Environmental Studies.
- BA (Honours) Creative Arts and Culture / Visual Arts / Music — co-delivered with strong industry placement.
- BSc (Honours) Sports Science and Coaching.

### 2.3 Faculty of Humanities

Houses Chinese language, literature, history, and Chinese-medicine-adjacent humanities programmes — especially relevant for students intending to teach Chinese language or Chinese history at the secondary level.

### 2.4 Co-terminal Double Degrees

EdUHK's co-terminal BA/BSc/BSocSc & BEd programmes (5 years) are an under-rated route: students earn a content-area degree plus a teaching qualification simultaneously — significantly more efficient than a 4-year bachelor's followed by a 1-year PGDE.

## 3. JUPAS Admission: How EdUHK Calculates Your Score

EdUHK's JUPAS scoring formula in 2026 is straightforward but has two non-obvious twists that catch applicants out.

### 3.1 The Best 5 Formula

EdUHK uses **Best 5 subjects**, regardless of whether they are core or elective, **excluding Citizenship and Social Development (CSD)**. CSD is graded only Attained / Not Attained and counts only as a minimum-met requirement — it does not contribute to your numerical score.

Subject grades convert to scores as follows:
- 5\*\* = 7, 5\* = 6, 5 = 5, 4 = 4, 3 = 3, 2 = 2, 1 = 1.
- M1 / M2 grades are usually weighted at the same scale as a regular elective (i.e., 5\*\* = 7).

Maximum theoretical score: **35 (Best 5)**.

### 3.2 Programme-Specific Weightings

Many BEd programmes apply a weighting to subjects directly relevant to the teaching specialisation. For example:
- BEd English Language: English Language is typically given an additional weight (e.g., x1.5 or x2) up to a stated maximum.
- BEd Mathematics: Mathematics Compulsory Part and M1/M2 may receive enhanced weighting.
- BEd Chinese Language: Chinese Language and Chinese Literature/Chinese History weighted upward.

Always check the programme-specific weighting on the EdUHK Apply portal before finalising your JUPAS Band A choices — the same Best 5 grade profile can yield very different programme-level scores.

### 3.3 Holistic Review and Interviews

For most BEd programmes and several FLASS programmes, EdUHK applies a **genuinely holistic** review. The admission decision considers:
1. HKDSE Best 5 result (after weighting).
2. JUPAS band-choice priority.
3. Interview performance — typically required for most BEd programmes and selected FLASS programmes.
4. School Reference Report (SRR).
5. Other Experiences and Achievements (OEA) — verified extracurriculars, awards, leadership.

The interview is decisive for borderline candidates, especially for teacher-education programmes where communication ability, classroom presence, and articulated motivation to teach matter as much as DSE scores. Group discussions, individual interviews, and sometimes micro-teaching exercises are common formats.

### 3.4 Indicative 2025 JUPAS Median Scores (Best 5)

These are useful 2026 reference points (medians, not minima — you can still be admitted below median if interview and OEA are strong):

- Highly competitive: BEd English Language, BSocSc Psychology, BEd Mathematics — median often around 23–26.
- Mid-tier: BEd Primary Education, BEd Early Childhood, BA Language Studies — median around 19–23.
- Entry-tier: Several FLASS BA programmes — median around 17–20.

For the most current cycle's data, always consult the official EdUHK Apply JUPAS Scores page rather than relying on year-old medians.

## 4. Non-JUPAS Admission for Mainland (Gaokao) and International Students

EdUHK accepts three main non-local admission tracks:

### 4.1 Mainland Gaokao Direct Admission

EdUHK participates in independent Mainland recruitment (not the early-batch government-allocated route used by HKU/CUHK/HKUST). This gives applicants the advantage of **applying directly without affecting Gaokao Mainland admission slots** — your Mainland university applications proceed independently.

Indicative requirements:
- Gaokao total score typically at the **First-Tier (一本) cut-off line** of your home province, with stronger competition for psychology, English-related, and visual-arts programmes.
- English single-subject score: typically **120/150** or above for English-medium programmes; some programmes require 110+.
- Interview (online or in-person) for most programmes.
- Application opens annually around October–November of Year 12, closes around mid-June.
- Tuition: HK$140,000 per year for non-local students (2026/27 standard rate; a small premium applies to some specialised programmes).

### 4.2 International Qualifications

EdUHK accepts:
- IB Diploma: typical conditional offer 28–34 points depending on programme.
- GCE A-Level: typically BBB to AAA.
- SAT/ACT plus 3 AP / SAT Subject scores.
- Other recognised national qualifications (Australian ATAR, Canadian provincial diplomas, etc.).
- English proficiency: IELTS 6.0 (with no sub-band below 5.5) or TOEFL iBT 80 minimum; English-major and language-related programmes require higher.

### 4.3 Senior-Year / Transfer Admission

EdUHK admits Higher Diploma and Associate Degree graduates (from Hong Kong sub-degree institutions) into Year 2 or Year 3 of selected programmes. This is a significant pathway for self-financing-route students who choose to "convert" to a UGC-funded degree.

## 5. Tuition, Scholarships, and Total Cost

### 5.1 Tuition (2026/27 indicative)

- **Local UGC-funded students:** HK$42,100 per year (subject to Government Tuition Fee Subsidy adjustments).
- **Non-local students (Mainland + international):** HK$140,000 per year. EdUHK has historically been the most affordable of the eight UGC universities for non-locals.
- **Self-financing programmes:** vary by programme; check programme-specific pages.

### 5.2 Living Costs

Realistic budget for a non-local undergraduate (2026/27):
- On-campus hall: HK$15,000–22,000 per year (limited supply, demand-allocated).
- Off-campus shared accommodation (Tai Po area, near campus): HK$4,500–7,500 per month.
- Food, transport, insurance, miscellaneous: HK$4,500–6,500 per month.
- All-in annual cost (tuition + living, non-local): roughly **HK$220,000–270,000**.

### 5.3 Scholarships for Non-Local Students

EdUHK operates one of Hong Kong's most generous non-local scholarship pools relative to its size:
- **Entrance scholarships:** Full tuition + accommodation + stipend awards for top non-local entrants (highly competitive).
- **HKSAR Government Scholarship Fund Scholarship for Non-local Students:** worth HK$80,000 per year, with separate Mainland and international quotas.
- **Self-funded faculty / programme scholarships:** typically HK$20,000–80,000 per year, renewable subject to GPA.
- **Belt and Road Scholarship** for eligible nationalities.

For Mainland Gaokao applicants, securing an entrance scholarship significantly narrows the cost gap with a Mainland 985/211 university.

## 6. Campus, Student Life, and Location

EdUHK's main campus is in **Tai Po, in the New Territories** — about 45 minutes from Central by MTR + KCR. The campus is purpose-built (1990s) on a green hillside with:
- Three undergraduate halls + new Northcote Hall extension (limited spaces; non-local students get priority for Year 1).
- Modern teaching blocks including the John Lee Hall, the Mong Man Wai Building, and the Learning Commons.
- Sports facilities including the indoor sports centre, swimming pool, and athletics track — relevant for BEd PE and Sports Science students.
- Practicum schools network: EdUHK has formal practicum partnerships with hundreds of Hong Kong primary and secondary schools.

The Tai Po location is greener and quieter than HKU/CUHK but less "city centre" — many non-local students appreciate the lower cost of living and dedicated campus community, while others find weekend trips to Mong Kok / Central a necessity. The Tai Po Market MTR is a 5-minute shuttle bus from campus.

## 7. Career Outcomes and Graduate Employment

### 7.1 The Teacher Pipeline

EdUHK supplies the majority of newly registered teachers in Hong Kong's pre-primary, primary, and special-education sectors, and a substantial share in secondary. The Education Bureau's Qualified Teacher (QT) status is conferred automatically on BEd graduates, allowing immediate employment in DSS / aided / government schools.

Realistic 2026 graduate-employment context:
- Hong Kong's school-age population is contracting due to cohort declines, leading to school consolidations. Competition for permanent teaching posts has tightened — most fresh BEd graduates start with **contract / supply teaching** before securing a permanent post.
- Starting salary for a permanent Graduate Master/Mistress (GM) post in an aided secondary school is approximately **HK$36,000–42,000 per month** (Master Pay Scale Pt 15–16, 2026 figures), rising to HK$80,000+ with seniority.
- Demand remains relatively strong in **special education, early childhood, STEM (Mathematics, Science, ICT), Putonghua**, and **English Language** specialisations.

### 7.2 Non-Teaching Career Paths

The growing FLASS / Humanities programmes are explicitly designed for non-classroom careers:
- **Psychology graduates** progress to MSocSc/MA in Educational, Counselling, or Clinical Psychology — qualifying as registered psychologists in Hong Kong or Mainland.
- **Language Studies graduates** enter publishing, translation, content, ESL training (highly portable into the Greater Bay Area private-tuition market).
- **Creative Arts graduates** enter cultural institutions, museums, performing-arts companies, and increasingly the games / animation industry.
- **Sports Science graduates** enter the HKSAR Sports Institute, professional clubs, fitness, and corporate wellness.

### 7.3 IANG and Hong Kong PR

All Mainland and international graduates of an EdUHK full-time UGC-funded undergraduate programme qualify for the **Immigration Arrangements for Non-local Graduates (IANG) visa**, currently allowing a 24-month stay after graduation without a job offer. After **7 years of continuous ordinary residence in Hong Kong** (which counts both your study years and IANG/work years), you can apply for **Hong Kong Permanent Residency**.

## 8. Application Timeline and Practical Tips

### 8.1 JUPAS (DSE Candidates) Calendar

- **Early December 2026:** JUPAS application opens.
- **Mid-December 2026:** Band A deadline (do not change Bands after this date).
- **April–June 2027:** EdUHK programme interviews (most BEd programmes). Group + individual format.
- **Mid-July 2027:** DSE results released. JUPAS Late Modification window opens for ~3 days.
- **Early August 2027:** Main Round offer release.

### 8.2 Non-JUPAS / Mainland / International Calendar

- **Mid-October 2026:** Online application opens.
- **Early January 2027:** Main round deadline (programmes with quotas may close earlier).
- **March–May 2027:** Interview windows.
- **Conditional offers** issued from January; final offers after Gaokao results / IB results in July.

### 8.3 Five Practical Tips

1. **Take the interview seriously.** For BEd programmes, the interview can swing the admission decision more than ±2 score points. Prepare authentic answers to "Why teach?" and a short micro-lesson if invited.
2. **Apply for entrance scholarships in your main application** — there is no separate form; selection is automatic from the application file.
3. **For Mainland applicants**: applying to EdUHK does **not** consume any Mainland Gaokao slot, so you can keep both pathways open until July.
4. **Choose your Best 5 strategically** — drop your weakest non-CSD subject; remember M1/M2 count.
5. **Verify programme-specific subject weightings** on the EdUHK Apply portal — a single weighted subject can move your effective score by 1.5–3 points.

## 9. Is EdUHK Right For You? A Decision Framework

Choose EdUHK if you fit at least two of the following:
- You want a clearly defined career identity (teacher / educational psychologist / special-needs practitioner / sports scientist).
- You value a top-10-globally subject ranking in education over a marginally higher overall-university brand.
- Your DSE Best 5 is in the 19–26 range and you want a UGC-funded degree at a publicly-recognised university.
- You are a Mainland student looking for a competitive Hong Kong university at the lowest non-local tuition tier.
- You prefer a smaller, dedicated campus community over a sprawling city campus.

Look at HKU / CUHK / HKUST first if your Best 5 is 30+ and your career goal has no link to education, psychology, language, or creative-arts disciplines.
