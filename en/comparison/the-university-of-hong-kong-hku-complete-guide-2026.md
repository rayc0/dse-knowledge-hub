# The University of Hong Kong (HKU) Complete Guide 2026

The University of Hong Kong (HKU) is the oldest and most internationally recognised university in the city, founded in 1911. For students applying through international qualifications, the HKDSE, or as non-JUPAS local candidates, HKU is consistently the most competitive destination in Hong Kong. This guide walks through everything you need to plan a 2026 application: rankings, programmes, entry requirements, the application timeline, tuition, scholarships and what life on campus actually looks like.

## Why HKU?

HKU is ranked **#11 in the QS World University Rankings 2026**, making it one of the highest-ranked universities in Asia and consistently inside the global top 20. That ranking matters in practical terms: HKU degrees carry strong recognition with employers across Greater China, Southeast Asia, the UK and North America, and the university's research output and faculty reputation feed directly into graduate employability.

Just as important for applicants from outside Hong Kong, HKU is genuinely international. The student body draws from **97 countries and regions**, and **over 55% of students are non-local**. English is the medium of instruction across virtually all undergraduate programmes, so you do not need Cantonese to study or graduate — though picking up conversational Cantonese will significantly improve daily life and internship access.

The campus sits in Pokfulam on Hong Kong Island, connected by its own MTR station (HKU Station on the Island Line), which puts the rest of the city within easy reach.

## Undergraduate Programmes

HKU offers **57 undergraduate programmes** across a wide spread of disciplines. The major faculties and schools include:

- **Architecture** — including the BA(Architectural Studies) and surveying-related programmes
- **Arts** — humanities, languages, history, philosophy
- **Business and Economics** — the BBA family, including the highly selective BBA(Law), BBA(IBGM) and BFin programmes
- **Dentistry** — the BDS, one of only two dental programmes in Hong Kong
- **Education** — including the double-degree BEd&BSc/BA teaching streams
- **Engineering** — civil, mechanical, electrical, plus Biomedical Engineering
- **Law** — the LLB and the highly competitive double degrees
- **Medicine** — the MBBS at the LKS Faculty of Medicine, plus Nursing, Pharmacy, Biomedical Sciences and Chinese Medicine
- **Science** — including the flagship BSc and Computing & Data Science
- **Social Sciences** — including the BSocSc, journalism/media, psychology and the BSocSc(Government & Law)

Newer interdisciplinary offerings — **Computing and Data Science**, **Innovation and Technology**, and **Media Studies** — reflect the university's pivot toward technology and AI. If you are unsure of your exact major, several faculties admit on a broad-based basis in Year 1 and let you declare a major later, which reduces the risk of locking into the wrong programme at 18.

The most competitive programmes every cycle are MBBS (Medicine), BDS (Dentistry), the Law double degrees (especially BBA(Law) and BSocSc(Government & Laws)), and BFin/quantitative finance streams. These typically require interviews and additional assessments on top of strong academics.

## Entry Routes: JUPAS vs Non-JUPAS vs International

How you apply depends on your qualification:

- **HKDSE students at local schools** apply through **JUPAS** (the Joint University Programmes Admissions System). HKU programmes have published JUPAS minimum requirements and competitive median scores; the standard general entry requirement is "332A33" (Chinese 3, English 3, Maths 2, Citizenship & Social Development "Attained", plus two electives at 3).
- **Local students with non-HKDSE qualifications** (e.g. IB, GCE A-Levels at a Hong Kong international school) apply through the **non-JUPAS** route.
- **Non-local / international students** — including those completing the IB, A-Levels, SAT/AP, or other national qualifications outside the Gaokao — apply through the **International / non-JUPAS** admissions scheme. (Mainland Gaokao candidates apply through HKU's separate Mainland direct-admission scheme, not covered in detail here.)

This guide focuses on the international and non-JUPAS routes, which is where most overseas-based applicants sit.

## Entry Requirements

HKU sets benchmark standards for each major qualification. Indicative competitive levels are:

- **IB Diploma:** Strong applicants typically present 38–43+ points; the most selective programmes (Medicine, Dentistry, Law) push toward the high end. Note: HKU offers a **full scholarship to IB applicants scoring 44 and above**.
- **GCE A-Levels:** Generally AAA and above for competitive programmes; applicants achieving **5 A* grades in one academic year are eligible for a full scholarship**.
- **SAT/AP:** Considered alongside a strong high-school transcript and AP subject results.

Subject prerequisites apply for specific programmes — for example, Medicine, Dentistry and many Science/Engineering streams require Chemistry and/or Biology/Physics, and quantitative business programmes expect strong Mathematics.

### English Language Requirement

An **IELTS score of 6.5 (with no subtest below 5.5) is the compulsory minimum** for any HKU bachelor's degree (TOEFL is accepted at the equivalent level). Some programmes set higher thresholds. A critical 2026 rule change: **starting from the 2026 intake, IELTS and TOEFL results must have been taken within two years prior to 1 September of the admissions year.** If your test is older than that window, you will need to re-sit it — plan test dates accordingly.

## Application Timeline & Deadlines (2026 Entry)

HKU operates a two-stage rolling system for international/non-JUPAS applicants:

- **First Round Evaluation deadline:** 12:00 noon, **26 November 2025** (Hong Kong Time). Applying in the first round maximises your chance at competitive programmes and entrance scholarships.
- **Final deadline:** Applications continue to be accepted until 12:00 noon, **21 August 2026** (Hong Kong Time), considered on a **rolling basis subject to places availability**.

The practical takeaway: although the door stays open until August, popular programmes fill from the first round. Treat the November deadline as your real target, especially for Medicine, Dentistry, Law and Business. Submit your application via the HKU online portal, upload supporting documents (transcripts, English test, personal statement, references where required), and monitor the offer-holder portal after submission.

## Tuition Fees

For non-local undergraduate students, HKU tuition for 2026 entry sits in the range of approximately **HK$182,000 per year** for most programmes, with professional programmes (notably Medicine and Dentistry) at a higher band. Across the full spread of programmes and including some premium streams, published figures range from roughly **HK$154,000 to HK$324,000 per year** depending on the programme. Local students pay the government-subsidised rate of HK$44,500 per year, which does not apply to non-local students.

On top of tuition, budget for accommodation, food, transport, insurance and personal expenses. A realistic non-local living budget is in the order of **HK$50,000–80,000 per year** beyond tuition, depending on whether you secure a residential hall place.

## Scholarships & Financial Aid

HKU runs one of the most generous entrance scholarship systems in Hong Kong:

- **Automatic consideration:** Once you submit a complete admission application, you are **automatically considered for an entrance scholarship** — no separate application is needed. Admission and scholarship results are announced together.
- **42 scholarships** are specifically available to students presenting international qualifications.
- **IB full scholarship:** applicants scoring **44 or above** in the IB Diploma.
- **A-Level full scholarship:** applicants achieving **5 A* grades in one academic year**.
- Scholarships range from one-off awards to renewable packages covering tuition and living expenses, selected on academic merit with consideration of non-academic achievements (leadership, competitions, community contribution).

There is also the **HKU Scholarship for Non-Academic Talents** direct-admission scheme for applicants with exceptional talent in sport, music, the arts or other areas. If you have a strong non-academic profile, research this scheme early — it has its own pathway and timeline.

## Student Life & Campus

HKU's main campus is compact and historic, anchored by the iconic Main Building and the modern Centennial Campus, with the dedicated HKU MTR station underneath. Key features of student life:

- **Residential halls and colleges:** Hall life is a defining HKU experience, with strong traditions, sports, cultural activities and tight communities. Demand far exceeds supply, so hall places are competitive and not guaranteed for all years — apply early and have a private-rental backup plan.
- **Exchange and global opportunities:** HKU has one of the largest student exchange networks in Asia, with partners worldwide; many students spend a semester abroad.
- **Internships and employability:** Hong Kong's position as a finance, legal and logistics hub means strong internship access, particularly for Business, Law and Engineering students. The university's careers service and alumni network are extensive.
- **Languages:** Teaching is in English, but free Cantonese and Putonghua classes are widely available and worth taking for daily life and the local job market.

## Is HKU Right for You?

HKU is the strongest fit if you want a globally top-ranked, English-medium degree in Asia with deep employer recognition across Greater China and internationally, and you can meet genuinely competitive entry standards. It is the most selective option in Hong Kong, so applicants should also build a balanced list — CUHK and HKUST are comparably ranked and may suit specific programme strengths better (see our HKU vs CUHK vs HKUST comparison).

### Action Checklist for 2026 Entry

1. Confirm your qualification route (international / non-JUPAS / JUPAS).
2. Sit IELTS/TOEFL **within the two-year validity window** before 1 September of your entry year — aim for 6.5+ early.
3. Check programme-specific subject prerequisites and any interview/assessment requirements.
4. Target the **First Round deadline (26 November 2025)** for the best shot at offers and scholarships.
5. Submit a complete application — scholarship consideration is automatic.
6. Plan finances: roughly HK$182,000 tuition for most programmes plus HK$50,000–80,000 living costs per year.
7. Apply for residential halls early and prepare a rental backup.

HKU rewards early, well-prepared applicants. Lock your test scores, hit the first round, and present a clear, evidenced profile — both academic and beyond — to give yourself the strongest chance at Hong Kong's flagship university.
