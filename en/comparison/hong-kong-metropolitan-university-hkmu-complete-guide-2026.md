# Hong Kong Metropolitan University (HKMU) Complete Guide 2026

When DSE candidates and international applicants think about Hong Kong's universities, the conversation almost always starts with the eight UGC-funded institutions — HKU, CUHK, HKUST, PolyU, CityU, HKBU, EdUHK and Lingnan. Yet sitting outside that "Group of Eight" is a university that quietly enrols around 12,000 full-time undergraduates, runs the largest nursing pipeline in Hong Kong, and in 2024 became the territory's first ever University of Applied Sciences (UAS): **Hong Kong Metropolitan University (HKMU)**.

In 2026, HKMU is no longer a niche option for second-chance learners. It debuted in the QS World University Rankings 2026 in the 781–790 band — the first self-financing institution and first UAS in Hong Kong to be ranked — and six of its subjects appeared in the QS World University Rankings by Subject 2026, three of them in the global top 150. For a university that began life in 1989 as the distance-learning Open Learning Institute, that arc is striking.

This guide walks through HKMU's identity, its five schools, the JUPAS and non-JUPAS application routes, tuition after government subsidy, scholarships, campus and accommodation, and where graduates actually end up. If you are weighing a self-financing offer against a UGC programme, or considering HKMU as your insurance choice, this is the context you need.

---

## 1. What HKMU Is — and Why Its Status Matters

HKMU is unique in Hong Kong in three ways simultaneously:

1. **It is the only self-financing university established by the Hong Kong Government.** Other self-financing institutions (such as Hang Seng University, Saint Francis University, or Tung Wah College) were founded by private bodies. HKMU traces its statutory origin to the 1989 Open Learning Institute Ordinance.
2. **It is Hong Kong's first University of Applied Sciences (UAS).** In March 2024, the Education Bureau formally designated HKMU as the city's inaugural UAS, modelled after the German *Fachhochschule* tradition: degree programmes built around industry practice, applied research, and credentials valued by employers rather than purely academic prestige.
3. **It is Hong Kong's largest self-financing university by enrolment**, with around 12,000 full-time students plus a sizeable part-time and distance-learning population that traces back to its open-university roots.

The UAS designation is the most important development for prospective students. It signals a deliberate policy direction: the Government wants HKMU to anchor a parallel, application-oriented degree track alongside the research-heavy UGC sector. In practice that translates into more compulsory internship hours, closer ties with regulators (Nursing Council, Physiotherapy Board, Civil Aviation Department), and programmes deliberately scoped to address Hong Kong's skills shortages.

In 2021 the institution also rebranded from "Open University of Hong Kong" to "Hong Kong Metropolitan University" to reflect that more than 90% of its students are now full-time, on-campus learners — the open-learning legacy is no longer the dominant identity.

## 2. The Five Schools and Their Programme Portfolio

HKMU organises its undergraduate teaching across five schools. Each one has carved out a clear niche rather than trying to mirror the UGC universities.

### School of Arts and Social Sciences

Programmes in creative writing, film, animation and visual effects, journalism and communication, applied psychology, social sciences, sports and recreation management. The creative-media offerings are particularly well-regarded: graduates are visible in Hong Kong's animation, post-production and gaming industries, and the school operates one of the few dedicated motion-capture and VFX studios on a Hong Kong campus.

### Lee Shau Kee School of Business and Administration

Bachelor of Business Administration programmes with concentrations including accounting, finance, marketing, international business, and the flagship **Aviation Services Management** programme — built with Hong Kong's airport ecosystem in mind and supported by a dedicated cabin training simulator. The school is named after the late property magnate whose foundation funded the building.

### School of Education and Languages

The largest provider of pre-service language teachers among self-financing institutions. Five-year double-degree programmes pair a BEd with a BA in Chinese, English or Putonghua education — graduates qualify directly as registered teachers in Hong Kong primary or secondary schools without needing a separate PGDE. There are also stand-alone language and translation degrees.

### School of Nursing and Health Sciences

This is HKMU's defining strength. The School produces more nursing graduates each year than any other Hong Kong institution — a critical pipeline given chronic shortages in the public hospital system. Programmes include the five-year Bachelor of Nursing (with both general and mental-health streams), the Bachelor of Science (Hons) in Physiotherapy launched in recent years, and the Bachelor of Science (Hons) in Medical Laboratory Science. These are all SSSDP-subsidised and lead to professional registration.

### School of Science and Technology

Engineering and computing programmes positioned around the UAS philosophy. Offerings include Computer Science, Cybersecurity, Data Science, Building Services Engineering and Sustainable Development, Civil Engineering, Electronic and Computer Engineering, Testing and Certification, Environmental Studies, and Life Sciences. Many programmes are accredited by the Hong Kong Institution of Engineers or by professional bodies in their respective sectors.

For the 2026/27 academic year, HKMU is offering **50 JUPAS programmes** across these five schools, plus a wider portfolio when non-JUPAS, top-up, and senior-year-entry options are counted.

## 3. JUPAS Admission to HKMU

HKMU has been a full participating institution in the **Joint University Programmes Admissions System (JUPAS)** since 2007. Local DSE candidates apply through the same JUPAS portal, slot HKMU programmes into Bands A–C, and receive offers in the same Main Round as the eight UGC universities.

A few HKMU-specific rules are worth memorising:

- **Best-of-multiple-sittings, no penalty.** HKMU considers the best grade attained in each subject across any HKDSE sittings. Re-takers are not penalised, which is a meaningful contrast with some UGC programmes that prefer single-sitting candidates.
- **Subject weighting varies by programme.** Most BBA programmes weight English and Mathematics; the BEng programmes weight Mathematics and a science subject; nursing and physiotherapy weight Biology or Chemistry. Always read the programme-specific weighting on the JUPAS site rather than relying on a generic 4C+2X assumption.
- **Interview is common but not universal.** Nursing, physiotherapy, education, and aviation programmes typically require interviews; many BBA and arts programmes use grades alone.
- **Lower published score thresholds than UGC.** Indicative median admission scores for many HKMU programmes sit in the **15–22 (4C+1X)** range, depending on programme. Nursing, physiotherapy, and the popular BEd double degrees typically demand the upper end of that range, while less competitive arts and BBA streams are accessible with the JUPAS minimum.

For DSE students who fall short of UGC offers but still want a recognised, government-supported degree, HKMU is one of the most logical insurance choices precisely because the JUPAS process is identical and the funding is parallel.

## 4. Non-JUPAS, Sub-degree Articulation, and International Routes

HKMU runs three other inflows worth knowing:

- **Non-JUPAS (Local).** For local applicants holding qualifications other than current-year DSE — IB, A-Level, GCE, HKALE, Associate Degrees or Higher Diplomas. Applications are submitted directly through the HKMU admissions portal.
- **Sub-degree articulation.** Holders of Associate Degrees or Higher Diplomas from HKCC, CCCU, IVE, HKU SPACE, and other local providers can apply for senior-year (Year 3) entry into a matching bachelor programme. This is one of the most common pathways into HKMU and is responsible for a significant share of each year's intake.
- **International / Mainland applicants.** Mainland students apply via the HKMU Mainland Admissions route (separate from the Gaokao single-track scheme used by UGC universities). International students apply through the Non-JUPAS portal and submit IELTS/TOEFL or equivalent. HKMU programmes are eligible for Hong Kong student visas, and graduates qualify for the **IANG** post-study work scheme on the same basis as UGC graduates.

## 5. Tuition, SSSDP, NMTSS, and the Real Cost

This is the area most prospective students misunderstand. "Self-financing" sounds like "expensive" — but in HKMU's case the headline tuition is heavily offset by Government subsidy.

All HKMU full-time undergraduate programmes are funded under one of two Government schemes:

- **SSSDP (Study Subsidy Scheme for Designated Professions/Sectors).** Targeted at programmes addressing manpower shortages — nursing, physiotherapy, medical laboratory science, building services, computer science, testing and certification, creative industries, and several BBA streams. Students pay an annual tuition comparable to the standard UGC tuition (HK$44,500/year for the 2026/27 cohort, subject to confirmation).
- **NMTSS (Non-means-tested Subsidy Scheme).** Applies to most other full-time self-financing degree programmes. For 2025/26, the annual subsidy was raised to **HK$35,120**, with a further review for 2026/27 indexed to the Composite Consumer Price Index. Net tuition typically lands in the HK$45,000–60,000/year range depending on programme.

In practical terms, a Hong Kong student admitted to HKMU through JUPAS into an SSSDP programme pays roughly the same out-of-pocket tuition as a CUHK or HKU student in a comparable subject. The financial penalty for choosing HKMU over UGC is much smaller than the "self-financing" label suggests.

Government student finance (TSFS, NLSFT, EFAS) is fully available to eligible local students at HKMU on the same terms as UGC institutions.

## 6. Scholarships — The DSE Star Scholarship

HKMU runs an aggressive entrance scholarship policy designed to attract high-DSE candidates who might otherwise default to a UGC offer:

- **HKDSE Star Scholarship.** Since the 2022/23 cohort, JUPAS-admitted students who achieve **5\* or 5\*\* in any Category A subject** in the year of admission receive **HK$40,000 per qualifying subject**. There is no cap on the number of subjects — a student with three 5\*\* grades receives HK$120,000. The scholarship is awarded on top of any other merit award.
- **Other entrance scholarships** include the President's Scholarship, dean's-list awards, and programme-specific awards in nursing, physiotherapy, aviation, and the creative arts.
- **Continuing scholarships** are available each academic year for high-GPA students.

For a DSE candidate hovering at the boundary between a competitive UGC programme and an HKMU offer in their target field, the Star Scholarship plus government subsidy can produce a financial package that is materially better than the unsubsidised cost of attending a less-competitive UGC degree.

## 7. Campus and Student Life

HKMU's main campus sits in **Ho Man Tin, Kowloon**, on Good Shepherd Street, supplemented by the **Jockey Club Campus** nearby. Both are within walking distance of Ho Man Tin MTR station on the Tuen Ma and Kwun Tong Lines, putting students fifteen to twenty minutes from Mong Kok, Kowloon Tong, and the cross-harbour core.

Smaller annexes house specialist facilities — the cabin simulator for aviation services, the nursing skills laboratories, the VFX and motion-capture studios, and the building services labs. The compactness of the urban campus is a trade-off: students don't get the green expanse of CUHK or HKUST, but they do get genuinely central Kowloon, with internships and part-time work realistically reachable by MTR.

**Accommodation.** Historically a weakness for HKMU. The 2024 acquisition of a former hotel in Hung Hom, rebranded as **MU88**, added 255 rooms and around 400 beds, primarily for non-local and exchange students. Local students typically commute from home — a normal pattern across most Hong Kong universities.

**Student life.** Around 80 student societies cover academic, cultural, religious, and sporting interests. The HKMU Students' Union runs the central social calendar; sports facilities include a multi-purpose hall and access to government recreation grounds. The university's competitive sports teams, particularly in basketball and dragonboat, regularly feature in inter-university tournaments.

## 8. Graduate Outcomes and Industry Recognition

HKMU does not publish a single headline employment-rate figure to match the UGC standard, but a few signals are robust:

- **Nursing graduates** enter the Hospital Authority pipeline directly upon registration. Demand has consistently exceeded supply for more than a decade. Starting salaries for newly registered nurses in HA are around HK$32,000/month (Master Pay Scale Point 14).
- **Physiotherapy graduates** are similarly absorbed by HA, private clinics and elderly-care providers, with starting pay at a similar level subject to professional registration.
- **Education graduates** with the BEd double degree enter Hong Kong primary and secondary schools as registered teachers without further qualification — the same status as EdUHK BEd graduates. Starting pay on the Master Pay Scale Civil Service Salaries is around HK$36,000/month.
- **Aviation Services Management graduates** are a deliberate feed for Cathay Pacific, Hong Kong Airlines, the Airport Authority and ground-handling operators.
- **Creative arts graduates** populate Hong Kong's animation, gaming, and post-production firms — notable employers include local studios working on regional broadcast and feature-film projects.

The QS subject rankings 2026 placed three HKMU subjects in the global top 150 — a useful signal for international recognition, particularly for students considering postgraduate study abroad.

## 9. HKMU vs UGC Universities — When Does It Make Sense?

HKMU is the right choice rather than a fallback in several specific scenarios:

- **You want to be a registered nurse, physiotherapist, or medical laboratory scientist** and your DSE results put you below the cut-off for the equivalent CUHK, HKU or PolyU programme. HKMU produces more nursing graduates than any UGC institution and the professional registration outcome is identical.
- **You want to be a primary-school or kindergarten language teacher** and the EdUHK BEd is out of reach. The HKMU double-degree route delivers the same registration outcome.
- **You want a creative-industries career** and you have a strong portfolio. The applied, studio-based approach often suits creative practitioners better than the more theoretical art-history orientation at UGC institutions.
- **You are an international or Mainland student** seeking a Hong Kong qualification with IANG eligibility at lower published tuition than the UGC top tier.
- **You hold an Associate Degree or Higher Diploma** and need a clean senior-year articulation route.

HKMU is a weaker choice if you want a research-heavy academic career (the UGC universities offer deeper research mentoring), if you specifically need Hong Kong's most prestigious institutional name on your CV for finance or law recruitment, or if your target programme (Medicine, Law, Architecture) is simply not offered at HKMU.

## 10. Application Timeline for the 2026/27 Cycle

For DSE candidates targeting HKMU through JUPAS:

- **December 2025**: JUPAS application opens; programme rankings can be revised through to the deadline.
- **Late February 2026**: Final deadline to revise programme choices (subject to JUPAS-published date).
- **April–May 2026**: HKMU may invite you for interview for nursing, physiotherapy, education and aviation programmes. Interviews are typically held in May–June.
- **July 2026**: HKDSE results released; JUPAS Main Round offers issued shortly after.
- **August 2026**: Confirmation, registration, and orientation.

For non-JUPAS, sub-degree articulation, and international applicants, HKMU operates rolling admissions through its dedicated portal at admissions.hkmu.edu.hk, with main intake decisions issued from spring through to summer 2026.

## Bottom Line

Hong Kong Metropolitan University is no longer correctly described as a "second-tier alternative" to the UGC eight. It is Hong Kong's first University of Applied Sciences, its largest self-financing institution, and the dominant supplier of nursing, language-teaching and aviation graduates to the local economy. With government subsidies aligning net tuition with UGC rates, an aggressive merit scholarship for high-DSE candidates, and QS-ranked academic recognition arriving in 2026, the case for HKMU has shifted from "fallback" to "specifically chosen". For DSE candidates whose career target lies in nursing, physiotherapy, language teaching, creative media, aviation, or applied engineering, an HKMU offer in 2026 deserves to be evaluated on its own merits — not as a runner-up.
