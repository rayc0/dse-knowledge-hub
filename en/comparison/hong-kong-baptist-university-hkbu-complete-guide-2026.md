# Hong Kong Baptist University (HKBU) Complete Guide 2026

Among Hong Kong's eight UGC-funded universities, Hong Kong Baptist University — HKBU, or simply BU as students call it — does not try to compete with HKU, CUHK and HKUST on overall research firepower. Instead, it has spent the last three decades building unrivalled depth in three quite specific areas: **Communication and Media Studies (the leading school of its kind in Asia)**, **Chinese Medicine (the first Hong Kong university to offer a Bachelor of Chinese Medicine)**, and **Creative Arts (a 2022-formed academy that consolidates film, music and visual arts under one roof)**.

If you are a DSE candidate or an international applicant whose interests lie in journalism and broadcasting, film and music production, visual arts, Chinese medicine, applied data science, or social sciences with a strong humanities flavour, HKBU offers something its larger peers cannot: small classes, an intimate campus in the heart of Kowloon Tong, and faculties that are global leaders in their narrow specialisms rather than middle-of-the-pack contributors to a giant comprehensive university. With only around 5,800 undergraduates, the student-to-faculty ratio sits near 1:14, and signature programmes such as Journalism, Film & Television, and Music routinely run in classes of 15–25.

This guide covers the 2026/27 admissions cycle: the QS rankings story, the eight faculties, the JUPAS and non-JUPAS application routes, tuition fees and scholarships, the flagship programmes you should know about, and what graduates actually do after they leave the Kowloon Tong campus.

## 1. What kind of university is HKBU?

HKBU was founded in **1956** as Hong Kong Baptist College and gained full university status in **1994**, making it the **second-oldest higher education institution in Hong Kong** after HKU. Its main campus sits on Waterloo Road and Renfrew Road in **Kowloon Tong**, directly served by the MTR Kwun Tong Line and East Rail Line interchange — Festival Walk shopping mall is across the street, and Central is roughly 30 minutes away by metro. Among the eight UGC-funded universities, HKBU's location is one of the most convenient.

The "Baptist" in the name reflects the institution's founding by the Baptist Convention of Hong Kong in the 1950s, but HKBU today operates as a fully secular public research university. There is no religious requirement at admission, and Christianity-related courses appear only as electives within the General Education programme. The motto, **"Truth and Service"** (篤信力行), has remained unchanged since the college era.

### Size and campus character

- Around **5,800 undergraduates** and **5,000 postgraduates**, total enrolment of about 11,000 — one of the smallest among the eight UGC-funded universities
- **Student-to-faculty ratio of approximately 1:14**, with small-class teaching the norm in Communication, Creative Arts and Chinese Medicine programmes
- Operates **Hong Kong's most fully equipped university broadcasting and recording facilities**, attached to the School of Communication
- The campus is **physically compact** — the entire main and Shaw campuses can be walked end-to-end in under fifteen minutes
- A **secondary campus** for Creative Arts and Chinese Medicine sits a short walk away in the Ho Sin Hang Campus and the Kai Tak development is hosting the new School of Chinese Medicine teaching hospital opening in phases through 2026 and 2027

The campus's small size is a real advantage for incoming students: you bump into your classmates, your professors and the campus restaurants in the same buildings every day. It is the closest thing in Hong Kong to a liberal-arts-college feel attached to a research university.

- **Communication & Media Studies** — QS world top 51–100, the strongest position of any HKBU subject and a long-standing leader within Asia

The takeaway: HKBU's prestige is concentrated, not spread thin. If your target subject is on the strong list, the university is genuinely competitive with anything in the region.

## 3. The eight faculties and what they offer

HKBU restructured its undergraduate offering in 2022/23, splitting the historic Faculty of Social Sciences from the Faculty of Arts and giving the Academy of Visual Arts, the Academy of Film and the Department of Music a unified home as the **School of Creative Arts**. The 2026/27 structure is:

1. **Faculty of Arts** — English Language and Literature, Translation, Humanities, History, Religious Studies and Philosophy, plus Chinese Language and Literature
2. **Faculty of Social Sciences** — Sociology, Government and International Studies, Geography, Social Work, Education Studies and Sport Studies
3. **Faculty of Science** — Biology, Chemistry, Mathematics, Physics, Computer Science, Statistics, Environment Science, Applied Biology, and a flagship undergraduate AI programme launched in 2024
4. **School of Business** — Accountancy, Economics, Finance, Marketing, Information Systems and Business Administration; AACSB- and EQUIS-accredited
5. **School of Communication** — Journalism, Public Relations and Advertising, Film, Television and Digital Visual Production; the historical core of the university's reputation
6. **School of Chinese Medicine** — Bachelor of Chinese Medicine and Bachelor of Science (Hons) in Biomedical Science (six-year combined programme), the first such offering at any Hong Kong university
7. **School of Creative Arts** — three academies under one school: the Academy of Film, the Department of Music and the Academy of Visual Arts
8. **Division of Transdisciplinary Studies** — bridge programmes such as Media Arts, Sustainability Management and AI and Social Innovation

The university advertises **22 admission programmes leading to more than 45 majors**, with a broad-based admission model that lets DSE students start in a faculty and declare a final major after their first year.

## 4. Flagship programmes you should know about

### School of Communication — Asia's leading communications school

The School of Communication is the single best-known unit at HKBU and the reason most international applicants put the university on their list. Its three undergraduate departments — **Journalism**, **Communication Studies** (Public Relations, Advertising, Organisational Communication), and **Film, Television and Digital Visual Production** — share one of the largest university-owned television studios, radio stations and recording facilities in the region.

- Journalism graduates routinely fill the editorial benches of South China Morning Post, RTHK, Hong Kong Free Press, Bloomberg HK, Reuters HK and a long list of mainland and international outlets
- The Film Academy has produced a disproportionate share of Hong Kong's working art-house and commercial directors over the last two decades
- The school's broadcast facilities are used for live student news bulletins and for a substantial volume of professional production work

JUPAS competition for Communication is among the highest at HKBU — admission scores typically sit at the upper end of the university's range.

### School of Chinese Medicine — Hong Kong's first BCM

HKBU's School of Chinese Medicine offers a **six-year combined Bachelor of Chinese Medicine and Bachelor of Science (Hons) in Biomedical Science** programme. It was the first programme of its kind anywhere in Hong Kong, and it is still the school most applicants think of when they consider Chinese medicine as a career. Graduates qualify to sit the Hong Kong Chinese Medicine Practitioners Licensing Examination and to practise across the territory and increasingly in the Greater Bay Area.

A new Chinese Medicine teaching hospital adjacent to the Kai Tak development is being commissioned in phases — the first major dedicated CM hospital in Hong Kong — and HKBU's school is the principal academic partner.

### School of Creative Arts — film, music and visual arts under one roof

Formed in 2022, the School of Creative Arts is HKBU's newest school but its constituent academies are decades old. The **Academy of Film** trains directors, screenwriters and producers; the **Department of Music** teaches Western classical performance, composition and music technology; and the **Academy of Visual Arts**, located on the historic Kai Tak Campus, is the territory's leading studio-based fine arts programme.

For a creative-arts applicant who wants a serious portfolio-based undergraduate education in Hong Kong, this is the most concentrated option in the city.

### School of Business — small but accredited

HKBU's School of Business is the smallest of the four AACSB+EQUIS double-accredited business schools in the eight-university system, but the small size translates into a high level of personal attention. Its **BBA in Global Business Management**, **BBA in Accounting**, and **BBA in Finance** routinely place graduates into Big Four audit roles, mid-tier investment banks and the Greater Bay Area corporate sector.

### Faculty of Science — AI and Data Science as new flagships

The most significant academic investment of the past three years has been the expansion of the Department of Computer Science and the launch of a dedicated **BSc in Artificial Intelligence** programme in 2024. Graduates of the new AI track are competing successfully for places in mainland tech firms, Hong Kong fintech roles, and overseas master's programmes.

## 5. Admission routes and entrance requirements 2026/27

HKBU recruits undergraduates through three main routes.

### Route A — JUPAS (HKDSE candidates)

The general entrance requirements (GER) for 2026 entry to a JUPAS programme require:

- **Four core subjects**: Chinese Language Level 3, English Language Level 3, Mathematics (Compulsory Part) Level 2, and Citizenship and Social Development "Attained"
- **Two elective subjects** at Level 3 or above (or equivalent, with M1/M2 mathematics and Other Language Subjects accepted)

Programme-specific entrance requirements (PER) sit on top of GER and vary widely. Communication, Chinese Medicine, the BBA suite and Computer Science have higher PERs than the university floor; some Faculty of Arts and Sport and Recreation Leadership programmes admit at GER. The 2025 admissions data shows **median Best 5 scores ranging from the upper teens to the mid-twenties** depending on programme.

Application is via the central JUPAS system. Bands and choice ordering follow the standard JUPAS rules — for HKBU, putting target programmes in Band A is essential for the most competitive seats.

### Route B — Non-JUPAS direct admission (international and mainland China applicants)

Applicants holding qualifications other than HKDSE — Gaokao, A-Level, IB, SAT/ACT, or recognised international school diplomas — apply directly to HKBU via the Online Application System.

For 2026/27 entry:

- **Mainland Gaokao**: HKBU is one of the eight UGC-funded universities recruiting on Gaokao scores via direct application (not via the central mainland 提前批 channel for HKBU). The university typically requires a score above the **Tier 1 (一本/特殊类型) line by 80–120 points** depending on programme, with English at **120 or above** for most programmes and **130 or above** for Communication, Translation and English Language and Literature. An admissions interview (in person or video) is standard
- **A-Level / IAL**: Three A2 passes, typically AAA–ABB depending on programme; specific science passes for Chinese Medicine and Science programmes
- **IB Diploma**: 30–38 points depending on programme, with HL subject requirements
- **SAT / ACT**: SAT 1300+ or ACT 28+ as a baseline, plus four AP scores or a US high school diploma

The application window for 2026/27 entry runs from **November 2025 to early January 2026** for the main round, with rolling admission for some programmes through to mid-year.

### Route C — Direct Admission Schemes (HKDSE candidates outside JUPAS)

A subset of HKDSE candidates — those holding additional international qualifications, or applying to Sport and Recreation Leadership, Music, Visual Arts or Film with strong portfolios — can apply through HKBU's Direct Admission Schemes. Acceptances under these schemes are conditional on meeting HKDSE benchmarks but allow earlier offers and reduce reliance on JUPAS band ordering.

## 6. Tuition fees, scholarships and total cost

### Local tuition (UGC-funded, HKDSE)

The standard UGC-funded undergraduate tuition fee for local students in 2026/27 is **HK$44,500 per year**. Local students with financial need can apply for the Tertiary Student Finance Scheme (TSFS) and Non-means-tested Loan Scheme (NLSPS) administered by the Hong Kong Working Family and Student Financial Assistance Agency.

### Non-local tuition

For 2026/27 entry, HKBU sets its non-local undergraduate tuition at:

- **General undergraduate programmes: HK$145,000 per year** — among the more affordable rates among the eight UGC-funded universities
- The university has confirmed tuition rises in line with sector trends but at the lower end of the band among the eight universities

### Living costs

For a non-local student living on or near the Kowloon Tong campus:

- **Hostel (on-campus, contested allocation)**: HK$10,000–18,000 per year
- **Off-campus rental in Kowloon Tong / Sham Shui Po / Mong Kok**: HK$6,000–10,000 per month for a shared room, HK$10,000–15,000 for a studio
- **Food**: HK$3,000–5,000 per month if you mostly eat on campus and at neighbourhood cha chaan tengs
- **All-in annual living budget**: HK$80,000–130,000

A non-local student should budget around **HK$220,000–280,000 per year all-in**, depending on accommodation choices. Over four years, that translates to roughly **HK$900,000–1,100,000** for a full undergraduate education.

### Scholarships

HKBU has expanded its entrance scholarship pool considerably in the last two cycles to attract top-band applicants:

- **HKBU Entrance Scholarship for Non-local Students** — full-tuition or half-tuition awards for outstanding Gaokao, A-Level and IB applicants
- **President's Scholarship** — full tuition plus an annual stipend for the top 1–2% of incoming students
- **School of Business Admission Scholarships** — HK$60,000 to full-tuition awards for top-band BBA applicants
- **Faculty-specific scholarships** in Communication, Chinese Medicine and Creative Arts

Mainland Gaokao applicants scoring in the top 0.5% of their province routinely receive at least a half-tuition award.

## 7. Hostel and student life

HKBU's two on-campus halls — **N.T. Wong Hall** and **Wing Lung Bank Hall** — house roughly 1,800 students between them. With around 5,800 undergraduates, the on-campus hall guarantee covers **first-year non-locals and a competitive scoring system** for upper-year students. Most non-local students will spend at least two of their four undergraduate years in private rental in Kowloon Tong, Sham Shui Po, Mong Kok or Tai Kok Tsui — all within 20 minutes of campus by MTR or bus.

The hostel culture is more low-key than at HKU or CUHK. There is a hall-life society, but the student union and academic society scenes — particularly the Communication Students' Association (傳播系學會) and the Film Academy student society — are more active.

The 9-month term structure mirrors the rest of the eight UGC-funded universities: Term 1 from early September to mid-December, Term 2 from mid-January to early May, with an optional summer term in June and July.

## 8. Graduate outcomes

The 2024 Joint University Programmes Admissions Council survey reports HKBU graduate average starting salaries in the **HK$22,000–28,000 per month** band overall, with significant variance by programme:

- **School of Business**: HK$25,000–32,000 starting; Big Four and mid-tier investment bank placements remain strong
- **School of Communication**: HK$20,000–26,000 starting in journalism and PR; higher in advertising and digital production
- **School of Chinese Medicine**: HK$30,000–40,000 once licensed and practising, with substantial demand from public CM clinics and the new Kai Tak teaching hospital
- **School of Creative Arts**: more variable — film and music graduates often go freelance or into postgraduate study; visual arts graduates increasingly find stable roles in art education and gallery work
- **Faculty of Science (AI track)**: HK$24,000–30,000 starting; competitive with Computer Science cohorts at the larger universities

A meaningful share of HKBU graduates go on to **postgraduate study at HKU, CUHK, mainland C9 universities or overseas Russell Group / Ivy-Plus institutions**. The university's small size means that a strong undergraduate transcript is well-known to faculty members writing references.

## 9. Who should apply to HKBU?

You should put HKBU near the top of your list if:

- You want **journalism, film, television, advertising, public relations or visual arts**, and you want a school with the deepest concentration of these programmes in Asia
- You want to study **Chinese medicine** at the only six-year integrated BCM-Biomedical Science programme in the city, with a brand-new teaching hospital coming online
- You want a **smaller, more personal undergraduate experience** with class sizes in the teens and twenties, in a campus that feels like a single community rather than a city
- Your DSE Best 5 sits in the upper teens to mid-twenties, and you want a programme where this score reaches Band A choices for competitive majors rather than the safety options at the larger universities
- You are a Gaokao applicant scoring 80–120 points above the Tier 1 line with strong English, and you want a Hong Kong UGC-funded university that offers tuition at the lower end of the non-local fee band

If your priority is global comprehensive ranking — engineering, medicine, hard sciences in the QS world top 100 — HKU, CUHK, HKUST, PolyU and CityU sit ahead of HKBU on most tables. But if any of the specialist domains above match your interests, HKBU's depth in those subjects is on a different scale from its size.

## 10. Application checklist for 2026/27

- [ ] Decide your route: JUPAS, Non-JUPAS direct, or Direct Admission Schemes
- [ ] Confirm GER and PER on the HKBU Undergraduate Admissions site for each target programme
- [ ] For Communication, Creative Arts and Music, prepare a portfolio or audition tape early — many accept submissions from October
- [ ] For non-JUPAS, register on the HKBU Online Application System and submit before the **early January 2026** deadline
- [ ] For Gaokao applicants, monitor provincial cutoff publication in late June 2026 and submit Gaokao results to HKBU as soon as they are released
- [ ] Apply for entrance scholarships — most require the same application form and a brief personal statement
- [ ] Apply for the on-campus hostel via the Office of Student Affairs once you have an offer
- [ ] If applying for a student visa, begin the IANG / 學生簽證 process via the Hong Kong Immigration Department as soon as the offer letter arrives — the process takes 6–8 weeks

HKBU's admission cycle is one of the more responsive in the eight UGC system, and the international admissions team typically replies to direct queries within a few business days during the application window. For DSE candidates, JUPAS rules and timing apply as standard; for everyone else, **apply early** — the most competitive Communication, Chinese Medicine and Creative Arts programmes fill in the first two rounds.
