# Macau Pathway for DSE Students: University of Macau, MUST, Macao Polytechnic English-Taught Programmes and Stay Permit Guide 2026

For most Hong Kong DSE candidates, "studying overseas" usually means London, Sydney, Toronto or Singapore. Macau rarely appears on the shortlist, yet it is one of the most underrated pathways available. The Special Administrative Region sits an hour by ferry or across the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macao Bridge, Cantonese works on every campus, the University of Macau is ranked in the QS global top 300, and full four-year tuition can come in cheaper than a single year in the UK. Crucially, time spent studying in Macau does not break Hong Kong permanent residency, so graduates can return to Hong Kong with their HKID intact and skip the "non-local graduate" label entirely.

This guide walks through every realistic option for a 2026 DSE leaver: admission requirements at the six main Macau universities, English-medium provision, tuition and scholarships, the non-resident student stay permit (學生逗留證), accommodation, and what happens after graduation if you want to return to Hong Kong, work in Macau, or move into the Greater Bay Area.

## 1. Why Macau Deserves a Place on Your Non-JUPAS Shortlist

**Friendlier admission thresholds.** The University of Macau (UM) accepts DSE 2024 candidates with 3-3-2 in core subjects plus Level 3 in two Category A electives. Compared with JUPAS Band A programmes at HKU/CUHK/HKUST, where actual cut-offs typically run at 5*5*4 or higher for competitive majors, Macau is roughly one tier easier across the board. MUST and Macao Polytechnic are looser still.

**Genuine English-medium teaching.** UM operates almost entirely in English. MUST teaches the majority of its undergraduate programmes in English, and the University of Saint Joseph (USJ) is fully English-taught. Students who scored DSE English Level 4-5 will not face a sudden Mandarin lecture wall the way they might at most mainland universities.

**Affordable total cost.** UM tuition for non-Macau residents sits at roughly MOP 122,600 per year (around HKD 134,000); MUST mainstream programmes are about MOP 170,000. Living costs run HKD 5,000-7,000 a month including hall fees. Four years total comes in around HKD 700,000-900,000 — less than a single UK year for some London business schools.

**You keep your Hong Kong PR.** Studying in Macau counts as absence from Hong Kong, but under the Immigration Ordinance, leaving for full-time study at a recognised institution does not by itself trigger loss of permanent resident status. You can return regularly, retain your HKID, and after graduation walk into Hong Kong's job market as a Hong Kong permanent resident — no IANG visa required, no employer sponsorship pain, no "non-local graduate" status.

**Independent admissions, no JUPAS slots used.** Macau universities run their own admissions outside the JUPAS system, so applying does not consume any of your 20 JUPAS choices. It is a clean parallel application.

## 3. University of Macau (UM): Admission Requirements in Detail

UM is the only Macau university in the QS top 300 and the most international option. Its DSE rules (applicable from the 2024 examination onward) are:

- **English Language: Level 3 or above**
- **Chinese Language: Level 3 or above**
- **Mathematics (Compulsory Part): Level 2 or above**
- **Citizenship and Social Development: Attained**
- **Two Category A electives: Level 3 or above each**

Programme-specific overlays:

- **Faculty of Science and Technology** (Computer Science, Engineering, Maths, Physics): one of the two electives must be Physics at Level 3 or above.
- **Faculty of Health Sciences** (Biomedical Sciences, Pharmaceutical Sciences): Chemistry at Level 3 or above is required among the electives.
- **Faculty of Business Administration**: no specific elective is mandated, but Mathematics M1/M2 at Level 3 strengthens applications.
- **Law (LLB)**: Chinese Language Level 4 is strongly recommended given Chinese-language case material.

Application window for the 2026/27 intake runs from **3 February to 9 April 2026**. Applicants submit through the UM Registry online portal, pay the application fee (around MOP 250), upload predicted grades, and may be invited to an interview for selected programmes (Law, English Studies, Communication). DSE results are released in mid-July; conditional offers are then converted to firm offers, with registration in late August.

## 4. Macau University of Science and Technology (MUST): Strongest Private Option

MUST is the largest private university in Macau and the highest-ranked private institution in the city. For DSE candidates the baseline requirement is **Level 3 in English, Chinese and Mathematics, plus Level 2 in any two electives** — meaningfully easier than UM.

Key programmes:

- **Faculty of Medicine** (MBBS, 6 years): the most competitive at MUST; expect Level 4+ in English and Biology/Chemistry, plus an interview.
- **Faculty of Chinese Medicine**: a flagship programme, registered globally. Bilingual Chinese/English instruction.
- **School of Business**: largest faculty, English-taught, strong in hospitality finance and gaming management.
- **Faculty of Hospitality and Tourism Management**: ranked top 30 globally for hospitality.

MUST runs a rolling admission system; the 2026/27 main window opens **November 2025 and closes around 31 May 2026**, with later waves running into July for unfilled seats. DSE candidates can apply with predicted grades and convert with results in mid-July. Tuition for most programmes is MOP 165,000-185,000 per year; medicine sits at around MOP 250,000.

## 5. Macao Polytechnic University, City University of Macau, USJ and UTM

**Macao Polytechnic University (MPU)** is public, applied-focused, and admits DSE candidates with Level 2 in core subjects. Translation (Chinese-English-Portuguese), nursing, art design and computing are the standout programmes. Tuition for non-residents sits at MOP 28,000-42,000 per year, the cheapest serious option in the region.

**City University of Macau** is private and known for business, education and psychology. Admission is generous (Level 2 across cores), tuition runs MOP 80,000-100,000 per year, and the campus is the most central in Macau peninsula.

**University of Saint Joseph (USJ)** is the only fully English-medium Catholic university in Macau. Strong for psychology, architecture and design. Class sizes are small and the atmosphere is closer to a UK liberal-arts college than a research university. Tuition around MOP 70,000-90,000.

**Macao University of Tourism (UTM)** — formerly the Institute for Tourism Studies (IFT) — is the place to be if hospitality, event management, or gaming management is the goal. UTM ranks top 10 globally in QS Hospitality and runs strong industry placements at Macau's integrated resorts.

## 6. English Requirements and Language Reality

UM, USJ and most MUST programmes accept DSE English Level 3 as meeting the language requirement. There is no separate IELTS requirement for DSE candidates at UM. MUST sometimes asks for IELTS 5.5-6.0 if DSE English is below Level 4, especially for medicine.

The lived reality is that Macau is trilingual — Cantonese is the working language outside lecture halls, Mandarin is increasingly common, Portuguese is ceremonial. DSE candidates already speak Cantonese, so social adjustment is near zero. Picking up working Mandarin is sensible, since most postgraduate or career options reach into the mainland Greater Bay Area.

**Scholarships.** UM offers entrance scholarships of MOP 30,000-122,600 (full tuition) for top-tier admissions, awarded automatically based on DSE results — Level 5* or above in core subjects and electives is competitive. MUST runs the "Macau Foundation Scholarship" and "MUST Excellence Scholarship", up to MOP 100,000 per year. The Macao SAR Government Tertiary Education Office (DSEDJ) also has a Special Scholarship Scheme open to non-Macau students. Apply early — most close in May.

## 8. The Non-Resident Student Stay Permit (學生逗留證)

Hong Kong students do **not** need a visa to enter Macau, but anyone studying full-time for more than 180 days needs a **Non-Resident Student Stay Permit (Autorização de Permanência para Estudante)** issued by the Macao Public Security Police Force (CPSP) Immigration Department.

**How it works:**

1. Once you accept your offer, the university issues an admission letter and submits your file to CPSP.
2. You attend the CPSP Immigration Department in Pac On with your HKID, HKSAR passport, admission letter, accommodation proof and a passport-size photo.
3. The permit is issued as a sticker in your passport and is valid for one academic year, renewed annually.
4. Holders may enter and exit Macau freely during the validity period.

There is no work component to this permit — students cannot legally work full-time in Macau, although unpaid internships and university teaching assistantships are permitted.

## 9. Accommodation: On-Campus, Off-Campus, or Commute from Zhuhai

UM has the most comprehensive halls in Macau — the Hengqin campus has 10 residential colleges modelled on Oxbridge, and first-year non-Macau students are guaranteed a place. MUST and MPU offer halls but with limited capacity, so apply within two weeks of your offer.

Off-campus rentals in Taipa or central Macau run MOP 6,000-9,000 per month for a shared two-bedroom flat. A growing number of UM students live in Zhuhai's Hengqin and walk through the dedicated UM-Hengqin tunnel — Hengqin rents are 30-40% cheaper, but the tunnel closes at midnight.

Commuting from Hong Kong is impractical for most students because of the time and ferry/bridge cost, but quarterly weekend trips home are easy: TurboJET ferry runs HKD 175 one-way and the bridge bus is HKD 80.

## 10. After Graduation: Three Realistic Routes

**Route A — Return to Hong Kong as a permanent resident.** This is the simplest path. You retain your HKID, walk into the Hong Kong job market with no visa restriction, and your overseas degree is recognised by HKQF (assuming it is from a recognised university — all six listed here are). UM, MUST and UTM degrees are well-known among Hong Kong employers in finance, hospitality, law and Chinese medicine.

**Route B — Stay in Macau on a work permit.** Macau employers must apply for a Non-Resident Worker's Identification Card (the "blue card") on your behalf. The Macau labour market is heavily concentrated in gaming, hospitality, finance and government, so non-Macau-resident graduates without local connections find it harder to land their first job locally than in Hong Kong. Realistic salaries: MOP 18,000-28,000 entry-level for hospitality and business graduates.

**Route C — Move into the Greater Bay Area.** Zhuhai, Shenzhen and Guangzhou increasingly recruit Macau graduates for cross-border roles. The Hengqin Cooperation Zone offers tax incentives and a streamlined Macau-resident-status track. UM's Hengqin campus location makes this route especially natural for tech and biotech graduates.

## 12. Common Pitfalls

- Treating Macau as a JUPAS substitute instead of a parallel non-JUPAS application — you can apply to both. Most Macau universities accept results well into August.
- Missing the UM 9 April deadline — UM is strict and does not run a major late round.
- Not budgeting for the stay permit and annual renewal fee (MOP 300).
- Assuming your HKID protects you in Macau — for everything from a SIM card to a hall booking, you need either the stay permit or your HKSAR passport.
- Picking a private university with weak global recognition for a portable career. UM, MUST and UTM are well-recognised; some smaller private colleges are not.

## 13. Final Verdict

For a Hong Kong DSE candidate weighing options outside the JUPAS top tier, Macau hits a rare combination: cheap, English-taught, geographically close, ranked respectably, and crucially compatible with Hong Kong permanent residency. The University of Macau is competitive with mid-tier Hong Kong universities on academic outcomes; MUST and UTM offer specialist strengths that are hard to replicate cheaply in Hong Kong. Add Macau to your non-JUPAS shortlist alongside one or two UK or Singapore options and you walk into August with a genuine portfolio of choices rather than a single bet on JUPAS allocation day.
