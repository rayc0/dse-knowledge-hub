Italy is one of Europe's most underrated study destinations for Hong Kong DSE students. The country hosts some of the oldest universities in the world (Bologna was founded in 1088), public tuition is dramatically lower than in the UK or US, and a growing share of bachelor's programmes are now delivered entirely in English. For DSE students who want a continental European experience without the German numerus clausus or the French dossier marathon, Italy offers a comparatively flexible path — provided you understand the difference between the Marco Polo channel, the regular international track, and the Universitaly pre-enrolment system.

This guide walks through the full 2026 application cycle: what Marco Polo actually is (and why most HK DSE students should not use it), how DSE results map to admission requirements, the Universitaly portal, tuition and ISEE-based fee discounts, the visa Type D and residence permit process, work rights during studies, and the 12-month post-graduation job-seeking permit that lets you stay in Italy to find work.

## Why Italy for DSE students

The case for Italy rests on four pillars.

**Cost.** Public university tuition typically ranges from €300 to €4,000 per academic year, and many students from low- and middle-income households pay the floor of that range after submitting an ISEE-Parificato (income equivalent) declaration. Compared to £21,000–£38,000 a year at a UK Russell Group university, the savings over a three-year bachelor's degree are substantial.

**English-taught programmes.** While Italian-language degrees still dominate the catalogue, the number of fully English-taught bachelor's (Lauree Triennali) has grown sharply over the past decade. Politecnico di Milano, Bocconi, Sapienza Università di Roma, Università di Bologna, Politecnico di Torino, Università di Padova and Università di Trento all run multiple English-medium undergraduate degrees in business, engineering, computer science, design, economics and international relations.

**Schengen mobility and lifestyle.** A residence permit issued in Italy gives you Schengen-area travel rights, which makes weekend trips, internships abroad and Erasmus+ exchanges straightforward.

**Post-study options.** Italy offers a 12-month residence permit for graduates to look for work, which can then be converted into a work permit when you find an employer.

The trade-offs are real: bureaucracy is heavy, the job market for non-Italian speakers is narrower than in the Netherlands or Germany, and salary levels in Italy are below the EU average. Italy is a strong choice for students who genuinely want to learn Italian and integrate locally, or who plan to use Italy as a low-cost EU launchpad.

## Marco Polo vs the regular international track

Most online articles aimed at Chinese students describe the **Marco Polo programme** as the standard route into Italian universities. For Hong Kong DSE students, the picture is more nuanced.

Marco Polo is a bilateral agreement signed in 2006 between the Italian and Chinese (PRC) governments specifically to facilitate Chinese students entering Italian universities. Two features matter:

1. **It is designed for PRC nationals.** Hong Kong SAR passport holders applying with DSE results are processed through the standard non-EU international student channel, not Marco Polo, even though the application portal (Universitaly) is the same.
2. **Marco Polo students must enrol in Italian-taught degrees.** Marco Polo includes a mandatory pre-academic year of intensive Italian language study (usually 10–11 months at B1/B2 level). If you intend to study in English, Marco Polo is not the right channel — you apply directly as a regular international (non-EU) student to the English-taught programme of your choice.

In practice, almost all HK DSE students choose **the regular international track with English-taught programmes**. Marco Polo only becomes relevant if you specifically want an Italian-taught degree and need a structured language preparation year — and even then, HK candidates would need to verify whether the host university extends the Marco Polo framework to non-PRC applicants on a case-by-case basis.

## DSE entry requirements

Italian public universities recognise the HKDSE as a valid secondary school qualification. Typical baseline requirements:

- **Minimum subjects.** At least four DSE subjects, including Chinese Language, English Language, Mathematics, and Liberal Studies / Citizenship and Social Development. Some universities also require at least one elective.
- **Minimum grades.** Most universities require a minimum of Level 3 in each core subject and Level 3 in two electives. More selective programmes (Bocconi, Politecnico di Milano engineering and design) may expect Level 4 or Level 5 in subjects relevant to the programme.
- **English proficiency.** For English-taught programmes, expect IELTS 6.0–6.5 overall (Bocconi: IELTS 6.5; Politecnico di Milano: IELTS 6.0 or TOEFL iBT 78). DSE English Level 4 is sometimes accepted in lieu of IELTS, but always confirm with the specific admissions office because policies vary by institution.
- **Mathematics for STEM.** STEM programmes typically require a recognised mathematics qualification. Most accept DSE Mathematics Compulsory Part at Level 4 or above; some programmes require an entrance test (TOLC) administered by CISIA.
- **Portfolio for design/architecture.** Politecnico di Milano's design school and IUAV Venezia require a portfolio and may interview candidates.

DSE results must be submitted via the official HKEAA transcript service to the Italian university's admissions office, and a **Dichiarazione di Valore (DoV)** — a declaration of equivalence issued by the Italian Consulate-General in Hong Kong — is normally required. CIMEA Diplome attestation is increasingly accepted as an alternative to the DoV, and some universities now process equivalence internally.

## The Universitaly pre-enrolment portal

Universitaly (universitaly.it) is the official MIUR portal through which all non-EU candidates submit their pre-enrolment application before applying for a visa. The flow is:

1. Receive a conditional or unconditional offer from your chosen Italian university (each university runs its own admissions cycle, often opening in November and closing between March and June 2026 for September 2026 entry).
2. Log into Universitaly and submit a pre-enrolment application linked to that offer, selecting the **regular international student** option (not Marco Polo) and the chosen programme.
3. The university validates your pre-enrolment online; the Italian Consulate-General in Hong Kong then receives your file and invites you for the visa interview.
4. Submit your visa application at the Consulate-General with the supporting documents (offer letter, DoV/CIMEA, proof of accommodation, financial means, health insurance).

Universitaly pre-enrolment for the 2026/2027 academic year typically opens in spring 2026 and closes in mid-July 2026 for most universities, though specific deadlines vary by institution. Start early: the bottleneck is almost never the academic decision — it is the consular appointment slot.

## Tuition fees, ISEE and scholarships

Italian public university tuition is income-tested through the **ISEE** (Indicatore della Situazione Economica Equivalente). For non-EU students, an ISEE-Parificato is calculated by an Italian patronato (welfare assistance office) based on documented family income and assets in Hong Kong.

Indicative annual tuition at major public universities for 2026/2027:

- **Sapienza Roma:** €0–€3,000 (means-tested), with the lowest band reserved for ISEE under €23,000.
- **Università di Bologna:** €0–€3,500 depending on ISEE and programme.
- **Politecnico di Milano:** €900–€4,000.
- **Università di Padova:** €0–€2,800.
- **Bocconi (private):** approximately €16,000–€17,000 — Bocconi is private and charges higher fees; generous merit scholarships available.

Key scholarships for HK DSE students:

- **DSU regional scholarships** (e.g. ER-GO in Emilia-Romagna, EDISU in Piemonte, LazioDisco in Lazio) — full tuition waiver plus a stipend of €5,000–€7,000 for low-income international students who meet academic milestones.
- **MAECI scholarships** from the Italian Ministry of Foreign Affairs — competitive scholarships for international students, awarded for 6 to 9 months.
- **University-specific merit awards** — Bocconi, Bologna and Politecnico di Milano all offer entrance scholarships of €6,000–€12,000 for top international applicants.

Always submit the ISEE-Parificato application as early as possible after enrolment; missing the deadline means you will be charged the maximum tuition band for that year regardless of family income.

## Visa Type D and the residence permit

Once your pre-enrolment is validated, apply for a **Visto per Studio Tipo D (long-stay study visa)** at the Italian Consulate-General in Hong Kong. Required documents typically include:

- Passport valid at least three months beyond intended stay
- Confirmed offer and Universitaly pre-enrolment receipt
- Proof of accommodation in Italy
- Proof of financial means (approximately €6,500–€7,000 per year)
- Health insurance valid in Italy and Schengen
- Round-trip flight booking and travel insurance
- DoV or CIMEA certificate

Within **8 working days of arrival in Italy**, you must apply for the **permesso di soggiorno per studio** (study residence permit) at the local post office (sportello amici dell'immigrazione) using the kit giallo. You will receive a receipt, attend a fingerprinting appointment at the Questura, and the physical permit arrives within 1–4 months. Keep the receipt safe — it is your legal proof of regular stay until the permit is issued.

The study residence permit must be renewed annually before expiry, with proof of academic progress (a minimum number of ECTS credits earned in the previous year, set by each university).

## Working during studies

Holders of the study residence permit are entitled to work **up to 20 hours per week during term** and **up to 40 hours per week during academic holidays**, capped at 1,040 hours per calendar year. Common student jobs in Italy include tutoring, hospitality, retail and English-language teaching assistance. Wages are modest — €7–€10 per hour is typical outside major cities, slightly higher in Milan and Rome — so part-time work covers small living expenses but rarely tuition.

Internships (tirocini) are widely available and often integrated into the curriculum. Paid internships with companies like Ferrari, Pirelli, Eni and Generali are competitive but accessible to international students with strong academic records and basic Italian.

## Post-graduation: the 12-month job-seeking residence permit

After completing your degree, Italy allows you to convert your study permit into a **permesso di soggiorno per attesa occupazione** (residence permit awaiting employment), which gives you up to 12 months in Italy to find work matching your qualifications. Key conditions:

- The degree must be from a MIUR-recognised Italian institution (all public universities and most accredited private universities qualify).
- You must register with the local Centro per l'Impiego (employment centre) within the application window.
- You must demonstrate financial means above the social allowance threshold (approximately €6,000 per year) and have valid health insurance.
- The permit is generally **non-renewable**; you have one year to convert to a work permit through an employer's Nulla Osta application.

Once you secure a job offer, your employer files a Nulla Osta (work authorisation) request, which converts your permit to a **permesso di soggiorno per lavoro subordinato**. After five years of legal residence in Italy (study years count partially toward this total under recent reforms), you may apply for **EU long-term residence**; after ten years of continuous legal residence, you may apply for **Italian citizenship**.

For HK DSE graduates planning to stay in Italy, the realistic strategy is:

1. **Learn Italian.** Even at English-taught programmes, conversational Italian (B1+) dramatically expands the job market beyond multinationals and tourism.
2. **Choose a sector with demand for English speakers.** Tech (Milan and Turin), fashion (Milan and Florence), automotive (Modena/Bologna), tourism management, and international business consulting are the strongest entry points.
3. **Use the university career service.** Most major Italian universities have dedicated career services with corporate relationships — Bocconi and Politecnico di Milano are particularly strong on graduate placement.

## Strengths and challenges, summarised

**Strengths**

- Public tuition often under €3,000/year, with ISEE-based discounts available
- Strong English-taught bachelor's catalogue at top universities
- Schengen mobility and Erasmus+ access during studies
- 12-month post-study residence permit with conversion path to work permit and eventual EU long-term residence
- Lower cost of living than UK, France or Netherlands in most cities outside Milan and Rome

**Challenges**

- Heavy bureaucracy: DoV/CIMEA, codice fiscale, permesso di soggiorno, ISEE-Parificato, annual permit renewals
- Job market outside multinationals expects working Italian
- Salary levels below EU average (entry-level €25,000–€32,000)
- Marco Polo branding can be misleading for HK students — the regular international track is what most DSE candidates should use

## Action checklist for the 2026/2027 cycle

1. **By September 2025:** shortlist 4–6 programmes, confirm DSE/IELTS requirements with each admissions office in writing.
2. **November 2025 – March 2026:** submit university applications (deadlines vary; Bocconi and Politecnico di Milano open earliest).
3. **After receiving offers (March–May 2026):** complete Universitaly pre-enrolment as a regular international student.
4. **April–June 2026:** request DoV from the Italian Consulate-General in Hong Kong, or apply for a CIMEA Diplome attestation.
5. **June–July 2026:** book the visa appointment at the Consulate-General, prepare financial proof and accommodation booking.
6. **August–September 2026:** travel to Italy, apply for the codice fiscale and submit the kit giallo for the residence permit within 8 working days of arrival.
7. **October 2026 onwards:** submit ISEE-Parificato application via an Italian patronato to lock in the lowest tuition band.

Italy rewards students who plan early and accept a learning curve on bureaucracy. The combination of low tuition, English-taught programmes, Schengen mobility and a structured post-study work pathway makes it one of the most cost-effective European destinations for HK DSE graduates willing to invest in the Italian context.
