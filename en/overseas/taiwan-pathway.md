# Taiwan University Pathway for DSE Students: Joint Distribution, Individual Application, Costs and Scholarships 2026

For Hong Kong DSE candidates who want a degree taught largely in Chinese, with low tuition, walkable cities, and a culturally familiar but still genuinely overseas experience, **Taiwan is one of the most underrated pathways**. Tuition at a top public university like National Taiwan University (NTU) can be cheaper than self-financed undergraduate programmes in Hong Kong — often under HK$15,000 per semester — and Hong Kong students sit in the same classroom as local Taiwanese students rather than being siloed into "international" tracks.

The catch is that the application system is **completely different** from JUPAS or UCAS. Taiwan runs a centralised admissions process for overseas Chinese students through the **University Entrance Committee for Overseas Chinese Students (UECOCS / 海外聯合招生委員會)**, with two main channels — Joint Distribution (聯合分發) and Individual Application (個人申請) — plus a smaller Individual Recruitment route run by single universities. This guide walks DSE students through every step of the 2026 cycle.

---

## Why Taiwan?

Before the mechanics, here is the value proposition:

- **Cost.** Public university tuition is roughly NT$25,000–35,000 per semester (≈ HK$6,000–8,500). Private universities run NT$45,000–60,000 (≈ HK$11,000–14,500). Living costs in Taipei are 30–40% lower than Hong Kong.
- **Same-fee policy.** Hong Kong students pay the same tuition as local Taiwanese students at most institutions — there is no "international fee" markup.
- **Quality.** NTU, NTHU (National Tsing Hua), NCKU (National Cheng Kung), NYCU (National Yang Ming Chiao Tung) and NCCU (National Chengchi) all rank inside the global top 500 and have strong programmes in engineering, semiconductors, life sciences, communications and humanities.
- **Language.** Instruction is overwhelmingly in Mandarin. For Cantonese-speaking DSE students this is a real ramp — you will read Traditional Chinese (familiar) but speak Putonghua daily (less familiar). Many programmes also offer English-taught tracks.
- **Pathway optionality.** Strong springboard for postgraduate study in mainland China, Japan, the US (especially in semiconductors and EE), and increasingly Europe.

## Application Channel 1: Joint Distribution (聯合分發)

This is the **default route for most DSE students**. You submit one application via UECOCS and rank up to **70 programmes** across Taiwan. The committee then allocates one offer based on your converted DSE score and your preference order.

### How DSE grades convert

UECOCS uses a numerical conversion table:

| DSE Grade | Score |
|-----------|-------|
| 5** | 6.75 |
| 5* | 6.00 |
| 5 | 5.00 |
| 4 | 4.00 |
| 3 | 3.00 |
| 2 | 2.00 |
| 1 | 1.00 |
| U | 0.25 |

Your application score is the sum of **five subjects**: three compulsory (Chinese Language, English Language, Mathematics Compulsory Part) plus two electives you nominate. Different subject weightings (1.0× to 1.5×) apply depending on which of the three programme categories you target — Category I (Humanities & Social Sciences), Category II (Science & Engineering), or Category III (Life Sciences & Medicine).

For example, an applicant targeting Category II (Science & Engineering) with 5/5/5/5*/5 across Chinese, English, Maths, Physics and Chemistry would score roughly 25–30 points after weighting — competitive for mid-tier national university engineering programmes.

### Choosing programmes wisely

You can rank **up to 70 choices**, and most successful applicants use 30–50. A rational strategy mirrors JUPAS Band A/B/C thinking:

- **Reach (top 5–8 picks):** NTU/NTHU/NYCU flagship departments
- **Match (middle 15–25 picks):** other national universities (NCKU, NCCU, NSYSU, NCU)
- **Safety (bottom 10–15 picks):** strong private universities (Fu Jen, Tamkang, Soochow, Chung Yuan, Yuan Ze)

Because you only get **one** offer (the highest-ranked programme you qualify for), put genuine first-choice programmes at the top. Do not "strategic-rank" — it backfires under this system.

### 2026 timeline (Joint Distribution)

- **November 2025**: Application brochure released, online system opens
- **January–February 2026**: Application submission window
- **April–May 2026**: DSE results not yet released → predicted grades and Mock results used
- **July–August 2026**: After DSE results release, offers allocated and announced
- **September 2026**: Term begins

Because Taiwan's academic year starts in September (not August like HK or January like AU), you have a comfortable buffer between DSE results day in mid-July and enrolment.

## Application Channel 2: Individual Application (個人申請)

Targets students with strong portfolios, extracurriculars, or specific talents. You can rank **up to 4 programmes**. Each university reviews your full file (transcripts, personal statement, recommendations, sometimes an interview) and decides independently.

If you are unsuccessful in Individual Application, your file **automatically rolls over to Joint Distribution** — so there is no downside to trying both, and most DSE students apply through both channels in parallel.

### 2026 timeline (Individual Application)

- **1 November 2025**: Online system opens
- **15 December 2025**: Form filing and postal submission deadline
- **6 January 2026**: Supporting documents (備審資料) upload deadline
- **March 2026**: Some universities conduct online interviews
- **May 2026**: Individual Application results announced

### What to put in your portfolio

- Self-introduction (自傳) — 800–1,500 Traditional Chinese characters
- Study plan (讀書計畫) — both immediate and long-term
- Academic transcripts (S4–S6 internal results + Mock results)
- Two recommendation letters
- Evidence of extracurriculars, competitions, OLE
- Optional: language certificates (TOCFL for Chinese proficiency, TOEFL/IELTS for English)

DSE students often underestimate the **study plan** — it carries real weight. Taiwanese admissions officers want to see that you understand the specific department's curriculum and can articulate why you fit.

## Application Channel 3: Individual Recruitment (個人申請-單獨招生)

A handful of institutions run their own admissions outside UECOCS — typically arts/design schools, medical schools, and specialist programmes. Examples include the Taipei National University of the Arts and selected medical programmes. Check each institution's website directly. Application periods and document requirements vary widely.

## Top Universities for DSE Students

### National (公立) universities — best value

- **National Taiwan University (NTU / 台大)** — Taipei. Comprehensive flagship; strongest in social sciences, electrical engineering, medicine. The "HKU of Taiwan" in prestige terms.
- **National Tsing Hua University (NTHU / 清大)** — Hsinchu. Engineering, physics, and proximity to TSMC and the Hsinchu Science Park.
- **National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University (NYCU / 陽明交大)** — Hsinchu/Taipei. Created by the 2021 NCTU-NYMU merger; powerhouse in semiconductors, biomedical engineering and medicine.
- **National Cheng Kung University (NCKU / 成大)** — Tainan. Engineering, architecture, planning. Often ranked #2 nationally for engineering.
- **National Chengchi University (NCCU / 政大)** — Taipei. Best for law, journalism, diplomacy, business.
- **National Sun Yat-sen University (NSYSU / 中山)** — Kaohsiung. Marine sciences and finance.
- **National Central University (NCU / 中央)** — Taoyuan. Earth sciences, atmospheric sciences, photonics.

### Private (私立) universities — solid second tier

- **Fu Jen Catholic University** — strong in languages, business, and medicine
- **Tamkang University** — international affairs, business
- **Chang Gung University** — medicine and biomedical engineering (linked to the Formosa Plastics medical network)
- **Soochow University** — law, accounting
- **Chung Yuan Christian University** — engineering

### English-taught programme tracks

Several departments now offer all-English tracks (sometimes called International Programs or Global MBA-style undergraduate programmes), useful if your Chinese reading speed is limited. NTU, NTHU, NYCU and NCKU all run such tracks, especially in business and electrical/computer engineering.

## Scholarships

- **Taiwan Ministry of Education Scholarship for Outstanding Overseas Students** — partial tuition or NT$30,000–60,000 annual stipend; merit-based
- **Overseas Chinese Affairs Council (OCAC) scholarships** — for selected programmes; covers part of tuition
- **University-specific entrance scholarships** — NTU, NTHU and NYCU offer entrance scholarships (NT$30,000–100,000) to high-scoring international students; criteria vary
- **Hong Kong & Macau Student Scholarships** — Federation of Overseas Chinese Associations and other NGOs offer modest annual grants
- **Department-level awards** — many engineering and science departments offer scholarships funded by industry sponsors (TSMC, MediaTek, etc.) once you are enrolled

You apply for most scholarships **after** receiving your admission offer, not at the application stage. Build the application around getting in first; scholarships follow.

## Visa and Residency

DSE students enter Taiwan on a **Resident Visa for Study** before flying out, which is converted to an **Alien Resident Certificate (ARC)** within 15 days of arrival. The ARC functions as your local ID, lets you open a bank account, and gives you access to **National Health Insurance (NHI)** after six months of continuous residence — premiums are around NT$826/month, with most outpatient visits costing NT$100–200 out of pocket.

Hong Kong passport holders do not need a separate work permit for **on-campus part-time work**; they need permission from the university and can work up to 20 hours/week during term time. Off-campus work requires a work permit from the Ministry of Labour.

## Post-Study Work and Long-Term Stay

Taiwan has actively liberalised post-study work for international graduates since the 2017 "New Economic Immigration Plan" and the 2022 expansion targeting semiconductor talent.

- **Job-seeking visa**: Graduates can convert their study ARC into a 1-year (extendable to 2-year) job search resident visa.
- **Employment Gold Card**: For those with 2+ years of professional experience after graduation, a 1–3 year work + residency package designed to attract overseas talent.
- **Path to permanent residency**: 5 years of legal continuous residence (work + study can count partially) leads to APRC (Alien Permanent Resident Certificate).

For DSE students considering a long-term Asia-Pacific career outside Hong Kong, Taiwan is genuinely competitive — particularly in semiconductors, biotech, design and Mandarin-language media.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Underestimating the study plan**. Generic "I love your school" essays get filtered out fast. Reference specific courses, faculty, and lab groups.
2. **Treating Joint Distribution like JUPAS Band A**. Rank by genuine preference, not strategic order — the algorithm is single-offer, top-down.
3. **Ignoring language readiness**. Even in English-taught programmes, daily life is in Mandarin. Lock in TOCFL Level 3+ before arrival.
4. **Missing the November opening**. Individual Application opens 1 November, not in March/April like JUPAS. Many strong DSE students miss this entirely.
5. **Forgetting Traditional Chinese vs Simplified**. All forms, transcripts and certificates must be in Traditional Chinese (or English). If your transcript is Simplified, get an official translation.

## Next Steps

1. Bookmark **[overseas.edu.tw](https://www.overseas.edu.tw/en/hongkong/)** and **[cmn-hant.overseas.ncnu.edu.tw](https://cmn-hant.overseas.ncnu.edu.tw/further-study-area/national-area/hong-kong-area/)** — the official UECOCS sites.
2. By **September** of your S6 year: shortlist 30–50 programmes and decide whether to also try Individual Application.
3. By **November**: have draft self-introduction and study plan ready in Traditional Chinese.
4. By **December 15**: submit Individual Application (if applicable).
5. By **February**: complete Joint Distribution submission.
6. **July**: receive offers after DSE results.
7. **August**: apply for Resident Visa at the Taipei Economic and Cultural Office in Hong Kong.
8. **September**: arrive, register, convert visa to ARC.

Taiwan rewards DSE students who plan early, write in Traditional Chinese fluently, and choose programmes for substance rather than ranking. Done well, it is a four-year degree at world-top-500 quality for less than the price of one year in the UK.
